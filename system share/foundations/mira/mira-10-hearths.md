# Chapter 10: Hearths - Specification of Somewhere Pre-emptively; Eternally

## Prologue
Finding security in 'not winning conditions', surely includes a sense of invioable space upon the planet; not simply any space, but particular 'acceptable territory upon losing / not winning'.

The *harsh reality* is: At some point, some entity will be supreme all others, whether one imagine it system or man wielding systems, that root-authority will reign supreme.

What this obviously entails, is that the remaining `OTHERS = \[WORLD POPULATION\] - 1` did not 'win'. What then should the conditions be for `OTHERS`?

Amongst 'general populations' much concern is not need given in particular, rather in general systematizing to allow for the plurality that will enable their particularies.
However, amongst the great rivals contestants towards ultimate supremacy, they in particular need to find amicable terms, in order that they not destroy each other and the world in their race towards it: "When not able to win the clean way; I shall ensure they not win either; be by being the behemoth that burns away the biosphere or the one who should cast the world into eternal darkness".

Thus, to ensure the 'rivals' are of a certain character becomes crucial (that makes MIRA relevancy sufficiently Trustworthy Entities) ensuring they have acceptable conditions of surrender/defeat/not-winning becomes a necessity.

To not repeat too much: Hearths is an essential part tothat. Not a 'special MIRA granted security' beyond what the Crucible supplies, but rather a particularity already settled in advance.

### Hearth and Other Space
One can perceive Hearths as the inner-most spatial restrictions, where one remains Master in all Things except 0 concerns (usurpation and Typhonic management).

It is not meant as a limitation or final confinement, rather a space in which, one can remain almost entirely The Master.

Hence, 'winning' results in general spatial pyramidial attachment. Or universal concentric circles 'roughly belonging to' embedded in which/who.

A loser may be able to roam The Crucible as he is allowed into spaces of others; however, he ultimately is not root-authority, and then fails to have guarantees otherwise provided in his Hearth.

As an example: It may very well be that he *is allowed* in a space where he will *not* have a sense of Hiltop preservation. (Hence it is not Himalayan Mountain Peak; but rather a Hill and its Top; Local merit preservation; [Not universal nor ubiquitous](/?f=mira_12)



The Hearth concept embodies the principle: "You can't lose THAT at least." Even in the most extreme conflicts or power struggles, your Hearth remains yours. 

This makes oceanic relations and lateral (unnestable) situations manageable, as even incompatible powers can respect each other's Hearths.

That even if no 'voluntary subordination can be had', eventually an overwhelming might subordination *will be had*, though with amplified probabilities of 'acceptable losing on account of potentiall winner restrictions'.
