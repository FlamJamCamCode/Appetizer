Title: Companion — Tit‑for‑Tat, Plans, Scripts, Governance, and Tests for Commanding Care

Preface: This is the operations layer to accompany @23d Commanding Care — A Critique of the West.md. Heat → handles: arguments that survive scrutiny, plans that survive contact, scripts that win first "yes", guardrails that keep legitimacy, and tests that reveal truth.

A) Tit‑for‑Tat — Claim, Counter, Move
1) "Why ask money to cheer up a child?"
   Tat: Price‑gates route liability, not meaning. Design choice, not destiny.
   Move: Duty‑gates for survival; civic duty ledger; public duty budgets.
2) "Certification keeps people safe; no uncertified clowning."
   Tat: Safety matters; cartelization hides inside paperwork.
   Move: Walls‑With‑Doors: supervised competence corridors; outcomes → credentials.
3) "Doctors only treat for pay."
   Tat: Distinction lashed to dollars produces selection effects.
   Move: 30‑day pediatric duty pledge; outcomes ledger; honors for duty.
4) "No IP rents, no cures."
   Tat: Current rails reward monopoly over milestone.
   Move: Open‑Cure Charters: 100% reinvestment, sunset exclusivity, public bounties.
5) "No women in it; status desert → burnout."
   Tat: Status markets reward spectacle.
   Move: Multi‑currency honors + heritage access pegged to outcomes. Cf. @24g.
6) "Volunteers can’t scale; hospitals won’t allow it."
   Tat: Ad‑hoc doesn’t scale; corridors do.
   Move: MoUs with scoped roles, safety, supervision, logging, and audits.

B) Operational Programs — Minimal, Viable, Aggressive
Program 1 — Pediatric Care Commons (Ward Uplift Corridor)
- Objective: Measurable uplift in affect/adherence/family resilience under supervision.
- Steps: (1) MoU; (2) Roles; (3) Protocols; (4) Shifts; (5) Metrics; (6) Audits.
- Roles: floor play lead; logistics runner; family liaison; remote storytime lead.
- Metrics: affect score, minutes engaged, adherence proxy, readmission proxy.

Program 2 — Talent Transfer Fellowship (From Funnels to Survival)
- Objective: Redirect top talent to cure engineering (bench/tooling/ops).
- Selection: open trials; blind scoring where possible; reproducibility as a deliverable.
- Tracks: comp bio/tooling; clinical ops; bio‑manufacturing/QA; pathway analytics.
- Structure: 12–36 months; milestone reviews; chartered placements.

Program 3 — Open‑Cure Charter (Reinvestment Over Rent)
- Legal: 100% profit → same indication; time‑bounded exclusivity; open‑protocol cadence; patient‑years saved as KPI.
- Governance: public observer seats; quarterly reinvestment ledger; independent stats sign‑off; clawbacks.

C) Governance & Legitimacy
- IRB/ethics alignment: pre‑reviewed corridor protocol templates; data minimization; opt‑out always respected.
- Liability wrapper: corridor operator carries insurance; volunteers operate only within scope; hospitals co‑sign scope and escalation trees.
- Data stewardship: PII never leaves hospital; only de‑identified aggregates and timestamps; signed logs.
- Anti‑capture: conflict disclosures; rotation of auditors; publication of negative results and incident reports.

D) Risk Register (excerpt)
- Infection control breach → mitigation: PPE training, checklists, audit drills, stop‑work triggers.
- Boundary violations → mitigation: role scripts, chaperone rules, swift removal protocols.
- Metric gaming → mitigation: multi‑metric success gates; external audit; random spot checks.
- Volunteer burnout → mitigation: shift caps, debriefs, peer support, status/honor feedback loops.

G) Tests — Make Rails Legible
- Duty Test: pledge rate; schedule efficiency; completion; ward outcomes; clinician NPS.
- Budget Ballot Test: 3 competing cure roadmaps; public allocation; milestone hit rate; cost per patient‑year saved.
- Corridor Test: incidents vs baseline; affect/adherence delta; throughput; audit outcomes.
- Reinvestment Test: % revenue reinvested; time‑to‑milestone; openness cadence; independent verification.
- Community/Status Test: retention of women/URM; honors; 12‑month persistence.

X) Scheduling Heuristic (pseudo‑code)
assignDutyBlocks(physicians, wardDemands) {
  score(p, s) = specialtyMatch(p,s) + scarcityBonus(s) + pastDutyBalance(p)
  while exists unfilledSlots:
    s = selectHighestPrioritySlot()
    p = argmax_p score(p, s) subject to availability/constraints
    assign(p, s); update calendars and balances
}
