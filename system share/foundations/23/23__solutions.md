- Rent: $1,500 (landlord extraction)

**Precedent**: Military barracks provide housing, food, healthcare at fraction of civilian cost. NSS scales this to civilian population.

### Layer 2: Trust Networks - Competence Over Credentials

**Current credential system**:
- 12-year training delay (age 16-28)
- $300K-$500K education cost (debt trap)
- Test-taking selection (not competence)
- Professional monopoly (illegal to practice without license)

**Trust network system**:
- **Proof of Person** (PoP): Real human verification, pseudonym binding
- **Proof of Utility** (PoU): Outcome-based reputation (did they deliver?)
- **Trust rankings**: Performance history visible, searchable
- **Open access**: Anyone can attempt, competence gates advancement

**Mechanism**:
1. Person attempts task (healing, teaching, building)
2. Recipient confirms outcome (healed, learned, built)
3. Trust network records (blockchain-verified, reputation grows)
4. Future access based on track record (not credentials)

**What changes:**
- Best healers (by outcomes) get highest access
- Credentials irrelevant (what matters is cure rates)
- Open entry (no $300K barrier, no 12-year delay)
- Natural selection (good performers rise, bad performers exit)

**Precedent**: eBay seller ratings, Uber driver ratings, GitHub contribution graphs—all outcome-based reputation systems.

### Layer 3: Zero-Profit Vital Domains

**Current pharma model**:
- $1B drug development cost
- Patent monopoly (20 years)
- Price extraction ($100K/year treatments)
- Profit maximization (not cure optimization)
- **Result**: Trillion-dollar industry, diseases managed not cured

**Zero-profit model**:
- All research costs funded by duty/bounties (public/philanthropic)
- Zero profit allowed (100% reinvestment in R&D)
- Open formulas (no patents on vital medicines)
- Cure optimization (incentivized by bounties + status)
- **Result**: Diseases cured rapidly, treatments cost manufacturing only

**Mechanism**:
1. Government/philanthropists fund research via bounties
2. Researchers compete for bounties (milestone-based)
3. Successful cures rewarded with: status, pregnancy promises, heritage locations
4. Formulas released openly (manufacturing at cost)
5. Profits legally prohibited (charter requirement)

**What changes:**
- $1,000/month cancer drug → $50/month (manufacturing cost only)
- Research optimizes for cures (not chronic management)
- 100% of funding reaches research (zero profit extraction)
- Best researchers motivated by status/legacy (not money)

**Precedent**: Jonas Salk polio vaccine (gave it away, saved millions). NASA (zero-profit space exploration). University research (discoveries not patented).

**What changes:**

- Status inversion: Healers high-status, marketers low-status

**Precedent**: Female choice in mate selection (universal across species). Historical hero worship (women sought warriors, leaders, inventors). Modern dating (women prefer successful men—we just redefine "successful").

### Layer 5: Triadization - Voluntary Sovereignty

**Current democracy**:
- Forced integration (incompatible groups under same rules)
- Compromise cascade (dilutes everyone's preferences)
- Short time horizons (elections every 2-4 years)
- Will-disruption (nobody gets what they want)

**Triadization system**:
- **Voluntary association**: Rally around compatible leaders/values
- **Exit rights**: Leave incompatible Triads freely
- **Competing systems**: Multiple governance models in parallel
- **Natural selection**: Successful Triads grow, failures shrink

**Mechanism**:
1. Leader proposes vision + values + rules (Triad charter)
2. Compatible people rally (voluntary membership)
3. Triad establishes sovereignty (internal rules)
4. Continuous exit option (leave if incompatible)
5. Performance-based growth (success attracts, failure repels)

**What changes:**
- No forced compromise (find compatible Triad or start new one)
- Long time horizons (leaders can plan 20-30 years)
- Clear accountability (leader responsible for outcomes)
- Will-coherence (compatible values enable action)

**Precedent**: Corporate governance (employees choose companies). Religious communities (voluntary association). Startups (founder vision, aligned team).

### Layer 6: Competence Training - Time Compression

**Current MD training**:
- 4 years undergrad (mostly irrelevant)
- 4 years medical school (2 years classroom, 2 years rotations)
- 4 years residency (hands-on but padded with hazing)
- **Total**: 12 years, $300K-$500K debt

**Competence training**:
- **Year 1**: Intensive fundamentals (anatomy, physiology, pathology, pharmacology)
  - 60 hours/week, competence-gated (can't advance until mastery)
  - Hands-on from day 1 (cadaver lab, supervised patients)
- **Year 2**: Specialized or general practice track
  - 12 months supervised patient care
  - Outcomes tracked (diagnostic accuracy, treatment effectiveness)
- **Year 3**: Independent practice with mentorship
  - Practicing as healer
  - Continuous outcome tracking (cure rates, satisfaction)
  - Rankings visible (social currency access based on performance)

**Total**: 3 years for general practice, 5 years for specialists

**What changes:**
- 75% time reduction (3 years vs 12 years)
- $0 debt (training funded by society)
- Selection for competence (outcomes matter, not test scores)
- Continuous improvement (rankings incentivize excellence)

**Precedent**: Combat medics (6-12 months training, perform emergency surgery in war zones). Coding bootcamps (3-6 months, produce professional developers). Apprenticeship systems (learn by doing, not classroom).
