## Introduction: Correcting the Surface Understanding

**The error of modern scholarship**: Fixating on attempts to explain (family, romantic, friendship) rather than understanding the **deeper meanings** of Greek love concepts.

**The ancient Greeks understood**:
- **Storge**: Parent → Child (unidirectional protective flow)
- **Costorge/Gaias**: Child → Parent (love of your world/origin)
- **Philia**: Brotherly/peer love (Plato's "non-envy sufficient to enable having a state together")
- **Eros**: Masculine-feminine spiritual completion, creation itself, One becoming Many, Two becoming One
- **Erotic**: Sexual subset within Eros
- **Pragma**: Gain-related love (derivative, amplifies other types)
- **Mania**: Infatuation moments within Eros (hormone-driven teenage madness)
- **Agape**: Divine universal love (finding beauty in negative spaces, loving despite negatives)
- **Autophilia**: Self-love (excess from belief in one's excellence = Narcissism)

**This document analyzes** these proper meanings through friend-enemy and power lenses.
