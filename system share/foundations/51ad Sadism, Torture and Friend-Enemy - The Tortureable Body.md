## Introduction: The Enemy as Tortureable

**Central Question**: Who can be tortured, and why?

**The friend-enemy answer**: Those designated as enemies become **tortureable**—stripped of protections that prevent friends from being subjected to extreme violence. Torture operates through friend-enemy logic:

- **Friends**: Protected bodies (cannot be tortured without scandal, outrage, justice)
- **Enemies**: Exposed bodies (can be tortured with impunity, legal sanction, or public approval)

**This document analyzes**:
1. How friend-enemy distinctions create categories of tortureable bodies
2. Theoretical frameworks for understanding torture as political enmity
3. The psychology and philosophy of sadistic domination
4. Who tortures whom and under what justifications
5. The relationship between sovereignty, enmity, and the right to torture
6. Resistance to and abolition of torture

**Unique theoretical perspectives from thinkers who specifically analyze torture, pain, the body, and violence.**

## ELAINE SCARRY: THE BODY IN PAIN - TORTURE AS WORLD DESTRUCTION

### Torture Unmakes the Victim's World, Makes the Torturer's Reality

**Key Work:**
- *The Body in Pain: The Making and Unmaking of the World* (1985)

**Core Thesis:**

Elaine Scarry (1946-) provides perhaps the most profound philosophical analysis of torture, arguing that torture is fundamentally about **unmaking the victim's world** while **making the torturer's power real**. The enemy is not just physically harmed but ontologically destroyed—their reality, language, and humanity unmade.

**Key Concepts:**

1. **Pain as World-Destroying**:
   - Severe pain destroys language (cannot be expressed in words)
   - Pain fills entire consciousness (obliterates all else)
   - Victim's world collapses to single point: the pain
   - **Enemy reduced to pure suffering** (all identity, world, meaning erased)

2. **Torture as Inversion**:
   - Normal objects become weapons (chair becomes instrument of torture)
   - Victim's needs weaponized (hunger, thirst, exhaustion, need for toilet)
   - Victim's body betrays them (involuntary responses—screaming, soiling, breaking)
   - **Enemy's world turned against them**

3. **The Confession as Fiction**:
   - Torture doesn't extract truth
   - Produces confession (victim will say anything)
   - But real purpose: **Demonstrate torturer's power**
   - Confession proves: Torturer can make victim say/do anything
   - **Enemy's voice becomes torturer's voice**

4. **Pain Destroys Language**:
   - Torture reduces victim to pre-language state
   - Screams replace words
   - "Question and answer" torture mimics language but destroys it
   - **Enemy loses humanity through loss of language**

5. **Making Reality**:
   - Torture makes torturer's regime "real"
   - The confession, screams, submission prove power exists
   - Regime "creates reality" through destroying victim's reality
   - **Friend-enemy through creation/destruction of worlds**

**Friend-Enemy Implications:**

1. **The Tortureable Enemy**:
   - Friends have worlds (language, meaning, relationships, future)
   - Enemies have worlds **unmade** through torture
   - **Torture = ontological enmity** (destroying being itself)

2. **Asymmetry is Absolute**:
   - Torturer has all power (makes reality)
   - Victim has no power (reality destroyed)
   - **Perfect friend-enemy asymmetry**: Creator vs. destroyed

3. **The Civilized vs. The Tortured**:
   - Those who torture claim civilization (law, order, truth)
   - Those tortured reduced to pre-civilized (screaming, helpless)
   - **Friend-enemy through civilization** (who makes world vs. whose world is unmade)

4. **Language as Friend-Enemy Marker**:
   - Friends articulate (have voice, representation, speech)
   - Enemies reduced to screams (pre-linguistic, non-human)
   - **Torture strips enemy of language** = strips of humanity

5. **Confession Makes Regime Real**:
   - Tortured enemy confesses to regime's narrative
   - This "proves" regime is right (even though confession forced)
   - **Enemy's broken voice validates torturer's world**

**Examples:**

**CIA Torture:**
- "Enhanced interrogation" (euphemism showing language inversion)
- Waterboarding (victim's need to breathe weaponized)
- Sleep deprivation (basic need weaponized)
- Confessions produced (but information unreliable)
- Real purpose: Make US power real, make "terrorist" confess
- Friend-enemy: US (world-makers) vs. terrorists (worlds destroyed)

**Inquisition:**
- Torture for confessions of heresy
- Confessions proved Church right (heretics admitted guilt)
- Pain destroyed victim's ability to maintain their truth
- **Friend-enemy**: Church makes reality vs. heretic's reality destroyed

**Political Torture:**
- Dictatorships torture dissidents
- Force confession of crimes (usually fabricated)
- Confession broadcast (proves regime's narrative)
- Dissenter's world destroyed, regime's world made real
- Friend-enemy: Regime (truth) vs. dissident (world unmade)

**Scarry vs. Foucault:**

- **Foucault**: Torture as spectacle of sovereign power (display)
- **Scarry**: Torture as world-unmaking (ontological destruction)
- **Foucault**: Power productive
- **Scarry**: Torture purely destructive (of victim's world)

**Scarry vs. Agamben:**

- **Agamben**: Homo sacer (juridically excluded)
- **Scarry**: Torture victim (ontologically destroyed)
- **Agamben**: Enemy outside law
- **Scarry**: Enemy's world unmade

**Key Insight for Comprehensive Analysis:**

Scarry reveals torture as **ultimate enmity**—not just physical harm but destruction of victim's entire world. Language destroyed (reduced to screams), meaning destroyed (all collapses to pain), relationships destroyed (tortured in isolation). The enemy through torture becomes **pure suffering without world**. Torturer makes their reality real by unmaking victim's reality. Friend-enemy becomes ontological: Those whose worlds exist vs. those whose worlds are destroyed.

**Critical Addition**: Provides **phenomenological analysis** of torture as world-destruction. Explains why torture doesn't actually get reliable information (not its purpose), why confessions produced (make torturer's reality real), why pain is indescribable (destroys language), and why torture is ultimate form of enmity (unmakes human world). Shows friend-enemy as **world-making vs. world-unmaking**. Essential for understanding torture's actual function (power demonstration, not information extraction).

## DARIUS REJALI: TORTURE AND DEMOCRACY - CLEAN TORTURE AND THE DEMOCRATIC ENEMY

### Democracies Torture Too, Just Leave No Marks

**Key Work:**
- *Torture and Democracy* (2007)

**Core Thesis:**

Darius Rejali demonstrates that **democracies torture systematically**, but develop "clean torture" (methods leaving no visible marks) to avoid accountability. Democratic friend-enemy operates through **invisible torture**—the enemy is tortured but in ways that can be denied, hidden from public, and leave no photographic evidence.

**Key Concepts:**

1. **Clean Torture**:
   - Methods that leave no scars (stress positions, temperature torture, sleep deprivation, sensory deprivation)
   - Versus "scarring torture" (beating, burning, cutting—leaves evidence)
   - **Democratic innovation**: Torture without proof
   - **Enemy tortured invisibly** (friends never see marks)

2. **Stealth Torture**:
   - Done in secret (black sites, offshore prisons, rendition)
   - Denied officially
   - Victims cannot prove it happened (no scars)
   - **Friend-enemy**: Friends don't know enemies are being tortured

3. **The Democratic Constraint**:
   - Democracies can't openly torture (public wouldn't accept)
   - So torture secretly with clean methods
   - **Friend-enemy managed through invisibility**
   - Friends protected from seeing what happens to enemies

4. **Torture Creep**:
   - Starts with "exceptional" cases (ticking bomb)
   - Spreads to routine (all enemies eventually tortureable)
   - Methods escalate (clean → scarring as desensitization occurs)
   - **Friend-enemy expands**: More people designated tortureable

5. **Monitoring and Innovation**:
   - Torturers share techniques internationally
   - Democratic police train dictatorships
   - Clean methods spread globally
   - **Friend-enemy knowledge transfer**: Democracies teach allies to torture cleanly

**Friend-Enemy Implications:**

1. **The Invisible Enemy**:
   - Democracies torture enemies invisibly
   - Public doesn't see (black sites, rendition, clean methods)
   - **Friend-enemy hidden** from democratic oversight

2. **The Terroristenemy**:
   - Post-9/11: "Terrorists" designated tortureable
   - Not POWs (protected by Geneva Conventions)
   - Not criminals (protected by constitutional rights)
   - New category: **Unlawful enemy combatant** (tortureable)

3. **Offshore Torture**:
   - Guantanamo (not US soil, different rules)
   - Black sites (CIA secret prisons)
   - Rendition (send enemies to countries that torture openly)
   - **Geographic friend-enemy**: Torture enemies outside friendly territory

4. **Euphemism and Denial**:
   - "Enhanced interrogation" not "torture"
   - "Stress positions" not "torture"
   - **Language hides enmity**: Call it something else, torture becomes acceptable

5. **The Tortured Are Always Enemies**:
   - Never torture your own (citizens, friends)
   - Always torture theirs (foreigners, terrorists, enemies)
   - Rare exception proves rule (when citizen tortured, it's scandal)
   - **Friend-enemy determines torturability absolutely**

**Examples:**

**CIA Black Sites:**
- Secret prisons (Poland, Romania, Thailand, etc.)
- Waterboarding, stress positions, temperature torture, sleep deprivation
- No marks left, no trials, no oversight
- Friend-enemy: Americans (protected) vs. terrorists (tortured secretly)

**British in Northern Ireland (1970s):**
- "Five techniques" (hooding, stress positions, white noise, sleep deprivation, food deprivation)
- No marks, but severe psychological damage
- European Court ruled it torture (1978)
- Friend-enemy: British vs. IRA (clean torture to avoid accountability)

**French in Algeria:**
- Systematic torture (gégène—field telephone electric shock)
- Clean method (no marks)
- Widespread but officially denied
- Friend-enemy: French vs. Algerian resisters (invisible torture)

**US Police:**
- "Third degree" tactics
- Stress positions, temperature torture
- Psychological torture (threats, sensory manipulation)
- No marks, hard to prove
- Friend-enemy: Police vs. suspects (especially racialized)

**Rejali vs. Popular Belief:**

- **Popular belief**: Dictatorships torture, democracies don't
- **Rejali**: Democracies torture more than dictatorships (but hide it better)
- **Popular belief**: Torture is savage, primitive
- **Rejali**: Modern torture is scientific, clean, innovative

**Rejali vs. Scarry:**

- **Scarry**: Phenomenology of pain (world-unmaking)
- **Rejali**: Politics of torture (why democracies use clean methods)
- **Scarry**: Victim's experience
- **Rejali**: Institutional innovation
- Both: Torture doesn't work for information (different purposes)

**Key Insight for Comprehensive Analysis:**

Rejali reveals **democratic torture**—clean methods leaving no marks, done in secret, denied officially. Democracies designate certain enemies (terrorists, insurgents) as tortureable but must hide it from friendly citizens. Friend-enemy operates through **visibility**: Friends must not see enemies being tortured (would create moral crisis). So torture goes offshore, underground, leaves no evidence. The enemy is tortured, but invisibly.

**Critical Addition**: Provides **empirical analysis** of democratic torture. Explains CIA black sites (offshore torture), clean techniques (no scars), rendition (send to others to torture), and euphemisms ("enhanced interrogation"). Shows democracies torture systematically but innovate methods to hide from friendly citizens. Friend-enemy through **visibility management**—enemies tortured out of sight. Essential for understanding post-9/11 torture, police torture, and how democracies torture while maintaining denial.

## JEAN AMÉRY: AT THE MIND'S LIMITS - THE VICTIM'S PERSPECTIVE

### Torture Destroys Trust in the World Forever

**Key Work:**
- *At the Mind's Limits: Contemplations by a Survivor on Auschwitz and its Realities* (1966)

**Core Thesis:**

Jean Améry (1912-1978), Austrian writer and Holocaust survivor who was tortured by Gestapo, provides the definitive **victim's perspective** on torture. His central insight: Torture destroys **trust in the world**—the fundamental confidence that others won't hurt you. Once tortured, you can never trust again. The enemy is not just physically harmed but existentially isolated from humanity forever.

**Key Concepts:**

1. **The First Blow**:
   - The moment torturer first hits you
   - Shatters trust that world is safe
   - Realize: Another human wants to hurt you
   - **Enemy revealed through first blow**: You are alone

2. **Trust in the World Destroyed**:
   - Pre-torture: Implicit faith that others recognize your humanity
   - Post-torture: Certainty that you can be reduced to meat
   - **World becomes permanently hostile**
   - Cannot trust anyone ever again

3. **The Torturer's Sovereignty**:
   - Torturer decides your reality
   - Can make you scream, break, confess to anything
   - **Absolute power over you**
   - You learn you have no sovereignty over your own body/mind

4. **Irrecoverability**:
   - Améry argues: Cannot fully recover from torture
   - Some wounds don't heal
   - Suicidal ideation persists (Améry killed himself 1978, 33 years after liberation)
   - **Enemy status is permanent** (tortured once = existentially marked forever)

5. **Resentment (Ressentiment)**:
   - Not forgiveness but permanent resentment
   - Tortured person cannot and should not forgive
   - Demands world acknowledge the horror
   - **Friend-enemy permanent**: Cannot be reconciled after torture

**Friend-Enemy Implications:**

1. **Torture Makes Enmity Permanent**:
   - Before torture: Enmity might be temporary, political, resolvable
   - After torture: **Enmity becomes existential, irreconcilable**
   - Améry: Once tortured, you and torturer are enemies forever
   - No forgiveness possible (or desirable)

2. **Trust as Friend Marker**:
   - Friends are those you trust (won't hurt you)
   - Enemies are those revealed through violation of trust
   - **Torture is ultimate violation**: Proves definitively they are enemy
   - Can never be friends after (trust destroyed)

3. **The Isolated Victim**:
   - Torture isolates absolutely
   - Victim realizes: No one will save them
   - World is indifferent to their suffering
   - **Friend-enemy**: Victim vs. entire world (which permitted this)

4. **The German as Eternal Enemy**:
   - Améry (Austrian Jew) tortured by Germans
   - Could never see Germans as friends again
   - Even "good Germans" were enemies (allowed it to happen)
   - **Collective enmity**: Not just torturer but nation that tortured

5. **Resentment as Ethics**:
   - Victim should resent (not forgive)
   - Resentment keeps memory alive
   - Prevents normalization
   - **Friend-enemy**: Maintain enmity to prevent forgetting

**Examples:**

**Gestapo Torture:**
- Améry tortured for resistance activities
- Hung from wrists (strappado)
- Shoulders dislocated
- Experienced world-collapse (pain fills everything)
- Friend-enemy: Realized Germans were absolute enemies (would kill him)

**Abu Ghraib Victims:**
- Iraqi detainees tortured by US soldiers
- Even after release: Trust destroyed
- Cannot trust Americans, often cannot trust anyone
- Friend-enemy: US made permanent enemies through torture

**Survivors of Dictatorships:**
- Argentina, Chile, Brazil—torture survivors
- Decades later: Still traumatized, still resentful
- Cannot forgive, cannot forget
- Friend-enemy: Perpetual (torturer and victim never reconcile)

**Améry vs. Frankl:**

- **Frankl**: Auschwitz survivor who found meaning, forgave
- **Améry**: Cannot find meaning in torture, refuses forgiveness
- **Frankl**: Reconciliation possible
- **Améry**: Enmity is permanent, should be permanent
- Different responses to same horror

**Améry vs. Levinas:**

- **Levinas**: Face of Other creates infinite responsibility
- **Améry**: Torturer denies your face, you cannot maintain responsibility toward them
- **Levinas**: Ethics precedes politics
- **Améry**: Torture destroys possibility of ethics (creates permanent political enmity)

**Key Insight for Comprehensive Analysis:**

Améry reveals torture as **existential enmity**—destroys trust in world, creates permanent isolation, makes reconciliation impossible. The tortured person and torturer are enemies forever. Trust, once broken through torture, cannot be restored. Friend-enemy after torture is absolute, irreconcilable, permanent. The victim learns definitively: These people are your enemies, the world permitted it, you are alone. This makes torture not just physical pain but **ontological destruction of possibility of friendship**.

**Critical Addition**: Provides **victim phenomenology** of torture. Explains why torture creates permanent trauma (trust destroyed), why forgiveness impossible (resentment is ethical), why tortured people remain hostile to perpetrator nations (collective enmity), and why torture should never be normalized (creates irreversible existential damage). Shows friend-enemy as **created permanently through torture**—torturer and victim can never be friends, nations that torture and nations tortured maintain enmity across generations. Essential for understanding torture victim's perspective and ethics of resentment.

## KLAUS THEWELEIT: MALE FANTASIES - FASCIST SADISM AND THE FEMALE BODY AS ENEMY

### The Woman-Flood and Armor-Male

**Key Work:**
- *Male Fantasies* (1977-1978, two volumes)

**Core Thesis:**

Klaus Theweleit (1942-) analyzes Freikorps (proto-Nazi paramilitary) writings, diaries, and novels to reveal the **psychological structure of fascist masculinity**. He argues fascist men experienced women, fluidity, and "the feminine" as **existential threats** requiring violent destruction. Friend-enemy operates through gender: The armored male (friend to himself) vs. the flowing female (enemy to be penetrated, destroyed, rigidified).

**Key Concepts:**

1. **The Armored Male**:
   - Fascist man as machine, hardened, armored
   - Fear of internal dissolution (fluidity, emotion, desire)
   - Must be hard, rigid, controlled
   - **Friend-enemy**: Hard male (self) vs. soft/fluid (Other, enemy)

2. **The Woman-Flood**:
   - Women experienced as threatening flood
   - Femininity as dissolution, chaos, engulfment
   - Red Flood (communism, revolution) coded feminine
   - **Enemy as flood**: Must be dammed, dried up, destroyed

3. **Two Types of Women**:
   - **White Woman**: Asexual, pure, nurse, mother, sister (friend, but neutered)
   - **Red Woman**: Sexual, communist, whore, threatening (absolute enemy)
   - Friend-enemy through femininity: Desexualized friend vs. sexual enemy

4. **Violence as Hardening**:
   - Fascist violence makes male hard (literally and figuratively)
   - Killing makes soldier into man
   - Penetrating enemy with bayonet (phallic) hardens self
   - **Friend-enemy through violence**: I become friend to myself by destroying enemy

5. **The Body of the Enemy**:
   - Female communist as ultimate enemy (sexual + political threat)
   - Must be penetrated (raped) and destroyed (killed)
   - **Sexual violence as political**: Destroying threatening femininity

**Friend-Enemy Implications:**

1. **Gender as Friend-Enemy**:
   - Fascist male sees women as threats (flood, dissolution)
   - Must be controlled (white woman) or destroyed (red woman)
   - **Gendered friend-enemy**: Armored male vs. flowing female

2. **Sexual Violence as Political Victory**:
   - Raping enemy women = defeating enemy
   - Rape proves mastery (penetrate, destroy, harden self)
   - **Friend-enemy sexually enacted**: Violence against women's bodies

3. **The Communist-Woman**:
   - Communism coded feminine (flood, chaos, masses)
   - Female communists doubly enemy (woman + political)
   - Must be destroyed to prevent engulfment
   - **Friend-enemy intensified at intersection** (gender + politics)

4. **Violence Creates Identity**:
   - Fascist male becomes self through destroying Other
   - **Friend to self through enmity to Other**
   - Violence is self-creation
   - Friend-enemy: Created through violent differentiation

5. **Fear as Motivation**:
   - Fear of own dissolution (becoming fluid, losing boundaries)
   - Project fear onto women/communists (they're the threat)
   - Destroy them to destroy fear
   - **Friend-enemy through projection**: Own feared internal state externalizes as enemy

**Examples:**

**Freikorps in Germany (1918-1923):**
- Proto-Nazi paramilitaries fighting communists
- Extreme violence against female communists
- Rosa Luxemburg: Murdered, body thrown in canal (communist woman = ultimate enemy)
- Writings describe women as floods, dirt, chaos
- Friend-enemy: Armored soldiers vs. female-coded revolution

**Rape in War:**
- Systematic rape during genocides (Bosnia, Rwanda, Bangladesh)
- Women's bodies as battlefield
- Rape as victory over enemy nation
- Friend-enemy: Male victors vs. enemy women (vessels of enemy nation)

**Fascist Aesthetics:**
- Hard, rigid, steel, clean lines (friendly)
- Soft, fluid, organic, messy (enemy)
- Architecture, art, bodies all must be hardened
- Friend-enemy: Through aesthetic (rigid vs. fluid)

**Misogyny in Authoritarian Movements:**
- Women as threats to male order
- Must be controlled (traditional roles) or destroyed (if resist)
- Friend-enemy: Authoritarian men vs. autonomous women

**Theweleit vs. Fanon:**

- **Fanon**: Colonized as feminized (passive) by colonizer
- **Theweleit**: Fascist feminizes enemy (communist) to destroy
- Both: Feminization as enmity tactic

**Theweleit vs. Federici:**

- **Federici**: Witch hunts targeted autonomous women
- **Theweleit**: Fascists fear/destroy female autonomy
- Both: Women's autonomy creates enmity (in certain male psychologies)

**Key Insight for Comprehensive Analysis:**

Theweleit reveals **gendered sadistic enmity**—fascist masculinity experiences women and femininity as existential threats (flood, dissolution, chaos) requiring violent destruction. Friend-enemy operates through gender: Hard/armored/male (friend to self) vs. soft/flowing/female (enemy to be penetrated, destroyed, rigidified). Sexual violence becomes political (destroying enemy women's bodies = defeating enemy). This explains systematic rape in war, femicide, and misogynistic violence as expressions of friend-enemy through gendered sadism.

**Critical Addition**: Provides **psychoanalytic-political analysis** of gendered sadism. Explains systematic rape in genocides (enemy women's bodies as battlefields), fascist aesthetics (hard vs. fluid), violence against female communists/revolutionaries (doubly enemy), and misogynistic violence as political (destroying threatening femininity). Shows friend-enemy as **gendered through male fear of dissolution**. Essential for understanding sexual violence in war, femicide, and fascist psychology.

## SUSAN SONTAG: REGARDING THE PAIN OF OTHERS - SPECTATORSHIP OF ENEMY SUFFERING

### Photography Makes Torture Visible, Creates Distance

**Key Works:**
- *On Photography* (1977)
- *Regarding the Pain of Others* (2003)

**Core Thesis:**

Susan Sontag (1933-2004) analyzes how **photography of suffering** creates complex relationship between viewers and victims. Images of torture/death turn suffering into spectacle, create aesthetic distance, and can either mobilize outrage or produce numbness. Friend-enemy operates through **who we look at suffering** (enemies' pain photographed, displayed) vs. **who we look away from** (friends' pain too disturbing to see).

**Key Concepts:**

1. **The Photographed Body**:
   - Camera makes suffering into object
   - Victim becomes image (not person)
   - **Enemy photographable** (their pain can be displayed)
   - Friends rarely photographed in agony (too disturbing)

2. **Distance Through Image**:
   - Photo creates safety (viewer not in danger)
   - Suffering becomes aesthetic (composition, lighting)
   - **Enemy's pain becomes art/document** (consumable by friends)

3. **The Atrocity Image**:
   - War photography shows enemy dead, wounded
   - Two functions:
     - **Against war**: "Look what war does" (humanizes enemy)
     - **For war**: "Look what they did" (dehumanizes enemy, justifies revenge)
   - Same image, opposite uses

4. **Pornography of Pain**:
   - Some suffering images consumed for pleasure
   - Lynching photos (white Americans collected as postcards)
   - Abu Ghraib (guards posing with tortured prisoners)
   - **Enemy suffering as entertainment**

5. **Compassion Fatigue**:
   - Too many images of suffering → numbness
   - First photo shocks, thousandth doesn't
   - **Enemy suffering normalized through repetition**

**Friend-Enemy Implications:**

1. **Whose Pain is Photographed**:
   - Enemy suffering: Photographed, displayed, circulated
   - Friend suffering: Too disturbing (suppressed or not taken)
   - **Friend-enemy through visibility**: Enemies made spectacle

2. **The Trophy Photo**:
   - Perpetrators photograph their torture
   - Lynching photos (white Americans posing with Black victims)
   - Abu Ghraib (soldiers posing with prisoners)
   - **Enemy suffering as pride** (photograph proves domination)

3. **Atrocity Photography Weaponized**:
   - Show enemy's atrocities (mobilize your side)
   - Hide your atrocities (don't photograph, or suppress images)
   - **Friend-enemy asymmetry**: We show their crimes, hide ours

4. **The Suffering Spectacle**:
   - Enemy suffering becomes entertainment
   - Gladiatorial games (watch enemies die)
   - Public executions (spectacle of enemy's death)
   - Modern: ISIS videos (display enemy suffering)
   - **Friend-enemy**: Friends watch enemies suffer

5. **Compassion Limited by Friend-Enemy**:
   - Feel compassion for friends' suffering (mobilizes action)
   - Numbed to enemies' suffering (see too much, become indifferent)
   - **Friend-enemy determines empathy allocation**

**Examples:**

**Lynching Photographs:**
- White Americans photographed Black lynch victims
- Made into postcards, mailed to friends
- Posing smiling next to corpses
- Friend-enemy: White torturers (friends to each other) vs. Black victims (enemies displayed)

**Abu Ghraib:**
- Soldiers photographed torture sessions
- Shared images (pride, entertainment)
- Leaked images created scandal
- Friend-enemy: Americans (shouldn't see this) vs. Iraqis (enemy suffering displayed)

**Napalm Girl Photo:**
- Vietnamese girl running from napalm attack
- Became anti-war symbol
- But also: Evidence of US inflicting suffering on enemies
- Friend-enemy: Photo humanized enemy (created opposition to war)

**ISIS Execution Videos:**
- Filmed beheadings, burnings, drownings
- Distributed online
- Purpose: Terror + pride (display power over enemies)
- Friend-enemy: ISIS views vs. enemies suffer (filmed for display)

**Sontag vs. Butler:**

- **Butler**: Frames of war determine whose lives are grievable
- **Sontag**: Photographs determine whose pain is visible
- Both: Visual politics of friend-enemy

**Sontag vs. Scarry:**

- **Scarry**: Pain destroys language (incommunicable)
- **Sontag**: Photography communicates pain (makes it visible)
- **Scarry**: Victim's experience
- **Sontag**: Viewer's experience

**Key Insight for Comprehensive Analysis:**

Sontag reveals **visual friend-enemy**—whose suffering is photographed (enemies), whose is hidden (friends). Photography makes enemy suffering into spectacle, object, entertainment, or documentation. Trophy photos show perpetrators proud of torturing enemies. Atrocity images weaponized (show their crimes, hide ours). Compassion limited by friend-enemy (care about friends' suffering, numbed to enemies'). Torture becomes visual (photographed, displayed, consumed).

**Critical Addition**: Provides **visual/photographic analysis** of friend-enemy in torture. Explains lynching photos (proudly displaying Black enemy suffering), Abu Ghraib (soldiers posing with tortured enemies), ISIS videos (enemy suffering as propaganda), and compassion fatigue (too many enemy images → numbness). Shows friend-enemy through **whose pain is visible, consumable, displayable**. Essential for understanding photography of atrocity and visual politics of suffering.

## TALAL ASAD: TORTURE, LIBERALISM, AND SECULAR VIOLENCE

### How Liberals Justify Torturing Enemies

**Key Works:**
- *Formations of the Secular* (2003)
- "On Torture, or Cruel, Inhuman, and Degrading Treatment" (2003 essay)

**Core Thesis:**

Talal Asad (1932-) analyzes how secular liberal states justify torturing enemies despite claiming to oppose torture. Through **ticking bomb scenarios**, **legal redefinitions**, and **moral calculus**, liberals create exceptions allowing torture of designated enemies. Friend-enemy operates through who gets human rights (friends/citizens) vs. who doesn't (enemies/terrorists).

**Key Concepts:**

1. **The Ticking Bomb Scenario**:
   - Hypothetical: Bomb about to explode, terrorist knows location, torture might prevent disaster
   - Used to justify torture as "lesser evil"
   - **Enemy designated tortureable** through utilitarian calculation
   - Problem: Real torture doesn't match scenario (victim often innocent, no bomb, torture doesn't work)

2. **Liberal Exception**:
   - Liberals: Torture is absolutely wrong (principle)
   - But: Create exceptions (terrorists, emergencies)
   - **Friend-enemy**: Friends protected absolutely, enemies protected conditionally

3. **Secularism and Cruelty**:
   - Secular states claim to oppose religious violence (Inquisition)
