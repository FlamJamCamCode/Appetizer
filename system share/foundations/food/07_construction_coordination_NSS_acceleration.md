## I. Introduction - Abandoning Capital-Time

**Traditional assumption**: "This will take 50 years to build."

**Why that assumption exists**:
- Capital allocation is slow (investors deliberate, governments bureaucratize)
- Labor is bought at market rates (artificial scarcity, gatekeeping)
- Materials are profit-extracted (markups at every layer)
- Coordination is hierarchical (command chains, approval processes)
- Innovation is stolen (employment contracts capture value)

**What if all of that is removed?**

**What if instead**:
- Resources allocated directly (no capital gatekeeping)
- Labor coordinated by will-coalescence (people work because they want to build this)
- Materials flow without profit extraction (cost = marginal production cost)
- Coordination is gamified (AR/LLL + NSS, not hierarchies)
- Innovation rewarded directly (merit flows to creators)

**How fast then?**

**This document answers that question using NSS/Sol-Jord coordination architecture.**

## II. The NSS/Sol-Jord Coordination Framework - How It Works

### A. Natural Selection Systems (NSS) - Core Principles

From `@24 nss.md`:

**Performance-based resource flow**:
- Resources flow to those who perform best, not those who own most
- Excellence in one domain grants no authority over others
- Continuous competition ensures optimal allocation
- Exponential growth for superior performers

**Three-layer architecture**:
1. **Architecture/Intent Space**: What needs to be done (human will)
2. **Logic/Reasoning Space**: How to do it (algorithms, plans)
3. **Machine/Actuator Space**: What can do it (physical resources, workers, robots)

**Actualization Layer**: Where natural selection operates
- Real-time visibility of all resources (workers, materials, equipment)
- Performance measurement (speed, quality, efficiency)
- Automatic routing to best performers
- Continuous competition and improvement

**Key insight**: This eliminates gatekeeping, profit extraction, and coordination overhead.
