## VII. The Pension Question - Is It Justified?

### A. What Is a Pension?

**Definition**: Regular payment to person who no longer works
- Based on: Previous employment (contributed to pension fund)
- Justification: "Earned" via years of work
- Age: Typically 65-67 (varies by country)

**The memetic justification**: "You worked 40 years, you deserve rest."

**The question**: Do you?

---

### B. Historical Context - Pensions Are Recent

**Pre-1900s**: 
- Most humans worked until death (or near death)
- No "retirement" concept (farmers farmed until physically unable, then relied on family)
- Life expectancy: 40-50 years (most died before "old age")

**1889**: Germany introduces first pension system (Bismarck)
- Age 70 initially (most died before receiving pension!)
- Funded by: Payroll taxes (workers pay for retirees)
- Justification: Social stability (prevent elderly poverty)

**1935**: US Social Security Act
- Age 65 (life expectancy was 61 for men - most died before receiving!)
- Expanded over time (more benefits, younger retirement, longer lifespans)

**Today**: 
- Life expectancy: 75-85 years
- Retirement: 65-67
- **Pension duration**: 10-20 years (vs. 5 years originally)
- **Cost exploding**: Retirees outnumber workers (demographic crisis)

**The shift**: From "support elderly who can't work" to "pay people for 10-20 years to not work"

---

### C. Work Capacity of Elderly

**Question**: Can 67-year-olds work?

**Evidence**:
- Many do! (politicians, CEOs, professors work into 70s-80s)
- Work capacity depends on: Health, job type, motivation
- **Physical labor**: Declines with age (50% capacity at 70 vs. 30)
- **Knowledge work**: Often improves (experience, wisdom)
- **Light work**: Most 67-year-olds capable (part-time, flexible)

**Medical reality**:
- Age 67 "young old" (vs. "old old" at 80+, "oldest old" at 90+)
- Most are healthy (chronic conditions manageable)
- **Capable of 20-40 hours/week** light work (not manual labor)

**The question**: Should healthy 67-year-olds receive pension while not working?

**Possible answers**:
1. **Yes** (current system): They "earned" it (paid into system)
2. **No** (harsh view): They're capable, they should work (no free ride)
3. **Nuanced** (BARRACKS): They can choose - retire with modest pension, OR work for purpose + provisions + tokens (extra wealth)
