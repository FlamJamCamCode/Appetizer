## The Two Core Insights

### 1. The Daemon-Alignment Problem: WHO Gets The Money Is Everything

**The core principle**: Luxury goods have high margins → Power is transferred to the seller → If seller is not daemon-aligned, your money UNDERMINES your goal.

**The mechanism**:
- **Production cost** of life-aged meat: ~€50/kg (land, feed, care over 15 years)
- **Sale price**: €500/kg
- **Margin**: €450/kg = **PURE POWER** given to seller
- **What they spend it on determines if you helped or harmed your mission**

**The failure mode**:
- **Farmer NOT daemon-aligned**:
  - Spends profits on: Fur coats (more animal death), yachts (otium luxury), expanding meat operations elsewhere
  - Result: YOUR €500/kg payment financed MORE death than you prevented
  - **Money leakage** to anti-aligned actors

**The success mode**:
- **Farmer IS daemon-aligned**:
  - Spends profits on: Expanding sanctuary capacity, better conditions, AR research, more animals saved
  - Result: YOUR €500/kg payment AMPLIFIES your goal
  - **Closed-loop economy**: Money circulates within movement, never leaves

**The strategic implication**:
- **Farmer grants**: High risk (can't verify spending, money might leak)
- **Hostile takeover**: Low risk (YOU control facilities → YOU control profit allocation → No leakage)

**The rent-seeking parallel**:
- Current urban housing: Landlord extracts 80% of worker income, spends on:
  - Buying MORE properties (not building new housing—converting existing housing to rent-seeking)
  - Building with intentional vacancies (10-20% empty to maintain scarcity, maximize prices)
  - Lobbying to block new construction (zoning laws protect scarcity)
- Result: YOUR rent payments WORSEN the problem (fund scarcity-maintenance and parasitism)
- **Key insight**: Not building to meet demand, but **extracting from scarcity AND maintaining it**
