# Practical Example: Danish Pig Farmer with 250 Pigs

## Current Situation
- **Current herd**: 250 pigs
- **Current model**: Intensive farming, slaughter at 6 months
- **Current revenue**: Let's estimate ~€150,000/year (variable)
- **Current costs**: Feed, housing, medication, labor
- **Moral state**: "I hate this but it's the only way to survive"

## The Transformation Calculation

### Step 1: Land Assessment
**Question**: How many pigs can your land support for full 15-year lifespans?

**Calculation**:
- Assume you have 20 hectares (50 acres) of land
- For pigs to live well: Need ~0.5-1 hectare per pig for full lifespan
- **Sustainable population: 20-40 pigs** (let's use 30 for calculations)

**Reality check**: Yes, this is 88% reduction from current 250 pigs. But...

### Step 2: Revenue Model

**Current model** (250 pigs):
- 250 pigs × 2 cycles/year = 500 pigs/year slaughtered
- Revenue per pig: €300-400 (market price)
- **Total annual revenue: €150,000-200,000**
- But: Complicit in killing 500 animals/year

**New model** (30 pigs):
- 30 pigs × €7,500/year contract value = **€225,000/year**
- Zero pigs slaughtered (during their lifetime)
- After 15 years: Natural death, meat delivered to contract holders

**The math**: 
- **12% of animals = 112% of revenue**
- **Zero killing vs 500 killings/year**

### Step 3: Cost Analysis

**Current costs** (250 pigs intensive):
- Feed (imported): €80,000/year
- Medication/vet: €15,000/year
- Housing (confined): €10,000/year
- Labor: €30,000/year
- **Total: ~€135,000/year**
- **Profit margin: €15,000-65,000/year**

**New costs** (30 pigs extensive):
- Feed (land-based): €5,000/year (supplemental only)
- Medication/vet: €3,000/year (better health from outdoor life)
- Housing (open shelters): €5,000/year (initial investment amortized)
- Labor: €20,000/year (different work—stewardship, not processing)
- Video documentation: €3,000/year
- **Total: ~€36,000/year**
- **Profit margin: €189,000/year**

**The transformation**:
- Current: €15-65k profit, 500 animals killed/year, moral suffering
- New: €189k profit, zero animals killed, moral redemption

### Step 4: The Transition Challenge

**Problem**: You have 250 pigs now. You can only sustain 30.

**Options**:

1. **Gradual reduction** (recommended):
   - Stop breeding immediately
   - Current 250 pigs will naturally die or be slaughtered over 6 months (existing system)
   - During those 6 months: Select best 30 pigs for transition
   - Move those 30 to extensive/life-farm model
   - Start selling contracts for those 30 pigs
   - No new breeding

2. **Immediate transition**:
   - Select best 30 pigs immediately
   - For remaining 220 pigs: Humane slaughter OR find other transforming farmers to take some
   - Move 30 to extensive model immediately
   - Start selling contracts
   - This is faster but requires difficult decisions

3. **Hybrid approach**:
   - Transition in stages: 30 pigs to life-model now
   - Continue conventional farming with remaining 220 while you prove the model
   - Once contracts prove viable: Stop conventional farming entirely
   - This is safest but means continued killing during transition

**Recommended: Option 1** (gradual reduction with immediate breeding stop)

### Step 5: Contract Structure

**For each of your 30 pigs**:

**Contract terms**:
- Consumer purchases "future meat from Pig #[ID]"
- Contract duration: Remaining lifespan (if pig is young) or full 15 years (if starting fresh)
- Annual payment: €7,500/year
- Total contract value: €112,500 over 15 years
- documentedJoy multiplier: 2.5x (your target for exemplary welfare)

**What consumer gets**:
- Quarterly video updates of their specific pig
- Photos and life milestones
- Upon pig's natural death: ~100kg of premium life-aged pork
- Moral satisfaction of funding a full animal life

**What you get**:
- €7,500/year × 30 pigs = €225,000/year immediate revenue
- Steady income during pigs' lives (not waiting 15 years)
- Moral transformation from death-dealer to life-steward

- Create individual profiles for each pig
- Upload to secure platform where contract holders access their pig's videos
- Include: Seasonal changes, enrichment activities, social interactions, land improvements

**This is your competitive advantage**: The documentation IS the product value.
