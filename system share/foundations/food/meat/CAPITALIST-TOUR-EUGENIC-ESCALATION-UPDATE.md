## The Escalation Dynamic

### **Why Slaughterhouse Owners Will Try to Price Gouge**

**Their thinking**:
- "These are animal rights activists with deep pockets"
- "They're desperate to save animals"
- "We can charge 2-3× fair market value and they'll pay"
- "This is our chance to cash out big"

**Their mistake**:
- They think you're **only** motivated by saving animals (not true)
- They don't realize you're also **motivated by winning** (destroying their industry)
- They don't understand your **willingness to escalate** (if they abuse situation)

### **The Escalation Ladder**

**Level 1: Fair negotiation**
- Offer fair market price (€10-50M depending on facility)
- Professional, businesslike, straightforward

**Level 2: Slight premium**
- If they negotiate hard, offer 10-20% above market
- Still reasonable, willing to pay for speed/certainty

**Level 3: Regulatory pressure**
- If they price gouge (2-3× market), start investigating violations
- Health codes, environmental regulations, labor laws
- Make operating expensive/difficult

**Level 4: Media exposure**
- Undercover footage of violations
- Worker testimonials (PTSD, unsafe conditions, animal cruelty)
- Public shaming campaign

**Level 5: Direct action**
- Coordinate with activists (legal protests, supply chain disruption)
- Make facility uneconomical to operate

**Level 6: Wait for collapse**
- Many slaughterhouses barely profitable
- Stop buying (they lose key customer—you)
- Wait for bankruptcy
- Buy at fire-sale prices (€2-5M instead of €50M)

**The message**: "Play fair, we'll pay fair. Try to exploit us, we'll destroy you."
