## Guide Structure (8 Minutes, 8 Steps)

### **Step 1: The Situation**
- You're pregnant. You can't be a mom.
- Maybe you're 17. Maybe you're in abusive relationship. Maybe you were raped.
- **Whatever the reason**: There's a life growing in you.

### **Step 2: The Hard Truth**
- **You must bear this burden**. No one can do it for you.
- You brought this life into existence (or it was forced upon you).
- **Now**: There's a tiny human with their own DNA, structures, future.
- **You don't have to love them. But you do have to let them live.**

### **Step 3: Brain Activity Criterion**
- If found out early (week 1-4, before brain activity): Human organism, but not yet baby (abortion morally ambiguous)
- If found out later (week 5-6+, brain activity detected): **Baby** (abortion is MURDER)
- **Check the scan**: Is there brain activity? If YES → It's a baby. You MUST carry.

### **Step 4: Support Available (Depends on Triad)**
- **Financial support**: $20,000+ to carry to term
- **Medical care**: Free prenatal, delivery, postnatal
- **Housing**: If homeless/unsafe
- **Counseling**: If traumatized (rape, etc.)
- **Adoption infrastructure**: Fast-track, no red tape
- **C-section at viability**: If pregnancy causing severe damage (week 23-24), early delivery, baby to state care

### **Step 5: Range of Triad Policies**
- **HARSH Triads**: You carry, you parent for 18 years. Minimal support.
- **MODERATE Triads**: You carry, we pay you, you give up for adoption after.
- **GENEROUS Triads**: You carry until viable (week 23-24), we do c-section, baby to our care (Neverland/boarding school).
- **EXIT RIGHTS (MBC 3)**: You can ALWAYS leave. If Triad's policy is unacceptable, pack up, go to one that supports you.

### **Step 6: Perspective Shift**
- **The baby didn't choose this either**.
- They didn't ask to be conceived. They didn't choose their father. They didn't choose circumstances.
- **They're innocent**. Don't direct anger at them.
- You don't have to love them. But you do have to let them live.

### **Step 7: What Happens After Birth?**
- **Option 1**: Immediate handoff (never see them, sign papers, leave)
- **Option 2**: Brief goodbye (hold them once, say goodbye, hand over)
- **Option 3**: Open adoption (get updates, occasional visits)
- **Where baby goes**: Adoptive families, foster care, Neverland schools, boarding schools, monastery living
- **All designed for children to thrive** (low-cost, automated, peer-driven, will-coalescent)

### **Step 8: After It's Over**
- **Your life continues**. You are NOT defined by this pregnancy.
- **Recovery**: Physical (weeks/months) and emotional (grief, relief, numbness—all normal)
- **Prevention**: Get sterilized ($10k), use contraception, abstain. Don't let this happen again.
- **Moving on**: You can still finish school, build career, find love. This doesn't define you.

### **Final Word**: 
- **You did the right thing** (if reading after). You gave them life. That took strength.
- **Choose life** (if reading before deciding). Choose the hard path. Let them live.
- **The burden was yours. The gift was theirs.** You gave them what no one else could: life itself.
