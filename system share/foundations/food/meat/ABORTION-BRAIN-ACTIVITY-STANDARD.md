## The Core Principle: Focus on Organism's State, Not Temporal History

**The critical insight**: **What it IS, not how much time it has lived.**

**Why this matters**:
- Development **varies** between embryos (some faster, some slower)
- Some develop brain activity at week 5, some at week 7
- **Calendar dates are arbitrary** (what if brain activity at 5.5 weeks? Still protected? Or killed because "not 6 yet"?)
- **The organism's observable state determines protection**: Does THIS organism have brain activity? That's what matters.

## The Brain Activity Criterion

### **Why Brain Activity is the Right Threshold**

**1. Observable phenomenon** (not arbitrary):
- Neural firing can be measured (EEG-like activity in neural tissue)
- Detectable as early as week 5-6 (primitive, but present)
- **Not based on time**, but on **actual developmental state**

**2. Errs on side of protecting life** (conservative principle):
- Brain activity = potential for consciousness
- Even if primitive, it's the **beginning** of consciousness potential
- **Better to protect too early than kill too late**

**3. Clear dividing line**:
- **Before brain activity**: Human organism (cells with human DNA), but no brain → Lower moral weight
- **After brain activity**: Baby (organism with brain) → Full moral weight, protect

**4. Focuses on WHAT THE ORGANISM IS**:
- Prevents gaming the system based on dates
- Scan shows brain activity → Protected (regardless of "weeks")
- Scan shows NO brain activity → Not yet baby (but still human life—discourage, but not ban)
