## Why This Makes Action Easier

### **Not Obstructed at Every Turn**

**Old problem**:
- "Why focus on pigs when children starve?"
- "Why focus on animals when humans suffer?"
- "Why focus on this when that is worse?"
- **Result**: Paralysis. No one acts. Everyone points to "bigger priorities."

**New solution (Spirals plural)**:
- "I'm doing BOTH. Are you doing ANY?"
- "I'm attacking death from THIS angle. What's YOUR spiral?"
- "You focus on children. I focus on animals. Together, we tighten all spirals."
- **Result**: Everyone acts. From their angle. All spirals tighten simultaneously.

### **No Whataboutism Defense**

**When death is attacked from ALL angles**:
- Can't deflect ("but what about X?") → "We're working on X too."
- Can't hide behind other priorities → "All priorities being addressed."
- **Death becomes NAKED** (no excuses, no deflections, must be confronted)

### **Subjective Focus, Collective Force**

**Each person's spiral**:
- Based on their daemon (what resonates with them)
- Based on their capacity (how much they can contribute)
- Based on their skills (what they're good at)
- **All spirals strengthen each other** (via nakedness principle—when one form of death is eliminated, others become more outstanding)
