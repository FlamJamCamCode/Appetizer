### Chapter 2a: Low-Tech Sanctuary Mode (Token Economy Realms)

Not every Neverland needs enchanted headsets or holographic dragons to work. For field deployments tied to humanitarian corridors—or whenever electricity is scarce—the realm can run in **low-tech sanctuary mode**:

- **Secured play yards** with shade sails, obstacle trails, garden beds, and quiet story circles become the “zones” children rotate through. Physical dividers and scheduled sorting prevent incompatible kids from clashing, so the space runs safely without constant adult supervision.
- **Quest boards** are literal chalkboards and parchment sheets. Facilitators pin up daily quests (shared chores, scavenger hunts, “repair the hand pump” mini-projects) alongside long-form projects that mimic local adult work: start a pop-up clinic, run a textiles stall, orchestrate a mini build.
- **Token economy** replaces AR loot. Children earn stamped copper/silver/gold coins for completing chores, inspiring others, or presenting solutions. They spend coins to “hire” adults as consultants, rent tools, or bring in tougher expertise when a project exceeds their current skill.
- **Business stalls** made of scrap wood or fabric act as storefronts. Kids practice ledgers, negotiations, and logistics by running bakeries, repair kiosks, or tutoring stands inside the sanctuary perimeter. Adults drop in as “contractors” when hired, then return to medical or build duty.
- **Learning moments** stay embedded: math through ledger balancing, literacy via quest write-ups, civics through marketplace disputes resolved by the children’s own council.
- **Sorting happens morning, midday, and evening.** Adults review disputes, skill gaps, and friendships to reorganize play clusters. Complementary differences are maximized, persistent conflicts are split, and every child knows the staff are watching the mix.

In this mode the magic is social: the coin economy, the cooperative projects, and the sense of “we run our village” give the same daemon clarity and confidence as the high-tech version—without needing more than tokens, paper, and a fence.
