## The Pattern: Selective Visibility Creates Witch Hunts

**The general mechanism** (applicable to many domains):

1. **Widespread behavior** exists (millions engage in it)
2. **Most remain invisible** (private behavior, no public record)
3. **Few become visible** (public figures, documented evidence)
4. **Collective frustration** exists (many feel harmed/betrayed by the behavior)
5. **No proportional mechanism** exists (can't address systemic issue)
6. **Visible few become targets** (disproportionate fury focused on them)
7. **Systemic behavior continues** (invisible majority unaffected)

**This is the accountability paradox**: Visibility creates vulnerability to disproportionate consequences while the systemic pattern persists.

- Result: Either suppress preferences (impossible) or face conflict (current state)

**The visibility trap**:

- Result: Injustice either way (invisible escape consequences, visible face disproportionate harm)

### The Triadization Solution

**The mechanism**:

**Voluntary separation by values/preferences**:
- **Triad A**: Those who prefer/accept interracial relationships, specific preferences
- **Triad B**: Those who prefer in-group relationships, traditional patterns
- **Triad C**: Those who don't care, mixed preferences
- **Etc.**: As many Triads as there are distinct preference clusters

**No forced coexistence**:

- But no "witch hunt" possible (everyone in Triad A chose to be there, accepts the preferences)

- Result: No disproportionate targeting (everyone sorted by preference)

**Proportional consequences**:
- If you have preferences incompatible with Triad B → You're not in Triad B (natural sorting)
- If you have preferences compatible with Triad A → You're in Triad A (natural sorting)
- No one punished for preferences (just sorted into compatible groups)

**No systemic frustration**:
- Those who want in-group preference → Get it (in Triad B)
- Those who want out-group preference → Get it (in Triad A)
- No sense of betrayal (everyone in your Triad shares your values)
