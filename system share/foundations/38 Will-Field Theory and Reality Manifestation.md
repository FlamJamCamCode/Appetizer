## Book III: Magnetic Coalescence and Critical Density

### Chapter 6: The Magnetic Pull

**The phenomenon**: Once field reaches certain strength, it starts pulling resources/people toward it (like gravity well).

**Below critical density**: Field exists but weak (individuals feel pull but not strong enough to overcome friction, few act on it).

**At critical density**: Field strength overcomes friction (tipping point—suddenly many act on pull simultaneously, avalanche begins).

**Above critical density**: Field dominant (overwhelming pull, everyone nearby drawn in, becomes new normal).

---

**Example—Vegan Movement Timeline**:

**1970s**: Scattered will, no field (few vegans, isolated, no infrastructure, very difficult).

**1990s-2010s**: Field forming (vegans connecting, restaurants appearing, but still friction-heavy, requires commitment).

**2015-2020**: Approaching critical density (vegan food improving rapidly, more people trying, easier than before, but still niche).

**2020-2025**: Near tipping point (vegan food competitive with meat in many contexts, significant population shifting, infrastructure building fast).

**2025-2030**: Critical density likely (if vegan food becomes superior + cheaper, mass shift happens, new normal established).

**2030+**: Above critical density (veganism default, meat-eating rare/shameful, field dominant).

---

**The magnetic pull mechanism**:

**Weak field**: Few restaurants (hard to eat vegan) → Few people try (too difficult) → Weak demand → Little investment → Remains difficult (vicious cycle).

**Strengthening field**: Some investment → Better food → More people try → More demand → More investment → Even better food → Even more people → ... (virtuous cycle).

**Critical density**: Pull overwhelming → Mass investment floods in → Food becomes superior + cheaper → Everyone shifts → New equilibrium.

### Chapter 7: The Tipping Point Dynamics

**What triggers tipping point**: Field strength reaches level where switching FROM old TO new is easier than maintaining old.

**Before tipping**: 
- Default is old (meat eating default, veganism requires effort)
- Switching to new requires conscious choice (must choose vegan deliberately)
- Most stay with default (inertia, habit, convenience)

**At tipping**: 
- Both equally easy (vegan as easy/cheap/tasty as meat)
- Switching either direction requires no special effort
- Population splits rapidly (some stay, some switch, both valid)

**After tipping**:
- Default is new (veganism becomes normal, meat-eating requires special choice)
- Switching to old requires conscious choice (must choose meat deliberately, justify why)
- Most flow to new default (inertia now favors new)

---

**The critical insight**: You don't need to convince majority (impossible, too much effort).

**You just need**: Make new option superior (through supply transformation, infrastructure building) → tipping point inevitable → majority shifts automatically.
