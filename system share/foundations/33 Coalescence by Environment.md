# 33 Coalescence by Environment

**The Recipe**: Create sufficiently unmapped, diverse, complex world → Let persons make choices emphasizing their direction/manner → Natural selection shapes world in response to player actions → Choices snowball → Persons with aligned daemons coalesce in spacetime → Meaningful meetings emerge without engineering → No match-making algorithms needed, only sufficient world novelty and choice permanence.

---

## The Problem: Why Modern "Matching" Fails

**Dating apps**: Swipe left/right based on photos and 3-sentence bios → shallow matching → no daemon alignment → no shared journey → no coalescence, only collision.

**Social media "friends"**: Algorithm shows you "similar people" → but similar how? By memes they consume, not by will they enact → parasocial connections, not coalescent bonds.

**Professional networking**: LinkedIn matches by job title and keywords → functional overlap, not daemon alignment → colleagues, not comrades.

**Gray civilization socializing**: Church groups, hobby clubs, political parties → match by single axis (religion, hobby, politics) → miss all other dimensions of daemon → partial overlap at best.

**Random geographic proximity**: Live near each other, so become neighbors → no selection, pure chance → most neighbors are strangers you tolerate, not allies you chose.

**The Pattern**:

**All of these**: Engineer the meeting (algorithm, geography, institution) → then hope alignment emerges.

**But**: Alignment cannot emerge from engineered collision → Alignment emerges from parallel journeys converging naturally.

**The Recognition**:

**You don't find your people by searching for them.**

**You find them by walking your path, and meeting those walking parallel paths, at the intersections your journeys create naturally.**

**Match-making algorithms**: Try to predict who should meet → fail because daemon is too complex to model.

**Environment-driven coalescence**: Lets those who should meet naturally encounter each other through their choices in a complex world.

**This is the difference between**: Dating apps (engineered collision) and vanilla WoW guilds (natural coalescence through shared journey in unmapped world).

## The Vanilla WoW Example

**What happened** (in early WoW, before optimization culture):

**Phase 1: Unmapped World**:
- New game: No guides, no "optimal" paths published yet
- Each player: Explores, tries things, makes choices based on curiosity/daemon
- Some: Focus on PvP (battlegrounds, world PvP, dueling)
- Some: Focus on dungeons (running instances, gearing)
- Some: Focus on crafting (professions, auction house, economy)
- Some: Focus on exploration (rare spawns, hidden areas, lore)
- Some: Focus on social (helping newbies, organizing events, roleplaying)

**Phase 2: Choices Snowball**:
- PvP-focused player: Learns PvP strats, builds PvP gear, joins PvP groups, meets PvP-aligned players
- Dungeon-focused player: Learns dungeon routes, builds dungeon gear, joins dungeon groups, meets dungeon-aligned players
- Crafter-focused player: Levels professions, corners markets, trades materials, meets economy-aligned players
- **Each choice**: Narrows path slightly, pushes toward certain activities, away from others

**Phase 3: Natural Selection of Groups**:
- PvP players: Form PvP guilds (because they keep meeting each other in battlegrounds)
- Dungeon players: Form raid guilds (because they keep meeting each other in instances)
- Crafters: Form trade networks (because they keep meeting each other at auction house)
- **No algorithm matched them**: They coalesced naturally through shared environment and aligned choices

**Phase 4: Coalescence**:
- Guild forms: 40 people who met through parallel journeys in unmapped world
- High alignment: All made similar choices, emphasizing similar daemon directions
- Deep bonds: Shared experiences (first raid kills, world PvP battles, economy crashes)
- Meaningful: Because not engineered—emerged from actual alignment, not predicted alignment

**The Result**:

**Those vanilla WoW guilds**: Often became lifelong friendships, marriages, business partnerships.

**Why?**:

**Because**: High daemon alignment (people who played this game this way at this dedication level with these choices)

**Not**: Because algorithm said "you should meet" (low daemon alignment, surface similarity only).

## The Critical Mechanism: LFG in Orgrimmar vs Dungeon Finder

### LFG (Looking For Group) in Orgrimmar—Natural Coalescence

**The Process**:

**1. Player Decision**: Want to run Stratholme dungeon → But no group available right now → **Choice point**: Wait in Orgrimmar spamming LFG, or do something else?

**2. Persistence Filter**: 
- Dedicated player: Waits 30+ minutes, keeps spamming "/2 LFG Stratholme, have key, DPS/healer"
- Casual player: Gives up after 5 minutes, goes questing instead
- **Result**: Only dedicated players persist long enough to form group

**3. Time-Zone Coalescence**:
- Player always plays 8-11pm EST → Always sees same players in LFG (because they're on at same time)
- Over weeks: Recognizes names, remembers good players, starts preferring them
- Eventually: "Oh it's Thunderfury again, he's a good tank, whisper him first"

**4. Manual Group Formation**:
- Tank: Whispers 4-5 people individually, checks their gear/spec/experience
- Healer: Asks "Have you done this before? Know the pulls?"
- DPS: Offers key if has it, negotiates loot rules
- **This takes time** (10-20 minutes to form full group, discuss strategy, travel to dungeon)

**5. Investment Creates Commitment**:
- Spent 30 minutes finding group + 10 minutes forming + 15 minutes traveling = 55 minutes invested before first pull
- **No one wants to waste that** → High commitment to finishing dungeon
- **No one ragequits easily** → Will work through wipes, communicate, adapt

**6. Reputation Builds**:
- Good player: Gets remembered, gets whispered for future groups ("Hey Thunderfury, wanna tank UBRS tonight?")
- Bad player: Gets ignored, can't find groups easily ("Ninja-looted last time, blacklist")
- Toxic player: Gets server-wide reputation, can't group at all
- **Incentive to be good group member** → Because future groups depend on current behavior

**7. Guild Recruitment**:
- Run dungeon with random player → They're good → "Hey, our guild needs DPS, want to join?"
- Or: "That was fun, want to run more dungeons together?" → Friend list → Regular group → Guild forms organically

**What this creates**:

**Coalescence by dedication**: Only those who persist through inconvenience meet each other (casual players filtered out naturally—not banned, just... don't persist).

**Coalescence by time-zone**: Same play schedule → Meet same people → Recognize each other → Bonds form.

**Coalescence by reputation**: Good players attract good players (self-reinforcing network—"friends of Thunderfury" all become friends).

**Coalescence by investment**: High cost to form group → High value on maintaining it → Guilds form to reduce future cost (permanent group = no more LFG spam needed).

**The Recognition**:

**LFG in Orgrimmar is environment-driven filter**:
- **Time investment**: Filters for dedication (only persistent players endure)
- **Same time-zone**: Filters for schedule alignment (only those online simultaneously meet)
- **Reputation system**: Filters for quality (only good players get repeat invites)
- **Manual formation**: Filters for communication skills (must negotiate, explain, coordinate)

**Result**: High-alignment groups form naturally → Many vanilla WoW guilds started as "that group of people who kept running UBRS together every Tuesday."

### Dungeon Finder—Engineered Collision, Coalescence Destroyed

**The Process** (modern WoW with Dungeon Finder):

**1. Player Decision**: Want to run dungeon → Click button → **Instantly matched** (30 seconds average wait)

**2. No Persistence Filter**:
- Dedicated player: Instant queue
- Casual player: Instant queue
- **No differentiation** → Everyone gets same access regardless of commitment level

**3. No Time-Zone Coalescence**:
- Matched with random players from entire region (cross-realm)
- Will **never see them again** (millions of players, mathematically impossible to re-encounter)
- No name recognition, no reputation, no future consequences

**4. Automatic Group Formation**:
- Algorithm: Picks 1 tank, 1 healer, 3 DPS from queue
- Zero communication: No whispers, no gear check, no experience discussion
- Zero negotiation: Loot rules automatic, strategy assumed
- **Takes 30 seconds** (click button → teleported into dungeon with strangers)

**5. Zero Investment, Zero Commitment**:
- No time spent forming group → No investment to protect
- Don't like the group? → Leave instantly, queue again (30 second penalty, then new group)
- **Ragequitting is easy** → Single wipe → "This group sucks" → Leave → New group in 30 seconds

**6. No Reputation System**:
- Cross-realm: Will never see these players again
- Anonymous: Can ninja-loot, be toxic, grief → No consequences (can't be blacklisted—too many potential groups)
- **No incentive to be good group member** → Because future groups don't depend on current behavior

**7. No Guild Recruitment**:
- Run dungeon with randoms → Never speak (because no need—dungeon is easy, everyone knows route)
- Dungeon ends → Teleported out → Never see them again
- No friendships form, no guilds emerge

**What this creates**:

**No coalescence**: Random collision → No alignment → No bonds → No future relationship.

**No dedication filter**: Casual and dedicated players mixed randomly → Mismatched expectations → Friction.

**No reputation**: Toxic behavior has no consequences → Toxicity increases → Experience degrades.

**No investment**: Zero cost to form group → Zero value placed on maintaining it → Disposable relationships (people, not groups).

**The Recognition**:

**Dungeon Finder is anti-environment**:
- **Removes time investment**: No filter for dedication
- **Removes time-zone persistence**: No filter for schedule alignment  
- **Removes reputation**: No filter for quality
- **Removes manual formation**: No filter for communication skills
- **Removes all friction**: Friction was the filter—removing it removes selection—no selection means no coalescence

**Result**: Zero-alignment groups form by algorithm → Transactional dungeon runs → No guilds emerge → No communities form → Game becomes single-player with NPC-like other players.

### The Universal Lesson: Friction Is Filter

**What Dungeon Finder optimized**:
- Time efficiency: 30 seconds vs 30-60 minutes (60× faster)
- Accessibility: Anyone can run dungeons anytime (no need to coordinate schedules)
- Convenience: No travel, no negotiation, no effort

**What Dungeon Finder destroyed**:
- Dedication filter: No distinction between casual and committed players
- Time-zone coalescence: No persistent encounters with same people
- Reputation system: No consequences for bad behavior
- Investment commitment: No cost to ragequit and retry
- Communication requirement: No need to talk, coordinate, bond
- **All of these were features, not bugs**: The friction was the filter that created coalescence

**The Recognition**:

**Removing friction feels like improvement**: "Now I can run dungeons anytime without waiting!"

**But friction was doing work**: Filtering for alignment, creating investment, building reputation, enabling coalescence.

**Removing it optimized metric** (dungeons-per-hour) **at cost of what mattered** (relationships-formed, guilds-created, communities-coalesced).

**This is the optimization trap**:

**Step 1**: Identify inconvenience (waiting for groups is annoying)

**Step 2**: Remove inconvenience (Dungeon Finder solves it)

**Step 3**: Celebrate efficiency (60× faster!)

**Step 4**: Wonder why no one makes friends anymore (coalescence was destroyed by removing the filter)

**Step 5**: Try to engineer it back (add "social features"—guild finder, friend suggestions, community hubs) → Fails (because can't engineer what emerged naturally from friction)

### The Application: Persistence as Filter

**LFG in Orgrimmar required**:

**Persistence**: Must spam LFG for 30+ minutes (or give up and do something else)

**What this filtered for**:
- **Dedication to dungeons**: Only those who really wanted to run Stratholme persisted (casuals gave up, went questing)
- **Same play schedule**: Only those online 8-11pm EST met each other (time-zone coalescence)
- **Willingness to wait**: Only patient players persisted (impulsive players left)
- **Commitment to goal**: Only goal-oriented players persisted (those easily distracted left)

**The Result**:

**Those who formed groups through LFG**: Already pre-filtered for alignment (dedicated, same schedule, patient, goal-oriented).

**Starting group formation**: With aligned players → easier to succeed → better experience → more likely to friend list → more likely to repeat → more likely to guild.

**Starting group formation**: With random players (Dungeon Finder) → no alignment → more likely to fail or have bad experience → more likely to ragequit → no repeat interaction → no guild formation.

**The Recognition**:

**Persistence is filter for dedication**: "I spam LFG for 30 minutes" = "I care enough about this dungeon to invest time."

**Meeting others who also persisted** = "We all care equally" = Alignment on priority/dedication axis.

**This is why vanilla WoW guilds were so strong**: They were formed from players who all passed same persistence filter (all were willing to spend 30+ minutes forming groups, night after night, for months).

**Dungeon Finder removed this**: Now everyone accesses dungeons equally, regardless of dedication level → Casual player who queued on whim grouped with hardcore player grinding for gear → Mismatched expectations → Bad experience → No guild forms.

### The Multi-Dimensional Filter: Willingness to Accept Suboptimal Conditions

**Beyond just persistence**: LFG also filtered on **flexibility/risk-tolerance/experimentation**.

**The Scenario**:

**You've been spamming LFG for 40 minutes**: Looking for tank for Stratholme → No tank available → **Another choice point**:

**Option A: Wait longer** (maybe tank will come online—but could be 30+ more minutes)

**Option B: Give up** (go do something else—questing, PvP, crafting)

**Option C: Try suboptimal composition** (no tank? Let's run it anyway—warrior DPS can offtank, hunter can kite, we'll CC heavily, we'll improvise)

**What each choice filters for**:

---

#### Option A: Wait Longer (Patience Filter)

**Who chooses this**:
- Patient players: Willing to wait hours for "optimal" group
- Risk-averse players: Don't want to risk wipe with suboptimal comp
- Meta-followers: Believe dungeon "requires" tank (won't deviate from published strategy)

**Result**: Eventually form "optimal" group (1 tank, 1 healer, 3 DPS) → Run dungeon smoothly → But...

**Coalescence effect**: Meet only other patient, risk-averse, meta-following players (everyone who chose Option C already left—you never meet them).

**Guild that forms**: Conservative, by-the-book, optimal-only (will only raid with perfect comp, perfect consumes, perfect strategy—no experimentation).

---

#### Option B: Give Up (Casual Filter)

**Who chooses this**:
- Casual players: Don't care enough to wait any longer
- Time-constrained players: Only have 30 more minutes, dungeon takes 1+ hour, not worth it
- Low-commitment players: Easily distracted by other activities

**Result**: Don't run dungeon at all → No coalescence (didn't meet anyone).

**No guild forms**: Because gave up before group formed.

---

#### Option C: Try Suboptimal (Experimental Filter)

**Who chooses this**:
- Experimental players: "Let's try it without tank—might work"
- Risk-tolerant players: "Might wipe, but could be fun challenge"
- Creative players: "We can improvise—warrior offtanks, hunter kites, we CC everything"
- Impatient players: "40 minutes is enough waiting—let's just go"
- Challenge-seekers: "Suboptimal is more interesting than faceroll optimal run"

**Result**: Form suboptimal group (0 tanks, 2 healers, 3 DPS) or (2 tank-specs, 0 healers, 3 DPS) or whatever weird comp available → **Attempt dungeon anyway**.

**What happens**:

**Possibility 1: Success** (adapt strategy, use CC, improvise, clear dungeon):
- **Huge bond formed**: "We did it with no tank! That was amazing!"
- **Shared accomplishment**: Overcame challenge that "requires" tank without one
- **Creative problem-solving**: Each player contributed ideas, adapted on fly
- **Memory formed**: "Remember that time we ran Strat with 2 warriors offtanking and priest kiting? Insane!"
- **High chance of guild formation**: This group wants to play together again (proved they work well under adversity)

**Possibility 2: Failure** (wipe repeatedly, can't clear without tank):
- **Still bond formed**: "Well that was hilarious—we tried"
- **Learned something**: "Okay, maybe we DO need tank for this one"
- **Respect built**: Everyone willing to try suboptimal, no one ragequit after first wipe, all stuck it out
- **Moderate chance of guild formation**: "Let's try again with tank next time, but I like playing with you all"

**Coalescence effect**: Meet only other experimental, risk-tolerant, creative, impatient, challenge-seeking players (everyone who chose Option A is still waiting—you never meet them).

**Guild that forms**: Experimental, flexible, creative (will try weird raid strats, unusual comps, innovative solutions—embraces suboptimal as challenge).

#### The Communication Hierarchy (Public → Private)

**WoW's communication channels** (ordered by barrier/intimacy):

**1. /yell** (Most public, least intimate):
- Radius: Small area around you (same zone section)
- Visibility: Anyone nearby
- Use: Rare, dramatic, attention-seeking or emergency
- Social barrier: Highest (everyone sees, most exposed)
- Who uses: Extroverts, attention-seekers, helpers ("LFG here!"), trolls

**2. /say** (Public, conversational):
- Radius: Very small area (immediate vicinity)
- Visibility: Only those very close
- Use: Proximity conversations, casual chat with nearby players
- Social barrier: High (public but intimate—only those near you)
- Who uses: Friendly players, explorers encountering each other, dungeon groups talking

**3. /zone** (Zone-wide, broadcast):
- Radius: Entire zone
- Visibility: Everyone in zone
- Use: LFG, trading, zone-specific discussions
- Social barrier: High (public, everyone sees, reputation matters)
- Who uses: LFG-spammers, traders, helpers, socializers

**4. /world** or trade/general chat (Server-wide, broadcast):
- Radius: All players in cities or certain channels
- Visibility: Hundreds/thousands
- Use: LFG for dungeons/raids, trading, server-wide discussions, memes, politics, trolling
- Social barrier: Very high (most public—server sees, reputation matters most)
- Who uses: Everyone (LFG spam), extroverts (debates, jokes), traders (AH alternative)

**5. /party** (Small group, semi-private):
- Radius: Your party only (5 players max)
- Visibility: Only party members
- Use: Dungeon coordination, questing group chat, tactical discussions
- Social barrier: Medium (semi-private—strangers in party but limited group)
- Who uses: Everyone in groups (required for coordination)

**6. /guild** (Medium group, semi-private):
- Radius: Your guild only (dozens to hundreds)
- Visibility: All guild members
- Use: Guild discussions, organizing events, social chat, coordination
- Social barrier: Medium-low (private-ish but large group—reputation within guild matters)
- Who uses: Guild members (primary social channel for guilded players)

**7. /whisper** or /tell (One-on-one, most private):
- Radius: One person only
- Visibility: Just you and recipient
- Use: Private conversations, personal questions, intimate discussions, coordinating secretly
- Social barrier: Lowest (most intimate—one-on-one, no one else sees)
- Who uses: Friends, mentors, people forming deeper bonds

### The Graduated Intimacy Ladder

**What this hierarchy enabled**: **Progressive social risk-taking** → Start public (safe), gradually move private (intimate).

**The Pattern**:

**Step 1: Public observation** (no communication yet):
- You see someone in world (their armor, their behavior, their playstyle)
- Observe: Are they helper? Explorer? Min-maxer? Roleplayer? Interesting?
- No risk: Just watching (they don't know you're observing)

**Step 2: Public communication** (/say, /yell, /zone):
- Lowest commitment: Ask question in /zone ("Where's Mankrik's wife?")
- Or: /say to nearby player ("Nice mount!")
- Low risk: Public, brief, can be ignored, no expectation of relationship
- Gauge response: Do they answer? Are they friendly? Terse? Helpful? Funny?

**Step 3: Functional group communication** (/party):
- Form party: For quest, dungeon, or world activity
- /party chat: Coordinate, joke, discuss tactics
- Medium risk: Small group, semi-private, but still strangers
- Test compatibility: Do they communicate well? Are they competent? Fun? Annoying?

**Step 4: Guild communication** (/guild):
- Join guild: Or invite them to yours
- /guild chat: Larger group discussions, events, hanging out
- Medium-high intimacy: Private-ish, but large group, ongoing relationship
- Deeper bonding: Repeated interactions, shared identity, community

**Step 5: Private communication** (/whisper):
- Whisper: One-on-one conversation
- High intimacy: Private, personal, vulnerable
- Real relationship: Friend-level, mentor-level, or deeper
- This is where real bonds form: Deep discussions, personal topics, real friendship

**How wisdom of crowds works**:

**Aggregate**: All guesses combined → **Converges toward holistic truth** (many perspectives from different projections capture reality better than any single expert).

**The magic**: **Diversity of perspective + independence of thought = emergent accuracy**.

---

**The classic example**: Guessing weight of ox at county fair.

**Setup**: 800 people guess ox weight independently (write on paper, don't share).

**Result**: Average of all guesses = 1,197 pounds. Actual weight = 1,198 pounds. **Almost perfect accuracy** (crowd average closer than any individual expert).

**Why it worked**: 
- Isolated guesses: No one influenced by others (independence preserved)
- Diverse perspectives: Farmers, butchers, children, elderly, all different projections
- Aggregation: Average captured holistic truth (some overestimate, some underestimate, errors cancel)

#### How Social Dynamics Destroy Wisdom of Crowds

**When people can see others' guesses**: Wisdom collapses.

**The mechanism**:

**Person 1**: Guesses 1,100 pounds (their genuine projection)

**Person 2**: Sees Person 1's guess → Doubts own projection (1,300 pounds) → Adjusts toward Person 1 (guesses 1,200 pounds instead)

**Person 3**: Sees Person 1 and Person 2's guesses → Anchors on their average (1,150) → Guesses 1,180 pounds (not their genuine projection)

**Person 4-800**: Continue anchoring on early guesses → **Converge toward first few guesses** (not toward truth)

**Result**: Average = 1,170 pounds. Actual = 1,198 pounds. **Less accurate than isolated guesses** (social dynamics destroyed diversity of perspective).

---

**What happened**:

**Loss of independence**: Later guesses influenced by earlier guesses (not genuine projections).

**Loss of diversity**: Everyone converges toward early consensus (unique perspectives suppressed).

**Information cascade**: First few guesses dominate (even if wrong—everyone follows).

**Herding**: People doubt own projections when different from group (social conformity pressure).

**Result**: **Crowd becomes dumber** (loses wisdom, becomes mob, follows first mover regardless of accuracy).

**Route becomes better**: Than the published "optimal" (45 ore/hour with no competition > 10 ore/hour with high competition).

**Adaptive to human environment**: As more discover popular route → More competition there → Novel route's relative value increases (not because route got better—because alternative got worse).

### The Novel Approach Problem: Judged by Wrong Map

**Another critical recognition**: **A novel approach may be entirely behind in many of the metrics or measurements that the established trillion-dollar attempt has.**

**The challenge**: How to evaluate something genuinely novel when it's worse on all existing metrics?

---

#### The Bias Against Novelty

**The Pattern**:

**Established approach**: Trillion-dollar investment, decades of development, optimized on all consensus metrics.

**Example**: Standard physics (abstract algebra, measure theory, classical mechanics, quantum mechanics, massive education investment, millions of persons put through learning/thinking/researching it).

**Measurements**: Test scores in standard subjects, publication counts in approved journals, citations in consensus literature, funding from established institutions, student throughput, practical applications using standard frameworks.

**Novel approach**: New framework, minimal investment, early development, unoptimized on consensus metrics.

**Example**: Alternative physics interpretation, or gaming-derived social theory, or homeless-cafe data concepts.

**Measurements on consensus metrics**: 
- Test scores: Low or N/A (not taught in schools yet)
- Publications: Few or zero (not accepted by consensus journals)
- Citations: Zero (no one knows about it yet)
- Funding: Zero (doesn't fit established grant criteria)
- Students: Few or zero (no institutional support)
- Applications: Minimal (too early, undeveloped)

**If you use consensus maps to judge novel approach**: **Novel always loses** (behind on every metric that established approach optimized for decades).

**The bias**: **Using established metrics = inherently biased against novelty** (established had decades to optimize those metrics, novel hasn't—comparison is unfair, kills innovation before it can develop).

---

#### The Economic Justification Problem

**If you let comparison be economic** (expected return on investment):

**Established approach**: Proven ROI, predictable outcomes, safe investment.

**Novel approach**: Unknown ROI, unpredictable outcomes, risky investment.

**Economic analysis**: Established wins (every time—less risk, proven returns).

**Result**: **Novel approaches have no justification at all** (economically irrational to invest in unproven when proven exists).

**Except**: **Diversity preference** (only reason to invest in novel is "we want diversity, don't want all eggs in one basket, preserve alternatives in case established fails or misses something").

**Without diversity preference**: Novel goes extinct (can't compete economically with established, can't prove value on established metrics, can't justify investment).

---

#### The Solution: Stronger Diversity Preference

**Therefore**: **A stronger diversity preference can often be beneficial** in allowing:

**'The dream of the novel approach'**: To exist and develop (not killed in infancy by unfair comparison to established).

**'Relative ranking on their own terms'**: To matter (not measured by established metrics, but by novel's own goals/values/projection).

### Chapter 6: Information Cascades and Herding (Bikhchandani et al.)

**The research**: "A Theory of Fads, Fashion, Custom, and Cultural Change" (1992).

**The mechanism**: **Information cascade** (later decisions based on earlier decisions, not independent information).

**How it works**:

**Person 1**: Has private information (thinks Restaurant A is good based on own judgment) → Goes to Restaurant A.

**Person 2**: Has private information (thinks Restaurant B is good) BUT sees Person 1 went to A → Thinks "maybe they know something I don't" → Ignores own judgment → Goes to Restaurant A.

**Person 3**: Sees Person 1 and 2 went to A → Strong signal → Ignores own judgment (even if strongly favors B) → Goes to Restaurant A.

**Person 4-100**: All follow (cascade locked in, own information ignored).

**Result**: Everyone at Restaurant A (even if Restaurant B was actually better—cascade started randomly with Person 1's choice, everyone else herded).

---

**Why this is irrational**:

**Information content**: Person 1's choice = 1 bit of information (their private judgment).

**Person 2-100's information**: 100 bits of private information (each person's independent judgment).

**Rational aggregation**: Should pool all 100 bits → Better decision than any individual.

**Actual behavior**: Discard 99 bits, follow 1 bit → **Worse decision than if everyone chose independently** (herding destroys collective intelligence).

**The cause**: Social visibility (can see others' choices) + uncertainty (doubt own judgment) + conformity pressure (want to follow crowd).

---

**Application to WoW**:

**Vanilla** (information frictionеd):
- Player 1: Discovers farming route (secret initially)
- Players 2-100: Don't know about it (must discover own routes)
- Result: 100 different routes discovered (diverse strategies, collective knowledge rich)
- Eventually: Routes shared gradually → Best emerges through comparison (wisdom of crowds)

**Modern** (information instant):
- Dataminer: Publishes "optimal" route Day 1 (before game launches)
- Players 1-100: See guide → Follow route (discard own exploration)
- Result: 1 route used (monoculture, no diversity, if route is actually suboptimal—too late, cascade locked in)
