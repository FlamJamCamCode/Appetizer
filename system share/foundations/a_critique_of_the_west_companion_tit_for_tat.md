## The Core Problem

**The Tit-for-Tat Principle**: In game theory, "tit-for-tat" is one of the most successful strategies for cooperation - cooperate first, then mirror your opponent's previous move. Cooperate when they cooperate, defect when they defect.

**The Fundamental Requirement**: Tit-for-tat requires **identifying who acted** so you can respond to THAT actor.

**The Western Problem**: When you're constantly dealing with covers, abstractions, and lying names, you **cannot tit towards the tat**. You cannot reciprocate because you cannot identify the actor.

## The Tit-for-Tat Strategy in Game Theory

### The Classic Iterated Prisoner's Dilemma

**The Game**:
- Two players repeatedly interact
- Each can cooperate or defect
- Mutual cooperation → both gain
- Mutual defection → both lose
- One defects, other cooperates → defector gains, cooperator loses

**The Tit-for-Tat Strategy**:
1. Start by cooperating
2. Then do whatever the other player did last time
3. If they cooperated, you cooperate
4. If they defected, you defect
5. Repeat

**Why It Works**:
- Encourages cooperation through reciprocity
- Punishes defection immediately
- Forgives after punishment (returns to cooperation if they do)
- Simple, clear, and effective
- **Result**: Stable cooperation emerges

### The Requirement: Clear Identification

**For Tit-for-Tat to Work**:
- You must know WHO acted
- You must know WHAT they did
- You must be able to respond to THAT specific actor
- **Result**: Reciprocity requires identification

**When Identification Fails**:
- Cannot punish defectors
- Cannot reward cooperators
- Cannot establish reciprocity
- **Result**: Cooperation collapses, parasitism thrives
