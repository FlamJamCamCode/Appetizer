## Part I: Lewontin's Original Claim (1972)

### The Data

**Richard Lewontin's Analysis**:
- Analyzed genetic variation at multiple gene loci across human populations
- Found: **85% of genetic variance occurs WITHIN populations**
- Found: **8% of variance between populations within same race/continent**
- Found: **7% of variance between different races/continents**

**His conclusion**: "Since 85% of variation is within populations and only 15% between, racial classifications have limited biological significance."

---

### The Implication

**What Lewontin argued**:
- Race is NOT biologically meaningful (social construct only)
- Genetic differences between races are TINY (85% vs 15%)
- Any two individuals within a race are more different from each other than average difference between races
- Therefore: Race is useless for biological classification

**How this was used**:
- "Race is a social construct" (academic consensus)
- "Racial differences are negligible" (policy implications)
- "Categorizing by race is scientifically invalid" (anti-discrimination arguments)
- "Race realists are pseudoscientists" (dismissal of HBD)

## Part II: Edwards' Refutation (2003) - "Lewontin's Fallacy"

### The Error Lewontin Made

**A.W.F. Edwards (2003)**: "Human Genetic Diversity: Lewontin's Fallacy"

**The fallacy identified**:
- **Lewontin looked at single loci in isolation** (1D analysis of each gene separately)
- **He ignored correlation structure across loci** (didn't analyze multiple genes simultaneously)
- **When you analyze MULTIPLE loci together** (multi-dimensional), populations separate clearly

---

### The Mathematical Reality

**The key insight**: Small variance between groups at INDIVIDUAL loci becomes LARGE separation when viewing MULTIPLE loci together.

**Example**:

**Single SNP (1D analysis)**:
- SNP A: 85% variance within populations, 15% between
- Any individual European might have African variant at this locus
- ANY SINGLE gene shows overlap between races

**100 SNPs (100D analysis)**:
- Each SNP: 85% within, 15% between
- BUT: Correlation structure across all 100 SNPs creates TOTAL clustering
- Europeans cluster in one region of 100D space
- Africans cluster in different region of 100D space
- Overlap: Nearly zero (<1%)

**The result**: Can classify race with >99% accuracy using just 100 SNPs analyzed together.

### Edwards' Conclusion

**Race IS biologically real**:
- Can classify individuals into racial groups with >99% accuracy using multi-locus analysis
- The 15% between-group variance (which Lewontin dismissed as "small") is STRUCTURED across dimensions

**What Edwards proved**:
- Lewontin committed a fallacy (looked at 1D, ignored multi-D correlation structure)

## Part III: The 2021 Counter-Argument

### "Lewontin Did Not Commit Lewontin's Fallacy"

**The 2021 paper** (Kaplan & Winther): "Lewontin did not commit Lewontin's fallacy, his critics do"

**Their argument**:
- Lewontin's original critique was about **utility of racial classification**, not ability to classify
- Edwards misidentified Lewontin's aim (Lewontin never denied you COULD classify, just questioned whether you SHOULD)
- Considering correlation structure among local groups renders racial taxonomy "scientifically useless" for predicting individual traits

**Their conclusion**: Edwards attacked a strawman—Lewontin never denied clustering exists, just argued it's not useful.

---

### The Debate's Core Confusion

**Two different questions**:

1. **Can we classify humans into races using genetics?** 
   - Edwards: YES (multi-locus analysis allows >99% accuracy)
   - Lewontin: Never denied this (2021 paper claims)

2. **Should we classify humans into races for scientific/social purposes?**
   - Edwards: YES (race predicts traits, outcomes, matters biologically)
   - Lewontin: NO (race is social construct, classification is useless or harmful)

**The confusion**: Mixing descriptive question (can we?) with normative question (should we?).
