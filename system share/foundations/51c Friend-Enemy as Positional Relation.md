## 1. Premise

This document treats **friend** and **enemy** not as moral, emotional, or interpersonal categories, but as **positional relations** within systems of power, will, and domination.

A *relation* here is defined by **alignment or conflict of will over space, institutions, resources, and futures**, rather than by affection, hatred, or declared ideology.

## 2. The Structural Assumption

The analysis assumes a world where:

- Almost all actors (individuals, groups, states, movements) are embedded—knowingly or not—in **systems of domination**.
- These systems exert will over territory, bodies, norms, economies, symbols, and time.
- Neutrality is rare and usually temporary.

Under these conditions, relations are best understood as **vectors**, not sentiments.

---

## 3. Friend and Enemy as Positional Categories

### 3.1 Friend (Positional)

A *friend* is not one who wishes you well, but one whose will:

- Does not currently negate your will
- Aligns with or coexists alongside your assertion in shared or adjacent spaces
- Does not seek to subsume, dominate, or universalize itself over you

Friendship is therefore **contingent**, **local**, and **revocable**.

### 3.2 Enemy (Positional)

An *enemy* is one whose will:

- Actively negates your will
- Seeks domination over spaces you assert
- Imposes structures that constrain, overwrite, or erase your agency

Enmity does not require hatred, awareness, or intent—only **structural opposition**.

---

## 4. Classical Friend–Enemy Logic (and Its Failure)

Common formulations:

- The enemy of my enemy is my friend
- The friend of my friend is my friend

These assume a world of stable camps and shared projects. In domination-heavy systems, these assumptions fail.

---

## 5. Revised Relational Logic (Domination-Aware)

In a world structured by overlapping domination systems:

- **Enemy of my enemy** → *instrumental convergence*
- **Friend of my enemy** → *likely hostile alignment*
- **Friend of my friend** → *likely part of the same domination mesh*
- **Enemy of my friend** → *still an enemy*

Alignment here is **functional**, not ontological.

---

## 6. Instrumental Convergence

Instrumental convergence occurs when:

- Two or more actors oppose the same threat
- Without sharing a positive, generative project
- And without resolving underlying incompatibilities of will

Such convergence is:

- Temporary
- Brittle
- Often mythologized after the fact

Flags, names, and sacred narratives frequently arise to **mask contingency** and retroactively naturalize the alliance.

---

## 7. Negative Relations (Anti-Relations)

Some relations exist **only by opposition**.

Examples:

- Humans uniting against aliens
- Ideological opposites uniting after an external attack
- Political blocs converging under territorial threat

Characteristics:

1. No shared positive vision
2. Unity maintained by sustained threat
3. Suppression of internal difference
4. Rapid dissolution once the threat recedes

These are not alliances but **anti-relations**.

---

## 8. Memory, Myth, and Forgetting

After prolonged instrumental convergence:

- Shared struggle is mythologized
- Internal conflicts are erased from memory
- Former allies are recast as natural brothers

This forgetting enables future domination by disguising historical contingency as destiny.

---

## 9. The Economy of Threat Inflation

In contemporary systems, actors often compete to:

- Present themselves as existential threats
- Bundle unrelated agendas into a single enemy-package
- Force alignment through fear rather than consent

This produces:

- Permanent escalation
- Universal suspicion
- Moral exhaustion
- Layered domination (local → national → global)

---

## 10. Live and Let Live (Reframed)

Common misreading:

> Live and let live = Do not assert

This is then asserted universally—becoming domination by negation.

Correct framing:

> **Assert locally.**  
> **Do not universalize.**

That is:

- Will your own spaces
- Shape your own relations
- Defend your own boundaries
- Refuse to globalize your will into doctrine

---

## 11. Local Assertion vs Universal Domination

Local assertion:

- Respects plurality of wills
- Allows coexistence without convergence
- Accepts difference without erasure

Universal assertion:

- Treats difference as threat
- Requires global alignment
- Inevitably becomes imperial

---

## 12. Summary Principles

1. Relations are positional, not moral
2. Alignment born of opposition is temporary
3. Shared enemies do not erase incompatible wills
4. Universal assertions are domination by other means
5. Local assertion without universalization is the only non-imperial ethic
6. Myths often exist to forget contingency

---

## 13. Closing

Friend and enemy are not identities but **states of relation**.

To mistake them for moral essences is to misunderstand the mechanics of power—and to become easily absorbed into domination systems one does not control.
