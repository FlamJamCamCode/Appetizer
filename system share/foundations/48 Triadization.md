## The Two Types of Nesting

### Type 1: Spatial Embeddedness

**What it is**: One Triad physically inside another's territory.

**Example**: 
```
Spring-Triad (Viking warriors, 10,000 km²)
  └─ Contains Lake-Triad (Trading city, 100 km²) as island within
```

**Flow dynamics**:
- **Lake residents** can roam in surrounding Spring (subject to Spring's rules)
- **Spring residents** can enter embedded Lake (subject to Lake's rules)
- **Spring** has outer sovereignty (controls what crosses its borders from outside)
- **Lake** has inner sovereignty (controls what happens within its borders)

**Size relationship**: If Lake is spatially inside Spring, Lake is geographically smaller (Spring surrounds it).

**Sovereignty subordination**: Lake must respect Spring's rules when crossing Spring territory. Spring must respect Lake's rules when entering Lake territory.

---

### Type 2: Fractal Pyramid Nesting (Hierarchical Authority)

**What it is**: Authority hierarchy independent of geography.

**Example**:
```
Emperor (Rome)
  └─ King (Gaul)
      └─ Duke (Brittany)
          └─ Count (Local region)
```

**The difference from spatial**: 
- Geographic: Lake physically inside Spring's land
- Hierarchical: King subordinate to Emperor's authority (but might rule distant territory)

**Plug-and-play property**: Can switch which pyramid you're subordinate to.

**Example**:
- Duke of Brittany currently under King of Gaul
- Duke decides: "I prefer Emperor's daemon over King's daemon"
- Duke leaves Gaul pyramid, joins Emperor's pyramid directly
- Now: Duke subordinate to Emperor, bypassing King entirely
