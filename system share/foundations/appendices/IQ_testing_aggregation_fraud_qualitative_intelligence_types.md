# IQ Testing: The Aggregation Fraud and Qualitative Intelligence Types

## I. Intelligence is Fundamentally Qualitative, Not Quantitative

### A. The Function vs The Mechanism

**What we functionally care about (pragmatic):**
- Can you solve this problem? (Yes/no)
- Can you design this building? (Yes/no)
- Can you write this code? (Yes/no)
- **Whether or not you can perform the function** (binary outcome)

**What actually matters for understanding (learning, prediction, matching):**
- WHY can you solve this problem? (What mechanism do you use?)
- HOW do you design buildings? (Visual-spatial 3D? Procedural? Intuitive?)
- WHAT enables you to write code? (Sequential memory? Pattern recognition? Verbal logic?)
- **The mechanism that enables the function** (qualitative understanding)

---

**The critical distinction:**

**Functionalism (dismal science approach):**
- "Person can solve problem X" (yes/no)
- "Score: 14/18" (quantitative)
- "IQ: 130" (aggregate metric)
- **Tells you:** Whether they performed (outcome)
- **Doesn't tell you:** How they did it (mechanism)

**Qualitative science (mechanism understanding):**
- "Person solved problem X using sequential-spatial mechanism"
- "Mechanism: Numberpad visualization, hundreds-of-digits capacity"
- "Type: Sequential Genius (specific qualitative type)"
- **Tells you:** How they think (mechanism)
- **Enables:** Prediction of OTHER tasks (if sequential works here, will work for programming too)

---

**Why mechanism matters MORE than outcome:**

**For extrapolative learning:**
- If I know: "You scored 14 on Digit Span" (outcome)
- I predict: ??? (tells me nothing about OTHER tasks you can do)
- **Weak prediction:** Can't extrapolate (one task outcome doesn't reveal general capacity)

**But if I know:** "You use sequential-numberpad mechanism for working memory"
- I predict: 
  - Programming: High success (sequential mechanism perfect for code)
  - Music: Low success (sequential doesn't help with auditory patterns)
  - Navigation: Moderate success (spatial component helps, but not optimized)
  - **Strong prediction:** Can extrapolate mechanism to many domains (know HOW you think → predict WHERE you'll succeed)

---

**Example (you):**

  - **Extrapolation possible:** Mechanism reveals what you CAN and CANNOT do across domains

---

**Intelligence is qualitative:**
- NOT "how much intelligence" (quantitative, one dimension)
- BUT "which KIND of intelligence" (qualitative, many incompatible types)
- NOT "score on THIS task" (specific outcome, doesn't extrapolate)
