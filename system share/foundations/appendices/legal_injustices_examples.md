**E. The Minimum Requirements for Legitimate Speech Law**

**The fundamental requirement:** At minimum, the state and courts must provide the boundaries of free speech in clear purview for anyone to understand what speech will land them in prison.

**What is required:**

1. **Explicit published list** of banned speech categories with specific examples
2. **Constantly updated registry** of court rulings that expand or clarify boundaries
3. **Clear examples** showing what speech gets conviction and why
4. **Public accessibility** to all relevant case documents and reasoning

**The retroactivity principle:**

- **Post hoc punishment forbidden:** Cannot be convicted for speech whose illegality wasn’t clear when spoken
- **First-time application must be warning only:** If a category of speech hasn’t been tried before, prosecution must give warning first
- **Recurrence after warning:** Only after explicit court ruling and public notice can same type of speech lead to conviction
- **No "interpretation surprise":** If court interpretation adds new boundaries, must be applied prospectively, not retroactively

**Current reality:**

- Speeches prosecuted without prior warning
- Boundaries unclear until conviction
- No registry of what's actually illegal
- Court interpretations applied retroactively
