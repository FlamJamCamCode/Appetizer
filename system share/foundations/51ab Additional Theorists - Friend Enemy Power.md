## WALTER BENJAMIN: DIVINE VIOLENCE VS. MYTHIC VIOLENCE

### The Critique of Violence and Friend-Enemy Foundations

**Key Work:**
- "Critique of Violence" (1921)
- "Theses on the Philosophy of History" (1940)

**Core Thesis:**

Walter Benjamin (1892-1940) distinguishes between two types of violence: **mythic violence** (law-making and law-preserving) and **divine violence** (law-destroying). This provides a radical framework for understanding how friend-enemy distinctions are either reinforced through state violence or potentially shattered through revolutionary violence.

**Key Concepts:**

1. **Mythic Violence**:
   - Violence that creates and preserves law
   - State violence (police, military, legal punishment)
   - Establishes boundaries between legal/illegal, citizen/criminal, friend/enemy
   - **Circular**: Law justifies violence, violence maintains law

2. **Divine Violence**:
   - Pure, immediate violence that destroys legal order
   - Revolutionary, messianic rupture
   - Breaks the cycle of mythic violence
   - Example: General strike (not to reform law but to destroy legal order itself)

3. **The Paradox of Police**:
   - Police both make law (decide who is threat) and enforce law
   - No clear distinction between law-making and law-preserving
   - Police violence defines friend-enemy in the moment
   - **Police are everywhere** in modern state

4. **Violence and the Sacred**:
   - Mythic violence creates sacred boundaries
   - Transgression (enemy action) justifies violent response
   - Legal system sacralizes its own violence
   - **Enemy is profaner of sacred order**

5. **The State of Exception as Permanent**:
   - Modern state operates through continuous suspension of law
   - Police violence is always exceptional (decides case-by-case)
   - Friend-enemy determined through violence, not law

**Friend-Enemy Implications:**

1. **Law Creates Enemies**: The legal system produces the criminal/enemy through its own categories. There is no "crime" or "enemy" before law defines it.

2. **Police Violence Defines Enmity**: Police have power to designate enemies in real-time:
   - "Suspicious person" → Enemy
   - "Resisting arrest" → Enemy
   - "Terrorist threat" → Enemy
   - No due process, immediate designation

3. **Mythic Cycle**: State violence against enemies justifies more state power, which creates more enemies, requiring more violence (endless cycle).

4. **Revolutionary Violence**: Only divine violence breaks friend-enemy cycle by destroying legal order that produces these categories.

5. **The Sovereign Ban**: Those labeled enemies are banned from legal protection (similar to Agamben's homo sacer) through mythic violence.

**Examples:**

**Police Killings:**
- Police determine "threat" in moment (mythic violence)
- Killing justified by designation "armed and dangerous"
- Legal system protects police (qualified immunity)
- Mythic cycle: Police violence creates more "criminals" requiring more police

**War on Terror:**
- "Terrorist" designation through executive power (mythic law-making)
- Indefinite detention, torture, assassination (mythic law-preserving)
- No legal recourse (Guantanamo, black sites)
- Cycle perpetuates: More violence creates more "terrorists"

**General Strike:**
- Not economic demands but **destruction of legal order**
- Refusal to participate in system
- Divine violence: Breaks cycle of mythic violence
- Example: 1968, revolutionary moments

**Benjamin vs. Schmitt:**

- **Schmitt**: Sovereign decides on exception
- **Benjamin**: Exception has become the rule (permanent state of emergency)
- **Schmitt**: Friend-enemy through political decision
- **Benjamin**: Friend-enemy through mythic violence (legal designation)

**Benjamin vs. Weber:**

- **Weber**: State has monopoly on legitimate violence
- **Benjamin**: No violence is truly "legitimate"—only mythic or divine

**Key Insight for Comprehensive Analysis:**

Benjamin reveals that friend-enemy distinctions are produced and maintained through **mythic violence**—the legal-police apparatus that designates enemies and justifies violence against them. This is a circular, self-perpetuating system. Only **divine violence** (revolutionary rupture) can break this cycle by destroying the legal categories that produce enemies.

**Critical Addition**: Shows friend-enemy as product of **legal violence** itself. The state doesn't respond to enemies; it creates enemies through legal designation backed by police violence. Explains why "law and order" politics always escalate—mythic violence requires continuous enemy production to justify itself.

## ANTONIO GRAMSCI: HEGEMONY AND THE WAR OF POSITION

### Cultural Domination and Friend-Enemy Without Violence

**Key Works:**
- *Prison Notebooks* (1929-1935)
- Writings on political theory, cultural hegemony, civil society

**Core Thesis:**

Antonio Gramsci (1891-1937) argues that dominant classes maintain power not just through force (violence against enemies) but through **hegemony**—ideological and cultural leadership that makes their rule seem natural and inevitable. This reveals a form of friend-enemy distinction based on **consent** rather than coercion.

**Key Concepts:**

1. **Hegemony**:
   - Ideological and cultural domination
   - Ruling class ideas become "common sense"
   - Dominated classes consent to their own domination
   - **Hegemony = cultural friend-enemy** (who belongs to "common sense" vs. who is "extremist")

2. **War of Position vs. War of Maneuver**:
   - **War of Maneuver**: Direct assault on state power (revolution, military coup)
   - **War of Position**: Gradual conquest of civil society (cultural institutions, education, media)
   - In advanced capitalism, war of position is primary
   - **Friend-enemy operates through culture, not just violence**

3. **Organic Intellectuals**:
   - Every class produces intellectuals who organize hegemony
   - Bourgeois intellectuals maintain capitalist hegemony
   - Working-class organic intellectuals challenge it
   - **Intellectual friend-enemy**: Who defines "common sense"?

4. **Civil Society**:
   - Schools, churches, media, unions, clubs
   - Site of hegemonic struggle
   - Ruling class dominates through these institutions
   - **Cultural institutions define friend-enemy** (normal vs. deviant)

5. **Historic Bloc**:
   - Alliance of classes under hegemonic leadership
   - Unity of economic base and ideological superstructure
   - Creates broad "friends" (those within hegemony) vs. enemies (those outside it)

**Friend-Enemy Implications:**

1. **Ideological Friend-Enemy**: Enemies aren't just political opponents but those who challenge **common sense**:
   - Socialists = "unrealistic"
   - Radicals = "extremists"
   - Critics = "divisive"
   - Friends = "pragmatic," "reasonable," "centrist"

2. **Hegemony Makes Enmity Invisible**: When hegemony succeeds, there appear to be no enemies—everyone shares common sense. Dissidents are "crazy," not enemies.

3. **Cultural Institutions as Battleground**: Friend-enemy operates through:
   - Education (who gets taught what?)
   - Media (who is represented how?)
   - Religion (who is virtuous/sinful?)
   - Popular culture (who is hero/villain?)

4. **Passive Revolution**: Ruling class makes concessions to prevent revolution:
   - Welfare state (neutralizes class enemy)
   - Representation (minorities included but not empowered)
   - **Enemies are absorbed into hegemonic bloc**

5. **Counter-Hegemony**: Oppressed classes must build alternative common sense:
   - Workers' education
   - Alternative media
   - Cultural production
   - Create new friends (solidarity) against hegemonic bloc

**Examples:**

**Cold War:**
- Hegemonic common sense: Capitalism = freedom, socialism = slavery
- Enemies not just Soviet Union but "socialists" internally
- War of position: Education, Hollywood, advertising all reinforce capitalism
- Anyone questioning capitalism = enemy of "freedom"

**Neoliberal Hegemony:**
- "There is no alternative" (TINA - Thatcher)
- Market logic becomes common sense
- Friends: "Competitive," "entrepreneurial," "flexible"
- Enemies: "Entitled," "dependent," "resistant to change"
- Critics dismissed as "unrealistic," not engaged as political enemies

**Cultural War:**
- Hegemonic struggle over values
- Friend-enemy: "Traditional values" vs. "political correctness"
- Media, schools, churches battlegrounds
- Not military conflict but cultural positioning

**Immigration:**
- Hegemonic common sense: "Nation of immigrants BUT..."
- Friends: "Legal," "assimilated," "contribute"
- Enemies: "Illegal," "refuse to assimilate," "take jobs"
- Cultural institutions (media, education) define these categories

**Gramsci vs. Lenin:**

- **Lenin**: Revolutionary vanguard seizes state (war of maneuver)
- **Gramsci**: Must win cultural hegemony first (war of position)
- **Lenin**: Clear friend (proletariat) enemy (bourgeoisie)
- **Gramsci**: Hegemony creates complex alliances and oppositions

**Gramsci vs. Schmitt:**

- **Schmitt**: Friend-enemy through political decision (sovereign)
- **Gramsci**: Friend-enemy through cultural hegemony (consent)
- **Schmitt**: Intensity of conflict defines political
- **Gramsci**: Invisibility of domination (hegemony) defines political success

**Key Insight for Comprehensive Analysis:**

Gramsci reveals that friend-enemy operates through **cultural hegemony**—defining what is "normal," "common sense," "realistic" versus "extreme," "unreasonable," "divisive." The most successful form of domination makes enemies invisible by making their positions unthinkable. Friend-enemy isn't just political or violent but ideological—who is within the boundaries of legitimate discourse vs. who is excluded.

**Critical Addition**: Shows friend-enemy as **cultural-ideological**—operating through schools, media, churches, not just state violence. Explains why certain positions are "mainstream" (friends) and others "fringe" (enemies), not through rational debate but through hegemonic control of institutions. The enemy isn't violently suppressed but made to seem unreasonable.

## GILLES DELEUZE & FÉLIX GUATTARI: THE WAR MACHINE VS. THE STATE APPARATUS

### Nomadic Friend-Enemy Against Sedentary Power

**Key Works:**
- *Anti-Oedipus* (1972)
- *A Thousand Plateaus* (1980) - especially "Treatise on Nomadology: The War Machine"

**Core Thesis:**

Gilles Deleuze (1925-1995) and Félix Guattari (1930-1992) argue that the **State apparatus** and the **war machine** represent fundamentally opposed modes of social organization. The State operates through hierarchical, territorial friend-enemy distinctions, while the war machine operates through nomadic, deterritorializing flows that resist State categorization.

**Key Concepts:**

1. **State Apparatus**:
   - Sedentary, territorial organization
   - Hierarchical (king, bureaucracy, subjects)
   - Captures and codes all flows (labor, desire, population)
   - Defines friends (loyal subjects) and enemies (rebels, barbarians)
   - **Striated space** (divided, organized, controlled)

2. **War Machine**:
   - Nomadic, deterritorializing force
   - Horizontal, non-hierarchical
   - External to State (originally barbarian hordes)
   - Not about war but about **movement and transformation**
   - **Smooth space** (open, undefined, uncontrolled)

3. **Capture and Escape**:
   - State tries to capture war machine (turn it into army)
   - War machine tries to escape State control
   - Friend-enemy: State vs. that which evades capture
   - State makes war to destroy war machines

4. **Lines of Flight**:
   - Escape routes from State control
   - Deterritorialization (leaving coded territory)
   - Examples: Nomads, migrants, revolutionaries, artists
   - State designates these as **enemies** (barbarians, criminals, terrorists)

5. **Rhizome vs. Tree**:
   - **Tree**: Hierarchical, State structure (root, trunk, branches)
   - **Rhizome**: Horizontal, spreading, war machine structure
   - State wants to organize everything as tree (command structure)
   - Resistance operates rhizomatically (decentralized, multiple connections)

**Friend-Enemy Implications:**

1. **State Friend-Enemy**:
   - Friends: Territorialized, coded, captured (citizens, workers, taxpayers)
   - Enemies: Deterritorialized, decoded, escaping (nomads, migrants, revolutionaries)
   - **Enmity = resistance to capture**

2. **War Machine is Not Military**:
   - Original war machine was nomadic horde (Mongols, Huns)
   - Modern war machines: Guerrillas, hackers, migrants, social movements
   - Not organized armies (those are captured war machines serving State)
   - **Enemy = uncapturable movement**

3. **Friend-Enemy Through Space**:
   - State creates striated space (borders, property, zones)
   - War machine operates in smooth space (desert, ocean, cyberspace)
   - Border control = attempt to striate smooth space
   - **Spatial friend-enemy**: Territorialized vs. nomadic

4. **Capture Produces Enemies**:
   - State captures flows (labor, resources, populations)
   - Those who escape capture become enemies
   - Example: Homeless (escape property system), undocumented (escape borders)
   - **Enmity = non-participation in capture**

5. **Absolute Deterritorialization**:
   - Revolutionary war machine destroys State apparatus entirely
   - Not to create new State but to maintain movement
   - Ultimate friend-enemy: Stasis (State) vs. transformation (war machine)

**Examples:**

**Mongol Hordes:**
- Pure war machine before Genghis Khan created State
- Nomadic, smooth space (steppe)
- Enemy to all sedentary civilizations
- Eventually captured (became dynastic state)

**Guerrilla Warfare:**
- War machine resisting State capture
- Operates in smooth space (jungle, mountains, urban labyrinth)
- State tries to striate (surveillance, checkpoints, walls)
- Friend-enemy: Organized military vs. decentralized resistance

**Hacking/Cypherpunk:**
- Digital war machine
- Smooth space: Internet before regulation
- State tries to capture (surveillance, data retention, borders)
- Friend-enemy: Controlled networks vs. anonymous flows

**Migration:**
- Deterritorializing flow
- People escape State control (borders, citizenship)
- State designates as enemy ("illegal immigrant")
- Walls, detention = attempts to striate smooth space of movement

**Deleuze/Guattari vs. Schmitt:**

- **Schmitt**: Friend-enemy defines the political (State decides)
- **D&G**: War machine is non-State, pre-political
- **Schmitt**: Sovereign exception creates law
- **D&G**: War machine operates outside law (smooth space)
- **Schmitt**: Enemy is existential threat
- **D&G**: Enemy is that which cannot be captured by State

**Deleuze/Guattari vs. Hobbes:**

- **Hobbes**: State necessary to end war of all against all
- **D&G**: War machine is not war but creative transformation
- **Hobbes**: Enemy = anyone outside sovereign protection
- **D&G**: War machine = alternative to State, not its enemy necessarily

**Key Insight for Comprehensive Analysis:**

Deleuze & Guattari reveal friend-enemy as opposition between **capture and escape**—the State designates as enemies all those who evade its coding and territorialization. The enemy is not political opponent but **nomadic, deterritorializing force** that refuses State organization. This explains State hostility to migrants, nomads, guerrillas, hackers—anyone operating in smooth space outside State control.

**Critical Addition**: Provides **spatial-topological** understanding of friend-enemy. Shows State enmity toward anything uncapturable—nomads, migrants, decentralized movements. Explains why States hate bordercrossers, why surveillance proliferates (striation of smooth space), why guerrillas are "terrorists" (they're war machines State can't capture). Friend-enemy as sedentary vs. nomadic.

## PAULO FREIRE: PEDAGOGY OF THE OPPRESSED - OPPRESSOR/OPPRESSED DYNAMICS

### Education as Liberation or Domination

**Key Work:**
- *Pedagogy of the Oppressed* (1968)

**Core Thesis:**

Paulo Freire (1921-1997) analyzes how **oppressor-oppressed** relationships (a form of friend-enemy) are internalized through education and culture. Liberation requires "conscientization"—critical consciousness of one's position in power relations and collective action to transform them.

**Key Concepts:**

1. **Banking Model of Education**:
   - Traditional education treats students as empty vessels to be filled
   - Teacher deposits knowledge, students receive passively
   - Creates docile subjects who accept oppression
   - **Reproduces friend-enemy**: Ruling class knowledge presented as universal truth

2. **Problem-Posing Education**:
   - Teacher and students co-create knowledge
   - Critical dialogue about lived reality
   - Reveals oppression and possibilities for transformation
   - **Challenges friend-enemy** by making power visible

3. **Conscientization (Critical Consciousness)**:
   - Process of recognizing oppression
   - Stages:
     - **Magical consciousness**: Fate, no agency
     - **Naïve consciousness**: Individuals to blame
     - **Critical consciousness**: Systems of oppression understood
   - Once conscious, oppressed recognize their enemies (oppressor class/systems)

4. **Internalized Oppressor**:
   - Oppressed internalize oppressor's values
   - Aspire to become oppressors themselves
   - Must overcome this to achieve authentic liberation
   - **Self as enemy**: Internal oppressor must be confronted

5. **Dialogue vs. Anti-Dialogue**:
   - **Dialogue**: Horizontal, mutual, liberating
   - **Anti-Dialogue**: Vertical, monological, oppressive (banking education, propaganda)
   - Oppressors use anti-dialogue to maintain power
   - **Friend-enemy through communication style**

**Friend-Enemy Implications:**

1. **Oppressor-Oppressed as Structural Enmity**:
   - Not individual bad actors but **systemic relationship**
   - Oppressor class requires oppressed class for its existence
   - Enmity is **structural, not personal**

2. **Education Produces Friends or Enemies**:
   - Banking education: Produces docile "friends" of ruling class
   - Problem-posing education: Produces critical "enemies" who challenge system
   - **Schools are battlefield** for friend-enemy formation

3. **Consciousness Determines Enemy Recognition**:
   - Magical consciousness: No enemies (fate)
   - Naïve consciousness: Wrong enemies (individuals, not systems)
   - Critical consciousness: Correct enemies (oppressive systems and those who maintain them)

4. **Liberation Requires Enemy Recognition**:
   - Must name oppressors as enemies
   - But ultimate goal: Transform relationship (oppressor and oppressed both dehumanized)
   - **Dialectical**: Recognize enmity to transcend it

5. **Internalized Oppression**:
   - Oppressed see themselves as worthy of oppression
   - Horizontal violence (oppressed attack each other, not oppressors)
   - **Must recognize external enemy before recognizing internal one**

**Examples:**

**Colonial Education:**
- French colonial schools taught Africans "our ancestors the Gauls"
- Internalized inferiority, saw colonizer as friend/savior
- Liberation movements required conscientization (Fanon, Cabral)
- Friend-enemy: Colonial knowledge vs. indigenous knowledge

**Standardized Testing:**
- Banking model par excellence
- "Objective" knowledge (ruling class knowledge)
- Working-class students fail, internalize inferiority
- Friend-enemy: Those who succeed (assimilate ruling class values) vs. those who fail

**Media Literacy:**
- Critical education reveals media as propaganda
- Banking: Media tells truth (passive acceptance)
- Problem-posing: Media serves ruling class interests (critical analysis)
- Friend-enemy: Mainstream narratives vs. counter-narratives

**Labor Education:**
- Banking: Workers need skills for jobs
- Problem-posing: Workers recognize exploitation, organize
- Capitalists oppose critical education (charter schools, attacks on unions)
- Friend-enemy: Trained workers vs. class-conscious workers

**Freire vs. Gramsci:**

- **Gramsci**: Hegemony through cultural institutions (intellectuals lead)
- **Freire**: Conscientization through dialogue (oppressed lead themselves)
- **Gramsci**: Organic intellectuals
- **Freire**: All can achieve critical consciousness
- Both: Culture is battlefield for friend-enemy formation

**Freire vs. Schmitt:**

- **Schmitt**: Friend-enemy is political intensity (given)
- **Freire**: Oppressor-oppressed must be recognized through conscientization (learned)
- **Schmitt**: Enmity unites friends
- **Freire**: Recognition of oppression unites oppressed

**Key Insight for Comprehensive Analysis:**

Freire reveals that friend-enemy consciousness must be **developed through education**. Oppressed don't automatically recognize their enemies—this requires conscientization. Education either produces docile subjects who see oppressors as friends (banking model) or critical subjects who recognize and challenge oppression (problem-posing). The enemy is not just external (oppressor class) but internalized (oppressor within).

**Critical Addition**: Shows friend-enemy as **pedagogical**—learned through education, not natural. Explains why ruling classes control education (to prevent enemy recognition) and why liberation requires "consciousness-raising." The oppressed must learn who their enemies are through critical dialogue. Education is the primary battlefield for friend-enemy formation.

## MICHEL FOUCAULT: DISCIPLINARY POWER AND THE MICRO-PHYSICS OF ENMITY

### Power Creates Subjects and Enemies at Capillary Level

**Key Works:**
- *Discipline and Punish* (1975)
- *The History of Sexuality* (1976)
- *Society Must Be Defended* (1975-76 lectures)

**Core Thesis:**

Michel Foucault (1926-1984) argues that modern power operates not through sovereign commands (Schmitt) but through **disciplinary mechanisms** that produce subjects at the micro level. Friend-enemy distinctions are internalized through surveillance, normalization, and examination—creating "docile bodies" (friends) and "abnormals" (enemies).

**Key Concepts:**

1. **Disciplinary Power**:
   - Not repressive but productive (creates subjects)
   - Operates through institutions: schools, hospitals, prisons, factories
   - Techniques: Surveillance, examination, normalization
   - **Friend = normal, enemy = abnormal**

2. **Panopticism**:
   - Bentham's Panopticon: Prison where all can be seen by central tower
   - Internalized surveillance: Subjects police themselves
   - Modern society = archipelago of panopticons (schools, workplaces, hospitals)
   - **Friend-enemy through visibility**: Those under surveillance vs. those who evade it

3. **Normalization**:
   - Power operates through norms (averages, standards)
   - Examination measures deviation from norm
   - **Normal = friend, abnormal = enemy**
   - Examples: Mental illness, criminal, homosexual, disabled

4. **Biopolitics**:
   - Power over populations (not just individuals)
   - Manages life: Health, reproduction, life expectancy
   - **Make live, let die** (vs. sovereign's "take life or let live")
   - Friend-enemy at population level: Healthy populations vs. dangerous populations

5. **Racism as Biopolitical**:
   - Modern racism isn't hatred but population management
   - Some races "endanger" population health
   - Justify "letting die" or killing
   - **Friend-enemy through biology**: Superior races vs. degenerative races

**Friend-Enemy Implications:**

1. **Enemy as Abnormal**:
   - Not political opponent but **deviant from norm**
   - Criminal, insane, pervert, sick
   - Disciplinary institutions "normalize" (make friends) or exclude (mark as enemies)

2. **Self-Surveillance Creates Friends**:
   - Internalized panopticism
   - Subject police their own behavior
   - **Friend = self-disciplining subject**
   - Those who won't self-discipline = enemies

3. **Micro-Physics of Power**:
   - Friend-enemy not just macro (State) but micro (every interaction)
   - Teacher-student, doctor-patient, manager-worker
   - Each involves friend-enemy distinctions (normal vs. abnormal)

4. **Population-Level Enmity**:
   - Biopolitics designates dangerous populations:
     - "Criminal classes"
     - "Welfare queens"
     - "Terrorists"
     - "Diseased" (HIV, COVID)
   - **Friend-enemy through statistical management**

5. **War as Social Relation**:
   - Foucault inverts Clausewitz: "Politics is war by other means"
   - Society is battlefield where races/classes struggle
   - Peace is just **suspended war**
   - Friend-enemy is permanent underlying condition

**Examples:**

**Schooling:**
- Examination creates normal (good students) vs. abnormal (bad students)
- Surveillance: Attendance, behavior monitoring
- Normalization: Grading curves, standardized tests
- Friend-enemy: Compliant students vs. disruptive students

**Prison:**
- Creates "criminal" category through discipline
- Surveillance (guards, cameras, informants)
- Normalization: Rehabilitate to norm or permanently mark as deviant
- Friend-enemy: Rehabilitated vs. recidivists

**Medicine:**
- Normal body vs. pathological body
- Medical gaze creates "patient" subject
- Public health: Quarantine enemies (diseased)
- Friend-enemy: Healthy vs. contagious

**COVID-19:**
- Biopolitical friend-enemy par excellence
- Vacc inated vs. unvaccinated
- Mask compliance vs. refusal
- Quarantine, contact tracing, passports
- Friend-enemy through public health norms

**Foucault vs. Schmitt:**

- **Schmitt**: Friend-enemy through sovereign decision (top-down)
- **Foucault**: Friend-enemy through disciplinary normalization (bottom-up, capillary)
- **Schmitt**: Exceptional, intense moments
- **Foucault**: Everyday, diffuse practices
- **Schmitt**: Political enemy
- **Foucault**: Abnormal enemy

**Foucault vs. Marx:**

- **Marx**: Class enemy (bourgeoisie vs. proletariat)
- **Foucault**: Multiple enmities (normal vs. abnormal in many domains)
- **Marx**: Repressive power (State serves ruling class)
- **Foucault**: Productive power (creates subjects)

**Key Insight for Comprehensive Analysis:**

Foucault reveals friend-enemy operates at **micro level** through disciplinary institutions—schools, hospitals, prisons, workplaces. Power creates enemies not through sovereign declaration but through **normalization**—measuring deviations from norms and excluding or "rehabilitating" abnormals. Every examination, every surveillance system, every standard creates friend-enemy distinctions. The enemy is the abnormal, the deviant, the one who won't self-discipline.

**Critical Addition**: Shows friend-enemy as **everyday, micro-political, institutional**. Explains how schools create "good" and "bad" students, how medicine creates "healthy" and "diseased," how workplaces create "productive" and "troublesome" workers. Friend-enemy isn't just political but operates through **normalization in every institution**. Also shows racism as biopolitical—population-level friend-enemy management.

## JUDITH BUTLER: PRECARIOUS LIFE AND GRIEVABLE VS. UNGRIEVABLE LIVES

### Whose Lives Matter? The Frame of War

**Key Works:**
- *Precarious Life: The Powers of Mourning and Violence* (2004)
- *Frames of War: When Is Life Grievable?* (2009)
- *The Force of Nonviolence* (2020)

**Core Thesis:**

Judith Butler (1956-) argues that friend-enemy distinctions operate through **differential allocation of grievability**—some lives are recognized as valuable and grievable (friends), others are seen as not fully human and ungrievable (enemies). This "framing" determines whose death matters and whose can be ignored or even celebrated.

**Key Concepts:**

1. **Precarious Life**:
   - All human life is vulnerable, dependent, precarious
   - But **precarity is differentially distributed**
   - Some protected from precariousness (friends)
   - Others exposed to it (enemies)

2. **Grievability**:
   - **Grievable life**: Death is mourned, recognized as loss
   - **Ungrievable life**: Death doesn't register as loss
   - Grievability determines which lives are valued
   - **Friend = grievable, enemy = ungrievable**

3. **Frames of War**:
   - How violence is framed determines what counts as violence
   - Some deaths "count" (terrorist attack), others don't (drone strike casualties)
   - **Framing creates friend-enemy**: Who is victim vs. who is target
   - Media, state, culture set the frames

4. **Recognizability**:
   - To be recognized as human requires conforming to norms
   - Those outside norms = not fully human
   - **Recognition = friend status, non-recognition = enemy status**
   - Examples: Queer lives, Palestinian lives, Black lives

5. **Ethical Violence**:
   - Violence against ungrievable lives doesn't count as violence
   - "Self-defense" against those whose lives don't matter
   - **Enemy designation = license to kill without murder**

**Friend-Enemy Implications:**

1. **Differential Grievability**:
   - American soldier killed: National mourning, name published, hero
   - Iraqi civilian killed: Statistics, collateral damage, unnamed
   - **Friend-enemy through mourning**: Who gets grief vs. who is invisible

2. **Framing Determines Enmity**:
   - "Terrorist attack": Mourning, outrage, response
   - "Drone strike": Strategic, necessary, calculated
   - Same act (killing) framed differently based on friend-enemy status

3. **Ungrievable as Killable**:
   - Those whose deaths don't matter can be killed
   - Similar to Agamben's homo sacer and Mbembe's necropolitics
   - **But Butler emphasizes affective dimension**: Failure to grieve

4. **Recognition Politics**:
   - Struggle to be recognized as human
   - Black Lives Matter: Demand that Black deaths be grieved
   - Palestinian rights: Demand that Palestinian deaths matter
   - **Political struggle over grievability**

5. **Violence Without Perpetrator**:
   - When deaths are ungrievable, no one responsible
   - "Deaths of despair," poverty deaths, climate deaths
   - **Enemy is system that produces ungrievability**

**Examples:**

**9/11 vs. Afghanistan/Iraq:**
- 3,000 American deaths: National trauma, memorials, "never forget"
- ~1 million Middle Eastern deaths: Rarely counted, certainly not mourned
- Friend-enemy: American lives grievable vs. Muslim lives ungrievable

**Black Lives Matter:**
- Police killing of Black people: Often unjustified, not prosecuted
- Demand: Recognize Black lives as grievable
- "Say their names": Make visible those rendered invisible
- Friend-enemy: Whose deaths matter to justice system?

**Palestine/Israel:**
- Israeli deaths: International mourning, condemnation of "terrorism"
- Palestinian deaths: "Militants," "human shields," statistics
- Framing creates friend-enemy: Grievable Israelis vs. ungrievable Palestinians

**COVID-19:**
- Initially: All deaths mourned
- Eventually: "Only elderly/sick" → ungrievable
- "Let them die to save economy"
- Friend-enemy: Productive lives (grievable) vs. dependent lives (ungrievable)

**Butler vs. Schmitt:**

- **Schmitt**: Friend-enemy is political decision (who you'd kill/die for)
- **Butler**: Friend-enemy is affective/ethical (who you'd grieve for)
- **Schmitt**: Intensity of conflict
- **Butler**: Intensity of mourning (or its absence)

**Butler vs. Agamben:**

- **Agamben**: Homo sacer = bare life, killable but not sacrificeable
- **Butler**: Ungrievable life = not recognized as loss
- **Agamben**: Legal abandonment
- **Butler**: Affective abandonment (no grief)
- Both: Some lives don't count as lives

**Key Insight for Comprehensive Analysis:**

Butler reveals friend-enemy through **differential grievability**—whose deaths matter (friends) and whose don't (enemies). This operates through framing: how media, state, and culture represent violence determines what counts as violence and who counts as victim. The enemy isn't just someone you'd kill but someone whose death you wouldn't mourn. Conversely, friends are those whose deaths would be grieved, recognized, memorialized.

**Critical Addition**: Shows friend-enemy as **affective and ethical**—based on who we mourn. Explains why some deaths make headlines while others are statistics, why some victims get names while others get numbers. Friend-enemy operates through **media framing** of whose lives matter. Also provides normative dimension: Argues all lives should be grievable, challenges friend-enemy distinction itself.
