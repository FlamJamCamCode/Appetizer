## Zone 1: Story Capture - The Narrative Prison

### Scenario: The Make-A-Wish Foundation

**You encounter a dying child.** The room shows medical data: curable disease, $50,000 treatment cost, family bankrupt from prior treatments.

**The child says**: "I wish for a trip to Disney World. I wish for a video game console. I wish for a puppy."

**But the child is crying at night**: "Mommy, why can't you fix me? Why can't you make them help?"

**The Foundation offers**: Luxury vacations, entertainment systems, pets.

**The Foundation does NOT offer**: Research funding, treatment access, systemic change.

### Your Diagnosis Question

**If you were the parent, what would you demand?**

A) **Entertainment for my dying child** (Story acceptance - "this is what children want")
B) **Systemic change in medical access** (Story rejection - sees the real failure)
C) **Both, but entertainment first** (Partial capture - story dominates)

**Pattern Recognition**: If you chose A or C, you suffer from **Story Capture**. Markets have taught you that shallow narratives are "what people want," blinding you to deeper needs.

**Restoration Path**: Practice seeing through marketing narratives. Ask "What would actually solve this?" instead of "What do people say they want?"
