# The First Conquest: A Warrior's Tale

*From the Early Days of the Great Steppe Games*

---

## Part I: The Waiting

Dmitri Volkov stood at the edge of the respawn zone, his heart pounding not from fear but from anticipation. Twenty-three years old. Russian Spetsnaz. Born too late for real war, too early to accept peace.

His grandfather had fought in Afghanistan. His great-grandfather at Stalingrad. Warriors, all of them. And now? Garrison duty. Training exercises. Soul-death.

Until the Games.

**The choice**: Long-term contraception or accept the consequences. He'd chosen the implant. Three years of protection. He wanted to fight, to conquer, to prove himself—but children? Not yet. Not like this.

**The stakes**: His faction (Red Banner) versus the Golden Horde. Total War Mode. Seven days. Everything allowed within MBC+ (1234).

**The prize**: Territory. Women. Glory. Wealth.

**The cost**: Pain. Humiliation. Temporary slavery if captured.

**The safety net**: "I leave the game!" and extraction within 15 minutes. Always.

He checked his gear. Non-lethal rounds. Shock weapons. Restraints. Medical beacon. And the knowledge that if he "died," he'd respawn in 24 hours.

**This was Valhalla made real.**

---

## Part II: The First Blood

The steppe stretched endlessly. Somewhere out there, the Golden Horde waited.

Dmitri moved with his squad—five men, all hungry for glory. They'd trained for this. Dreamed of this.

**Contact.**

Three riders appeared on the horizon. Kazakhs. Golden Horde scouts.

The firefight was brief. Non-lethal rounds, but they hit like hammers. One Kazakh went down. Another fled. The third—

Dmitri tackled him off his horse. They grappled in the grass. The Kazakh was strong, skilled, fierce.

But Dmitri was **hungrier**.

He got the restraints on. The Kazakh struggled, cursed in Russian and Kazakh, then went still.

**First capture.**

"What do we do with him?" asked Sergei, breathing hard.

Dmitri looked at the prisoner. Young, maybe twenty. Eyes burning with rage and shame.

**The rules**: They could enslave him. Ransom him. Humiliate him. Anything within MBC+.

"We take him back," Dmitri said. "He's ours now."

The Kazakh spat. "Fuck you, Russian dog."

Dmitri smiled. Not cruel. Almost respectful. "You'll get your chance. This is just Round One."

---

## Part III: The Slave Market

The Red Banner camp was chaos and celebration. They'd captured twelve Golden Horde warriors in the first day.

The slave market opened at sunset.

Dmitri watched as his Kazakh prisoner was brought to the platform. Stripped to waist. Hands bound. The auctioneer called out his stats—age, combat record, projected ransom value.

**Bidding started.**

Women from the Red Banner faction bid. Some wanted servants. Some wanted... more.

A woman named Katya won. Thirty years old. Veteran of the Games. She paid 50,000 rubles for one month of service.

The Kazakh's face was stone. But his eyes... Dmitri saw it. The calculation. The planning. **This wasn't over.**

"You'll try to escape," Dmitri said quietly as they transferred the prisoner.

The Kazakh looked at him. "Every day."

"Good," Dmitri said. "That's what makes it real."

---

## Part IV: The Reversal

Day Five. Everything changed.

The Golden Horde had been planning. Watching. Learning.

They hit the Red Banner camp at dawn. Not with brute force, but with **precision**. They'd studied the respawn patterns, the guard rotations, the weak points.

Dmitri woke to chaos. Smoke. Shouts. Non-lethal rounds tearing through tents.

He grabbed his weapon, but too late. A Kazakh warrior—**his prisoner from Day One**—tackled him.

"Remember me, Russian dog?"

They fought. Dmitri was good. But the Kazakh was **motivated**. Revenge. Honor. Rage.

The restraints clicked shut.

**Captured.**

---

## Part V: The Humiliation

The Golden Horde didn't enslave him. They did something worse.

They paraded him through their camp. Stripped. Bound. On display.

The women of the Golden Horde watched. Some laughed. Some looked at him with hunger. Some with contempt.

**This was the price of conquest.**

His Kazakh captor stood before him. "You said I'd get my chance. Here it is."

Dmitri expected a beating. Expected humiliation.

Instead, the Kazakh cut his bonds.

"Fight me. Right now. No weapons. Just us."

The camp formed a circle. Dmitri's body ached. His pride burned.

They fought.

It was brutal. Honest. Raw.

Dmitri lost. But he fought **hard**. Gave everything.

When it was over, both men lay in the grass, bleeding, exhausted.

The Kazakh extended his hand. "You're a warrior. Not just a Russian dog."

Dmitri took it. "You're a warrior. Not just a Kazakh."

**Respect earned.**

---

## Part VI: The Ransom

Red Banner paid 100,000 rubles for Dmitri's release. He respawned in his faction's camp, bruised but alive.

His commander asked, "Was it worth it?"

Dmitri thought about it. The conquest. The capture. The humiliation. The respect.

"Yes," he said. "Every second."

Because this was what his grandfather had known. What his great-grandfather had died for.

**The warrior's path.**

Not in some distant Valhalla. Not in memory or myth.

But **here**. **Now**. **Real**.

---

## Part VII: The Cycle

Dmitri fought in the Games for three years. He conquered. He was conquered. He enslaved. He was enslaved.

He took a woman's virginity in a raid—a Golden Horde girl who'd chosen no contraception, who wanted to experience the full reality of conquest. She bore his child. He paid support. They never spoke again, but he saw the boy sometimes, growing up in the Kazakh camps.

He was ravaged by a Mongolian woman who'd captured him, who used him for a week before releasing him with a smile and a scar.

He became a legend in Red Banner. "The Wolf of the Steppe." Feared. Respected. **Alive**.

And when he finally left the Games at twenty-six, he'd lived more than most men live in a lifetime.

**Valhalla wasn't a promise anymore.**

**It was a place you could visit.**

**And return from.**

**Forever.**

---

## Epilogue: The Legend Begins

*Year 2535 CE - 448 years after the Games began*

The children gather around the fire as the old storyteller begins:

"In the first days, when warriors still remembered the soul-death of peace, there came a man named Dmitri Volkov. The Wolf of the Steppe. First of the Conquerors. First of the Conquered..."

The legend has grown. Dmitri is no longer just a man. He's become something more.

**A demigod of the eternal war.**

**A symbol of the warrior's path.**

**Proof that Valhalla is real.**

And somewhere, in the respawn zones of the Great Steppe Games, men still fight. Still conquer. Still fall.

**Forever.**

**END**
