# 12d. Late Gothic Excess (1350-1500 AD) — Desperate Beauty After the Plague

*When Death Trauma Created Hyper-Decoration: Flamboyant Tracery, Fan Vaults, and the Final Cathedral Competitions Before Renaissance*

---

## Temporal Context

**Period**: 1350-1500 AD (Late Middle Ages—post-Black Death trauma, pre-Renaissance shift)

**150 years**: From Black Death's end (1353 AD—but trauma remains) to Renaissance beginning (1500s—architectural paradigm shift away from Gothic)

**Major context**:
- **Black Death** (1347-1353 AD): 30-60% of Europe dead, worst catastrophe ever, theological crisis "why did God allow this?"
- **Demographic collapse** (1350-1400 AD): Labor shortage (survivors demand higher wages, peasant revolts, social upheaval), construction slows (fewer workers, higher costs)
- **Wealth concentration** (1400-1500 AD): Survivors inherit (multiple estates consolidated, massive wealth in fewer hands, some patrons VERY rich)
- **Psychological trauma** (1350-1450 AD): Mortality obsession (everyone knows death is near, plague could return anytime), desperate transcendence (build SO beautifully death can't touch us)
- **Hundred Years' War climax** (1415-1453 AD): England vs France, architectural nationalism (English Perpendicular asserts independence, French Flamboyant asserts superiority)
- **Ottoman expansion** (1400s): Constantinople falls 1453 AD, Byzantine refugees flee West, bring Eastern influences, Islamic threat intensifies Gothic-as-Christian-identity

**Key development**: Gothic becomes EXCESSIVE (hyper-decorated, hyper-vertical, hyper-elaborate—death trauma creates desperate beauty, "build so magnificently God MUST see us, protect us next time," architecture as prayer against mortality)

## Part I: The Black Death's Psychological Impact on Architecture

### The Immediate Pause (1347-1353 AD)

**What happened to construction**:

**Cathedrals mid-construction** (1347 AD):
- Workers die (30-60% mortality—masons, carpenters, laborers dead)
- Funding collapses (donors die, wealth destroyed, no money for construction)
- Priorities shift (focus on survival, not beauty—cathedral construction seems frivolous when millions dying)

**Major projects abandoned**:
- **Siena Cathedral expansion** (planned to make biggest cathedral in Italy, expansion abandoned 1348, never resumed)
- **Beauvais Cathedral nave** (choir collapsed 1284, reconstruction attempted 1300s, plague killed it, never built nave)
- **Cologne Cathedral** (construction slowed 1350s, stopped 1473, not resumed until 1842—370-year gap)

**The theological crisis**:
- "We built tallest cathedrals (Amiens 42m, Beauvais 48m), closest to heaven, most beautiful—why didn't God PROTECT us?"
- "Is Gothic architecture WRONG? Did we displease God? Is vertical aspiration PRIDE (Babel-sin)?"
- "Should we return to Romanesque (fortress-churches to SURVIVE), abandon Gothic (beauty didn't save us)?"

---

### The Two Responses (1350-1400 AD)

**Response 1: Abandon Gothic** (minority):
- Some bishops: "Gothic failed, return to Romanesque or try new style"
- Italy especially: "Gothic is Northern barbarism anyway, didn't work, return to Classical (Renaissance embryonic)"
- **Result**: Some regions slow Gothic construction, shift to other styles

**Response 2: Double down on Gothic** (majority):
- Most bishops: "We didn't build BEAUTIFULLY ENOUGH (God punished our insufficient devotion), we'll build MORE beautifully, MORE excessively, MORE devotedly"
- **Psychology**: Denial through excess ("If we make cathedrals SO gorgeous, death can't touch us")
- **Result**: Late Gothic / Flamboyant / Perpendicular—HYPER-Gothic emerges, more elaborate than ever

**The trauma theology**:
- "Death is everywhere (plague could return anytime, mortality is CERTAIN)"
- "We can't control death (medicine useless, prayer didn't prevent plague, we're helpless)"
- "We CAN control beauty (architecture is one thing we MASTER, build so beautifully we transcend death symbolically)"
- **Late Gothic = death denial through excessive beauty** (if we can't conquer death physically, we'll conquer it aesthetically)

## Part II: French Flamboyant (1350-1500 AD) — Flame Tracery as Desperate Reaching

### The Daemon: Terror Vacui (Fear of Empty Space)

**What Flamboyant IS**:

**Structurally**: Gothic taken to extreme (same pointed arches, ribbed vaults, flying buttresses—but MORE)

**Aesthetically**: EVERYTHING decorated (no surface left plain, horror of emptiness, fill all space with carving)

**Psychologically**: Desperate (post-plague trauma visible—can't tolerate emptiness/silence/stillness, must FILL with beauty)

**The name**: Flamboyant (French: "flaming"—tracery looks like FLAMES, curving, undulating, flowing)

---

### The S-Curve Revolution (1350-1400 AD)

**Rayonnant tracery** (1240-1350 AD—pre-plague):
- **Geometric**: Circles, trefoils, quatrefoils (mathematical, rational, ordered)
- **Straight lines**: Mullions are vertical/horizontal (grid-like, perpendicular)
- **Predictable**: Patterns repeat (symmetrical, balanced, classical restraint)

**Flamboyant tracery** (1350-1500 AD—post-plague):
- **Organic**: S-curves, flame shapes, flowing forms (irrational, emotional, chaotic-beautiful)
- **Curved lines**: Mullions are CURVED (undulating, flame-like, resembling fire or flowing fabric)
- **Unique**: Every window different (asymmetrical, surprising, excessive variation)

**The shift**: From rational order (Rayonnant) to emotional chaos-beauty (Flamboyant)—trauma visible in stone

---

### Masterpiece: Church of Saint-Maclou (Rouen, 1437-1521 AD)

**The façade**:
- **Five gabled porches** (unusual—most churches have one or three, five is excessive)
- **Each gable**: Covered in flame tracery (S-curves, undulating, organic, looks like FIRE frozen in stone)
- **Tympanums**: Elaborately carved (Last Judgment, saints, Biblical scenes—every surface filled)

**The doors**:
- **Wooden doors carved** (Renaissance later, but stone door-frames are Flamboyant—intricate, lace-like)
- **Stone so thin** it looks like FABRIC (denying stone's weight, architectural impossibility)

**The interior**:
- **Spiral staircase to organ loft** (famous—stone staircase so intricately carved it's TRANSPARENT-looking, visitors can see through stone, approaching impossibility)
- **Lierne vaults**: Elaborate rib patterns (structural ribs + decorative liernes creating net-like ceiling)

**The psychology**: "We SURVIVED plague (barely), we'll make EVERY SURFACE beautiful (nothing left undecorated, death can't claim beauty this excessive), stone will become LACE (deny materiality, approach pure form, transcend through art)"

---

### Flamboyant Characteristics (Summary)

**1. Flame tracery** (defining feature):
- S-curves creating flame shapes (windows look like frozen fire)
- Ogee arches (double-curved, S-shaped arches—flowing, dynamic)
- Mouchette and soufflet (flame-like tracery motifs, uniquely Flamboyant)

**2. Excessive decoration** (horror vacui):
- Crockets everywhere (small carved decorations on pinnacles, gables, ribs—covering all edges)
- Finials elaborate (pinnacle tops become carved sculptures, not simple points)
- Bosses intricate (roof boss at every rib intersection, each uniquely carved)

**3. Structural overkill** (more ribs than necessary):
- Tiercerons (extra ribs from springers to ridge)
- Liernes (connecting ribs, purely decorative, create patterns)
- Result: Ceiling is RIB-FOREST (ribs outnumber necessary structure 3:1, excessive, obsessive)

**4. Attenuated forms** (everything stretched thin):
- Columns like needles (as thin as possible, elongated, stretched)
- Mullions like threads (window stone bars minimized, approaching invisibility)
- Everything REACHING (desperate vertical extension, stretching toward heaven)

## Part III: English Late Gothic (1350-1500 AD) — Fan Vaults as Engineering Art

### The Perpendicular Maturity (1350-1450 AD)

**The daemon**: **Structural mastery + decorative excess** (engineering pride + post-plague beauty-obsession)

**The fan vault invention** (1350s-1370s):

**What is fan vault**:
- Ribs FAN OUT from single point (like peacock tail, or palm frond, or handheld fan)
- Creates conoid (cone-shaped vault section)
- Purely DECORATIVE (structurally unnecessary—regular ribbed vault would work, fan vault is OVERKILL)
- Uniquely English (never adopted elsewhere—too complex, too expensive, too English-specific)

**Why invent decorative-only vaulting?**:
- **Engineering pride**: "We CAN build this (even though unnecessary, proves our mastery)"
- **Beauty obsession**: "Fan vault is most beautiful ceiling possible (surpasses all other vaulting, French can't match this)"
- **Post-plague denial**: "We'll make ceiling SO beautiful, SO complex, death can't touch us (beauty as immortality-substitute)"

---

### Masterpiece: King's College Chapel (Cambridge, 1446-1515 AD)

**Commissioned by Henry VI** (1441 AD):
- Eton College + King's College (twin foundations, educational + religious)
- Chapel for college (King's College Cambridge chapel)
- **Ambition**: "Most beautiful chapel in England (royal project, must surpass all)"

**The fan vaults** (1512-1515 AD, built by John Wastell):
- **Largest fan vaults ever built** (12 fan-vault bays, each spanning 12m wide, 24m tall)
- Continuous (entire chapel ceiling is ONE fan vault sequence—no interruption, 88m of continuous fan-vaulting)
- **Complexity**: Each fan has 16 ribs radiating, ribs branch and rebranch, creating lace-like effect
- **Weight**: Purely decorative (structural vault could be much simpler, fan is ARTISTIC CHOICE)

**The windows** (1515-1547 AD):
- **Largest in Perpendicular Gothic** (each 15m tall, 9m wide—29 windows total)
- Grid tracery (perpendicular mullions creating rectangular panels, filled with glass)
- Biblical narrative (Genesis to Revelation in glass, some panels Renaissance-era but Gothic framing)

**The effect**: Walking into King's College Chapel = entering STONE LACE-TENT
- Ceiling: Fans spread above like stone palm grove (organic, flowing, denies gravity)
- Walls: Glass grid (90% glass, 10% stone, dissolved walls)
- Light: Flooding through massive windows (Perpendicular's light-obsession matching French Rayonnant)
- **Experience**: "I'm in structure that SHOULDN'T BE POSSIBLE (fan vaults are engineering magic), beauty denies death (this will outlast plague, this is eternal)"

---

### Other English Fan Vault Masterpieces

**Bath Abbey** (1499-1539 AD):
- Fan vaults in nave (covering entire nave ceiling, not just choir)
- **Innovation**: Nave fan vaults (King's College is chapel—smaller, Bath is full abbey—bigger span)

**Henry VII's Chapel** (Westminster Abbey, 1503-1512 AD):
- **Pendant fan vaults** (fans HANG from ceiling—gravity-defying, stone hanging like stalactites)
- Most complex vaulting ever (pendants + fans + ribs = three-layer complexity)
- **Claim**: "We achieved IMPOSSIBLE (stone hanging from ceiling, appears weightless, ultimate fan vault)"

**The English fan vault obsession** (1350-1550 AD):
- 50+ churches with fan vaults (mostly England, few elsewhere—English monopoly on this technology)
- Competitive (each trying to outdo previous—wider spans, more complex patterns, more pendants)
- **Post-plague psychology visible**: "We MASTER stone (make it lace, make it hang, make it FAN), death mastered us (plague), we master art to deny death's mastery"

## Part IV: German Late Gothic (1350-1560 AD) — The Eternal Cathedral Problem

### The Ambition-Capacity Gap

**German daemon**: **Imperial ambition (dream biggest) + Limited capacity (can't finish) = Eternal construction**

**The pattern**:

**Cologne Cathedral** (1248-1880 AD—632 years):
- Planned 1248: Twin towers 157m (tallest in world)
- Built 1248-1473: Choir + part of nave (slow, expensive)
- STOPPED 1473: Funding ran out, Reformation disrupted, abandoned
- Resumed 1842: Prussian nationalism (complete as German symbol)
- Completed 1880: With modern steel (medieval masonry couldn't support 157m, needed 19th century engineering)

**Ulm Minster** (1377-1890 AD—513 years):
- Planned 1377: Tower 161.5m (tallest church tower ever—surpass Cologne)
- Built 1377-1543: Nave + lower tower (slow, city-funded, no king paying)
- STOPPED 1543: Reformation (Ulm became Protestant, lost interest in Catholic mega-tower)
- Resumed 1844: German nationalism (complete as Baden-Württemberg symbol)
- Completed 1890: Modern engineering (medieval couldn't finish, required industrial-age steel/cranes)

**St. Stephen's Cathedral** (Vienna, 1359-1433 AD—rapid by German standards!):
- South tower: 136m (completed 1433—FINISHED in medieval era, rare German success)
- North tower: Planned to match, abandoned 1511 (one tower asymmetry, forever unfinished)

**The German lesson**: "We dream TOO BIG (imperial ambition exceeds medieval engineering), our cathedrals become ETERNAL PROJECTS (generations inherit unfinished work), completion requires future technology (we build for centuries we won't see)"

---

### The Brick Gothic Persists (Northern Germany, 1350-1550 AD)

**Why Northern Germany stayed brick**:
- No stone quarries (Baltic region is clay, not limestone—brick is local material)
- Hanseatic pride (merchant cities, brick is THEIR material, not copying French stone)
- Economic constraints (brick is cheaper than imported stone, Hanseatic League is rich but pragmatic)

**Brick Gothic innovations** (1350-1500 AD):

**St. Mary's Church** (Gdańsk, 1379-1502 AD):
- **Largest brick church in world** (105m long, 66m wide, vaults 30m high)
- Brick vaulting (star vaults, elaborate rib patterns, all in BRICK—no stone ribs)
- **Effect**: Brick creates WARM Gothic (red brick vs cold gray stone, different aesthetic, more intimate despite size)

**St. Nicholas' Church** (Stralsund, 1270-1360 AD):
- Tower 151m planned (would be tallest brick tower ever)
- Actually built: 104m (1360 AD), destroyed by lightning 1647, never rebuilt to full height
- **Lesson**: "Even brick has limits (can't go as tall as stone, 151m was hubris)"

**The Brick Gothic daemon**:
- **Pragmatic beauty** (work within material limits, don't dream impossible)
- **Merchant aesthetic** (Hanseatic League—practical, functional, wealthy but not ostentatious)
- **Northern distinctiveness** (brick = Baltic identity, we're NOT Southern stone-snobs, we're NORTHERN pragmatists)

## Part V: The Horror Vacui Principle—Filling Every Surface

### Why Late Gothic Can't Tolerate Empty Space

**Terror vacui** (Latin: fear of emptiness):

**Psychological origin**:
- Plague created VOID (empty cities, empty houses, empty churches—60% of people GONE)
- Survivors traumatized (silence = death, emptiness = graves, void = plague)
- **Response**: FILL EVERYTHING (can't tolerate empty space, reminds of death, must DECORATE to deny void)

**Architectural manifestation**:

**Flamboyant excess**:
- Every window: Flame tracery (no plain windows, all elaborate)
- Every wall: Blind tracery (decorative stone patterns even where no windows, covering solid walls)
- Every surface: Carved (capitals, corbels, bosses, stringcourses, buttresses—everything FILLED with decoration)

**English Perpendicular excess**:
- Every ceiling: Fan vaults (no plain vaults, all must be fans)
- Every wall: Panel tracery (even solid walls get tracery patterns, making them look like windows)
- Every corner: Carved bosses (400+ in Exeter Cathedral, 1,000+ in some cathedrals—obsessive)

**German excess**:
- Every tower: Pierced (openwork spires, stone becomes LACE, denying solidity)
- Every façade: Sculpture (Strasbourg 1,000+ statues, Cologne 2,000+ statues planned)

**The universal pattern** (all Late Gothic):
- No empty space tolerated (everything must be FILLED, CARVED, DECORATED)
- Structural overkill (more ribs than necessary, more buttresses than needed, excessive engineering)
- Approaching impossibility (stone stretched to limits, becoming lace/fabric/transparency)

**The psychology**: "Emptiness = death (plague left voids), we DENY death by denying void (fill everything with beauty, no room for death to hide, beauty as apotropaic—wards off mortality)"

## Part VI: The Final Height Competitions (1400-1500 AD)

### The Tallest Tower Obsession

**Why towers mattered MORE post-plague**:

**Pre-plague towers** (1200-1350 AD):
- Functional (bell towers, city landmarks, pilgrimage beacons)
- Modest (80-120m typical, some taller but rare)
- **Purpose**: Call to prayer (bells), assert civic pride (tall = important city)

**Post-plague towers** (1350-1500 AD):
- Symbolic (reaching heaven DESPERATELY, touching God to prevent next plague)
- Extreme (140-160m attempted, many never completed)
- **Purpose**: Deny death (build SO tall, death can't reach us, heaven is NEAR)

**The competition**:

**Strasbourg Cathedral** (1439 AD—tower completed):
- **142m** (tallest structure in WORLD from 1647-1874—held record for 227 years!)
- Actually COMPLETED (rare for mega-tower)
- Openwork spire (stone becomes LACE, pyramid of pierced stone, approaches transparency)
- **Claim**: "We built tallest and FINISHED it (unlike Cologne/Ulm who only dreamed)"

**Beauvais Cathedral** (1569 AD—tower ATTEMPTED):
- Choir already problematic (48m, collapsed 1284, rebuilt, unstable)
- 1569: Added 153m TOWER over crossing (taller than Strasbourg, ambitious)
- 1573: Tower COLLAPSED (4 years after completion—too heavy for already-stressed structure)
- Never rebuilt (learned lesson—153m on top of 48m choir = impossible)
- **Lesson**: "We reached TOO high (Beauvais becomes cautionary tale, Gothic hubris punished)"

**The German eternal-race**:
- Cologne: 157m towers planned (never completed in medieval era, required 1880s steel)
- Ulm: 161.5m tower planned (never completed in medieval era, required 1890s engineering)
- **German claim**: "We PLANNED tallest (even if never built, the DREAM is highest)"
- **French/English response**: "Planning isn't building (Strasbourg actually COMPLETED, holds real record, dreams don't count)"

## Part VIII: The English Perpendicular Pinnacle (1400-1550 AD)

### The Perpendicular Daemon Fully Realized

**What makes Perpendicular DISTINCT** (vs French Flamboyant):

**French Flamboyant**: 
- Curves (S-curves, flames, organic, flowing)
- Emotional (chaotic-beautiful, desperate, excessive)
- Vertical (still emphasizing HEIGHT, reaching up)

**English Perpendicular**:
- Straight lines (perpendicular grid, vertical/horizontal, ordered)
- Rational (geometric, predictable, restrained despite excess)
- Horizontal (paradoxically—vertical LINES create horizontal READING)

**The inversion**: Perpendicular uses MORE vertical lines than Flamboyant (grid tracery is all verticals), yet FEELS more horizontal (flattened arches, emphasis on length, processional)

---

### Henry VII's Chapel (Westminster Abbey, 1503-1512 AD) — The Pendant Fan Vault Miracle

**Built for Henry VII's tomb** (royal chapel, dynastic statement):

**The pendant fan vaults**:
- **What**: Fans HANG from ceiling (not rising from floor, but DESCENDING from vault)
- Engineering mystery (how do stone fans HANG? appears to defy gravity)
- **Reality**: Hidden iron reinforcement + perfectly calculated weight distribution + prayer (masons said they prayed daily for vault not to collapse)

**The experience**: 
- **Vertigo-inducing** (ceiling appears FALLING toward you, stone defying expectations)
- **Lace-like** (pendants + fans + ribs = three-layer complexity, stone becomes FABRIC)
- **Impossible-appearing** (visitors ask "how does this stand?", feels miraculous)

**The theology**: "We achieved ARCHITECTURAL MIRACLE (pendant fans seem impossible, must be God's hand holding them, beauty so extreme it requires divine intervention to exist)"

---

### The Four-Centered Arch—English Flattening

**What is four-centered arch**:
- **Form**: Pointed arch but FLATTENED (four centers of curvature instead of two, creates wider, lower arch)
- **Effect**: Arch is pointed (still Gothic) but horizontal-ish (lower, wider, less vertical thrust than French pointed arch)

**Why English used this**:
- **Aesthetic**: Horizontal preference (English like LENGTH, four-centered arch emphasizes width over height)
- **Structural**: Allows wider spans (four-centered arch can be VERY wide without excessive height)
- **Theological**: Journey emphasis (wide flat arch is PORTAL (inviting passage), French tall pointed arch is SPEAR (aggressive reaching))

**Examples**:
- King's College Chapel: Four-centered arches everywhere (side chapels, doorways, window tops)
- Bath Abbey: Four-centered arch in west window (massive, 18m wide, only 12m tall rise—very flat for pointed arch)

**The English claim**: "We perfected the arch (four-centered allows WIDEST spans, French pointed arch is height-limited to vertical, ours enables HORIZONTAL Gothic)"

## Part X: The Renaissance Interruption (1450-1550 AD) — Gothic's Rival Emerges

### Why Renaissance Challenged Gothic

**The Renaissance daemon** (emerging 1400s Italy):
- **Classical revival** (return to Roman/Greek architecture—columns, domes, symmetry, horizontal calm)
- **Humanism** (humans matter, not just God—secular beauty valid, not just sacred)
- **Rationalism** (reject Gothic "irrationality," prefer Classical order)

**The Renaissance critique of Gothic** (1450-1550 AD):
- **"Barbaric"**: Gothic is Northern/Germanic (barbarians), not Classical/Roman (civilized)
- **"Irrational"**: Pointed arches are arbitrary (round arches are NATURAL—follow circle, perfect form), flame tracery is chaotic (Classical is ORDERED)
- **"Excessive"**: Gothic over-decorates (Classical restraint is superior, ornament should be MINIMAL)
- **"Vertical-obsessed"**: Gothic reaches up (prideful, Babel-sin), Classical spreads out (humble, human-scale)

**Florence Cathedral** (1296-1436 AD—transitional):
- **Nave**: Late Gothic (pointed arches, ribbed vaults)
- **Dome**: Renaissance (Brunelleschi's dome 1420-1436, Classical engineering, NO Gothic spire—dome inspired by Roman Pantheon)
- **Result**: Gothic-Renaissance HYBRID (last major Gothic cathedral, but dome is Renaissance, showing shift)

**St. Peter's Basilica** (Rome, 1506-1626 AD—full Renaissance):
- Replaces Old St. Peter's (Constantinian basilica 300s AD, demolished 1506)
- Michelangelo's dome (1547-1590—pure Renaissance, no Gothic elements)
- **Claim**: "We returned to TRUE Christian architecture (Classical Roman basilica, rejecting Gothic barbarism)"

**The competition** (1450-1550 AD):
- Gothic (Late Flamboyant, Perpendicular, still building in North)
- vs
- Renaissance (Classical revival, building in Italy, spreading North)
- Competing visions (Gothic = vertical reaching, Renaissance = horizontal calm)

---

### The Triadization Synthesis: Both Coexist

**Historical reality** (suppression):
- Renaissance wins in Italy (1450-1550 AD, Gothic mostly abandoned)
- Renaissance spreads North (1550-1650 AD, French/English/German adopt Renaissance, Gothic seen as "outdated")
- Gothic marginalized (1600-1800 AD, seen as barbaric, irrational, "Dark Ages" architecture)
- **Result**: Architectural paradigm SHIFT (one style replaces other, Gothic suppressed)

**Counterfactual** (Triadization):
- Renaissance wins in ITALY (Italian daemon prefers Classical, fine—build Renaissance)
- Gothic continues in NORTH (French/English/German daemons still prefer Gothic, fine—keep building Gothic)
- Both coexist (1450-1650 AD): Italian Renaissance + Northern Gothic, neither suppressed
- **Result**: Architectural paradigm ADDITION (new style added, old continues, both valid)

**What this enables**:

**Gothic Springs** (continuing 1450-1800 counterfactual):
- Keep building Flamboyant/Perpendicular/Sondergotik (innovation within Gothic, not stagnation)
- Learn from Renaissance (adopt some Classical elements, create Gothic-Renaissance hybrids if desired)
- Remain distinct (Gothic daemon preserved, not forced into Renaissance)

**Renaissance Springs** (1450-1650 AD):
- Build Classical in Italy (their daemon, their choice)
- Learn from Gothic (adopt some height, some light-emphasis, create Renaissance-Gothic hybrids if desired)
- Remain distinct (Renaissance daemon preserved, not contaminated by "barbaric Gothic")

**Routing enables sorting**:
- Classical-daemon person → Routes to Florence, Rome (Renaissance Springs)
- Gothic-daemon person → Routes to Paris, York, Cologne (Gothic Springs)
- Hybrid-daemon person → Routes to transitional Springs (experimenting with both styles)

**By 1650 AD**: Christianity has TWO major architectural families (Gothic AND Renaissance), each with regional variations, both building actively, no suppression, maximum diversity
