# 10b. The Papal-Imperial Schism (1054-1122 AD) — When Christianity Split Into Two Fractal Pyramids

*How the Fight Over Bishop Appointments Divided Christendom and Doomed Byzantium*

---

## The Dual Sovereignty Problem

### Christianity's Unique Challenge (0-1000 AD)

**Jesus' ambiguous legacy**:
- "Render unto Caesar what is Caesar's, unto God what is God's" (Matthew 22:21—implies TWO authorities, spiritual + temporal)
- "My kingdom is not of this world" (John 18:36—implies Christianity is SEPARATE from political power)
- BUT: "All authority in heaven and on earth has been given to me" (Matthew 28:18—implies Christ is SUPREME over everything, including emperors)

**The question**: If Christ is supreme, and Pope is Christ's vicar (representative), does Pope command Emperor? Or are they PEERS (each sovereign in their domain)?

**Two interpretations**:

**1. Gelasian Doctrine** (Pope Gelasius I, 494 AD): "TWO swords"
- Spiritual sword (Pope—governs souls, salvation, church)
- Temporal sword (Emperor—governs bodies, state, military)
- SEPARATE but EQUAL (neither subordinate to other, each supreme in their sphere)
- **Fractal implication**: Christianity has TWO apex sovereigns (Pope for spiritual fractal pyramid, Emperor for temporal fractal pyramid, both coexist)

**2. Papal Supremacy Doctrine** (develops 1000-1200 AD): "ONE sword"
- Pope holds BOTH swords (spiritual + temporal authority from Christ)
- Emperor receives temporal sword FROM Pope (emperor is delegated authority, subordinate to Pope)
- Pope can DEPOSE emperor (if emperor sins, Pope withdraws delegated authority)
- **Fractal implication**: Christianity has ONE apex sovereign (Pope), Emperor is subordinate node in papal pyramid

**The tension**: 494-1054 AD, Gelasian compromise works (Pope + Emperor cooperate, neither pushes supremacy claim too hard). After 1054 AD, collapses (Popes claim supremacy, Emperors resist, fractal pyramid splits).

## Part I: The East-West Schism (1054 AD)

### The Filioque Controversy

**The Nicene Creed** (325 AD, refined 381 AD):
- "We believe in the Holy Spirit, who proceeds from the Father" (Greek original—Holy Spirit comes from God the Father)

**Western addition** (Spain ~600 AD, spreads to Francia ~800 AD, Rome accepts ~1000 AD):
- "We believe in the Holy Spirit, who proceeds from the Father **and the Son**" (Latin: *filioque*—"and the Son")
- Theological claim: Holy Spirit proceeds from Father AND Jesus (not just Father)

**Why it matters**:
- **Greeks/Byzantines**: Adding *filioque* changes creed WITHOUT ecumenical council (no authority to alter Nicene Creed unilaterally, only ecumenical council can change it—last one was 787 AD)
- **Latins/Catholics**: *Filioque* is CLARIFICATION (doesn't change meaning, just makes explicit what was always implied)

**Deeper issue**: WHO HAS AUTHORITY?
- **Byzantine position**: Ecumenical councils (all five patriarchs—Rome, Constantinople, Alexandria, Antioch, Jerusalem—must agree to change doctrine, NO unilateral papal changes)
- **Roman position**: Pope is SUPREME (can alter doctrine as Christ's vicar, doesn't need other patriarchs' permission)

---

### The Mutual Excommunication (July 16, 1054 AD)

**The situation**:
- Pope Leo IX (1049-1054 AD—reformer, wants to centralize papal authority)
- Patriarch Michael Cerularius (Constantinople, 1043-1058 AD—resists papal claims, asserts Byzantine independence)

**The trigger**:
- Southern Italy (Norman conquest 1030-1060 AD—Normans take Byzantine southern Italy, bring Latin rites, suppress Greek rites)
- Cerularius responds (closes Latin churches in Constantinople—"if you suppress Greek rites in Italy, we suppress Latin rites here")

**Papal legates arrive** (1054 AD):
- Cardinal Humbert (Leo IX's legate—arrogant, aggressive, wants Byzantine submission to Rome)
- Demands: Byzantines accept *filioque*, papal supremacy, Latin rites

**Cerularius refuses**:
- "Rome is ONE patriarch (first among equals, but NOT supreme). Constantinople is SECOND patriarch (equal authority, just less seniority). We will NOT subordinate to Rome."

**July 16, 1054 AD**: 
- Cardinal Humbert places bull of excommunication on altar of Hagia Sophia (excommunicates Patriarch Cerularius—declares him heretic, cut off from Church)
- Cerularius responds (excommunicates Humbert and papal legates—but NOT Pope, claims Pope Leo IX is already dead so legates have no authority)

**Result**: Schism (Catholic West vs Orthodox East, separate fractal pyramids, 1054 AD onward—never fully reunited).

---

### The Fractal Implications

**Before 1054 AD**: ONE Christian fractal pyramid (theoretically)
```
CHRISTENDOM (one pyramid, 0-1054 AD)
  │
  ├─ Spiritual Apex: Pope (Rome) + Patriarchs (Constantinople, Alexandria, Antioch, Jerusalem)—peers in theory, Rome "first among equals"
  │
  └─ Temporal Apex: Byzantine Emperor (East) + Holy Roman Emperor (West)—both claim to be "Roman Emperor"
```

**After 1054 AD**: TWO Christian fractal pyramids
```
CATHOLIC CHRISTENDOM (Western pyramid, 1054 AD onward)
  │
  ├─ Spiritual Apex: Pope (Rome—SUPREME, claims authority over all Christians)
  │   └─ Subordinate: Catholic bishops, abbots, priests
  │
  └─ Temporal Apex: Holy Roman Emperor (Germany/Italy—subordinate to Pope? disputed)
      └─ Subordinate: Kings (France, England, Castile, Poland), dukes, counts

ORTHODOX CHRISTENDOM (Eastern pyramid, 1054 AD onward)
  │
  ├─ Spiritual Apex: Patriarch of Constantinople (FIRST among equals, but not supreme like Pope)
  │   └─ Peers: Patriarchs of Alexandria, Antioch, Jerusalem (autocephalous—self-governing)
  │
  └─ Temporal Apex: Byzantine Emperor (Constantinople—supreme temporal ruler, convenes church councils)
      └─ Subordinate: Rus (Kiev), Bulgars, Serbs (Orthodox kingdoms, allied to Byzantium)
```

**The problem**: When Byzantium needs HELP (1071 AD onward—Seljuk Turks conquer Anatolia), can they call on CATHOLIC West for aid? Or is schism too deep?

## Part II: The Investiture Controversy (1075-1122 AD)

### The Fractal Sovereignty Question

**The issue**: Who appoints bishops?

**Traditional practice** (500-1000 AD):
- Kings/emperors appoint bishops (bishops govern territories, are political + religious authorities—king needs loyal bishops, so king chooses them)
- Pope/patriarch confirms (ceremonial, but king's choice usually accepted)

**Why kings appointed**:
- Bishops are POWERFUL (control land, wealth, armies—in Holy Roman Empire, bishops rule cities, bishoprics are sovereign territories)
- If Pope appoints, bishops loyal to POPE (not king—king loses control over his own territories)

**Example**: Archbishop of Cologne (Holy Roman Empire)
- Rules Cologne (50,000 people, rich trade city)
- Commands army (5,000 soldiers)
- Collects taxes (from Cologne territory)
- Is prince-elector (votes for Holy Roman Emperor—one of seven electors)
- If POPE appoints Archbishop of Cologne → Pope controls WHO votes for emperor → Pope effectively chooses emperor → Emperor subordinate to Pope

**Emperors' position**: "We appoint bishops (they're our vassals, rule our territories, we need loyal men—if Pope appoints, our empire fractures into papal-controlled provinces)."

**Popes' position**: "Bishops are SPIRITUAL authorities (their authority comes from God via Pope, NOT from emperor—emperor cannot appoint God's servants, only Pope can)."

---

### The Dictatus Papae (1075 AD)

**Pope Gregory VII** (1073-1085 AD):
- Radical reformer (wants to centralize papal power, END lay investiture—kings appointing bishops)
- Issues *Dictatus Papae* (1075 AD—27 propositions asserting papal supremacy)

**Key propositions**:
1. "The Roman church was founded by God alone" (Pope's authority is divine, not delegated by emperor)
2. "The Roman pontiff alone is rightly called universal" (Pope is supreme over ALL Christians, including emperors)
3. "The Pope may depose emperors" (temporal authority subordinate to spiritual—Pope can remove bad emperors)
9. "All princes shall kiss the feet of the Pope alone" (emperors subordinate, must submit to Pope physically)
12. "The Pope may depose emperors" (repeated for emphasis—this is the nuclear option, threatens all kings)
19. "The Pope can be judged by no one" (Pope is accountable only to God, not to councils, emperors, or anyone)
27. "The Pope may absolve subjects from their allegiance to wicked men" (Pope can release subjects from oaths to kings—destroys feudal loyalty pyramid)

**The implication**: Gelasian "two swords" is DEAD (Gregory claims papal monarchy—Pope supreme over spiritual AND temporal, emperor is mere enforcer of papal will).

---

### The Conflict with Henry IV (1075-1122 AD)

**Henry IV** (Holy Roman Emperor, 1084-1105 AD):
- Rules Germany + Italy (but fractious—dukes constantly rebel, Henry needs loyal bishops to stabilize empire)
- Appoints bishops (traditional practice—Henry's grandfather, father, great-grandfather all appointed bishops, no pope objected seriously)

**Gregory VII's decree** (1075 AD):
- BAN on lay investiture (kings CANNOT appoint bishops anymore, only Pope can invest bishops with spiritual authority)
- Bishops already appointed by Henry: INVALID (must be reappointed by Pope or resign)

**Henry's response** (January 1076 AD):
- Calls synod of German bishops (Worms, 1076 AD—bishops loyal to Henry, not Pope)
- Declares Gregory VII DEPOSED (Henry claims Pope is heretic, bishops agree, issue letter: "Gregory, now not Pope but false monk, descend from throne of Peter!")

**Gregory's response** (February 1076 AD):
- EXCOMMUNICATES Henry IV (cuts Henry off from Church—no sacraments, no salvation)
- DEPOSES Henry IV (declares Henry no longer emperor—releases all subjects from oaths of loyalty to Henry, bishops/dukes CAN rebel lawfully)

**Result**: German dukes REBEL (1076 AD—Gregory's deposition gives rebels legitimacy, claim "we're not rebelling against emperor, emperor is deposed, we're choosing NEW emperor").

---

### The Walk to Canossa (January 1077 AD)

**Henry's dilemma** (1076-1077 AD):
- German dukes rebel (demand Henry reconcile with Pope or they'll elect new emperor)
- Henry's position collapsing (excommunication + deposition = subjects have no loyalty obligation, empire fracturing)

**Henry's decision** (winter 1077 AD):
- Go to Pope (Gregory is in Canossa, northern Italy—Henry crosses Alps in winter, arrives January 25, 1077)
- **Three days in snow** (Gregory refuses to see Henry—Henry waits outside castle gate, barefoot, in penitent's robe, for three days in January snow)
- **January 28, 1077 AD**: Gregory relents (lifts excommunication—Henry is Christian again, but NOT restored as emperor, Gregory reserves judgment)

**The symbolism**:
- Emperor subordinated to Pope (Henry HUMILIATED—barefoot in snow, begging Pope for forgiveness)
- Papal supremacy demonstrated (Pope can BREAK emperors—Henry was most powerful ruler in Christendom, Gregory brought him to knees)

**But Henry's revenge** (1080-1084 AD):
- Gregory excommunicates Henry AGAIN (1080 AD—claims Henry didn't truly repent, back to rebellion)
- Henry invades Italy (1084 AD—besieges Rome, captures city, deposes Gregory, installs ANTI-POPE Clement III)
- Gregory flees (dies in exile 1085 AD—last words: "I have loved justice and hated iniquity, therefore I die in exile")

**Result**: Stalemate (neither Pope nor Emperor can decisively win—Popes can excommunicate, Emperors can invade Rome, conflict continues).

---

### The Concordat of Worms (1122 AD)

**The compromise** (after 47 years of conflict):
- Pope Callistus II (1119-1124 AD) + Emperor Henry V (1111-1125 AD) negotiate

**The terms**:
1. **Spiritual investiture**: Pope invests bishops with SPIRITUAL authority (ring and staff—symbols of spiritual office, Pope gives these)
2. **Temporal investiture**: Emperor invests bishops with TEMPORAL authority (lands, secular jurisdiction—emperor gives these)
3. **Election process**: Cathedral chapters ELECT bishops (clergy of cathedral vote, choose bishop candidate), Pope confirms spiritually, Emperor confirms temporally

**The fractal resolution**:
- Bishops have DUAL sovereignty (spiritual sovereign under Pope, temporal sovereign under Emperor—two separate pyramids, bishop is node in BOTH)
- Pope CANNOT command emperor (spiritual authority doesn't include deposing emperors—Gregory VII's claim rejected)
- Emperor CANNOT command Pope (temporal authority doesn't include appointing bishops—Henry IV's claim rejected)
- **Gelasian "two swords" RESTORED** (but strained—Popes never fully abandon supremacy claim, Emperors never fully trust Popes)

## Part III: The Dooming of Byzantium (1054-1204 AD)

### The Cry for Help (1071-1095 AD)

**Battle of Manzikert** (August 26, 1071 AD):
- Byzantine Emperor Romanos IV Diogenes (1068-1071 AD) invades Seljuk territory (Anatolia—Turkish nomads conquering Byzantine heartland)
- **Seljuk Sultan Alp Arslan** (1063-1072 AD) defeats Byzantines (40,000 Byzantine troops vs 20,000 Seljuks, Byzantines LOSE—Romanos captured)
- Result: Anatolia LOST (Byzantine army destroyed, Seljuks conquer all of Anatolia 1071-1081 AD, Byzantine Empire reduced to Constantinople + Balkans + coastal strips)

**Byzantine population collapse**:
- Anatolia 1050 AD: 8 million Greeks (Byzantine heartland, granary of empire)
- Anatolia 1100 AD: 2 million Greeks, 3 million Turks (Seljuks settle, Byzantine refugees flee west)
- By 1200 AD: 1 million Greeks, 6 million Turks (Anatolia is now TURKISH, not Greek—Byzantine Empire's core territory lost)

**Emperor Alexios I Komnenos** (1081-1118 AD):
- Stabilizes empire (stops further losses, but can't reconquer Anatolia—lacks army, treasury empty)
- Calls for WESTERN HELP (1095 AD—sends envoys to Pope Urban II, Council of Piacenza: "We need soldiers! Seljuks threaten Constantinople! Christian holy sites in Muslim hands! Please send aid!")

---

### Why the West Didn't Help Earlier (1071-1095 AD)

**The schism problem**:
- East and West excommunicated each other (1054 AD—24 years before Manzikert, wound is fresh)
- Byzantines see Catholics as HERETICS (filioque is heresy, papal supremacy is pride, Latins are schismatics)
- Catholics see Byzantines as HERETICS (refusing papal authority is rebellion, Greeks are schismatics)

**Western priorities** (1071-1095 AD):
- Investiture Controversy (1075-1122 AD—Pope vs Emperor, West is in CIVIL WAR, can't send armies east when fighting internal battles)
- Islamic pressure in West (Al-Andalus raids Christian Iberia, Sicily under Muslim rule 878-1061 AD, West fights its own Muslims)

**Alexios' calculation** (1095 AD):
- "West won't help us for FREE (we're schismatics to them). But: If Pope CALLS Crusade (not to help Byzantium, but to RECONQUER JERUSALEM), Western knights will come. We can USE them (let them fight Turks, reconquer Anatolia, then continue to Jerusalem)."

---

### Pope Urban II's Crusade Call (November 27, 1095 AD)

**Council of Clermont** (France, 1095 AD):
- Pope Urban II (1088-1099 AD) addresses crowd (10,000+ French nobles, knights, clergy)

**Urban's speech** (paraphrased from accounts):
- "Brothers! The Turks have conquered the East! They desecrate holy sites! They torment Christian pilgrims! Jerusalem, where Christ died and rose, is in INFIDEL hands!
- "The Byzantine Emperor begs for aid! Our Christian brothers in the East suffer under Muslim tyranny!
- "I call upon you: Take up the cross! March to Jerusalem! Liberate the Holy Land! God wills it! (*Deus vult!*)
- "And: All who die in this holy war—your sins are FORGIVEN! Crusade is pilgrimage, pilgrimage earns indulgence, you go STRAIGHT to heaven if you die liberating Jerusalem!"

**The crowd's response**: "*Deus vult! Deus vult!*" (God wills it!—spontaneous chant, thousands vow to crusade)

**Why it worked** (when earlier pleas for Byzantium failed):
- **Spiritual incentive**: Indulgences (forgiveness of sins—Medieval Europeans TERRIFIED of hell, promise of guaranteed salvation if you die in crusade is HUGE incentive)
- **Martial culture**: Knights WANT to fight (Medieval aristocracy is warrior class, crusade gives them honorable war instead of sinful inter-Christian feuding)
- **Economic incentive**: Younger sons (primogeniture = oldest inherits, younger sons get nothing—crusade offers land, wealth in East)
- **Apocalyptic timing**: 1000 AD millennial fever (many thought world would end 1000 AD, when it didn't, 1100 AD seemed significant—crusade to reclaim Jerusalem before Second Coming)

---

### The First Crusade (1095-1099 AD)

**The armies arrive** (1096-1097 AD):
- 60,000 crusaders (French, German, Norman, Italian—arrive at Constantinople in waves)

**Alexios' shock**:
- "I asked for 10,000 mercenaries (professional soldiers I can command, pay, integrate into Byzantine army)."
- "Instead: 60,000 FANATICS (undisciplined, violent, refuse Byzantine command, pillage Byzantine countryside, demand to go STRAIGHT to Jerusalem without helping reconquer Anatolia)."

**The compromise**:
- Alexios extracts OATHS (crusader leaders swear: "Any Byzantine territory we reconquer, we return to Alexios")
- Provides supplies, guides (Byzantine fleet transports crusaders across Bosphorus, guides through Anatolia)

**Crusader success** (1097-1099 AD):
- Nicaea reconquered (June 1097 AD—returned to Byzantium per oath)
- Antioch captured (June 1098 AD—Bohemond of Taranto BREAKS oath, keeps Antioch for himself, refuses to return to Alexios)
- Jerusalem captured (July 15, 1099 AD—crusaders massacre Muslims/Jews, establish Kingdom of Jerusalem, Godfrey of Bouillon becomes first king)

**Byzantine reaction**:
- "Crusaders BETRAYED us (Antioch was Byzantine city, Bohemond swore oath, then broke it—kept Antioch, founded Principality of Antioch, refuses Byzantine authority)."
- "We helped them get there, they stabbed us in back."

## Part IV: The Fractal Incompatibility (1100-1204 AD)

### The Three-Way Split

**By 1100 AD**: Christendom is THREE fractal pyramids (not two, not one)
```
1. CATHOLIC WEST (Pope-centered)
   ├─ Pope (Rome—spiritual apex, claims supremacy over all)
   ├─ Holy Roman Emperor (Germany/Italy—temporal apex, disputes papal supremacy)
   └─ Crusader States (Jerusalem, Antioch, Edessa, Tripoli—Latin kingdoms in Levant, subordinate to Pope)

2. ORTHODOX BYZANTIUM (Emperor-centered)
   ├─ Byzantine Emperor (Constantinople—temporal apex, convenes church councils)
   ├─ Patriarch of Constantinople (spiritual apex, first among equals)
   └─ Orthodox allies (Kievan Rus, Serbia, Bulgaria—autocephalous churches, allied to Byzantium)

3. CRUSADER STATES (Hybrid—Latin-ruled but geographically Eastern)
   ├─ King of Jerusalem (Latin Catholic, sworn to Pope, but 1,000 km from Rome)
   ├─ Should subordinate to Byzantium? (Geographically in former Byzantine territory, Alexios claims sovereignty)
   ├─ But refuses (Latin aristocracy won't accept Greek emperor, sees Byzantines as schismatics)
```

**The problem**: Crusader States are ORPHANED fractal
- Too far from Catholic West to effectively subordinate to Pope/Emperor (3 months travel, no rapid communication/support)
- Refuse to subordinate to Byzantium (Latin-Greek enmity, schism, crusaders see Greeks as heretics)
- Try to be INDEPENDENT (but surrounded by Muslims, weak, constantly need Western reinforcements)

---

### The Second Crusade Disaster (1147-1149 AD)

**The trigger**: County of Edessa falls (1144 AD—Seljuk Turks reconquer, first Crusader State to fall)

**Pope Eugenius III** calls Second Crusade (1145 AD—Bernard of Clairvaux preaches, King Louis VII of France + Emperor Conrad III of Germany take cross)

**The Byzantine betrayal** (alleged):
- Emperor Manuel I Komnenos (1143-1180 AD) DOESN'T fully support (provides minimal supplies, refuses to join crusade)
- Crusaders suspect (Byzantines allied with Turks? Manuel wants crusaders to fail so Byzantium can reconquer Levant without Latin competition?)
- Crusade fails (1148 AD—siege of Damascus collapses, crusaders retreat, Edessa not recovered)

**Latin-Greek enmity deepens**:
- Crusaders blame Byzantines ("Greeks betrayed us, allied with Muslims, sabotaged crusade")
- Byzantines blame Crusaders ("Latins are incompetent, reckless, refuse our guidance, then blame us for their failures")

---

### The Fourth Crusade Catastrophe (1204 AD)

**Pope Innocent III** calls Fourth Crusade (1198 AD—target: Egypt, reconquer Jerusalem via Cairo)

**The Venetian contract** (1201 AD):
- Crusaders hire Venetian fleet (transport 33,000 crusaders to Egypt—85,000 silver marks payment)
- Only 12,000 crusaders show up (far fewer than expected—can't pay Venetians)
- Venetians: "You owe us money. We offer alternative: Sack Zara (Adriatic port, Catholic city, Venetian rival—you sack it, keep loot, pay us from plunder)."

**Zara sacked** (November 1202 AD):
- Crusaders sack CATHOLIC city (Zara is Christian, Hungarian-ruled—Pope excommunicates crusaders for attacking Christians)

**Byzantine civil war intervention** (1203 AD):
- Alexios Angelos (exiled Byzantine prince) offers crusaders: "Help me reclaim Byzantine throne, I'll pay 200,000 silver marks + provide 10,000 troops for Egypt crusade"
- Crusaders accept (sail to Constantinople, install Alexios IV as emperor July 1203 AD)

**Alexios IV CANNOT pay** (1203-1204 AD):
- Promised 200,000 marks (Byzantine treasury doesn't have it—Alexios taxed Constantinople, people riot, Alexios deposed + murdered February 1204 AD)
- Crusaders: "New emperor (Alexios V Mourtzouphlos) refuses to pay. We'll TAKE payment by force."

**Sack of Constantinople** (April 12-15, 1204 AD):
- Crusaders SACK Constantinople (largest Christian city, 300,000 people—crusaders pillage, rape, murder)
- Hagia Sophia desecrated (crusaders loot gold mosaics, altars, holy relics—put prostitute on Patriarch's throne, mock Orthodox liturgy)
- Three days of pillage (50,000 killed, countless relics stolen—brought to Venice, Paris, Rome)
- Byzantine Empire DESTROYED (crusaders partition empire—Latin Empire of Constantinople 1204-1261 AD, Byzantine government flees to Nicaea)

**Result**: Catholic-Orthodox breach is PERMANENT
- Byzantines: "Latins are BARBARIANS (worse than Muslims—Muslims conquer, impose jizya, but don't desecrate churches like Latins did). We will NEVER trust West again."
- When Constantinople falls to Ottomans (1453 AD—249 years later), Byzantines refuse Catholic aid ("Better Turkish turban than Latin mitre"—prefer Muslim rule to Catholic "rescue").

## Conclusion: The Fractal Failure

**What was proven** (1054-1204 AD):

✗ **Two apex sovereigns is UNSTABLE** (Pope vs Emperor—both claim supremacy, cannot coexist peacefully, schism inevitable)

✗ **Schism dooms periphery** (Byzantium needs Western help vs Turks, but schism prevents cooperation—West prioritizes internal fights, Byzantium falls)

✗ **Crusades without unified command fail** (Crusader States are orphaned fractal—too far from West, refuse Byzantine authority, collapse without consistent support)

✗ **Papal indulgences create perverse incentives** (Fourth Crusade sacks CHRISTIAN city—economic incentives override religious mission, "holy war" becomes pillaging)

✗ **Latin-Greek enmity is civilizational wound** (1204 sack of Constantinople creates permanent breach—Byzantines prefer Ottoman conquest to Catholic "help," Orthodox-Catholic reunification attempts all fail)

**The counterfactual**: What if Gelasian two swords had been FORMALIZED as Triadization?
- Pope = Spiritual Apex (governs souls, doctrine, clergy—CANNOT depose emperors or call crusades without emperor consent)
- Emperor = Temporal Apex (governs states, warfare, secular law—CANNOT appoint bishops or change doctrine)
- Crusades = JOINT decision (Pope provides spiritual justification, Emperor provides military organization—no crusade without BOTH)
- Result: Byzantine calls for help (1071 AD) answered IMMEDIATELY (Pope + Emperor coordinate, send organized army, not undisciplined fanatics—Anatolia reconquered 1080s, Crusader States subordinate to Byzantium, no 1204 sack)
