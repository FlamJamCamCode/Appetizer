## Prologue: The Daemon Incompatibility (63 BC - 66 AD)

**Historical**:
- Pompey conquers Judea (63 BC—end of Hasmonean independence)
- Enters Temple's Holy of Holies (most sacred space in Judaism—only High Priest allowed, only on Yom Kippur)
- Finds: Empty room (no idol, no statue—just empty space)
- Pompey's reaction: Confusion ("Where is the god? How do Jews worship NOTHING?")

**The Roman daemon**:
- Polytheistic (many gods coexist—Jupiter, Mars, Venus, etc.)
- Syncretic (adopt foreign gods—Egyptian Isis, Persian Mithras, Greek Zeus)
- Political (emperor worship as unifying ritual—"Caesar is divine")

- Monotheistic (ONE God, no others—"Hear O Israel, the Lord is One")

**The collision**:
- Romans: "Just add emperor worship to your temple rituals (keep Jewish God, also honor Caesar—everyone does this)"

**The foreshadowing**: This daemon incompatibility will cause THREE revolts and near-extinction of Judean Jews.

## Part II: The Kitos War (115-117 AD)

### Historical: The Diaspora Explosion

**The trigger** (115 AD):
- Trajan invades Parthia (Rome's eastern enemy)
- Diaspora Jews revolt (in Cyrene, Egypt, Cyprus, Mesopotamia—hundreds of thousands of Jews rise up)
- Why? Messianic fervor (Jews believe Parthian war is end times—time to overthrow Rome)

**The devastation**:
- Cyrene: 220,000 Greeks and Romans killed by Jews (Dio Cassius' estimate)
- Cyprus: 240,000 Greeks and Romans killed
- Egypt: Unknown casualties, but Alexandria devastated (Jewish quarter destroyed by Roman counter-attack)
- Roman response: Massacre (hundreds of thousands of Jews killed in retaliation)
- Cyprus: Jews BANNED (117 AD—death penalty for any Jew found on Cyprus, ban lasts until modern era)

**The outcome**: Diaspora Jews decimated (from 3 million to ~1 million—2 million dead in revolts + Roman retaliation)

## Part III: The Bar Kokhba Revolt (132-135 AD)

### Historical: The Final Destruction

**The trigger** (132 AD):
- Hadrian bans circumcision (130 AD—applies to all subjects, not just Jews, but Jews take it as targeting them)
- Hadrian plans to rebuild Jerusalem as "Aelia Capitolina" (Roman pagan city—Temple of Jupiter on Temple Mount)
- Jews revolt (132 AD—led by Bar Kokhba, "Son of the Star"—proclaimed Messiah by Rabbi Akiva)

**The revolt**:
- Initial success (Bar Kokhba captures Jerusalem 132 AD, controls Judea for 3 years)
- Roman response (Hadrian sends 12 legions—60,000 soldiers, largest force since Parthian wars)
- Brutal suppression (Romans systematically destroy every Jewish village—985 towns razed, 580,000 Jews killed per Cassius Dio)
- Bar Kokhba dies (135 AD—last stand at Betar, wiped out)

**The aftermath**:
- Judea renamed "Syria Palaestina" (Hadrian erases name "Judea" from maps)
- Jerusalem becomes Aelia Capitolina (Jews banned from city—only allowed on Tisha B'Av to mourn)
- Temple Mount becomes Temple of Jupiter (Jewish holy site now pagan temple)
- Circumcision ban enforced (Jews forced to hide practice or face death)
- Jewish center shifts to BABYLON (outside Roman Empire—Parthian/Sassanian territory becomes Jewish refuge)
