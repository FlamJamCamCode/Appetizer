## Overview

This folder contains counterfactual historical analyses exploring what various governments could have achieved if they had adopted the Natural Selection System (NSS) model from 1980-2025, eliminating salaries and welfare transfers in favor of:

- **$3/day sustenance layer** (bunkbed, TRUTH food, utilities, healthcare)
- **Pure infrastructure investment** (research facilities, housing ladder, self-sufficiency, quality of life)
- **Performance-based substrate control** (ultimate performers → castles, medium → apartments, entry → bunkbeds)
- **Zero schools** (children function immediately in NSS from age-appropriate)
- **Zoning safety** (children autonomous from age 8-12, dangers separated by zones)
- **No legal punishment** (only gradual zoning consequences for norm violation)

**No schools needed**:

**Traditional**: Crime → Arrest → Trial → Prison → $60k/year incarceration cost

## Why No Country Did This

### The Political Impossibility

**Democratic Countries** (USA, Germany, France, Britain, Netherlands, Denmark, Japan):
- Voters want welfare (immediate benefit)
- Government employees vote for salaries (self-interest)
- Infrastructure has delayed benefit (politically unpopular)
- **Democracy → Parasitism** (majority votes for extraction from productive minority)

**Authoritarian Countries** (USSR/Russia, China):
- Elite maintains power through patronage (salaries to bureaucrats buy loyalty)
- Can't eliminate salaries without losing control
- Performance-based system threatens elite (merit replaces connections)
- **Authoritarianism → Parasitism** (elite self-preservation prevents reform)

**Result**: No political system can voluntarily choose infrastructure over parasitism
