#### The Geography of Desperation: Why Norwegian Fjords?

**The push**: Charlemagne's expanding "New Rome" (768-814 AD)
- Carolingian Empire controls: Francia, Italy, Germania to Elbe, Pannonia, Spanish March (1.2 million km²—nearly all Western/Central Europe)
- Refugees flee to ONLY remaining free territories (Scandinavia, Ireland, Scotland—periphery beyond Carolingian reach)

**The pull**: Norwegian fjords as last refuge
- **Inaccessible** (mountains, fjords—Carolingian armies can't reach, terrain too difficult)
- **Defensible** (narrow fjord entrances—easy to defend with small numbers, like Thermopylae)
- **Beyond Christian world** (Norway 800 AD is pagan, no churches, no Frankish presence—safe from forced conversion)

**The problem**: Norwegian fjords have NO arable land
- Fjord valleys: Steep, rocky, tiny pockets of soil (can't farm, can't sustain large population)
- Norway 750 AD: 100,000 people (sustainable with fishing + minimal farming)
- Norway 850 AD: 300,000 people (200,000 refugees arrived—3x population, CANNOT sustain with farming)

**The solution**: FISHING
- Only food source abundant enough (North Atlantic fish stocks—cod, herring, massive quantities)
- Requires boats (fishing in fjords = need boats, coastal fishing needs larger boats for deeper waters)
- Fishing culture develops (by necessity—farm or starve, only option is fish, everyone becomes boat-dependent)

#### From Fishing Boats to Longships: The Forced Innovation

**The fishing necessity → boat culture → raiding capacity**

**Phase 1: Subsistence fishing** (750-793 AD)
- Refugees arrive, need to eat, build fishing boats (expand on existing Scandinavian boat tradition)
- Boat-building accelerates (200,000 refugees × 5 people per boat = 40,000 boats needed by 800 AD)
- EVERYONE learns boats (in Norway fjords, if you can't boat, you can't fish, if you can't fish, you STARVE—universal boat skill by necessity)

**Phase 2: Offshore fishing** (780-793 AD)
- Inshore fjord fishing insufficient (too many people, overfishing, need to go offshore for more fish)
- Larger boats built (offshore North Atlantic is ROUGH—need seaworthy boats, develop clinker-built hulls, flexible construction)
- Navigation improves (following fish = learning currents, winds, stars—same skills needed for raiding)

**Phase 3: The Cimbri precedent** (120 BC recalled 793 AD)
- **Historical parallel**: Cimbri tribe (Germanic, fled Roman expansion 120 BC, had only boats, turned to raiding Roman coasts when resources exhausted)
- Viking elders remember: "Our ancestors the Cimbri faced same problem (pushed to periphery, only boats, attacked Rome). We're in same situation now. We have boats. Romans (Carolingians) are in OUR old lands. We'll do what Cimbri did."

**Phase 4: The first raid** (June 8, 793 AD—Lindisfarne)
- **NOT random**: Lindisfarne is Christian monastery (refugee warriors: "Franks burned our temples, we burn their churches—revenge")
- **Boat capability**: Norwegian fishing boats can now cross North Sea (years of offshore fishing perfected hulls, navigation—same boats that fish can RAID)
- **Success**: Lindisfarne pillaged (monks killed, gold stolen, no resistance—monastery has treasures but no army)
- **Realization**: "Raiding is EASIER than fishing (one raid = year's worth of fish-value in gold/silver, monasteries undefended, coastal towns vulnerable)"

**The shift**: Fishing boats → Longships (793-850 AD)
- Retrofit fishing boats (add weapons, warriors, keep basic design but optimize for raiding vs fishing)
- Purpose-built longships (850 AD onward—design specialized raiding ships, faster, shallower draft, hold more warriors)
- Fishing continues (but raiding becomes PROFITABLE—fishing feeds refugees, raiding enriches them, both use same boat culture)

## Part III: The Stalemate in Iberia (732-1000 AD)

### Why Charlemagne Didn't Push South

**After Tours** (732 AD):

**The cost**: Stalemate in Iberia (732-1000 AD—268 years Muslims control most of Hispania, slow Reconquista by tiny Christian kingdoms in north, would take until 1492 AD—760 years total to expel Muslims)
