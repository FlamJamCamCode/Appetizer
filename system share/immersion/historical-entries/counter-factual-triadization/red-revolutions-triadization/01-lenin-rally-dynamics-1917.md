## Temporal Context

**Period**: 1917 AD (Russian Revolution—the pivotal moment)

**Historical reality**:
- February Revolution: Tsar abdicates, Provisional Government forms
- Lenin returns from exile, Bolsheviks gain popularity in Soviets
- October Revolution: Bolsheviks seize power through military coup (almost bloodless in Petrograd, but violent elsewhere)
- Constituent Assembly dissolved (January 1918) when Bolsheviks lost elections
- Civil War begins (1918-1922): Red Army vs White Army, 8-12 million dead

## Part II: Historical Reality—The Bolshevik Coup

### What Actually Happened: October 1917

**The timeline**:

**Early 1917**:
- Tsar abdicates (February), Provisional Government weak, Soviets emerging
- Bolsheviks minority in Soviets initially, but growing through promises: "Peace, Land, Bread"
- Lenin returns (April), Bolsheviks organize, gain support through slogans and organizing

**October 25, 1917** (Julian calendar, November 7 Gregorian):
- Bolsheviks stage coup in Petrograd
- Red Guards seize key locations (bridges, telegraph, Winter Palace)
- Almost bloodless in Petrograd itself
- But signal for violence across Russia

**Results**:
- Provisional Government arrested
- "All Power to Soviets" becomes "All Power to Bolsheviks"
- Constituent Assembly elections (November 1917): Bolsheviks lost (23% votes, but controlled urban Soviets)
- Assembly dissolved January 1918 when Bolsheviks refused to accept electoral defeat

**The pattern**: **Military seizure → Eliminate opposition → Claim legitimacy from Soviets → Totalitarianism**

**The conflict**:
- **Red Army**: Bolshevik forces, controlled central Russia

- **8-12 million dead**: War, famine, terror, disease

**Why it happened**: 
- Bolsheviks eliminated all opposition through force
- White Army formed to reverse Bolshevik seizure
- Both sides totalitarian (Reds and Whites both eliminated dissent)

**The outcome**: 
- Red Army won through ruthless terror
- Bolsheviks established totalitarian state
- All opposition eliminated or suppressed

## Part III: Ownership Solutions—The Pragmatic Compromise

### "Seize What You Can Rally, Keep What Others Rally"

**The core principle**: 
- **Someone proposes collective ownership → Place created to try this → People rally there (thinking they might want this) → They try living it → Discover truth about what they want → Factory becomes collectively owned in that Triad if people stay**

### The Mechanism of Expropriation and Nationalization
*Imperial Russia had a lot of experience with these Hard Details; that was rarely the issue, the issue seemed to be, to have them want something less than it all. To have them accept not to be able to have 'their way everywhere' to allow others to go free from them.*

**The Hard Details**: How does property actually transfer when people rally to try different ownership models? How do factories get "expropriated" or "nationalized" when a communist Triad forms? How do estates get "redistributed" when an agrarian socialist Triad forms?

**The Rally Dynamics Game for Capital Owners**:

**The PR Challenge**: Capital owners (factory owners, estate owners, "big cats") must demonstrate that people want to rally under them as owners. If they fail to attract rallying support, they *may* lose chunks of their wealth -- if facilitation of Triads can not be had another way.

**Facilitation of Triads in 1917 Russia**: Though it may seem difficult, as hunger into starvation and poverty into freezing to death was common in many such cities as a consequence of industrialization depopulation of farmlands having to migrate to cities; and simultaneous population boom due to child mortality collapse amongst other reasons. Therefore, the core mechanism for making it actually possible, was to have enough surplus production, that could momentarily be redirected into construction of whatever was desired to be tried. *The Starter Kit for a Fair Shake at what one wishes to try*. Or, if that could not occur, then seizing of pieces of the pie from all 'the fat cats', then seizing all in small areas and compensating those that lost their real possessions fully in that area, with some of the 'fat cat shavings' and a question mark for them: "Where to, do you wish to rally?". Further, there is a question of Just Ownership and Lawful Ownership. Where the latter hinges upon technicalities that could be entirely removed from any sense of creation, maintenance, investment into it, resources acquired for it, utilization of it or whatever else. Ownership can be entirely void of anything other than: "Paper says yes". Therefore, it is very reasonable to assume that very many factories would simply be taken without much concern of the former owners.

**How it works**:
- **Capital owner proposes private ownership** → Place created to try this → People rally there (or don't)
- **If people rally**: Capital owner keeps ownership, demonstrates legitimacy through rally support
- **If people don't rally**: Capital owner loses wealth proportionally—those without rallying parties and vast ownership lose sizable chunks
- **The "big cats" need not be so big** if nobody wants them as leaders to rally under

**The Historical Context (1917)**:
- **In 1912 or even 1917**, with reasonable distribution of goods and compensations, reasonable starter kits, and proportional "taking of wealth there is" based on numeracy, it would have been relatively easy for the Tsar or Provisional Government to facilitate
- **Many schemes possible**: Countless ways to structure "seeming just" taking and distributing such that ALL and ANY can try their dream/color/Triad with starter kit and proportionality to numeracy
- **Since White Army was so strong, and Red Army was so strong**: The capital class could have been the ones having their "fat shaven, cut and dried up"—they would be the ones losing wealth if they failed to attract rally support

**Example Mechanisms**:
- **Proportional expropriation**: Capital owners who fail to attract rally support lose wealth proportional to how many people rally to alternative ownership models
- **Starter kits**: Those rallying to try new ownership models receive reasonable starter kits (materials, tools, resources) to begin their experiment
- **Compensation schemes**: Various compensation mechanisms possible—the key is that capital owners must "earn" their position through rally support, not just hold it by force
- **Numeracy-based distribution**: Wealth redistribution proportional to how many people rally to different models—if 30% rally to communism, reasonable proportion of capital gets redistributed to communist Triads; A sense of Justice and reason; a goal of facilitation.

**The Key Insight**: This turns ownership into a **PR game**—capital owners must show that persons want them as capital owners. If they fail miserably (no rally support), they lose wealth. If they succeed (people rally to them), they keep it. The mechanism is flexible—many schemes possible—but the principle is clear: **Rally support determines ownership legitimacy**.

### Historical Precedents: Tsarist Land Distribution to Jewish Groups and German Farmers

**From Solzhenitsyn's "200 Years Together"**: Throughout the 200 years of Jewish presence in Imperial Russia, the Tsarist government repeatedly attempted to give land to different Jewish groups, particularly in the eastern colonial/conquered territories. These historical examples demonstrate that similar mechanisms of "giving of tsarist property" have worked before—providing a model for how rally dynamics could facilitate land distribution in 1917.

**The Pattern**: The Tsar had colonial/conquered land to the east that needed development. Rather than forcing existing populations or leaving it empty, the Tsar gave land to groups who would develop it productively—Jewish agricultural colonies, German farmers, and other groups who demonstrated they could turn the land into productive villages and farms.

---

#### Jewish Agricultural Colonies in Imperial Russia

**Early Attempts (1800s-1850s)**:
- **The Pale of Settlement**: Jewish population concentrated in western provinces, but many restrictions on land ownership
- **Agricultural colonies**: Tsarist government created Jewish agricultural colonies in southern Russia (Kherson, Yekaterinoslav provinces)
- **Land grants**: Jewish families received land grants to establish farming communities
- **Purpose**: Develop empty or underutilized land, create productive agricultural settlements
- **Results**: Mixed—some colonies succeeded, others struggled, but the mechanism of "giving tsarist property" to groups who would develop it was established

**Later Attempts (1850s-1900s)**:
- **Eastern territories**: As Russia expanded eastward, new colonial/conquered land became available
- **Jewish settlement programs**: Tsarist government offered land in eastern territories to Jewish groups willing to develop it
- **Different Jewish groups**: Various Jewish communities (Hasidic, non-Hasidic, different regions) received different land grants
- **Development incentives**: Land given with expectation that recipients would turn it into productive villages and farms
- **The mechanism**: Government property (colonial/conquered land) distributed to groups who would rally to develop it

**The Rally Dynamics Parallel**:
- **Tsarist government proposes agricultural development** → Land made available to try this → Jewish groups rally there (thinking they might want to develop it)
- **They try living it**: Jewish families move to colonies, attempt to farm, develop villages
- **Discover truth through experience**: Some colonies succeed (discovered they do want this), some fail (discovered they don't), some adapt
- **Land ownership determined by success**: Those who successfully developed land kept it, those who didn't lost it or left
- **No forced settlement**: Voluntary rallying to try the opportunity, truth-discovery through experience

#### German Farmers in Imperial Russia

**The Volga Germans and Other German Settlements**:
- **Catherine the Great's invitation (1760s)**: German farmers invited to settle in Russia, particularly Volga region
- **Land grants**: German farmers received generous land grants to establish productive agricultural communities
- **Purpose**: Help develop underutilized land, create model productive villages and farms
- **The mechanism**: Tsarist property (land) given to German farmers who would rally to develop it

**How it worked**:
- **Tsarist government proposes agricultural development** → Land made available to try this → German farmers rally there (thinking they might want to develop it)
- **They try living it**: German families move to settlements, farm the land, build villages
- **Discover truth through experience**: Most German settlements succeeded—they discovered they did want this, created productive agricultural communities
- **Land ownership determined by success**: German farmers who successfully developed land kept it, created thriving communities
- **Model for others**: Successful German settlements became models for how to turn land into productive villages and farms

**The Rally Dynamics Parallel**:
- **Government property distributed to groups who would develop it**: Not forced, but voluntary rallying to try the opportunity
- **Truth-discovery through experience**: German farmers tried living it, discovered they wanted it, succeeded
- **Productive development**: Those who rallied and succeeded created thriving agricultural communities
- **Similar to rally dynamics**: People rally to try ideas, live them, discover truth through experience

- **"Elimination of bourgeoisie and aristocratic and regal and papal"**: Domination of the world; instead domination of all others, by the proletariet.

**Why rally dynamics were rejected**:
- Lenin believed masses couldn't achieve socialism without vanguard party guidance
- Lenin believed democracy would lead to bourgeois restoration
- Lenin believed violent seizure of power was necessary

**The Bolshevik daemon**: 
- **Totalitarian** (centralized control, elimination of opposition)
- **Expansionist** (world revolution, not voluntary federation)
- **Eliminationist** (remove bourgeoisie, not negotiate coexistence, a foregiveness and becoming, though not continuation of bourgeoisie or 'those above')

### The Historical Forces: Why Rally Dynamics Were Unlikely

**Russia 1917 conditions**:
- Total war exhaustion (WWI), economic collapse, mass hunger
- **Desperate conditions** → Desperate solutions → Totalitarian grab for power
- Rally dynamics require time, patience, voluntary association
- **Crisis conditions** → Fast solutions → Military coup

**The window for Triadization**:
- **Too narrow**—Russia collapsing, need for quick decisions
- **Too fragile**—competing forces would have fought regardless
- **Too idealistic**— Person's on all fronts who opposed taking any loss or settling for anything other than "it all" or "exactly what they believed fair or just or theirs". Rendering rally dynamics quite difficult: Constant funnelling all efforts into rallying towards 'having it all as a new global or empire encompassing utopia or fight against dystopia and chaos: upheaval'.
