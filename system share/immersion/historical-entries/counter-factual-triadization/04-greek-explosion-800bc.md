### The Greek Ethnonym Complexity

**"Greek" is not one people**:

**Major ethnic divisions** (800 BC):
- **Ionians** (Athens, Miletus, Ephesus—eastern Greece, Aegean coast)
  - Sub-varieties: Attic (Athens region), Euboean, Cycladic
- **Dorians** (Sparta, Corinth, Argos, Crete—southern Greece, Peloponnese)
  - Sub-varieties: Laconian (Sparta), Corinthian, Argive, Cretan, Rhodian
- **Aeolians** (Thebes, Thessaly, Lesbos—central/northern Greece)
  - Sub-varieties: Boeotian (Thebes), Thessalian, Lesbian
- **Achaeans** (northern Peloponnese, later southern Italy)
  - Sub-varieties: Achaean League varieties

**Genetic reality**:
- All Indo-European (migrated 2000-1600 BC from north)
- Mixed with pre-Greek populations (Pelasgians, Minoans, Lemnians—pre-Indo-European substrate)
- Significant regional variation (Ionians lighter, more mixed with Anatolians; Dorians darker, more endogamous)

**Linguistic reality**:
- All speak Greek (Hellenic language family)
- Mutually intelligible BUT distinct dialects (Attic vs Doric vs Aeolic—significant differences)
- Writing: Phoenician alphabet adapted to Greek (800 BC—adds vowels, revolutionary)

**Cultural reality**:
- Shared religion (Zeus, Athena, Apollo—pan-Hellenic pantheon)
- Shared epics (Homer—Iliad, Odyssey codified 750 BC, unifying myths)
- Shared games (Olympics 776 BC—pan-Hellenic identity ritual)
- BUT: Fierce local identities ("I am Athenian FIRST, Greek second" vs "I am Spartan, Greeks are others")

**The Triadization question**: Do Greeks form unified Greek pyramid OR thousands of independent Springs?

**Greek answer** (800 BC): **BOTH** (peer union of hundreds of Springs, no apex pyramid).

## The Sovereignty Spectrum: From Hermit to Empire

### Level 0: The Hermit Sovereign (Individual Dot)

**Example: Hesiod the Farmer** (750 BC, Boeotia)

**The claim**:
- Hesiod (poet-farmer) claims: "My farm is MY sovereignty (40 acres, no subordination to anyone)"
- No social contract (doesn't participate in village assemblies)
- No hierarchical relation (doesn't accept any superior)
- Exists as DOT (sovereign individual, no relations)

**The bubble up question**: Where does Hesiod ask for space?

**Answer**: NOWHERE (doesn't ask—he's already there, inherited farm, claims it as sovereign)

**The challenge**: Theban King sends tax collector: "You owe tribute (you're in Theban territory)"

**Hesiod's response** (750 BC):
- "I refuse subordination (this is MY land, I recognize no superior)"
- "If you want this land, TAKE IT BY FORCE (but I won't voluntarily subordinate)"

**The Theban King's calculation**:
- Force Hesiod? Cost: 10 warriors, half a day (doable)
- Benefit: 40 acres, minimal tribute (not worth it)
- **Decision**: Let Hesiod be sovereign (too costly to subordinate one stubborn farmer)

**Result**: 
- Hesiod remains SOVEREIGN (individual dot, no subordination)
- Theban King accepts loss (one farm is nothing—not worth enforcement cost)
- **Proof of principle**: Any man CAN be sovereign (if he can defend his claim OR if superiors don't care to enforce)

**The limit**: 
- Hesiod can't expand (no rally—one man can't build empire)
- Hesiod can't protect against serious threats (if Thebes REALLY wanted his land, he'd lose)
- Hesiod's sovereignty is **de facto** (practical, not recognized in any pyramid)

### Level 1: The Household Sovereign (Oikos = Castle)

**Example: Athenian Oikos** (800 BC)

**The structure**:
```
Household Head (Sovereign)
  ├─ Wife (subordinate, but honored)
  ├─ Sons (subordinate until inherit or leave)
  ├─ Daughters (subordinate until marry out)
  ├─ Slaves (subordinate, property)
  └─ Land (10-200 acres typical)
```

**The sovereignty claim**:
- "My home is my castle" (oikos = sovereign unit)
- Household head is KING of his oikos (absolute authority over family, slaves, land)
- No one can enter without permission (not even city magistrates without legal cause)

**The bubble up question**: Does oikos subordinate to Athens (city-state)?

**Answer**: PARTIAL subordination
- Oikos sovereign INTERNALLY (family matters, household economy, daily life)
- Oikos subordinate to Athens EXTERNALLY (war, justice beyond household, city defense)

**The dual sovereignty**:
- **Internal**: Father decides: Who sons marry, what slaves do, how land is farmed (city has NO say)
- **External**: City decides: War/peace, tribute, criminal justice, public works (father must obey)

**Result**: Oikos as NESTED sovereignty (sovereign within, subordinate without—fractal castle).

### Level 2: The Village Sovereign (Deme)

**Example: Marathon Deme** (800 BC, Attica region)

**The structure**:
```
Deme (Village)
  ├─ 50 households (each sovereign internally)
  ├─ Assembly (all household heads vote)
  ├─ Demarch (elected leader, 1-year term)
  └─ 1,000 acres (communal + private land mix)
```

**The sovereignty claim**:
- "Marathon is OUR sovereignty" (collective of households)
- Demarch is elected (bottom-up, not appointed top-down)
- Deme controls: Local roads, local festivals, local disputes, land allocation

**The bubble up question**: Does Marathon subordinate to Athens?

**Answer**: YES (Marathon is deme OF Athens—nested within Athens)

**But**: Marathon keeps internal sovereignty (deme assembly decides local matters, Athens can't override on purely local issues)

**The dual authority**:
- **Local**: Marathon assembly decides: Which families get new land, when is local festival, who mediates neighbor disputes
- **City**: Athens decides: War contributions from Marathon (soldiers, ships), Athenian law applies to Marathon citizens, Marathon participates in Athenian assembly

**Result**: Deme as NESTED sovereignty (local autonomy within city subordination).

### Democracy (Demos = People, Cracy = Rule)

**Example: Athens** (508 BC—Cleisthenes reforms, full democracy by 462 BC)

**The structure**:
```
Athens Democracy
  └─ Assembly (Ekklesia)
      ├─ ALL citizens vote (6,000+ men, direct democracy)
      ├─ Majority rule (simple majority decides)
      ├─ No representatives (direct, not representative democracy)
      └─ Rotating magistrates (chosen by lot, 1-year terms)
```

**The daemon**: "The people should rule" (collective sovereignty, equality of citizens)

**The Triadization question**: Is this ♣ or ♦?

**Answer**: **♦** (aggregation function—voting system rules, not specific person)

**But Athenians claim**: "We are free (we rule ourselves, no tyrant)"

**The reality**: 
- Voting system is ♦ (majority aggregation rules)
- But Athenians CHOOSE this system (voluntary subordination to democratic ♦)
- Exit possible (any citizen can leave Athens, move to oligarchic Corinth or monarchic Sparta)

**The justification**: "Democracy is OUR daemon (we prefer collective rule, even though it's ♦)"

**Result**: Athens as ♦ sovereignty (honestly labeled—democratic system rules) but voluntarily chosen by citizens who rally to democratic daemon.

### Oligarchy (Oligoi = Few, Cracy = Rule)

**Example: Corinth** (800-500 BC)

**The structure**:
```
Corinth Oligarchy
  └─ Council of 200 (richest families)
      ├─ Elected by property qualifications (must own 50+ acres)
      ├─ Serve for life (until death or exile)
      ├─ Council decides all policy (no popular assembly)
      └─ Appoint magistrates (executive positions)
```

**The daemon**: "The wealthy should rule" (property = competence, rich = qualified)

**The Triadization question**: Is this ♣ or ♦?

**Answer**: **♦** (council aggregation rules, not single person—unless council unanimity, then ♣s plurality)

**The justification**: "Oligarchy is efficient (small council decides fast, no mob rule like Athens)"

**Result**: Corinth as ♦ sovereignty (council system rules) but DIFFERENT ♦ than Athens (oligarchic aggregation vs democratic aggregation).

### Monarchy (Monos = One, Cracy = Rule)

**Example: Sparta** (800 BC onward)

**The structure**:
```
Sparta Dual Monarchy (unique—TWO kings)
  ├─ King 1 (Agiad family—hereditary)
  ├─ King 2 (Eurypontid family—hereditary)
  ├─ Gerousia (council of 28 elders + 2 kings—30 total)
  ├─ Ephors (5 magistrates, elected annually—check on kings)
  └─ Assembly (all Spartan warriors vote—limited power)
```

**The daemon**: "Kings should rule" (hereditary authority, military leadership, divine legitimacy)

**The Triadization question**: Is this ♣ or ♦?

**Answer**: **HYBRID** (two ♣s in tension, plus ♦ checks)
- Two kings = ♣+♣ (both can override, but if they disagree → ♦ tiebreaker through Gerousia)
- Ephors = ♦ check on kings (elected, can depose kings if unanimous)
- Assembly = ♦ check (votes on major decisions)

**The tension**: Sparta CLAIMS monarchy (kings rule) but REALITY is mixed (kings + council + ephors + assembly—complex system)

**Result**: Sparta as HYBRID sovereignty (attempts ♣ through dual kings, but ♦ elements constrain them—oxymoronic sovereignty).

### Tyranny (Tyrannos = Sole Ruler)

**Example: Corinth under Cypselus** (657-627 BC)

**The structure**:
```
Cypselus Tyranny
  └─ Cypselus (sole ruler)
      ├─ No council (bypasses oligarchic Council of 200)
      ├─ No assembly (bypasses popular vote)
      ├─ Appoints all magistrates (personal loyalty required)
      └─ Absolute authority (can override anyone)
```

**The daemon**: "ONE MAN should rule" (autocracy, personal sovereignty, ♣ supremacy)

**How Cypselus gained power**: 
- Corinth oligarchy corrupt (Council of 200 extractive, people resent)
- Cypselus (rich but excluded from Council) rallies people (promises reform)
- Cypselus overthrows Council (657 BC—violent coup)
- Rules for 30 years (627 BC death—passes to son Periander)

**The Triadization question**: Is this ♣ or ♦?

**Answer**: **♣** (Cypselus is ONE sovereign, can override everything)

**The justification**: "Tyranny is necessary (oligarchy was corrupt, only strong ♣ can clean it up)"

**The result**: Corinth under Cypselus = ♣ sovereignty (honestly autocratic) BUT gained through rally (people chose him over oligarchy).

**The succession problem** (627 BC):
- Cypselus dies → Son Periander inherits (627-587 BC)
- Periander dies → Nephew inherits (587-583 BC)
- Nephew killed → Oligarchy restored (583 BC—tyranny only lasted 2 generations)

**The lesson**: ♣ sovereignty through tyranny UNSTABLE (rally fades, heirs less competent, oligarchy returns).

## The Bubble Dynamics: Greek City-State Competition (800-500 BC)

### Example 1: Athens Expands (700-600 BC)

**The initial state** (750 BC):
- Athens: 1,000 km² (small polis, 10,000 people)
- Eleusis: 200 km² (independent polis, 3,000 people, 15 km from Athens)

**The conflict** (650 BC):
- Athens claims Eleusis (strategic—controls road to Megara)
- Eleusis claims independence (we've been sovereign 200 years)

**The MoWD game** (650 BC):
- **Game**: Agricultural competition ("Which polis can grow more grain on disputed land between us?")
- **Duration**: 10 years (650-640 BC)
- **Stakes**: Loser subordinates to winner (full annexation)

**The result** (640 BC):
- Athens: 5,000 tons grain (superior farming, more irrigation)
- Eleusis: 3,000 tons grain (traditional methods)
- **Athens wins**: Eleusis subordinates to Athens

**But**: Eleusis keeps INTERNAL sovereignty (becomes Eleusis deme, local assembly controls local matters)

**New state** (640 BC):
```
Athens: 1,200 km² (gained 200 km² from Eleusis annexation)
  └─ Eleusis Deme (nested within Athens, local autonomy preserved)
```

**Result**: Athens bubbled down (gained Eleusis through MoWD victory), Eleusis preserved as deme (not destroyed, just subordinated).

## The Phoenician Colonization: Mediterranean-Wide Springs (814-600 BC)

### The Carthage Foundation (814 BC)

**The story**:
- Dido (Phoenician princess from Tyre) flees (brother Pygmalion killed her husband)
- Sails west with followers (1,000 people—Tyrian merchants, sailors, artisans)
- Lands in North Africa (modern Tunisia, near Libyan-Numidian border)

**The land negotiation**:
- Dido asks Libyan King Iarbas: "Can I buy land?"
- Iarbas agrees: "As much land as an oxhide can cover" (thinks: tiny amount)
- Dido cuts oxhide into thin strips, surrounds large area (cleverness)
- Iarbas honors agreement (impressed by cunning, grants land)

**The Carthage founding** (814 BC):
```
Carthage Spring (15 km², 1,000 people initially)
  └─ Dido as Queen (♣ sovereign—founder, absolute authority)
  └─ Phoenician culture (language, gods, merchant-daemon)
  └─ Location: North African coast (strategic—controls Sicily strait trade)
```

**The subordination question**: Does Carthage subordinate to Tyre (mother city)?

**Answer**: NO (Carthage is INDEPENDENT—apex of its own pyramid)

**But**: Carthage maintains CULTURAL ties (speaks Phoenician, worships Phoenician gods, trades with Tyre)

**The growth** (814-500 BC):
- Carthage expands: 15 km² → 500 km² (conquers/annexes Libyan coast)
- Population: 1,000 → 100,000 (rally from Phoenicia + local Libyans assimilate)
- Carthage founds colonies (Ibiza, Sardinia, Sicily—Carthaginian Springs)

**New state** (500 BC):
```
Carthage Lake (500 km², 100,000 people)
  ├─ Carthage Spring (city proper, 50 km², 80,000 people)
  └─ Carthaginian colonies (450 km²—North African coast)
  
Carthaginian Empire (subordinate colonies):
  ├─ Ibiza Spring (Balearic Islands, 1,500 km², 10,000 people)
  ├─ Sardinian Springs (3 colonies, 5,000 km², 30,000 people)
  └─ Sicilian Springs (5 colonies, 8,000 km², 50,000 people)
```

**Result**: Phoenician culture spreads to western Mediterranean (Carthage as NEW apex, parallel to Tyre).
