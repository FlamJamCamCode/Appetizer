## Executive Summary

**The Question**: What physical infrastructure could Norway have constructed if the sovereign wealth fund allocated 4% or 20% annually to building a futuristic research city operating on Natural Selection System (NSS) principles?

**Critical Constraints**:
- **NO breakthroughs assumed**: Facilities are built but discoveries are NOT monetized
- **NO salaries**: Zero wage expenditure
- **NO maintenance budgets**: Assume self-maintaining/circular systems
- **NO food costs**: Hydroponics, aquaponics, closed-loop agriculture
- **ONLY construction costs**: Physical facilities and infrastructure
- **Eugenic breeding**: Population fills facilities through performance-based reproduction rights

**The NSS Model**:
- People grow into positions by utilizing substrate (facilities/equipment/infrastructure)
- When your work is used by others, you grow (gain reproductive/expansion rights)
- When you're "the bridge" to breakthrough, you grow significantly
- Womb occupancy controlled by Measure of Worth Dynamics (MoWD)
- No traditional employment - only substrate utilization rights based on performance

**The Two Scenarios**:
1. **Conservative (4% annual)**: $389B cumulative spending over 28 years
2. **Aggressive (20% annual)**: $600B cumulative spending over 28 years

## Part VI: The NSS Population Model

### NSS Principles Applied to City Growth

**No Traditional Employment**:
- No salaries paid
- No job applications
- No hiring/firing
- No managers or hierarchies

**Growth Through Substrate Utilization**:
- Research facility exists → People grow into using it
- When your work is used by others → Your "surface area" in system grows
- When you're bridge to breakthroughs → Exponential growth
- Growth = more space, more resources, more reproductive rights

**Measuring Worth Dynamics (MoWD)**:
```
Person's System Position = 
    Σ(Direct Utilization of Substrate) × 
    Σ(Others Building on Your Work) × 
    Σ(Critical Path Position in Discoveries) ×
    Time_Integrated_Performance

Reproductive Rights = f(System Position, Resource Availability)
```

### Eugenic Breeding Dynamics

**Not "Importing the Best Brains"** (which lowers global capacity):
- Instead: Breed the humans needed for the facilities
- Match reproduction to substrate utilization performance
- "We need more of that organism" → Promptly made

**The Process**:
1. **Facility constructed** (e.g., advanced materials lab)
2. **Initial population explores** (substrate utilization begins)
3. **Performance measured** (who uses it well?)
4. **High performers gain rights** (womb occupancy, space expansion)
5. **Children raised in context** (4-5 years old contributing ideation)
6. **Technical acquisition as fast as possible** (see prodigy examples)
7. **Continuous cycle** (population grows into facilities)

**Womb Occupancy Control**:
- Not "you can't have children" (reproductive restrictions)
- But "your excellent work grants you children" (positive selection)
- Matching based on substrate utilization performance
- MoWD game: Those who solve things reproduce
- Children inherit context & infrastructure familiarity

## Part VII: Facility Utilization in NSS Model

### Azure → Crimson → Gray Progression

**Azure** (Intellectual/Technical Growth):
- Initial exploration of facilities
- Learning substrate capabilities
- Developing competencies
- Ideation and dreaming (early age participation)

**Crimson** (Performance/Doing):
- Active utilization of substrate
- Creating value for others
- Being "used" by others in their work
- Becoming critical paths in discovery chains

**Gray** (Maintenance/Support):
- Ensuring substrate remains functional
- Supporting others' work
- System reliability
- (Minimized in self-maintaining design)

### Example: Materials Science Lab Lifecycle

**Construction** (Year 1-3): $400M, 50,000m², advanced equipment
- No one using it yet
- Sits empty but maintained

**Initial Exploration** (Year 3-5): Azure phase
- 20 people move in, explore capabilities
- **Children (age 4-7)**: Observe, touch materials, ask questions, dream possibilities (already contributing through ideation)
- Adolescents (12-16): Technical mastery emerging (by age 12, many at competent level)
- Adults: Develop protocols, test configurations

**Performance Emergence** (Year 5-10): Crimson phase
- 5 people prove exceptionally productive
- Their work gets used by 50 others in the city
- They become "bridges" to new discoveries
- **MoWD Response**: These 5 gain reproductive rights
- Matching: Pairs with complementary substrate utilization
- **Children: Raised in materials science context from age 0**
  - Age 0-4: Observe parents working
  - Age 4-7: Begin touching materials, asking questions, early ideation
  - Age 7-10: Understanding material properties through hands-on
  - **Age 8-10**: Autonomous (can roam freely in child-safe lab zones, zoning protects from dangers)
  - Age 10-12: Contributing meaningfully (data collection, material testing)

**Mature Utilization** (Year 10-20): Full ecosystem
- Original 5 now have families (15-20 people total lineage)
- 100+ people using the facility actively
- 300+ people building on work from this facility
- **Second-generation children (now 10-15 years)**: Already competent (10 years hands-on experience)
  - Some at senior researcher level by age 15
  - Never needed school (learned by doing from age 4)
  - Autonomous since age 8-10 (roamed labs freely, zoning ensured safety)
- Facility fully integrated into city research network

**The Key**: No one was "hired" for materials science. No one was "schooled" in materials science. The facility was built. People grew into using it (from age 4-7). Children began contributing immediately (age-appropriate). By age 15, second generation surpasses first generation's age-30 capability. No schools needed. No waiting period. Carpe diem from the start.

- Not a "job" but a way to use substrate
- Example: Building systems monitor themselves
- When issue detected → Opportunity to solve it
- Solving it well → Performance increase

- Cleaning: Automated systems
- Inspection: Sensor networks + drones
- Repairs: Robotic assistance guided by humans
- Monitoring: AI prediction systems
- **Humans intervene only for complex issues**

**Circular Economy Approach**:
- Materials recycling built-in
- Waste streams become inputs
- Closed-loop systems (water, nutrients, energy)

- **Self-replenishment without external purchases**

```

→ No monetary cost, maintenance becomes opportunity
```

**Specific Systems**:

- **Water**: Gravity-fed where possible, solar pumping, recycling loops
- **Energy**: Solar/wind/geothermal = no fuel costs, minimal moving parts
- **Cleaning**: Robotic systems + materials that resist dirt
- **Structural**: Designed for 100+ year lifespan, robust materials
