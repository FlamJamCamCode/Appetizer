## The Shift: From Inverted Pyramid to Dominus Pyramid

**The Greek experiment**:
- Every man sovereign (oikos = castle)
- Democracy dominant (Assembly rules, not kings)
- Peer unions (300+ independent poleis, no Greek Empire)
- Inverted pyramid (power flows from many → representatives act slavishly on their behalf)

**The Greek problem**:
- **Chaos**: 300+ Springs constantly war (Peloponnesian War 431-404 BC—Athens vs Sparta devastates Greece)
- **Weakness**: Divided Greeks vulnerable (Macedon conquers 338 BC—Philip II unites Greece by force)
- **Instability**: Democracy → Tyranny → Oligarchy cycles (Athens alone: Democracy 508 BC, Thirty Tyrants 404 BC, Democracy restored 403 BC)

**The Greek question left unanswered**: "If everyone is sovereign, how do we align for collective strength?"

## The Roman Leverage Stack: Why Subordination Was Rational

### Layer 1: Military Dominance (Qualitative Advantage)

**Roman military**:
- Professional army (25 legions, 150,000 soldiers, 20-year service—career warriors)
- Superior tactics (testudo formation, maniple system, siege engineering)
- Discipline (training, hierarchy, punishment for failure—daemon of obedience)
- Equipment (gladius, pilum, lorica segmentata—better weapons/armor than barbarians)

**The advantage**: 
- Rome beats EVERYONE (Gauls, Germans, Carthaginians, Greeks, Egyptians—all conquered)
- 3:1 odds typical (one Roman legion defeats three barbarian armies)

**The leverage**: 
- "Subordinate to Rome → Roman legions PROTECT you (no more tribal raids, banditry, invasions)"
- "Refuse Rome → Roman legions DESTROY you (see Carthage, Corinth, Jerusalem)"

**Result**: Rational to subordinate (Roman protection > independence vulnerability).

---

### Layer 2: Infrastructure Dominance (Qualitative Advantage)

**Roman infrastructure**:
- Roads (80,000 km by 0 AD—all-weather, straight, fast travel)
- Aqueducts (bring fresh water to cities—Rome has 11 aqueducts, 1 million m³/day)
- Ports (Ostia, Alexandria, Carthage—massive harbors, facilitate trade)
- Baths (public health—Romans live longer, healthier than barbarians)
- Sewers (Cloaca Maxima—sanitation, disease prevention)

**The advantage**:
- Roman cities are PARADISE (clean water, sanitation, public baths, entertainment) vs barbarian villages (mud huts, no infrastructure)

**The leverage**:
- "Subordinate to Rome → You get Roman roads (connect to trade network, travel fast), aqueducts (clean water, health), baths (luxury)"
- "Refuse Rome → Stay in mud huts (no infrastructure, short brutal life)"

**Result**: Rational to subordinate (Roman infrastructure > barbarian subsistence).

---

### Layer 3: Legal Dominance (Qualitative Advantage)

**Roman law**:
- Codified (Twelve Tables 450 BC → Developed corpus juris—written, knowable, stable)
- Universal (applies to all Roman citizens—predictable, not arbitrary)

- Property rights (ownership protected, contracts enforced—economic stability)

**The advantage**:
- Roman law is PREDICTABLE (know what's legal, what's not—can plan long-term) vs barbarian law (tribal customs, vendetta, chief's whim)

**The leverage**:
- "Subordinate to Rome → You get Roman law (property protected, contracts enforced, appeals possible, can become citizen eventually)"
- "Refuse Rome → Stay in tribal chaos (vendetta, blood feuds, no legal recourse, chief's whim is law)"

**Result**: Rational to subordinate (Roman law > barbarian arbitrariness).

---

### Layer 4: Economic Dominance (Quantitative Advantage)

- Trade routes (Mediterranean, Silk Road, Rhine-Danube—global reach)

**The advantage**:

**The leverage**:
- "Subordinate to Rome → You access Roman market (sell your goods across empire, buy Roman luxuries, get rich)"
- "Refuse Rome → Stay subsistence (no trade, no specialization, no wealth)"

**Result**: Rational to subordinate (Roman wealth > barbarian poverty).

## The Carthage-Rome Dynamics: From Adversarial to Subordinated

**Historical timeline**:
- First Punic War (264-241 BC): Rome wins Sicily (Carthage loses first overseas territory)
- Second Punic War (218-201 BC): Hannibal invades Italy (almost defeats Rome, but loses at Zama 202 BC)
- Third Punic War (149-146 BC): Rome destroys Carthage (city burned, inhabitants enslaved, territory becomes Roman province)

**Historical outcome**: Carthage DESTROYED (no Carthaginian culture survives—total annihilation).

---

### Counterfactual: Lateral Competition → MoWD Games → Eventual Subordination

**Phase 1: The equal powers** (300-250 BC)

**Carthage's position** (300 BC):
- Naval superpower (controls western Mediterranean—Sicily, Sardinia, Iberia, North Africa)
- Merchant empire (Phoenician trade networks, purple dye monopoly, silver from Iberia)
- 500,000 people (Carthage city 400,000, colonies 100,000)

**Rome's position** (300 BC):
- Land superpower (controls Italy, expanding into Magna Graecia)
- Agricultural base (grain from Italy, wine from Campania)
- 2 million people (Rome city 200,000, Italy 1.8 million)

**The tension**: 
- Sicily disputed (both claim—Carthage has western Sicily, Rome wants eastern Sicily)
- Mutual exclusivity (can't both control Sicily—zero-sum territory)

**Historical**: First Punic War (264-241 BC—brutal, 23 years, 250,000+ dead)

**Counterfactual**: MoWD Game (264 BC—avoid war)

**The result** (244 BC):
- Sicilian cities vote: 55% for Rome (prefer Roman law, roads, citizenship path over Carthaginian trade)

**The resolution** (244 BC):
