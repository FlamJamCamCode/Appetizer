## The Etymology: "Bulgar" = "Mixed"

### What "Bulgaria" Means

**The name** (Bulgar, Old Turkic: *bulgha-* "to mix, stir, confuse"):
- NOT ethnic name (not "tribe of Bulgars" like "tribe of Franks")
- But DESCRIPTIVE name ("the mixed people," those formed by mixing many groups)

**What mixed**:
1. **Turkic Bulgars** (Oghur Turkic tribe, came from Pontic Steppe 680 AD—warriors, horse archers, ruled)
2. **Slavic substrate** (South Slavs, agricultural, migrated to Balkans 500-600 AD—farmers, majority population)
3. **Thracian remnants** (ancient Indo-Europeans, pre-Roman Balkans inhabitants—mostly absorbed/extinct by 680 AD, but some cultural remnants)
4. **Byzantine Greeks** (refugees from Arab/Avar raids, fleeing north—brought literacy, Christianity, Roman law)
5. **Avar remnants** (after Charlemagne destroyed Avar Khaganate 791-796 AD, some Avars fled to Bulgaria, absorbed)
6. **Roman Roads and Legacy of Alexander The Great**. (Hence the Roma and Roaming)

**Result**: "Bulgaria" by 900 AD is MELTING SEA (Turkic ruling class, Slavic language/culture, Byzantine Christianity, Thracian place names, Avar military techniques—synthesis of five groups)

### The First Bulgarian Empire (681-1018 AD)

**The structure** (700 AD):
```
BULGAR KHANATE (700 AD—Turkic ruling, Slavic substrate)
  │
  ├─ KHAN (Asparukh, then successors—♣ Apex, Turkic title, rules from Pliska)
  │   ├─ Bulgar nobles (bolyars—Turkic warriors, own estates, command armies)
  │   ├─ Turkic law (customary, steppe traditions, Khan is absolute)
  │   └─ Tengri religion (Sky God worship, shamanistic, Turkic paganism)
  │
  └─ SLAVIC SUBSTRATE (200,000+ South Slavs—farmers, pay tribute to Bulgars)

      ├─ Slavic paganism (Perun, Veles—coexists with Bulgar Tengri, distinct but tolerated)
      └─ Slavic language (South Slavic dialects—will become Bulgarian language)
```

**The paradox**: Turkic Bulgars RULING Slavic majority
- Bulgars: 20% of population (50,000 by 750 AD—warriors, nobles, rulers)

**The question**: Will Bulgars assimilate Slavs to Turkic? Or will Slavs assimilate Bulgars to Slavic?

**The answer** (by 900 AD): Slavs WIN (Bulgars adopt Slavic language, Bulgars disappear as distinct ethnicity, "Bulgarians" become SLAVIC people with Turkic NAME—reverse of Norman Conquest where Normans kept French language despite English majority)

**Why Slavs won**:

2. **Cultural** (Slavic agricultural culture more developed than Bulgar nomadic—farming villages, permanent settlements, Bulgars settle down, adopt Slavic lifestyle)
3. **Linguistic** (Slavic has advantages for ruling Slavs—Bulgar Khan needs to communicate with Slavic subjects, learns Slavic, eventually Turkic fades)

## Part II: The Christianization (864 AD)

### Boris I's Conversion

**Khan Boris I** (852-889 AD):
- Rules Bulgar Khanate (by 860s, Bulgars are already partially Slavicized—language mixing, intermarriage, cultural fusion ongoing)
- Faces pressure: Byzantine Empire wants Bulgaria Christian (easier to manage Christian neighbors, Orthodox Christianity spreads Byzantine influence)

**864 AD**: Boris converts to Orthodox Christianity
- Baptized by Byzantine priests (takes Christian name "Michael" after Byzantine Emperor Michael III, his godfather)
- Converts nobility (Bulgar bolyars forced to convert—some resist, Boris executes 52 bolyar families who rebel, enforces Christianity)
- Converts population (mass baptisms, pagan temples destroyed, Tengri/Slavic paganism suppressed—forced conversion, like Charlemagne's Saxons)

**But**: Boris plays Byzantium vs Rome (864-870 AD)
- **864**: Converts to Byzantine Orthodox (Byzantine priests, Greek liturgy)
- **866**: Switches to Rome (invites Catholic missionaries, threatens Byzantium—"I'll become Catholic unless you give me independent church")
- **870**: Returns to Byzantine Orthodox (Pope refuses autocephalous Bulgarian Church, Byzantium grants it—Bulgaria gets autonomous archbishopric, not fully independent patriarchate but close)

**Result**: Bulgaria is Orthodox (Byzantine sphere), but with autonomy (Bulgarian Archbishop, not subordinate to Constantinople Patriarch directly)

### Cyril and Methodius (863 AD)—The Slavic Alphabet

**The Byzantine brothers**:
- **Cyril** (Constantine, 827-869 AD) and **Methodius** (815-885 AD), Greek monks from Thessalonica (spoke Slavic, had Slavic mother)
- Sent to Greater Moravia (863 AD—Slavic kingdom, modern Czech/Slovakia, Prince Rastislav requested missionaries who could teach in Slavic, not Latin/Greek)

**The innovation** (863 AD):
- **Glagolitic alphabet** (Cyril invents—first Slavic alphabet, based on Greek + some Hebrew letters, designed to write Slavic phonetically)
- **Slavonic liturgy** (translate Bible, liturgy into Old Church Slavonic—first time Slavic written, first time non-Latin/Greek Christian liturgy)

**Why revolutionary**:
- Before: Christianity = Greek/Latin ONLY (liturgy in Greek (Byzantium) or sometimes Latin (Rome) or Hebrew, locals didn't understand, priests were foreign)
- After: Christianity in LOCAL language (Slavs hear Gospel in Slavic, understand, can READ Bible themselves—democratizes Christianity)

**Bulgarian adoption** (886 AD):
- Cyril died (869 AD), Methodius expelled from Moravia (885 AD—Frankish/German pressure, banned Slavonic liturgy, insisted on Latin)
- Methodius' disciples flee to BULGARIA (Boris I welcomes them, 886 AD—establishes Slavonic liturgy in Bulgaria)
- **Preslav Literary School** + **Ohrid Literary School** (890s AD—Bulgarian centers, develop Cyrillic alphabet from Glagolitic, simpler, spreads to Rus, Serbia, later Russia)

**Result**: Bulgaria becomes FIRST Slavic Christian civilization (Slavic language, Cyrillic script, Orthodox faith—model for Rus, Serbia, later Russia)

## Part III: The Golden Age (893-927 AD)

### Simeon I the Great

**Simeon** (864-927 AD, ruled 893-927 AD):
- Boris I's son (educated in Constantinople, fluent in Greek, studied theology, philosophy—most educated Balkan ruler)
- Ambition: "Bulgaria should be NEW Byzantium (we're Slavic-Orthodox, we can replace Greeks as Christian empire)"

**The wars** (894-927 AD):
- Four Byzantine-Bulgarian wars (894-896, 913-927—Simeon invades Byzantine Thrace repeatedly, attempts to conquer Constantinople)
- **Battle of Achelous** (917 AD): Bulgarians DESTROY Byzantine army (60,000 Byzantines killed—worst Byzantine defeat since Manzikert 1071 AD, but 150 years EARLIER)
- Simeon proclaims himself "Tsar" (Caesar—claims to be Roman Emperor, like Byzantine Emperor, 917 AD)

**The cultural flowering**:
- **Preslav** (capital 893-972 AD—Simeon builds palace, churches, competes with Constantinople in grandeur)
- **Golden Age of Bulgarian literature** (893-927 AD—translates Greek texts to Slavonic, develops original Slavic theological works, Bulgarian becomes literary language)
- **Cyrillic standardization** (Simeon's court refines alphabet—Cyrillic becomes standard for all Slavs, spreads to Rus 988 AD)

**By 927 AD**: Bulgaria is regional power (300,000 km², 2 million people—rivals Byzantium in Balkans, claims to be equal Christian empire)
