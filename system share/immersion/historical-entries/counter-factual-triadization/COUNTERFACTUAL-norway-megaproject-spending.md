---

## Introduction: The Thought Experiment

**The question**: What if Norway's sovereign wealth fund (GPFG), instead of investing in stocks/bonds, spent a portion on megaprojects, fusion research, space colonization, life extension, advanced R&D—and got ZERO financial returns?

**Critical assumption**: **All R&D spending generates ZERO monetary profit**
- Fusion power discovered → given away for free (open source)
- Life extension achieved → distributed freely (no patents)
- Nanotech breakthroughs → published openly (no licensing fees)
- **Only benefit**: "Internal spending efficiency" (things work better, but no money captured)

**Why this assumption?**
- Tests whether megaproject spending is sustainable even WITHOUT capturing value
- Addresses "free rider problem" (everyone benefits, fund captures nothing)
- Shows pure cost of building civilization-level infrastructure
- Relevant for MIRA provision model (Position 0 provides to all without coercion)

**The two scenarios**:
1. **Conservative (4% annual spending)**: Modest but sustained megaproject budget
2. **Aggressive (20% annual spending)**: Massive spending, likely depletes fund
