## Part I: Charlemagne's Rise (768-800 AD)

### From Frankish King to Roman Emperor

**Charlemagne** (742-814 AD, King 768-814 AD, Emperor 800-814 AD):
- Inherits Francia (768 AD—shared with brother Carloman, who dies 771 AD, Charlemagne sole king)
- Conquers: Lombards (774 AD—Italia), Saxons (772-804 AD—32-year war!), Avars (791-796 AD—Hungary), Frisians (785 AD—Netherlands)
- By 800 AD: Rules 1 million km² (Francia, Italia, Germania west of Elbe, Pannonia, northern Hispania—most of Western Europe)

**The coronation** (Christmas Day, December 25, 800 AD):
- Pope Leo III crowns Charlemagne (St. Peter's Basilica, Rome)
- Title: "Imperator Romanorum" (Emperor of Romans—claims to restore Western Roman Empire)
- Byzantines FURIOUS (Empress Irene—"There's only ONE Roman Emperor, in Constantinople! Charlemagne is usurper!")

**But Charlemagne's empire is DIFFERENT from Roman Empire**:
- Roman Empire: UNITARY (one emperor, one law, one culture—provinces governed by appointed governors, no internal sovereignty)
- Carolingian Empire: FRACTAL (one emperor, but subordinate kings/dukes keep internal sovereignty, multiple cultures, federal structure)

## Part III: The Carolingian Renaissance (780-820 AD)

### The Intellectual Flowering

**Charlemagne's vision**:
- Renovatio Imperii Romani (Renewal of Roman Empire—not just political, but CULTURAL)
- Gather scholars (from across Christendom—Italy, Britain, Ireland, Hispania, bring to Aachen)
- Establish schools (cathedral schools, monastic schools—educate clergy, nobles, even some commoners)
- Standardize liturgy (Roman Rite imposed across empire—unify Christian practice)
- Carolingian minuscule (new script—easier to read than previous scripts, enables book production explosion)

**The scholars** (Charlemagne's court, 780-800 AD):
- **Alcuin of York** (English, 735-804 AD—Charlemagne's chief educational advisor, runs Palace School at Aachen)
- **Theodulf of Orléans** (Visigothic, 750-821 AD—poet, theologian, bishop)
- **Paul the Deacon** (Lombard, 720-799 AD—historian, wrote Lombard history, proves Lombard culture preserved under Franks)
- **Einhard** (Frankish, 775-840 AD—Charlemagne's biographer, writes Vita Karoli Magni, models on Suetonius)
- **Peter of Pisa** (Italian, 730-799 AD—grammarian, teaches Latin)

**The result**: 
- 100+ manuscripts produced per year (vs 10 per year before Charlemagne—10x increase)
- Classical texts preserved (monks copy Virgil, Cicero, Livy, etc.—without Carolingian Renaissance, many would be lost)
- Literacy expands (from 1% to 5% of population—still tiny, but 5x improvement)

**The Cathedral School Network** (800 AD):
```
Carolingian Educational System (Fractal knowledge Springs)
  │
  ├─ Aachen Palace School (Apex—Charlemagne's court, 50 scholars, trains empire's elite)
  │   └─ Alcuin directs (curriculum: Trivium (grammar, rhetoric, logic) + Quadrivium (arithmetic, geometry, music, astronomy))
  │
  ├─ Cathedral Schools (20+ across empire—each major city)
  │   ├─ Tours (Alcuin becomes abbot 796 AD, 200 students—largest school)
  │   ├─ Orléans (Theodulf's school, 100 students—focuses on poetry, theology)
  │   ├─ Fulda (German monastery, 150 students—focuses on copying manuscripts)
  │   └─ [20+ schools, each sovereign Spring, each with curriculum autonomy]
  │
  └─ Monastery Schools (100+ across empire—each monastery)
      └─ Benedictine monasteries (each trains 20-50 monks in literacy, copying, theology)
```

**The fractal education**:
- Each school is SPRING (internal sovereignty—abbot/bishop decides curriculum, teaching methods, which texts to copy)
- All share HALO (Christian theology, Latin language, classical education—but interpretation varies by school)
- Students can TRANSFER (study at Tours, then Fulda, then Aachen—move between Springs freely, accumulate knowledge)

**Result**: Educational fractal (knowledge production distributed across 100+ Springs, each contributing, all sharing, no central monopoly).
