## Part I: Otto's Rise (936-962 AD)

### From Saxon Duke to Roman Emperor

**Otto I** (912-973 AD, Duke of Saxony 936 AD, King of Germany 936 AD, Emperor 962 AD):
- Son of Henry the Fowler (first Saxon king of Germany—Carolingian line ended 911 AD, Germans elected Saxon duke as king)
- Elected king (936 AD—by German dukes, Aachen coronation, continues Carolingian tradition)
- Suppresses rebellions (936-941 AD—his own brothers, dukes of Bavaria and Lotharingia rebel, Otto crushes them)
- Defeats Magyars (955 AD—Battle of Lechfeld, stops Magyar raids into Germany, becomes hero of Christendom)
- Invades Italia (951 AD—marries Adelaide, widow of Italian king, becomes King of Italy)
- Crowned emperor (962 AD—Pope John XII needs protection from Italian nobles, offers imperial crown to Otto)

**Otto's empire** (962 AD):
- Germany (600,000 km², 8 million people—five major duchies: Saxony, Bavaria, Swabia, Franconia, Lotharingia)
- Italy (300,000 km², 6 million people—northern Italy, Papal States allied, southern Italy contested with Byzantines)
- Total: 900,000 km², 14 million people (smaller than Charlemagne's 1 million km², but more consolidated)

### The Ottonian Dilemma: Centralize or Fractalize?

**Otto's problem** (962-973 AD):
- **Wants**: Centralized empire (like Roman Empire—one emperor, direct control, appointed governors, unitary state)
- **Reality**: Germania is TRIBAL (five duchies, each with own duke, own culture, own army, own law—dukes are effectively sovereign)
- **If Otto centralizes**: Dukes rebel (they did in 936-941 AD—Otto's brothers led rebellions, Otto barely won)
- **If Otto fractalizes**: Dukes stay loyal (but empire is federal, not unitary—Otto's authority is apex of pyramid, not monopoly of power)

**Otto's historical choice**: ATTEMPT centralization (failed—dukes too powerful, kept rebelling)

**Counterfactual**: EMBRACE fractalization (recognize Germania's fractal nature, formalize it)

---

**Otto's decree** (962 AD, after imperial coronation):

**The Ottonian Constitution** (formalizing fractal empire):

"I am Otto, Emperor of Romans, King of Germany and Italy. I have learned: Germany CANNOT be ruled like ancient Rome (centralized governors, unitary law, single culture).

Why? Because Germany is TRIBAL. Five great duchies (Saxony, Bavaria, Swabia, Franconia, Lotharingia), each with distinct culture, law, identity. I tried to centralize (936-941 AD, suppressed ducal revolts)—it failed (dukes rebelled, I won through war, but resentment remains).

Therefore: I formalize FRACTAL EMPIRE (Germania as confederation, not unitary state).

**The Ottonian principle** (formalized 962 AD):

**"Unity through fractal sovereignty"**:
- Empire is CONFEDERATION (not unitary state—dukes are sovereigns internally)
- Emperor is APEX (foreign policy, imperial law, coronation authority—but LIMITED internally)
- Election is SAFETY (prevents bad emperors—if emperor fails, dukes elect better one next time, not stuck with hereditary incompetent)
- Fractal is INEVITABLE (Germania's tribal diversity makes centralization impossible—formalize fractal, stop fighting it)

**Result**: Holy Roman Empire as FIRST INTENTIONALLY FRACTAL CHRISTIAN POLITY (recognizes and formalizes what Triadization prescribes—distributed sovereignty, voluntary subordination, plug-and-play reorganization).

## Part II: The Electoral System (962-1000 AD)

### The Prince-Electors as Fractal Governance

**The development** (962-1002 AD):
- Otto I establishes precedent: Emperor is ELECTED (not hereditary—though in practice, electors usually choose previous emperor's son if competent)
- Electors are: Major dukes (Saxony, Bavaria, Swabia, Lotharingia) + major archbishops (Mainz, Cologne, Trier)
- By 1000 AD: System stabilizing (7 prince-electors—4 secular, 3 ecclesiastical)

**Formalized** (Golden Bull of 1356 AD—400 years later, but based on Ottonian practice):
- **7 Prince-Electors**:
  1. Archbishop of Mainz (ecclesiastical)
  2. Archbishop of Cologne (ecclesiastical)
  3. Archbishop of Trier (ecclesiastical)
  4. King of Bohemia (secular)
  5. Count Palatine of Rhine (secular)
  6. Duke of Saxony (secular)
  7. Margrave of Brandenburg (secular)

**The election process**:
- Emperor dies (or is deposed by electors—rare but possible)
- Seven electors meet (Frankfurt typical location)
- Debate candidates (usually previous emperor's son, but can choose anyone—dukes, foreign kings, even non-Germans if qualified)
- Vote (simple majority—4 votes needed, unanimous ideal but not required)
- Crown emperor (Aachen for German king coronation, Rome for imperial coronation by Pope)

---

**The fractal safety** (why election prevents tyranny):

**Scenario 1**: Emperor is GOOD (competent, just, strong)
- Electors happy (re-elect his son when he dies, dynasty continues)
- Example: Ottonian dynasty (962-1024 AD—Otto I, Otto II, Otto III, Henry II, four emperors over 62 years, all elected)

**Scenario 2**: Emperor is BAD (incompetent, tyrannical, weak)
- Electors REMOVE (depose or elect different dynasty when emperor dies)
- Example: After Henry II dies (1024 AD—no sons, Ottonian line ends), electors choose SALIAN dynasty (Conrad II—from Franconia, different family, but competent)

**Scenario 3**: Emperor tries to CENTRALIZE (reduce duke sovereignty)
- Dukes RESIST (refuse to elect his son, elect weaker emperor next time who won't threaten ducal sovereignty)
- Example: Frederick Barbarossa (1155-1190 AD—tries to centralize Italy, Italian cities resist, emperor weakened, next emperor less centralizing)

**Result**: Election mechanism is FRACTAL SAFETY VALVE (prevents concentration of power—bad emperors can be replaced, over-ambitious emperors can be checked by electing weaker successors).
