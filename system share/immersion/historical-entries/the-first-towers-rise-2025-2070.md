## Prologue: The Recognition (2024-2025 CE)

### The Crisis of Institutional Capital

**2024 CE:** The world held $150 trillion in institutional capital (pension funds, sovereign wealth funds, asset managers). 

**The paradox:** Trillions available, but deployed into:
- Index funds (predictable returns, no innovation)
- Real estate (rent extraction, no creation)
- Government bonds (safe, no advancement)
- **Result:** Massive capital sitting idle while civilization's grand challenges (fusion, aging, AGI, climate) remained unsolved

**The barriers** (as documented in ANALYSIS-institutional-capital-capacity.md):
- Fiduciary duty (can't risk long-term R&D)
- Quarterly pressure (short-term thinking)
- Evaluation impossibility (can't measure breakthrough probability)
- Free rider problem (if you fund fusion, everyone benefits, you can't capture value)
- No merit capture mechanism (breakthrough creators don't profit from scale)
- **All ♦ (system) barriers preventing ♣ (individual) deployment of patient capital**

**The opportunity:** If even 1% of institutional capital ($1.5 trillion) deployed into long-term capacity building with zero profit expectation, Position 0 could be achieved by 2050.

**The question:** Who would be first?
