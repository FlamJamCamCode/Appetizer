# The Ideological Ratchet
*Why Every Philosophy Tends Toward Totalitarian Control*

---

## The Universal Pattern

**Every ideology**: Begins with a vision of The Good.

**Every ideology**: Encounters reality (people don't naturally do The Good).

**Every ideology**: Concludes enforcement necessary (must force people toward The Good).

**Every ideology**: Ratchets toward totalitarian control (more laws, more surveillance, more punishment—forever increasing, never decreasing).

**This is universal**: Communism does it. Liberalism does it. Conservatism does it. Theocracy does it. Democracy does it. Every ♠ (authoritarian rule) and every ♦ (algorithmic rule) that asserts "this is Good for all" inevitably does it.

## Book I: The Communist Ratchet

### Chapter 1: The Marxist Vision

**The promise**: "From each according to ability, to each according to need."

**The vision**: Classless society, no exploitation, abundance for all, workers liberated, state withers away.

**The appeal**: Beautiful (who wouldn't want abundance, equality, liberation?).

---

### Chapter 2: The Encounter with Reality

**The problem**: People don't naturally work according to ability and take according to need.

**What happens instead**:
- Some work hard, others shirk (free-rider problem)
- Some take more than need (greed doesn't disappear)
- Some want private property (human nature resists communal ownership)
- Some want freedom (resist collective control)

**The communist diagnosis**: "People are corrupted by capitalism" (false consciousness, bourgeois ideology, need re-education).

---

### Chapter 3: The Enforcement Solution

**If people don't naturally do The Good**: Must force them.

**The ratchet begins**:

**Stage 1**: Persuasion (propaganda, education, appeals to solidarity).
- Doesn't work (some persuaded, many not, free-riders persist).

**Stage 2**: Incentives (rewards for good workers, shame for shirkers).
- Doesn't work (not enough, people still shirk, still want private property).

**Stage 3**: Coercion (forced labor, punishment for shirking, confiscation of property).
- Works partially (people work under threat, but resentfully, inefficiently).

**Stage 4**: Total control (secret police, surveillance, gulags, executions—eliminate resisters entirely).
- Works temporarily (dissent suppressed, but economy collapses, requires constant terror to maintain).

---

**The pattern**: Each stage fails → Ideology blames "insufficient enforcement" → Next stage increases control → Repeat until totalitarian.

---

### Chapter 4: Why the State Never Withers

**Marx promised**: State withers away (once communism achieved, no need for state, natural harmony emerges).

**What happened**: State grew (USSR, China, Cuba, North Korea—all became totalitarian, state never withered, only expanded).

**Why**: Because people's nature didn't change (still wanted private property, still shirked, still resisted—state had to enforce forever, couldn't relax or system would collapse).

---

**The recognition**: **"From each according to ability, to each according to need" requires totalitarian enforcement** (because not natural to humans, must be forced constantly, state can never wither).

**Yet**: Liberal democracies become ever more controlling (more laws, more regulations, more surveillance, more punishment for wrongthink).

**Liberal response**: "That's intolerant! Must be stopped!" (enforce tolerance, punish intolerance).

**Result**: **Intolerance of intolerance** (liberalism becomes authoritarian to suppress illiberalism, freedom destroyed to protect freedom).

**The ratchet**: 
- Stage 1: Tolerate all (pure liberalism)

---

**The pattern**: Each stage fails → Ideology blames "insufficient enforcement" → Next stage increases control → Repeat until totalitarian.

## Book VI: The Universal Mechanism

### Chapter 18: Why Every Ideology Ratchets Toward Control

**The fundamental pattern**:

**Step 1**: Ideology asserts "X is Good" (equality, freedom, tradition, piety, democracy, whatever).

**Step 2**: Reality doesn't naturally produce X (people's nature/desires don't align with X).

**Step 3**: Ideology blames reality (people are corrupted/ignorant/evil, need correction).

**Step 4**: Ideology attempts enforcement (laws, regulations, punishment to force X).

**Step 5**: Enforcement fails partially (some comply, many resist, X still not achieved).

**Step 6**: Ideology blames insufficient enforcement (need more laws, more surveillance, more punishment).

**Step 7**: Return to Step 4, increase control (ratchet tightens).

**Step 8**: Repeat forever (until totalitarian or collapse).

---

**Why this is universal**:

**Any ideology that asserts universal Good**: Will encounter people who don't naturally do that Good.

**Any ideology that encounters resistance**: Will attempt enforcement (otherwise admit ideology is wrong—unthinkable).

**Any enforcement that fails**: Will be blamed on insufficient enforcement (not on ideology being unnatural—that's unthinkable).

**Therefore**: **Every ideology ratchets toward totalitarianism** (unless it gives up on universal Good and allows diversity of goods—but then it's not an ideology, it's ♣ anarchy or Triadization).

---

### Chapter 19: The Ideological Trap

**The trap**: Ideology cannot admit it's wrong (would destroy ideology's claim to truth).

**Therefore**: When reality doesn't match ideology, must blame reality (not ideology).

**Therefore**: Must change reality (force people to match ideology).

**Therefore**: Must increase enforcement (when people resist).

**Therefore**: Must become totalitarian (to fully enforce ideology on resistant reality).

---

**The alternative**: Admit ideology is not universal (what's Good for some is not Good for all, allow diversity).

**But**: Ideology can't do this (would mean "our Good is just one Good among many, not The Good"—destroys ideology's foundation).

**Therefore**: Ideology trapped (must ratchet toward totalitarianism or cease to be ideology).

---

### Chapter 20: The Substratum Problem

**Every ideology**: Encounters "substratum of a particular instantiation or state."

**What this means**: The actual material conditions, actual human nature, actual culture/history of the place where ideology is applied.

**The problem**: Substratum doesn't match ideology's assumptions.

---

**Example 1: Communism in Russia**:
- **Ideology assumes**: Workers naturally want communal ownership, will work for collective good.
- **Russian substratum**: Peasants want private land (centuries of serfdom, dream of owning land, resist collectivization).
- **Result**: Forced collectivization (millions dead, constant enforcement needed, never achieved natural communism).

**Example 2: Liberalism in Afghanistan**:
- **Ideology assumes**: Everyone naturally wants individual freedom, gender equality, secular democracy.
- **Afghan substratum**: Tribal, Islamic, patriarchal (centuries of tradition, values honor/family/faith over individual freedom).
- **Result**: Forced liberalization (20 years of war, trillions spent, collapsed immediately when enforcement withdrawn—never achieved natural liberalism).

**Example 3: Democracy in Iraq**:
- **Ideology assumes**: People naturally want one-person-one-vote, will form political parties around policy, compromise.
- **Iraqi substratum**: Sectarian (Sunni/Shia/Kurd identities primary, vote along ethnic/religious lines, zero-sum competition).
- **Result**: Forced democracy (ethnic cleansing, civil war, constant instability—never achieved natural democratic consensus).

---

**The pattern**: **Ideology meets substratum → Substratum doesn't naturally produce ideological Good → Ideology attempts to force substratum to change → Requires ever-increasing control → Totalitarianism or collapse.**

- Worked temporarily (economy recovered, but "not real communism," ideologues dissatisfied).

**The lesson**: Communism required totalitarian enforcement (never achieved natural communism, state never withered, collapsed when enforcement failed).
