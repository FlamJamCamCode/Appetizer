## Book I: The Pre-Unleashed Games Era (2020-2087)

### Chapter 1: The Combat Sports Hunger

**The UFC Era**: For decades, combat sports fans watched men fight in cages. Joe Rogan commentated. Dana White promoted. Millions watched.

**But something was missing.**

The fights were **real**, yes. The blood was real. The knockouts were real.

But the **stakes**? Limited.

- Win: Get paid, get famous, maybe get a title
- Lose: Get paid less, lose ranking, maybe get cut
- **No territory conquered**
- **No women won**
- **No slavery**
- **No permanent consequences beyond career**

**The hunger remained**: For something **MORE**.

*Most of all* The Hunger of the many practicing martial arts or thirst of physical combat lingered with no consequential impact.

The Masculine Instinct of *so many* lay dormant in waste and frustrating suppression, yearning for a calling: meaning and purpose of *its way* in the wake of obsolescence with modernity.

**Strategy games** (Civilization, Total War, etc.): Millions managed empires.

**But it was all pixels.**

No real blood. No real conquest. No real stakes. All intelligent, performant and strategic effort remained without impact or optimizing effect upon the world.

What does it take to make them consider real world games sufficiently entertaining, where real stakes are won. The mergin of game metrics and visuals in large campaign or familiar game varieties or functions. AR Overlay to enforce Aejectivity of the Game for easy display of all. No possibility of disputing what the game state is

**How it worked**:

- Build infrastructure, manage supply lines
