## Altruism Country Deep Dive – Egypt (EG)

> **Status**: First-pass scaffold with approximate ranges (**\***). Egypt is large and complex; this is an initial pass.

### 1. Macro Context

- Population: ~110M\*
- GDP (nominal): ~USD 400–450B/year\*.
- GDP per capita: ~USD 3,500–4,200/year\*.
- Large North African/Arab state, heavily concentrated along the Nile, with significant debt and subsidy history.

### 2. Government Social Spending and Fiscal Flows

Approximate:

- Total public spending: ~30–35% of GDP\*.
- Social spending (health, education, social protection): ~15–18% of GDP\*.
  - Health: ~5–6% of GDP\*.
  - Education: ~4–5% of GDP\*.
  - Social protection and subsidies (food, fuel, cash transfers): ~5–7% of GDP\*.

Debt service:

- Very large; constrains fiscal space.

### 3. ODA and Regional Role

- ODA recipient:
  - World Bank, IMF, EU, GCC countries, US (especially in past decades).
- Also provides:
  - Some aid and technical assistance to African neighbours and Gaza.

ODA as % of GNI:

- Moderate (~1–2% when including concessional flows)\*.

### 4. Private Altruism, Zakat, and Informal Support

Informal:

- Zakat and religious giving are widespread.
- Strong family and neighbourhood networks.

Formal:

- Charitable associations (some under tight regulation).
- Business-linked philanthropy.

### 5. Cause-Area Context

Egypt’s CESET profile:

- Under‑5 mortality: ~20–25 per 1,000\* (dramatically reduced vs historical).
  - Neonatal disorders.
  - Pneumonia, diarrhea in poorer regions.
- NCDs:
  - CVD, diabetes, obesity-related conditions very prominent.
- Other burdens:
  - Road injuries.
  - Some hepatitis and other infectious disease legacies.

### 6. Cluster Scenarios & Triads

Clusters:

- High-income Egyptians.
- Gulf-backed capital.

Triads:

- **Nile Child & NCD Transition Triad**:
  - Simultaneously finishing off residual child mortality and attacking diabetes/CVD.
- **Regional Refugee & Gaza Support Triad**:
  - Egypt as a potential corridor and host for humanitarian flows to Gaza and Sudan.
