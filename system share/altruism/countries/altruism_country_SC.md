## Altruism Country Deep Dive – Seychelles (SC)

> **Status**: First-pass scaffold with approximate ranges (**\***). To be refined with Seychellois budget, financial, and health data.

### 1. Macro Context

- Population: ~0.1M (100k)\*
- GDP (nominal): ~USD 1.5–2.0B/year\*
- GDP per capita: ~USD 15,000–20,000/year\*
- Small, high-middle-income island state in the Indian Ocean, reliant on tourism, fisheries, and offshore financial services.

### 2. Government Social Spending and Fiscal Flows

Approximate:

- Total government expenditure: ~35–40% of GDP\*.
- Social spending (health, education, social protection): ~15–18% of GDP\*.
  - Health: ~5–6% of GDP\*.
  - Education: ~4–5% of GDP\*.
  - Social protection and housing: ~4–6% of GDP\*.

### 3. Financial Centre / Tax Haven Role

Seychelles:

- Functions as an offshore centre:
  - International business companies.
  - Trusts and other structures.
- Compared to Panama or Cayman, smaller scale, but:
  - A significant ratio of offshore assets to domestic economy.

### 4. Private Philanthropy and Informal Altruism

Formal:

- Some environmental and conservation NGOs and foundations (e.g. marine conservation).

Informal:

- Family and community support.
- Church and religious charities.

### 5. Cause-Area Context

Domestic:

- Leading causes of death:
  - NCDs (CVD, diabetes, obesity-related).
  - Cancers.
  - Injuries.
- Child mortality:
  - Low in absolute terms; under‑5 mortality ~<10/1,000\*.

Environmental vulnerability:

- Sea-level rise and climate change.
- Ocean health and fisheries sustainability.

### 6. Cluster Scenarios

Clusters:

- Offshore clients and wealth structures.
- Eco-tourism businesses and visitors.

If:

- Seychelles adopted a:
  - Voluntary 0.1–0.2% levy on AUM or on high-end tourism revenue for CESET purposes.

Outcomes:

- Could generate tens of millions USD/year:
  - Enormous in relation to domestic economy.
  - Could also support regional Indian Ocean health and climate projects.

### 7. NSS/Triad Considerations

Potential Triads:

- **Blue Economy and Ocean Health Triad**:
  - Centre on Seychelles as a pilot of aligning offshore wealth and marine conservation.
- **Small-Island Resilience Triad**:
  - Linking Seychelles with other small island developing states (SIDS).
