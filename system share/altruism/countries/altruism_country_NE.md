## Altruism Country Deep Dive – Niger (NE)

> **Status**: First-pass scaffold with approximate ranges (**\***). To be refined with Niger’s budget, health, and aid data.

### 1. Macro Context

- Population: ~26M\*
- GDP (nominal): ~USD 15–20B/year\*
- GDP per capita: ~USD 600–800/year\*
- Among the world’s poorest countries, largely rural, landlocked, and heavily vulnerable to climate and conflict spillovers.

### 2. Government Social Spending and Fiscal Flows

Approximate:

- Total government expenditure: ~25–30% of GDP\* (aided by ODA).
- Social spending (health, education, social protection): ~10–12% of GDP\*.
  - Health: ~4–5% of GDP\*.
  - Education: ~4–5% of GDP\*.
  - Social protection: small but growing.

Fiscal space is constrained; a large share of social spending is externally financed.

### 3. ODA – Heavy Recipient

- Niger is a **heavy ODA recipient**:
  - ODA inflows often **>10% of GNI**\*.
  - Major donors: EU, France, multilateral agencies, global health funds.

Aid supports:

- Basic health and nutrition.
- Education.
- Food security and emergency relief.
- Governance and security sector support.

### 4. Private Philanthropy and Informal Altruism

Formal:

- Very limited domestic philanthropic infrastructure.

Informal:

- Strong family and village-level mutual support.
- Religious giving (Islamic almsgiving).

### 5. Cause-Area Context

From [Our World in Data – Child mortality](https://ourworldindata.org/child-mortality) and [Causes of Death](https://ourworldindata.org/causes-of-death), Niger is:

- One of the **highest under‑5 mortality** countries.
  - Under‑5 mortality >100 per 1,000 live births\*.
- Leading child causes:
  - Neonatal disorders.
  - Diarrheal diseases.
  - Pneumonia.
  - Malaria.
  - Malnutrition.

Adult burdens:

- Infectious diseases.
- Maternal mortality.
- Increasing NCD burden from a low baseline.

### 6. Cluster Scenarios

Potential external clusters:

- Diaspora Nigeriens in Europe and North America.
- Global donors prioritizing extreme poverty and child mortality.

Examples:

- If external altruistic flows targeted at Niger increased by USD 1B/year:
  - That is a large addition relative to current ODA.
  - At USD 5,000 per child life saved\*, could avert up to 200,000 child deaths/year (capped by actual baseline deaths).

### 7. NSS/Triad Considerations

Niger is a prime candidate for:

- **Child Survival and Nutrition Triads**:
  - Classic package: vaccines, ORS/antibiotics, malaria prevention, nutrition.
- **Sahel Resilience Triads**:
  - Combining climate adaptation, food security, and conflict-sensitivity.
