## Altruism Country Deep Dive – Chile (CL)

> **Status**: First-pass scaffold with approximate ranges (**\***).

### 1. Macro Context

- Population: ~19M\*
- GDP (nominal): ~USD 320–350B/year\*
- GDP per capita: ~USD 17,000–19,000/year\*.
- High-income/upper-middle-income South American country with strong institutions and high inequality.

### 2. Government Social Spending and Fiscal Flows

Approximate:

- Total government expenditure: ~26–30% of GDP\*.
- Social spending (pensions, health, social programmes): ~15–18% of GDP\*.
  - Pensions: ~6–7% of GDP\*.
  - Health: ~6–7% of GDP\*.
  - Other social protection: ~3–4% of GDP\*.

Other:

- Education: ~5% of GDP.

### 3. Official Development Assistance (ODA)

- Chile is mainly a **graduating recipient** with some emerging donor role.
- ODA given as % GNI: very small (<0.05%)\*.

### 4. Private Philanthropy and Informal Altruism

Formal:

- Corporate philanthropy and family foundations (especially from mining and retail sectors).

Informal:

- Strong mutual aid during disasters (earthquakes, tsunamis).

### 5. Cause-Area Context

Domestic:

- Leading causes:
  - CVD.
  - Cancers.
  - Diabetes and obesity-related diseases.
- Child mortality:
  - Under‑5 mortality ~7–9/1,000\*.

### 6. Cluster Scenarios & Triads

Clusters:

- High-net-worth individuals and families.
- Pension funds and institutional investors.

Triads:

- **Andean Health & Resilience Triad**:
  - Disaster resilience and NCD prevention.
