## Altruism Country Deep Dive – Colombia (CO)

> **Status**: First-pass scaffold with approximate ranges (**\***).

### 1. Macro Context

- Population: ~52M\*
- GDP (nominal): ~USD 350–380B/year\*
- GDP per capita: ~USD 6,500–7,500/year\*.
- Upper-middle-income country in northern South America with long history of conflict and a large urban middle class.

### 2. Government Social Spending and Fiscal Flows

Approximate:

- Total government expenditure: ~28–32% of GDP\*.
- Social spending (pensions, health, social programmes): ~15–18% of GDP\*.
  - Health: ~7–8% of GDP\*.
  - Pensions and social security: ~6–7% of GDP\*.
  - Other social programmes: ~2–3% of GDP\*.

Other:

- Education: ~5% of GDP.

### 3. Official Development Assistance (ODA)

- Colombia is largely a **recipient** of ODA:
  - Peace process support.
  - Rural development.
  - Drug policy and alternative livelihoods.
- ODA given as % GNI: minimal (<0.05%)\*.

### 4. Private Philanthropy and Informal Altruism

Formal:

- Corporate foundations (banking, telecom, energy).
- Nonprofits focused on peacebuilding, education, and poverty.

Informal:

- Strong family and community support networks.

### 5. Cause-Area Context

Domestic:

- Leading causes:
  - CVD.
  - Cancers.
  - Violence and homicide (historically high, now lower but still significant).
  - Road injuries.
- Child mortality:
  - Under‑5 mortality ~13–16/1,000\*.

### 6. Cluster Scenarios & Triads

Clusters:

- Colombian diaspora (US, Spain).
- High-income urban residents (Bogotá, Medellín, Cali).

Triads:

- **Peace and Rural Development Triad**:
  - Strengthening post-conflict rural regions.
- **Andean Violence Reduction Triad**.
