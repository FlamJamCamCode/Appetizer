## Altruism Country Deep Dive – Botswana (BW)

> **Status**: First-pass scaffold with approximate ranges (**\***).

### 1. Macro Context

- Population: ~2.5M\*
- GDP (nominal): ~USD 20–22B/year\*
- GDP per capita: ~USD 8,000–9,000/year\*
- Upper-middle-income, landlocked Southern African country with diamond-driven wealth, relatively strong governance, and high inequality.

### 2. Government Social Spending and Fiscal Flows

Approximate:

- Total public expenditure: ~35–40% of GDP\*.
- Social spending (health, education, social protection): ~18–20% of GDP\*.
  - Health: ~6–7% of GDP\*.
  - Education: ~7–8% of GDP\*.
  - Social protection: ~4–5% of GDP\*.

### 3. ODA and External Finance

- Receives modest ODA compared to lower-income neighbours:
  - Focus on health (HIV) and environment.
- ODA given as % GNI: small but not zero; Botswana supports some regional initiatives.

### 4. Private Philanthropy and Informal Altruism

Formal:

- Corporate philanthropy from mining and financial sectors.

Informal:

- Family and community networks, especially in rural areas.

### 5. Cause-Area Context

Botswana’s CESET profile:

- Under‑5 mortality: ~35–40 per 1,000\* (declining).
  - Neonatal disorders.
  - Pneumonia, diarrhea.
- Very high historical HIV prevalence:
  - Adult mortality significantly impacted but much improved with ART.
- Growing NCD burden (CVD, diabetes).

### 6. Cluster Scenarios & Triads

Clusters:

- Diamond and mining sector revenue.

Triads:

- **Diamond-for-Health Triad**:
  - Small, predictable slices of mineral revenue dedicated to child survival, HIV care, and NCD prevention domestically and regionally.
