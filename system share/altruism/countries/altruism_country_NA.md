## Altruism Country Deep Dive – Namibia (NA)

> **Status**: First-pass scaffold with approximate ranges (**\***).

### 1. Macro Context

- Population: ~2.6M\*
- GDP (nominal): ~USD 13–15B/year\*
- GDP per capita: ~USD 5,000–6,000/year\*
- Upper-middle-income, sparsely populated Southern African country with mining, fishing, and tourism; high inequality and apartheid legacy.

### 2. Government Social Spending and Fiscal Flows

Approximate:

- Total public expenditure: ~38–42% of GDP\*.
- Social spending (health, education, social protection): ~18–20% of GDP\*.
  - Health: ~7% of GDP\*.
  - Education: ~8% of GDP\*.
  - Social grants and housing: ~3–5% of GDP\*.

### 3. ODA and External Finance

- Moderate ODA inflows:
  - Focus on health, environment, and governance.

### 4. Private Philanthropy and Informal Altruism

Formal:

- Corporate CSR (mining, fishing, tourism).

Informal:

- Family and community support, especially in rural and informal settlements.

### 5. Cause-Area Context

Namibia’s CESET profile:

- Under‑5 mortality: ~40–50 per 1,000\*.
  - Neonatal disorders.
  - Pneumonia, diarrhea.
- HIV/AIDS and TB:
  - Significant burden but improved with ART.
- NCDs rising (CVD, diabetes).

### 6. Cluster Scenarios & Triads

Clusters:

- Mining and tourism sectors.

Triads:

- **Namibia Health & Land Triad**:
  - Combining health interventions with land reform and housing security for marginalized groups.
