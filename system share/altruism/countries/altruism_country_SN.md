## Altruism Country Deep Dive – Senegal (SN)

> **Status**: First-pass scaffold with approximate ranges (**\***).

### 1. Macro Context

- Population: ~18M\*
- GDP (nominal): ~USD 27–30B/year\*
- GDP per capita: ~USD 1,500–1,700/year\*
- Lower-middle-income West African coastal country with relative political stability and Dakar as a regional hub.

### 2. Government Social Spending and Fiscal Flows

Approximate:

- Total government expenditure: ~25–30% of GDP\*.
- Social spending (health, education, social protection): ~12–14% of GDP\*.
  - Health: ~4–5% of GDP\*.
  - Education: ~4–5% of GDP\*.
  - Social protection: ~2–3% of GDP\*.

### 3. ODA – Significant Recipient

- ODA inflows: around **USD 1–1.5B/year**\*.
- Major donors: France, EU, multilaterals, global health funds.

### 4. Private Philanthropy and Informal Altruism

Formal:

- Local NGOs and faith-based organizations.

Informal:

- Strong Sufi brotherhoods and religious networks that channel almsgiving.
- Diaspora remittances are very important.

### 5. Cause-Area Context

Senegal’s burden:

- Under‑5 mortality: ~38–45 per 1,000\*.
  - Neonatal disorders.
  - Malaria.
  - Diarrheal diseases and pneumonia.
- NCDs rising (CVD, diabetes).

### 6. Cluster Scenarios & Triads

Clusters:

- Senegalese diaspora in Europe and the US.

Triads:

- **Senegal & Sahel Child-Malaria Triad**.
- **Coastal Urban Youth Employment Triad** (around Dakar).
