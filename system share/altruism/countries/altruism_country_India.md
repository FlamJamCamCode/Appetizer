## Altruism Country Deep Dive – India

> **Status**: First-pass scaffold. Ranges and several numbers are approximate and marked with **\***. This file is designed for iterative refinement with more precise data and models.

### 1. Macro Context

- Population: ~1.4B\*
- GDP: ~USD 3.4–3.7T/year\*
- GDP per capita: ~USD 2,400–2,700/year\*
- Large internal diversity by state, caste, religion, and urban–rural status.

### 2. Government Social Spending and Fiscal Flows

Approximate social expenditure (union + state governments)\*:

- **Total social spending**: ~10–12% of GDP.
  - Public health: ~1.5–2.5% of GDP.
  - Social protection, pensions (including civil service, schemes like NPS): ~4–6% of GDP\*.
  - Food subsidies and welfare (e.g. PDS, MNREGA, PM-KISAN, etc.): ~3–4% of GDP\*.

Other:

- Defense: ~2–3% of GDP.
- Education: ~3–4% of GDP.

### 3. Official Development Assistance (ODA)

- India is primarily **a recipient** of ODA, but:
  - It also provides some aid to neighboring countries, especially in South Asia and Indian Ocean region.
- ODA given as % of GNI: small (<<0.1%)\*.

### 4. Private Philanthropy and Informal Altruism

Formal philanthropy:

- Estimated formal charitable giving (registered NGOs, CSR, family foundations) ~USD 8–15B/year\*.
- Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR) law:
  - Mandates some companies to spend 2% of profits on CSR activities, generating several billion USD/year.

Informal altruism:

- Enormous flows not fully measured:
  - Family remittances (within India and from diaspora).
  - Religious giving (temples, mosques, gurdwaras, churches).
  - Community-level mutual aid.

Religious traditions:

- Hinduism, Islam, Sikhism, Christianity, Jainism, Buddhism all have strong almsgiving/service norms (Dana, Zakat, Seva, etc.).

### 5. Volunteering and Service

India has:

- A large network of:
  - Religious volunteer organizations.
  - NGOs focused on education, health, disaster response.
  - Informal community volunteers (panchayats, youth groups).

Quantitative volunteering data is limited; future iterations should:

- Use surveys (e.g. CAF World Giving Index) for % volunteering and helping strangers.

### 6. Cause-Area Context (India and Global)

India-specific challenges tied to global cause-of-death patterns ([Our World in Data: Causes of Death](https://ourworldindata.org/causes-of-death), [Child mortality](https://ourworldindata.org/child-mortality)):

- Child mortality:
  - Major reductions over recent decades, but still significant under‑5 deaths from:
    - Neonatal complications.
    - Diarrhea, pneumonia.
    - Malnutrition.
- Adult mortality:
  - Rising share of non-communicable diseases:
    - Cardiovascular diseases, diabetes, chronic respiratory diseases.
  - Infectious diseases (TB, some HIV) remain important.

Altruistic resources in India (public + private, domestic + diaspora) can thus:

- Target both:
  - Remaining **communicable/child** death burden.
  - Rapidly growing **non-communicable disease** burden.

### 7. Microdistribution and Cluster Potential

Income/wealth distribution:

- High inequality; top deciles have very high incomes relative to median.

Cluster examples:

- High-wealth families / business owners:
  - If 1M such households gave an extra **INR 1–5M/year** (~USD 12,000–60,000) each, that alone could mobilize **USD 12–60B/year** for CESET-oriented interventions.
- Urban, educated, childless professionals:
  - If 5M such individuals gave 5–10% of income (say USD 20,000/year each on average), flows of **USD 5–10B/year** would be possible.

### 8. Triad and NSS Considerations

Potential India-centered Triads:

- **Child survival and malnutrition Triad**:
  - Focused on under‑5 mortality reduction, leveraging cheap, proven interventions.
- **TB and respiratory health Triad**:
  - Targeting TB, indoor air pollution, smoking.
- **Non-communicable disease Triad**:
  - Lifestyle, early detection, and treatment for cardiovascular disease and diabetes.

Global interplay:

- India as both:
  - A beneficiary of global altruistic flows (ODA, philanthropy).
  - A potential **source** of large altruistic flows (CSR, diaspora, high-wealth clusters) for global CESET issues.
