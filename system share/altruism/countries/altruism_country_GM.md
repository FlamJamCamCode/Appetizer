## Altruism Country Deep Dive – The Gambia (GM)

> **Status**: First-pass scaffold with approximate ranges (**\***).

### 1. Macro Context

- Population: ~2.7M\*
- GDP (nominal): ~USD 2–3B/year\*
- GDP per capita: ~USD 700–1,000/year\*
- Small low-income West African country surrounding the Gambia River, with agriculture, tourism, and remittances.

### 2. Government Social Spending and Fiscal Flows

Approximate:

- Total government expenditure: ~25–30% of GDP\* (ODA-supported).
- Social spending (health, education, social protection): ~11–13% of GDP\*.
  - Health: ~4–5% of GDP\*.
  - Education: ~4–5% of GDP\*.
  - Social protection: ~2–3% of GDP\*.

### 3. ODA – Heavy Recipient

- ODA inflows: on the order of **USD 0.2–0.3B/year**\*.
- Focus:
  - Health (particularly child/maternal health, malaria).
  - Education and governance.

### 4. Private Philanthropy and Informal Altruism

Formal:

- NGOs and faith-based organizations.

Informal:

- Family and community support.
- Remittances from diaspora (Europe, US) are crucial.

### 5. Cause-Area Context

Burden:

- Under‑5 mortality: ~55–70 per 1,000\*.
  - Neonatal disorders.
  - Malaria.
  - Diarrhea and pneumonia.
- Maternal mortality high.

### 6. Cluster Scenarios & Triads

Clusters:

- Gambian diaspora.

Triads:

- **Gambia River Health & Education Triad**:
  - Intensive under‑5 and maternal health focus, leveraging relatively small population.
