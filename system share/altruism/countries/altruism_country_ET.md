## Altruism Country Deep Dive – Ethiopia (ET)

> **Status**: First-pass scaffold with approximate ranges (**\***). To be refined with Ethiopian budget, philanthropy, and health data.

### 1. Macro Context

- Population: ~120M\*
- GDP (nominal): ~USD 120–130B/year\*
- GDP per capita: ~USD 900–1,100/year\*
- Low-income country with rapid population growth, some recent conflict, and large rural population.

### 2. Government Social Spending and Fiscal Flows

Approximate:

- Total government expenditure: ~15–20% of GDP\*.
- Social spending (health, education, social protection): ~8–10% of GDP\*.
  - Health: ~3–4% of GDP\*.
  - Education: ~4–5% of GDP\*.
  - Social protection: still limited coverage.

Constraints:

- Low revenue base.
- Debt and macroeconomic pressures.

### 3. ODA – Recipient

- Ethiopia is a **major ODA recipient**:
  - Bilateral and multilateral aid for:
    - Health (HIV, malaria, child health).
    - Food security and agriculture.
    - Infrastructure and governance.
- ODA inflows: several billion USD/year\*; ODA often >5% of GDP\*.

### 4. Private Philanthropy and Informal Altruism

Formal:

- Limited formal philanthropic infrastructure.
- Emerging domestic NGOs and diaspora-funded projects.

Informal:

- Strong community-based support:
  - Religious networks (Orthodox, Protestant, Muslim).
  - Informal rotating savings and credit associations.
  - Local solidarity mechanisms during crises.

### 5. Cause-Area Context

Within Ethiopia:

- High burden of:
  - Under‑5 mortality:
    - Neonatal disorders.
    - Diarrhea.
    - Pneumonia.
    - Malnutrition.
  - Maternal mortality.
  - Infectious diseases (e.g. malaria in some regions).

From [Our World in Data – Child mortality](https://ourworldindata.org/child-mortality) and [Causes of Death](https://ourworldindata.org/causes-of-death), Ethiopia is a key country for:

- Child survival.
- Basic health systems strengthening.

### 6. Cluster Scenarios

Clusters:

- Ethiopian diaspora in high-income countries.
- Domestic high-net-worth individuals (small but growing group).

Examples:

- If 1M diaspora Ethiopians\* gave an extra USD 500/year:
  - ~USD 0.5B/year for CESET-focused projects.
  - At USD 5,000 per child life saved\*, that implies up to ~100,000 child lives/year impacted.

### 7. NSS/Triad Considerations

Potential Triads:

- **Child Survival Triad**:
  - Vaccination, ORS/antibiotics, nutrition, neonatal care.
- **Maternal Health Triad**:
  - Skilled birth attendance, emergency obstetrics.
- **Resilient Agriculture and Nutrition Triad**:
  - Drought-resistant crops, community nutrition, shock-resilience.

Ethiopia is emblematic for:

- The “classic” global health/international development cause-area targeted by altruistic flows.
