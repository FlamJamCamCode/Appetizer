### 4. Interpretive Notes for Bangladesh (Dense, Climate-Exposed, NGO-Rich \*)

- **Population and vulnerability**  
  - Bangladesh has ~170 million people, very high population density, and substantial vulnerability to **floods, cyclones, and sea-level rise**.  
  - It has a strong tradition of **NGO-led development and microfinance** (e.g., BRAC, Grameen), with many programs directly targeting the poorest.

- **Engine’s package logic**  
  - Packages funneling resources into **basic health**, **deworming/NTD-like interventions**, **cash transfers**, and **education** are modeled as very high-impact.  
  - Angels-lite and Tax & Match scenarios, with a committed minority funding these interventions intensively (domestic + international donors), generate large DALY-equivalent gains in the model.

- **Climate and future generations**  
  - Climate-related components in packages (e.g., in Corporate & Billionaire Pledges or Solidarity Surcharges) are especially critical given Bangladesh’s exposure.  
  - The equity layer assigns a notable share of these benefits to **future_global** as well as to **domestic poor**, reflecting both current adaptation needs and long-term protection.

- **Realistic channels**  
  - Most high-impact flows in reality would go through:
    - **Government social and climate programs**.  
    - Large, sophisticated **NGOs** and community-based organizations.  
    - **International climate and development finance**.  
  - Packages that combine modest tax measures, strong donor commitments, and NGO execution align best with the country’s institutional landscape.

- **Next refinement steps**  
  - Incorporate Bangladesh-specific cost-effectiveness data for cyclone shelters, flood defenses, and health/education programs.  
  - Reflect the scale and targeting of existing NGO and social protection programs in package parameterization.
