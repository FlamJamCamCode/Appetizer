## Altruism Country Deep Dive – Tunisia (TN)

> **Status**: First-pass scaffold with approximate ranges (**\***). To be refined with Tunisian budget, aid, and philanthropy data.

### 1. Macro Context

- Population: ~12M\*
- GDP (nominal): ~USD 45–50B/year\*
- GDP per capita: ~USD 3,700–4,200/year\*
- Upper-middle-income North African country with significant political transitions over the last decade.

### 2. Government Social Spending and Fiscal Flows

Approximate:

- Total government expenditure: ~35–40% of GDP\*.
- Social spending (health, education, social protection): ~15–18% of GDP\*.
  - Health: ~6–7% of GDP\*.
  - Education: ~6–7% of GDP\*.
  - Social protection: ~3–4% of GDP\*.

### 3. ODA – Moderate Recipient

- Tunisia receives:
  - Significant concessional finance and some ODA from EU and multilaterals.
- ODA as % of GNI: modest (~2–3% or less)\*.

### 4. Private Philanthropy and Religious Giving

Formal:

- NGOs and foundations active in social and rights issues.

Informal and religious:

- Zakat and other Islamic giving.
- Family and community support.

### 5. Cause-Area Context

Domestic:

- Leading causes of death:
  - NCDs (CVD, cancers, diabetes).
- Child mortality:
  - Under‑5 mortality ~14–18/1,000\*; dominated by neonatal conditions and remaining infections.

Economic and social challenges:

- High youth unemployment.
- Regional inequalities (coastal vs interior).

### 6. Cluster Scenarios

Clusters:

- Educated urban youth and professionals.
- Tunisian diaspora (France, Europe, Gulf).

If:

- 300,000 diaspora/professional Tunisians\* contribute an extra EUR/USD 300/year:
  - ~USD 90M/year additional flows.

Targeted to:

- Youth entrepreneurship.
- Mental health and NCD prevention.
- Rural and interior-region services.

### 7. NSS/Triad Considerations

Potential Triads:

- **Maghreb Youth and NCD Triad**:
  - Jointly addressing youth unemployment and NCDs across Tunisia, Algeria, Morocco.
- **Democracy and Stability Triad**:
  - Focusing on civic education, rule of law, and social cohesion.
