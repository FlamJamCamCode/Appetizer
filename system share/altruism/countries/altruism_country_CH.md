## Altruism Country Deep Dive – Switzerland (CH)

> **Status**: First-pass scaffold with approximate ranges (**\***). To be refined with Swiss budget, philanthropy, and financial-centre data.

### 1. Macro Context

- Population: ~8.8M\*
- GDP (nominal): ~USD 900–1,000B/year\*
- GDP per capita: ~USD 90,000–100,000/year\*
- Very high-income country, major financial centre and hub for international organizations.

### 2. Government Social Spending and Fiscal Flows

Approximate social expenditure\*:

- Total social spending: ~20–22% of GDP.
  - Pensions: ~8–9% of GDP\*.
  - Health: ~11–12% of GDP\* (high on a per-capita basis).
  - Other social programmes: ~3–4% of GDP\*.

Other:

- Education: ~5–6% of GDP.
- Defense: ~1% of GDP.

### 3. Official Development Assistance (ODA)

- ODA/GNI: ~0.5–0.6%\*.
- Annual ODA volume: ~USD 4–5B/year\*.

Focus areas:

- Humanitarian aid.
- Development cooperation.
- Environment and climate.

### 4. Private Philanthropy and Financial Hub Role

Switzerland hosts:

- A very large wealth management industry and many family offices.
- Numerous international NGOs and foundations (e.g. Geneva).

Formal philanthropy:

- Private giving ~0.5–0.8% of GDP\*.
- Strong foundation culture.

Informal:

- Community-level solidarity.
- Association-based volunteering.

### 5. Volunteering and Civil Society

- High rates of volunteering and civic engagement in:
  - Local associations.
  - Sports, culture, and environment.
  - NGOs and international organizations.

### 6. Cause-Area Context

Domestic:

- Leading causes of death:
  - Cardiovascular diseases.
  - Cancers.
  - Dementia and other NCDs.
- Very low child mortality and infectious disease burden.

International:

- Through:
  - ODA.
  - Philanthropy.
  - International organizations headquartered in Switzerland.

Switzerland plays a **disproportionately large role** in:

- Global health governance.
- Humanitarian responses.
- International law and human rights.
