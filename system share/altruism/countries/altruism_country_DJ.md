## Altruism Country Deep Dive – Djibouti (DJ)

> **Status**: First-pass scaffold with approximate ranges (**\***).

### 1. Macro Context

- Population: ~1.1M\*
- GDP (nominal): ~USD 3–4B/year\*.
- GDP per capita: ~USD 3,000–3,500/year\*.
- Strategic port state on the Horn of Africa, hosting multiple foreign military bases, with an economy based on logistics and services.

### 2. Government Social Spending and Fiscal Flows

Approximate:

- Total public expenditure: ~30–35% of GDP\*.
- Social spending (health, education, social protection): ~12–15% of GDP\*.
  - Health: ~4–5% of GDP\*.
  - Education: ~4–5% of GDP\*.
  - Social protection: ~2–3% of GDP\*.

Financed by:

- Port revenues and rents.
- External finance (China, multilaterals).

### 3. ODA and External Flows

- Djibouti is both:
  - ODA recipient (World Bank, EU, Gulf states, etc.).
  - Recipient of significant foreign investment and base-rent income from:
    - US, France, China, others.

ODA scale:

- Hundreds of millions USD/year\* (when including concessional finance).

### 4. Private Altruism and Informal Support

Informal:

- Family and clan-based support.
- Religious giving (zakat).

Formal:

- Small domestic philanthropic and NGO sector, overshadowed by state and external actors.

### 5. Cause-Area Context

Djibouti’s CESET profile:

- Under‑5 mortality: ~45–60 per 1,000\*.
  - Neonatal disorders.
  - Pneumonia, diarrhea.
  - Malnutrition.
- NCDs:
  - CVD and diabetes rising.
- High exposure to:
  - Heat stress.
  - Water scarcity.

### 6. Cluster Scenarios & Triads

Clusters:

- Foreign militaries and logistics companies based in Djibouti.

Triads:

- **Bases-for-Benefit Triad**:
  - Earmarking small fractions of base rents and logistics margins into:
    - Health, water, and nutrition projects for Djiboutian population.
    - Regional refugee support (Somalia, Yemen).
