## Altruism Country Deep Dive – Indonesia (ID)

> **Status**: First-pass scaffold with approximate ranges (**\***). To be refined with Indonesian budget, philanthropy, and health data.

### 1. Macro Context

- Population: ~280M\*
- GDP (nominal): ~USD 1.4–1.6T/year\*
- GDP per capita: ~USD 5,000–6,000/year\*
- Lower-middle to upper-middle-income archipelago with vast diversity and strong Islamic influence.

### 2. Government Social Spending and Fiscal Flows

Approximate:

- Total government expenditure: ~17–20% of GDP\*.
- Social spending (health, education, social protection): ~10–12% of GDP\*.
  - Health: ~3–4% of GDP\*.
  - Education: ~3.5–4.5% of GDP\*.
  - Social protection: ~2–3% of GDP\*.

### 3. ODA – Donor and Recipient

- Indonesia is primarily a **recipient** of ODA but increasingly also:
  - Provides development cooperation to smaller neighbours.
- ODA received: several billion USD/year\* (declining as incomes rise).

### 4. Private Philanthropy and Religious Giving

Formal:

- Growing philanthropic sector:
  - Corporate CSR.
  - Foundations.

Religious giving:

- Largest Muslim-majority country; zakat and other Islamic charity flows (zakat, infaq, sadaqah, wakaf) are very large.
  - Some estimates put potential zakat collection in tens of billions USD/year\*.

### 5. Volunteering and Informal Altruism

- Strong mutual aid:
  - Community-based organizations (RT/RW structures).
  - Religious organizations and pesantren.
- High levels of helping behaviour in CAF World Giving Index.

### 6. Cause-Area Context

Within Indonesia:

- Child mortality has fallen but remains above high-income levels.
- Major burdens:
  - Neonatal disorders.
  - Diarrheal diseases and pneumonia in poorer regions.
  - Dengue and other vector-borne diseases.
  - Non-communicable diseases (CVD, diabetes) rising rapidly.
  - Disaster risk (earthquakes, tsunamis, volcanic eruptions).

### 7. Cluster Scenarios

Clusters:

- Middle and upper-middle class Muslims in cities (Jakarta, Surabaya, Bandung, etc.).
- Indonesian diaspora in richer countries.

If, for example:

- 10M middle/upper-income Indonesian Muslims\* consistently allocated an extra ~2% of income to CESET-optimized zakat distribution:
  - At an average income of USD 10,000/year\*, this is ~USD 2B/year.

Combined with:

- Improved zakat collection and targeting → large improvements in:
  - Child nutrition and survival.
  - Disaster relief.
  - Education access.

### 8. NSS/Triad Considerations

Indonesia is an ideal base for:

- **Zakat Optimization and Impact Triads**:
  - Modernizing zakat infrastructures to rigorously target CESETs.
- **Disaster Resilience Triads**:
  - Focusing on preparedness and rapid response across the archipelago.
- **NCD Transition Triads**:
  - Addressing urban lifestyle diseases emerging with development.
