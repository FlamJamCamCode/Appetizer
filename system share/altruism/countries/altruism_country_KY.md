## Altruism Country Deep Dive – Cayman Islands (KY)

> **Status**: First-pass scaffold with approximate ranges (**\***). To be refined with Cayman Islands financial, budget, and health data.

### 1. Macro Context

- Population: ~70k\*
- GDP (nominal): ~USD 5–6B/year\*.
- GDP per capita: ~USD 70,000–90,000/year\* (very high).
- British Overseas Territory, major offshore financial centre and hedge-fund hub.

### 2. Government Social Spending and Fiscal Flows

Approximate:

- Total government expenditure: ~25–30% of GDP\*.
- Social spending (health, education, social protection): ~12–15% of GDP\*.
  - Health: ~5–6% of GDP\*.
  - Education: ~4–5% of GDP\*.
  - Social protection: ~2–3% of GDP\*.

### 3. Financial Centre / Tax Haven Role

Cayman Islands:

- One of the largest offshore centres in the world by:
  - Number of registered funds and companies.
  - Assets under management (hundreds of billions to trillions USD\*).
- Minimal direct taxes on:
  - Income.
  - Capital gains.
  - Wealth.

This makes Cayman:

- A prime candidate for **Offshore Altruism** mechanisms.

### 4. Private Philanthropy and Informal Altruism

Formal:

- Domestic philanthropy for:
  - Education.
  - Community organizations.
  - Environment.
- Global philanthropy primarily depends on choices of:
  - Fund managers.
  - UHNWI clients using Cayman structures.

Informal:

- Family and community giving within local population.

### 5. Cause-Area Context

Domestic:

- Small population; main health issues similar to other high-income locales:
  - NCDs (CVD, diabetes).
  - Cancers.

Global:

- The real impact lever is:
  - The huge scale of assets passing through, not domestic needs.

### 6. Cluster Scenarios

Clusters:

- Global hedge funds and investment vehicles domiciled in Cayman.
- UHNWI and family offices using Cayman structures.

Scenarios:

- A **0.01–0.05%** voluntary commitment of AUM per year into CESET-optimized funds could:
  - Mobilize **multi-billion USD/year** flows.

These could be:

- Globally directed:
  - Child survival.
  - Global health.
  - Animal welfare.
  - Climate/energy transitions.

### 7. NSS/Triad Considerations

Potential Triads:

- **Cayman Global Altruism Triad**:
  - A platform where funds domiciled in Cayman commit small fractions of assets/returns to global CESET relief.
- **Financial-System Responsibility Triad**:
  - Shared with other hubs (Luxembourg, Switzerland, Singapore).
