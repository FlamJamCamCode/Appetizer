## Altruism Country Deep Dive – Tanzania (TZ)

> **Status**: First-pass scaffold with approximate ranges (**\***).

### 1. Macro Context

- Population: ~65M\*
- GDP (nominal): ~USD 80–90B/year\*
- GDP per capita: ~USD 1,200–1,400/year\*
- Low-income East African country with large rural population and significant tourism.

### 2. Government Social Spending and Fiscal Flows

Approximate:

- Total government expenditure: ~20–24% of GDP\*.
- Social spending (health, education, social protection): ~10–12% of GDP\*.
  - Health: ~3–4% of GDP\*.
  - Education: ~4–5% of GDP\*.
  - Social protection: limited but growing.

### 3. ODA – Major Recipient

- ODA inflows: several billion USD/year\*.
- Focus:
  - Health (HIV, malaria, TB, child health).
  - Education.
  - Infrastructure and agriculture.

### 4. Private Philanthropy and Informal Altruism

Formal:

- Domestic NGOs and foundations, including faith-based organizations.

Informal:

- Family and village-level support.
- Religious giving (Christian, Muslim).

### 5. Cause-Area Context

Tanzania’s burden:

- Under‑5 mortality: ~45–50 per 1,000\*.
  - Neonatal conditions.
  - Malaria.
  - Diarrhea and pneumonia.
- HIV/AIDS and TB.
- NCDs emerging with urbanization.

### 6. Cluster Scenarios & Triads

Clusters:

- Tanzanian diaspora.
- Tourism sector (safari and beach tourism).

Triads:

- **East Africa Child & Malaria Triad**:
  - Heavy focus on under‑5 deaths and malaria in Kenya/TZ/UG/RW.
