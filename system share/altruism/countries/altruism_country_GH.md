## Altruism Country Deep Dive – Ghana (GH)

> **Status**: First-pass scaffold with approximate ranges (**\***).

### 1. Macro Context

- Population: ~33M\*
- GDP (nominal): ~USD 75–80B/year\*
- GDP per capita: ~USD 2,200–2,500/year\*
- Lower-middle-income West African coastal state with democratic institutions, cocoa and gold exports, and recent debt stress.

### 2. Government Social Spending and Fiscal Flows

Approximate:

- Total government expenditure: ~25–30% of GDP\*.
- Social spending (health, education, social protection): ~12–15% of GDP\*.
  - Health: ~3–4% of GDP\*.
  - Education: ~4–5% of GDP\*.
  - Social protection: ~2–3% of GDP\*.

### 3. ODA – Significant Recipient

- ODA inflows: on the order of **USD 1–2B/year**\*.
- Focus:
  - Health (HIV, malaria, TB, maternal/child health).
  - Education and agriculture.
  - Governance, energy, infrastructure.

### 4. Private Philanthropy and Informal Altruism

Formal:

- Domestic foundations and corporate CSR (banks, telcos, mining).

Informal:

- Strong extended-family and church-based support networks.
- Remittances from Ghanaian diaspora (US, UK, EU) are large relative to the economy.

### 5. Cause-Area Context

Ghana’s burden:

- Under‑5 mortality: ~40 per 1,000 live births\*.
  - Neonatal disorders.
  - Malaria.
  - Diarrheal diseases and pneumonia.
- NCDs:
  - Growing burden of hypertension, stroke, diabetes.

### 6. Cluster Scenarios & Triads

Clusters:

- Ghanaian diaspora.
- Extractives and cocoa sector elites.

Triads:

- **Gulf of Guinea Child & Malaria Triad**:
  - Ghana + neighbours targeting under‑5 deaths.
- **Ghana NCD Transition Triad**:
  - Early prevention and treatment of hypertension/diabetes.
