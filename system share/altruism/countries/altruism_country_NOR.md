### 4. Interpretive Notes for Norway (High-Income, High-State Altruism \*)

- **Very high baseline altruism via the state**  
  - Norway has very high GDP per capita, extensive **social welfare**, and consistently high **ODA as % of GNI**, often exceeding international targets.  
  - Many “domestic social spending increases” imagined in global scenarios are already close to saturated here.

- **Where additional altruism matters most**  
  - In the engine, the largest incremental global impact from Norway-like countries comes from:
    - Maintaining and slightly increasing **ODA and climate finance**.  
    - Large **corporate and high-net-worth pledges** directed outward.  
  - Equity splits show most extra DALYs accruing to **foreign poor** and **future_global**, not Norwegian residents.

- **Cause priorities**  
  - High-impact causes for Norway-as-donor include:
    - **Global health (malaria-like, TB, NTDs)** in low-income countries.  
    - **Targeted cash transfers** and social protection in the poorest regions.  
    - **Climate mitigation and adaptation** with strong co-benefits for vulnerable populations.  
  - For Norway’s own population, incremental gains likely come more from:
    - Mental health, aging, and NCD-related interventions, where DALYs per dollar are lower than in the poorest countries.

- **Next refinement steps**  
  - Use Norway’s actual ODA portfolio to adjust cause weights for Norway’s “donor packages”.  
  - Model a realistic ceiling on domestic social spending and focus scenario variants on **global altruism** and **global public goods**.
