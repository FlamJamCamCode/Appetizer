## Altruism Country Deep Dive – Nigeria (NG)

> **Status**: First-pass scaffold with approximate ranges (**\***). To be refined with Nigerian budget, philanthropy, and CAF data.

### 1. Macro Context

- Population: ~220M\*
- GDP (nominal): ~USD 0.5–0.6T/year\*
- GDP per capita: ~USD 2,000–3,000/year\*
- Lower-middle-income country with very young population and high poverty rates.

### 2. Government Social Spending and Fiscal Flows

Approximate:

- Total government expenditure as % of GDP: relatively low (~10–15%)\*.
- Social spending (health, education, social protection): ~5–7% of GDP\*.
  - Health: ~1–2% of GDP\* (far below WHO recommendations).
  - Education: ~2–3% of GDP\*.
  - Social protection: limited coverage and low spending.

Constraints:

- Oil revenue volatility.
- Debt servicing burdens.
- Governance and corruption challenges.

### 3. ODA – Recipient

- Nigeria is a **major recipient** of ODA:
  - Global health (HIV, malaria, TB).
  - Immunization programmes.
  - Basic education, infrastructure.
- ODA inflows: several billion USD/year\*.

### 4. Private Philanthropy and Informal Altruism

CAF and other sources indicate Nigeria often scores **high** on:

- Percentage of people:
  - Helping strangers.
  - Donating money.
  - Volunteering time.

Even with low incomes, informal altruism is strong:

- Extended family support.
- Religious giving (tithes, zakat, offerings).
- Community fundraisers.

Formal philanthropy:

- Growing number of foundations set up by wealthy Nigerians (e.g. telecoms, banking, oil).
- Diaspora remittances:
  - Very large flows (USD 15–25B/year\*), much of which supports family welfare.

### 5. Cause-Area Context

Nigeria’s internal CESET profile is stark:

- High under‑5 mortality:
  - Major causes:
    - Neonatal complications.
    - Diarrheal diseases.
    - Pneumonia.
    - Malaria.
    - Malnutrition.
- Infectious diseases (HIV, TB, malaria) heavy burden.
- Maternal mortality relatively high.
- Conflict and violence in some regions (e.g. Boko Haram, farmer–herder conflicts).

From [Our World in Data – Child mortality](https://ourworldindata.org/child-mortality) and [Causes of Death](https://ourworldindata.org/causes-of-death), Nigeria is one of the largest contributors to global under‑5 deaths in absolute terms.

### 6. Cluster Scenarios and Resource Potential

Clusters:

- Nigerian diaspora in high-income countries:
  - If 2–3M diaspora members\* gave an extra USD 1,000/year each:
    - ~USD 2–3B/year additional flows to Nigeria.
- Domestic high-wealth elites:
  - If 50,000 wealthy Nigerians committed to 1–2% of net wealth/year:
    - Could generate USD 1–3B/year\* (depending on wealth estimates).

These flows, when directed to:

- High-impact health interventions (vaccines, nets, nutrition).
- Maternal health and basic infrastructure.

could avert **hundreds of thousands** of deaths over a decade and drastically reduce under‑5 mortality.

### 7. NSS/Triad Considerations

Potential Nigeria-centered Triads:

- **Under‑5 Survival Triad**:
  - Focus: vaccination, malaria prevention, ORS/antibiotics, neonatal care.
- **Maternal Health Triad**:
  - Skilled birth attendance, emergency obstetric care.
- **Violence Reduction and Stability Triad**:
  - Conflict mediation, education, livelihood alternatives.

These Triads could be:

- Co-funded by:
  - ODA.
  - Nigerian state budgets.
  - Domestic philanthropy and diaspora.
