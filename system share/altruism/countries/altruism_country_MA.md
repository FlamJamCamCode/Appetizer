## Altruism Country Deep Dive – Morocco (MA)

> **Status**: First-pass scaffold with approximate ranges (**\***). To be refined with Moroccan budget, aid, and philanthropy data.

### 1. Macro Context

- Population: ~37M\*
- GDP (nominal): ~USD 140–150B/year\*
- GDP per capita: ~USD 3,800–4,200/year\*
- Lower-middle to upper-middle-income North African monarchy with strong ties to Europe and the Arab world.

### 2. Government Social Spending and Fiscal Flows

Approximate:

- Total government expenditure: ~30–33% of GDP\*.
- Social spending (health, education, social protection): ~12–15% of GDP\*.
  - Health: ~5–6% of GDP\*.
  - Education: ~5–6% of GDP\*.
  - Social protection: ~2–3% of GDP\*.

### 3. ODA – Recipient and Emerging Donor

- Morocco:
  - Receives ODA (EU, multilaterals).
  - Also provides some South–South cooperation in Africa.
- ODA given as % of GNI: small (<0.1%)\*.

### 4. Private Philanthropy and Religious Giving

Formal:

- Growing philanthropic foundations (royal initiatives, business-linked foundations).

Informal and religious:

- Strong traditions of zakat, sadaqah, and community solidarity.

### 5. Cause-Area Context

Domestic:

- Leading causes of death:
  - Non-communicable diseases (CVD, cancers, diabetes).
  - Injuries (road accidents).
- Child mortality:
  - Much lower than Sahel; under‑5 mortality ~16–20/1,000\*; main causes are neonatal disorders and remaining infections.

International:

- Morocco-origin flows (ODA, philanthropy) can impact:
  - Sub-Saharan Africa via South–South cooperation.

### 6. Cluster Scenarios

Clusters:

- High-income urban Moroccans.
- Moroccan diaspora (Europe, Gulf).

If:

- 1M diaspora Moroccans\* gave an extra EUR/USD 300/year into CESET-optimized channels:
  - ~USD 300M/year.

Targets:

- Child and maternal health.
- Rural education and livelihoods.

### 7. NSS/Triad Considerations

Potential Triads:

- **Maghreb Child & Youth Triad**:
  - Addressing remaining child mortality and youth unemployment in Morocco, Algeria, Tunisia.
- **Morocco–Africa South–South Triad**:
  - Using Moroccan expertise and capital to support poorer African countries.
