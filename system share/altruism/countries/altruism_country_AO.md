## Altruism Country Deep Dive – Angola (AO)

> **Status**: First-pass scaffold with approximate ranges (**\***).

### 1. Macro Context

- Population: ~35M\*
- GDP (nominal): ~USD 75–85B/year\*.
- GDP per capita: ~USD 2,100–2,400/year\*.
- Oil-rich Southern African country with high inequality and post-civil-war reconstruction challenges.

### 2. Government Social Spending and Fiscal Flows

Approximate:

- Total public expenditure: ~30–35% of GDP\* (driven by oil revenues).
- Social spending (health, education, social protection): ~12–14% of GDP\*.
  - Health: ~4–5% of GDP\*.
  - Education: ~4–5% of GDP\*.
  - Social protection: ~2–3% of GDP\*.

### 3. ODA and External Finance

- ODA inflows: ~USD 0.5–1B/year\*.
- Large non-ODA flows:
  - Oil-backed loans and investments (e.g. China).

### 4. Private Philanthropy and Informal Altruism

Formal:

- Company and elite philanthropy (especially oil sector).

Informal:

- Family and community networks.

### 5. Cause-Area Context

Angola’s CESET profile:

- Under‑5 mortality: ~70–80 per 1,000\*.
  - Neonatal disorders.
  - Malaria.
  - Diarrhea and pneumonia.
- High maternal mortality.
- Post-conflict trauma and landmine injuries in some areas.

### 6. Cluster Scenarios & Triads

Clusters:

- Oil companies and state oil revenues.

Triads:

- **Oil-for-Health Triad**:
  - Dedicating a transparent slice of oil revenue to child/maternal health and NCD prevention.
