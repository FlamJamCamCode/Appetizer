## Altruism Country Deep Dive – Somalia (SO)

> **Status**: First-pass scaffold with approximate ranges (**\***).

### 1. Macro Context

- Population: ~17M\*
- GDP (nominal): very uncertain; rough range ~USD 7–10B/year\*.
- GDP per capita: ~USD 400–600/year\*.
- Fragile Horn of Africa state with decades of conflict, weak central institutions, and large pastoral and diaspora communities.

### 2. Government / De Facto Authority Social Spending

- Fragmented authority (Federal Government + member states + de facto administrations).
- Formal fiscal data is sparse; much service provision is:
  - ODA-funded.
  - NGO-implemented.

Ballpark:

- Total public spending (all levels): ~20–25% of (measured) GDP\*.
- Social spending (health, education, basic safety nets): ~8–10% of GDP\*, mostly ODA-financed.

### 3. ODA and Humanitarian Flows

- Somalia is a **major humanitarian recipient**:
  - ODA + humanitarian flows: several billion USD/year\*.
  - Focus:
    - Food security and famine prevention.
    - Health and nutrition.
    - Conflict- and drought-related relief.

### 4. Private Philanthropy, Zakat, and Remittances

Informal & religious:

- Very large remittances from diaspora (estimates in the billions USD/year\*).
- Zakat and sadaqah:
  - Important for social support.
  - Channeled both informally and via Islamic charities.

Formal:

- Local NGOs and Islamic charities.

### 5. Cause-Area Context

Somalia’s CESET profile:

- Under‑5 mortality: extremely high (~100+ per 1,000\*).
  - Neonatal disorders.
  - Pneumonia and diarrhea.
  - Measles outbreaks historically.
  - Severe acute malnutrition related to drought and conflict.
- Maternal mortality: very high.
- Conflict and violence.
- Recurrent droughts and locust outbreaks.

### 6. Cluster Scenarios & Triads

Clusters:

- Somali diaspora (Europe, North America, Gulf).
- Global Islamic philanthropic networks.

Triads:

- **Horn of Africa Famine & Child Survival Triad**:
  - Somalia as a key node alongside Ethiopia, Kenya, South Sudan.
- **Diaspora Remittance Optimisation Triad**:
  - Structuring some fraction of remittances for systemic CESET reduction (while preserving family support).
