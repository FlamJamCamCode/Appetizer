## Altruism Country Deep Dive – Rwanda (RW)

> **Status**: First-pass scaffold with approximate ranges (**\***).

### 1. Macro Context

- Population: ~13M\*
- GDP (nominal): ~USD 12–14B/year\*
- GDP per capita: ~USD 900–1,100/year\*
- Low-income, landlocked Great Lakes country with notable post-genocide governance and development reforms.

### 2. Government Social Spending and Fiscal Flows

Approximate:

- Total government expenditure: ~28–32% of GDP\* (aided by ODA).
- Social spending (health, education, social protection): ~12–15% of GDP\*.
  - Health: ~6–7% of GDP\*.
  - Education: ~4–5% of GDP\*.
  - Social protection: ~2–3% of GDP\*.

### 3. ODA – Significant Recipient

- ODA inflows: ~USD 1–1.5B/year\*.
- Known for:
  - Effective use of aid (health, governance, ICT).

### 4. Private Philanthropy and Informal Altruism

Formal:

- Emerging local NGOs and foundations.

Informal:

- Community institutions (e.g. Umuganda – community work days).
- Family and church-based support.

### 5. Cause-Area Context

Rwanda’s progress:

- Under‑5 mortality has dropped dramatically (~35–40 per 1,000\*).
- Main remaining causes:
  - Neonatal conditions.
  - Pneumonia and diarrhea.
  - Malaria in some regions.

Other burdens:

- HIV, now much better controlled.
- NCDs rising.

### 6. Cluster Scenarios & Triads

Clusters:

- Rwandan diaspora.
- Global health and governance enthusiasts (Rwanda as a “showcase”).

Triads:

- **Rwanda Model Health & Governance Triad**:
  - Using Rwanda as a laboratory and demonstration of high-efficiency CESET deployment.
