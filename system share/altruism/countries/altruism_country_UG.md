## Altruism Country Deep Dive – Uganda (UG)

> **Status**: First-pass scaffold with approximate ranges (**\***).

### 1. Macro Context

- Population: ~49M\*
- GDP (nominal): ~USD 45–50B/year\*
- GDP per capita: ~USD 900–1,100/year\*
- Low-income East African country with high fertility and large rural population.

### 2. Government Social Spending and Fiscal Flows

Approximate:

- Total government expenditure: ~20–24% of GDP\*.
- Social spending (health, education, social protection): ~9–11% of GDP\*.
  - Health: ~3–4% of GDP\*.
  - Education: ~4–5% of GDP\*.
  - Social protection: small but expanding.

### 3. ODA – Major Recipient

- ODA inflows: a few billion USD/year\*.
- Focus:
  - HIV/AIDS and TB.
  - Malaria.
  - Child and maternal health.
  - Education and agriculture.

### 4. Private Philanthropy and Informal Altruism

Formal:

- Active NGO sector (local and international).

Informal:

- Extended family and community support.
- Church-based giving.

### 5. Cause-Area Context

Uganda’s burden:

- Under‑5 mortality: ~45–50 per 1,000\*.
  - Neonatal disorders.
  - Malaria.
  - Pneumonia and diarrhea.
- HIV/AIDS burden historically high, improving with ART scale-up.
- NCDs rising from low base.

### 6. Cluster Scenarios & Triads

Clusters:

- Ugandan diaspora.
- Faith communities domestically and abroad.

Triads:

- **East Africa HIV & Child Health Triad**.
- **Great Lakes Health & Stability Triad** (with DRC, Rwanda, Burundi).
