## Altruism Country Deep Dive – Sweden (SE)

> **Status**: First-pass scaffold with approximate ranges (**\***). To be refined with Swedish budget, SCB, and philanthropy data.

### 1. Macro Context

- Population: ~10.5M\*
- GDP: ~USD 650–700B/year\*
- GDP per capita: ~USD 60,000–65,000/year\*
- Nordic high-income welfare state with strong social cohesion and generous ODA.

### 2. Government Social Spending and Fiscal Flows

Approximate social expenditure\*:

- Total social spending: ~26–28% of GDP.
  - Pensions: ~9–10% of GDP\*.
  - Health and long-term care: ~9–10% of GDP\*.
  - Other social protection: ~7–9% of GDP\*.

Other:

- Education: ~6–7% of GDP.
- Defense: ~1.3–1.7% of GDP (rising with NATO commitments).

### 3. Official Development Assistance (ODA)

- ODA/GNI: ~0.9–1.0%\* (one of the highest in the world).
- Annual ODA volume: ~USD 5–7B/year\*.

Focus areas:

- Poverty reduction and human rights.
- Gender equality.
- Climate and environment.
- Humanitarian assistance.

### 4. Private Philanthropy

Formal philanthropy:

- Private giving ~0.8–1.0% of GDP\*.
- Strong foundation and civil society sectors.

Informal:

- Considerable mutual support and volunteering, though often not labelled as “charity”.

### 5. Volunteering and Civil Society

Sweden has:

- High membership rates in associations (sports, culture, unions).
- Significant volunteering rates in civil society and NGOs.

### 6. Cause-Area Context

Domestic:

- Leading causes of death:
  - Cardiovascular diseases.
  - Cancers.
  - Dementia and Alzheimer’s.
- Very low child and infectious disease mortality.

International:

- Swedish ODA heavily contributes to:
  - Global health.
  - Education.
  - Governance and human rights.
  - Climate and environment.

### 7. Cluster Scenarios

Given Sweden’s already high state-based altruism:

- Cluster scenarios for private giving can be seen as:
  - “Layering” extra capacity on top of an already altruistic state.

If, for example:

- 200,000 high-income Swedes (\*top few percent) committed an extra 5–10% of income:
  - Could yield additional **USD 5–10B/year** for CESET-focused work.

### 8. NSS/Triad Considerations

Sweden is ideal “poster” for:

- Scenario B (“Top Altruist State As All”):
  - Shows what a high ODA + high social spending state looks like.
- Triads:
  - **Global Human Rights Triads**.
  - **Climate and Environment Triads**.
  - **Aid-Optimization Triads** (given strong development expertise).
