## Altruism Country Deep Dive – Afghanistan (AF)

> **Status**: First-pass scaffold with approximate ranges (**\***). To be refined with Afghan health, aid, and updated economic data.

### 1. Macro Context

- Population: ~40M\*
- GDP (nominal): ~USD 15–20B/year\* (post-2021 figures very uncertain).
- GDP per capita: ~USD 400–500/year\*.
- Landlocked, conflict-affected country with decades of war, political upheaval, and extreme poverty.

### 2. Government and De Facto Authority Spending

Post-2021:

- International recognition and official fiscal data are limited.
- Social spending is a mix of:
  - De facto authority expenditures.
  - Very large humanitarian and development flows via UN agencies and NGOs.

Pre-2021 patterns (rough):

- Total government expenditure: ~25–30% of GDP\*.
- Social spending: largely externally financed.

### 3. ODA and Humanitarian Flows

- Afghanistan has been among the **largest recipients of aid** for two decades:
  - ODA and military-related spending peaked at tens of billions USD/year during foreign presence.
  - After 2021, much bilateral support stopped, but:
    - Humanitarian aid and some development projects continue via multilateral channels.

Current flows (approximate):

- Billions of USD/year in:
  - Food aid.
  - Health and nutrition programmes.
  - Education (especially for younger children where allowed).

### 4. Private Philanthropy and Informal Altruism

Formal:

- Domestic philanthropic institutions limited and constrained.

Informal:

- Strong extended family and tribal/clan support networks.
- Religious giving (zakat, sadaqah).
- Remittances from Afghan diaspora:
  - Important for household survival and local economies.

### 5. Cause-Area Context

Afghanistan’s cause-of-death and burden-of-disease patterns (pre- and post-2021) include:

- High under‑5 mortality:
  - Neonatal disorders.
  - Diarrhea and pneumonia.
  - Malnutrition.
- Maternal mortality very high.
- Significant burdens from:
  - Conflict and violence.
  - Injuries (landmines, unexploded ordnance).
  - Infectious diseases (TB, etc.).
  - NCDs in older age groups.

Data from [Our World in Data](https://ourworldindata.org/causes-of-death) and WHO should be used with caution due to rapid regime and security changes.

### 6. Cluster Scenarios

Clusters:

- Afghan diaspora in Europe, North America, and neighbouring countries.
- Global humanitarian and peacebuilding donors.

Scenarios:

- An extra **USD 1–2B/year** from:
  - Diaspora giving.
  - Dedicated global Triads.

directed to:

- Child/maternal health.
- Nutrition and basic income support.
- Safe education access (especially girls, where possible).

could:

- Prevent large numbers of deaths and reduce enduring trauma, even under constrained political conditions.

### 7. NSS/Triad Considerations

Potential Triads:

- **Conflict and Humanitarian Health Triad**:
  - Specialized in operating under de facto authorities and conflict conditions.
- **Afghan Diaspora Support Triad**:
  - Structuring remittance and philanthropic flows to maximize CESET outcomes while navigating political complexities.
