## Altruism Country Deep Dive – Kenya (KE)

> **Status**: First-pass scaffold with approximate ranges (**\***).

### 1. Macro Context

- Population: ~55M\*
- GDP (nominal): ~USD 110–120B/year\*
- GDP per capita: ~USD 2,000–2,200/year\*
- Lower-middle-income East African hub with dynamic services sector and significant rural poverty.

### 2. Government Social Spending and Fiscal Flows

Approximate:

- Total government expenditure: ~26–30% of GDP\*.
- Social spending (health, education, social protection): ~12–15% of GDP\*.
  - Health: ~4–5% of GDP\*.
  - Education: ~5–6% of GDP\*.
  - Social protection: ~2–3% of GDP\*.

### 3. ODA – Major Recipient

- ODA inflows: several billion USD/year\*.
- Key sectors:
  - Health (HIV, malaria, TB).
  - Education.
  - Agriculture and infrastructure.

### 4. Private Philanthropy and Informal Altruism

Formal:

- Foundations and corporate CSR programmes.
- Nairobi as regional HQ for many NGOs.

Informal:

- Strong family and community support.
- Religious giving (churches, mosques).

### 5. Cause-Area Context

Kenya’s burden:

- Under‑5 mortality: ~40–45 per 1,000\* (declining).
  - Neonatal conditions.
  - Pneumonia, diarrhea.
  - Malaria in some regions.
- HIV/AIDS and TB.
- NCDs rising (CVD, diabetes).

### 6. Cluster Scenarios & Triads

Clusters:

- Kenyan diaspora (US, UK, Gulf).
- Nairobi tech and business elites.

Triads:

- **East Africa Health & Tech Triad**:
  - Combining digital health, mobile money, and CESET interventions.
