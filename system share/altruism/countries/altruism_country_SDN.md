### 4. Interpretive Notes for Sudan (Nile / Sahel Context \*)

- **Conflict and humanitarian needs**  
  - Sudan has experienced repeated conflict, political instability, and humanitarian crises, including large numbers of internally displaced people and refugees.  
  - Basic needs (food, health care, shelter) for vulnerable populations are often met through **international humanitarian aid** rather than domestic welfare systems.

- **Packages in a fragile state**  
  - Tax-heavy packages (e.g., Tax & Match) are harder to realize when state capacity and legitimacy are constrained.  
  - The engine’s most relevant packages for Sudan today are:
    - **Corporate & Billionaire Pledges** and **Solidarity Surcharges** globally, with funds directed to humanitarian and stabilization efforts in Sudan.  
    - **Volunteer & Talent Surge** in the form of humanitarian workers and Sudanese professionals (including diaspora) engaged in health, education, and conflict resolution.

- **Cause and equity patterns**  
  - Engine causes such as **cash transfers**, **basic health**, and **education** map well onto real needs:
    - Emergency cash and food support for displaced and conflict-affected households.  
    - Primary health and maternal/child care in crisis zones.  
    - Education continuity for children in and around conflict areas.  
  - Equity splits imply that most DALYs from these components go to the **domestic poor** and, in the climate-related parts, to **future_global**, given Sudan’s vulnerability to drought and desertification.

- **Next refinement steps**  
  - Incorporate Sudan-specific humanitarian and conflict data (e.g., OCHA, UNHCR) to better characterize the scale and distribution of needs.  
  - Explicitly represent humanitarian funding streams and NGOs as key channels in the Sudan package implementations.
