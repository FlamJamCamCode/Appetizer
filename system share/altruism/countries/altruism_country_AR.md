## Altruism Country Deep Dive – Argentina (AR)

> **Status**: First-pass scaffold with approximate ranges (**\***).

### 1. Macro Context

- Population: ~46M\*
- GDP (nominal): ~USD 600–650B/year\* (highly volatile due to inflation and FX).
- GDP per capita: ~USD 13,000–15,000/year\*.
- Upper-middle-income South American country with recurrent macro crises and strong social protection norms.

### 2. Government Social Spending and Fiscal Flows

Approximate:

- Total government expenditure: ~35–40% of GDP\*.
- Social spending (pensions, health, social programmes): ~20–24% of GDP\*.
  - Pensions: ~10–12% of GDP\*.
  - Health: ~7–8% of GDP\*.
  - Other social protection: ~3–4% of GDP\*.

Other:

- Education: ~5–6% of GDP.
- High and persistent public debt and inflation.

### 3. Official Development Assistance (ODA)

- Argentina is primarily a **recipient** of concessional finance in crises, but:
  - Not a major ODA recipient compared to low-income countries.
- ODA given as % GNI: minimal (<0.05%)\*.

### 4. Private Philanthropy and Informal Altruism

Formal:

- Foundations and corporate CSR, especially in Buenos Aires.

Informal:

- Strong family support networks.
- Mutual aid, especially during economic downturns.

### 5. Cause-Area Context

Domestic:

- Leading causes of death:
  - CVD.
  - Cancers.
  - NCDs related to lifestyle (e.g. diabetes).
- Child mortality:
  - Under‑5 mortality ~9–12/1,000\*.
- Social issues:
  - Poverty and inequality are cyclical with economic crises.

### 6. Cluster Scenarios & Triads

Clusters:

- High-income urban Argentines.
- Large diaspora (Spain, Italy, US).

Triads:

- **Southern Cone NCD & Social Protection Triad**.
- **Debt & Stability Triad**:
  - Focusing on protecting CESETs during macro crises.
