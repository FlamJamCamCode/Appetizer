### 4. Interpretive Notes for Eritrea (Horn of Africa Context \*)

- **Closed political environment and data scarcity**  
  - Eritrea is characterized by limited political openness, national service, and scarce publicly available economic and social data.  
  - This makes it harder to calibrate the engine’s assumptions for income distribution, social spending, and the role of private philanthropy.

- **Diaspora and external channels**  
  - Eritrean diaspora communities are significant, and remittances likely play an important role in household welfare.  
  - In practice, altruistic flows may arrive more via:
    - **Diaspora transfers and religious giving**.  
    - **NGO and humanitarian channels**, where permitted.
  - Packages like Angels-lite and Corporate & Billionaire Pledges, when interpreted as **global or diaspora-driven**, are more plausible than domestic tax-heavy scenarios.

- **Cause priorities**  
  - With poverty and health challenges similar to other low-income Horn countries, the engine’s emphasis on:
    - **Cash transfers** and **basic health** interventions, and  
    - Potentially **education** where feasible,  
  - is directionally appropriate, though the exact DALY-per-dollar values will need tailoring once better Eritrea-specific data are available.

- **Next refinement steps**  
  - Incorporate whatever reliable statistics exist on Eritrea’s health outcomes, poverty, and remittance volumes.  
  - Explicitly flag higher uncertainty for Eritrea in global comparisons due to limited data.
