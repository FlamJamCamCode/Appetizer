## Altruism Country Deep Dive – Democratic Republic of the Congo (DRC, CD)

> **Status**: First-pass scaffold with approximate ranges (**\***). To be refined with Congolese budget, health, and aid data.

### 1. Macro Context

- Population: ~100M\*
- GDP (nominal): ~USD 60–70B/year\*
- GDP per capita: ~USD 600–800/year\*
- Vast, resource-rich central African country with extreme poverty, weak infrastructure, and recurrent conflict.

### 2. Government Social Spending and Fiscal Flows

Approximate:

- Total government expenditure: ~15–20% of GDP\*.
- Social spending (health, education, social protection): ~6–8% of GDP\*.
  - Health: ~2–3% of GDP\*.
  - Education: ~3–4% of GDP\*.
  - Social protection: minimal.

Fiscal capacity:

- Severely constrained by:
  - Very low revenue base.
  - Governance challenges and corruption.
  - Security spending needs.

### 3. ODA – Major Recipient

- DRC is one of the **largest ODA recipients** in Africa:
  - ODA inflows: several billion USD/year\*.
  - ODA % of GNI often >10%\*.

Aid supports:

- Health (HIV, malaria, TB, Ebola).
- Child and maternal survival.
- Humanitarian relief (displacement, food insecurity).
- Governance, peacekeeping, and stabilization.

### 4. Private Philanthropy and Informal Altruism

Formal:

- Very limited domestic philanthropic institutions.

Informal:

- Family, clan, and church-based support networks.
- Religious giving (Catholic, Protestant, Pentecostal, etc.).

### 5. Cause-Area Context

From [Our World in Data – Causes of Death](https://ourworldindata.org/causes-of-death) and [Child mortality](https://ourworldindata.org/child-mortality), DRC has:

- Very high under‑5 mortality:
  - Neonatal disorders.
  - Malaria.
  - Diarrhea and pneumonia.
  - Malnutrition.
- High maternal mortality.
- Significant burdens from:
  - Infectious diseases (HIV, TB, Ebola outbreaks).
  - Conflict-related mortality and sexual violence.

### 6. Cluster Scenarios

External clusters:

- Global health donors and philanthropists.
- DRC diaspora (Europe, North America).

Scenarios:

- An extra **USD 2–3B/year** targeted at:
  - Proven child survival interventions.
  - Maternal health.
  - Conflict-sensitive service delivery.

could:

- Avert hundreds of thousands of deaths over a decade, depending on implementation, and materially shift cause-of-death profiles for children.

### 7. NSS/Triad Considerations

Potential Triads:

- **Great Lakes Child and Maternal Survival Triad**:
  - Covering DRC, Rwanda, Burundi, Uganda.
- **Conflict and Health Triad**:
  - Integrating peacekeeping, community protection, and health service provision.
