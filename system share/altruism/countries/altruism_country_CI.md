## Altruism Country Deep Dive – Côte d’Ivoire (CI)

> **Status**: First-pass scaffold with approximate ranges (**\***).

### 1. Macro Context

- Population: ~28M\*
- GDP (nominal): ~USD 70–80B/year\*
- GDP per capita: ~USD 2,500–2,800/year\*
- Lower-middle-income West African coastal nation, major cocoa exporter with Abidjan as an economic hub.

### 2. Government Social Spending and Fiscal Flows

Approximate:

- Total government expenditure: ~22–26% of GDP\*.
- Social spending (health, education, social protection): ~10–12% of GDP\*.
  - Health: ~3–4% of GDP\*.
  - Education: ~4–5% of GDP\*.
  - Social protection: ~2–3% of GDP\*.

### 3. ODA – Significant Recipient

- ODA inflows: ~USD 1B/year\*.
- Donors: France, EU, multilaterals, global health funds.

### 4. Private Philanthropy and Informal Altruism

Formal:

- Corporate CSR (agriculture, banking, telecom).

Informal:

- Extended-family support.
- Religious and community structures (churches, mosques).

### 5. Cause-Area Context

Burden:

- Under‑5 mortality: ~60 per 1,000\* (declining slowly).
  - Neonatal causes.
  - Malaria.
  - Diarrhea and pneumonia.
- NCDs:
  - Rising CVD and diabetes in urban populations.

### 6. Cluster Scenarios & Triads

Clusters:

- Cocoa-sector value chains (farmers and global buyers).

Triads:

- **Cocoa & Child Survival Triad**:
  - Linking ethical cocoa premiums to CESET reductions in producing regions.
