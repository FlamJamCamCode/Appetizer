### 4. Interpretive Notes for Somalia (Horn of Africa Context \*)

- **Fragility and diaspora dependence**  
  - Somalia is a fragile, conflict-affected state with limited central capacity and a very large **diaspora remittance** flow relative to GDP.  
  - In reality, much of the altruistic transfer into Somalia already occurs through **family remittances**, religious giving, and NGO/humanitarian channels rather than domestic taxation.

- **Implications for packages**  
  - Packages that rely heavily on **domestic tax increases** (e.g., Tax & Match) are less realistic without major political breakthroughs.  
  - By contrast, packages emphasizing:
    - **Diaspora giving and pledges** (Angels-lite style focused on Somalis abroad).  
    - **Corporate & billionaire pledges** internationally, earmarked for humanitarian and state-building efforts.  
    - **Volunteer & Talent Surge** via international staff and Somali professionals abroad.  
  - are better aligned with how resources can actually flow into Somalia.

- **Cause priorities under the engine’s logic**  
  - Given high poverty and humanitarian needs, the model’s **cash transfers** and **basic health** components correspond reasonably well to:
    - Humanitarian food/cash assistance.  
    - Primary health and maternal/child care in IDP camps and rural areas.  
  - Equity splits should heavily favor **domestic poor** and (for climate-related parts) the **future global** category, as Somalia is highly vulnerable to drought and climate shocks despite negligible emissions.

- **Next refinement steps**  
  - Explicitly represent remittances and humanitarian aid flows in the Somalia context.  
  - Adjust feasibility parameters so that large altruistic inflows are modeled as primarily **external/diaspora-driven**, not tax-based.
