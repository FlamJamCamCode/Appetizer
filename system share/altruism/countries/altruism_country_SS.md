## Altruism Country Deep Dive – South Sudan (SS)

> **Status**: First-pass scaffold with approximate ranges (**\***).

### 1. Macro Context

- Population: ~11M\*
- GDP (nominal): ~USD 3–5B/year\* (very volatile due to oil dependence and conflict).
- GDP per capita: ~USD 300–500/year\*.
- World's newest state (since 2011), landlocked, heavily conflict-affected, with extreme poverty.

### 2. Government Social Spending and Fiscal Flows

Approximate:

- Total public spending: ~25–30% of GDP\* (heavily oil-based and opaque).
- Social spending:
  - Very low in practice; much health/education delivered by NGOs.

### 3. ODA and Humanitarian Assistance

- South Sudan is:
  - One of the highest per capita recipients of humanitarian aid.
- ODA + humanitarian inflows: **over USD 1B/year**\*.
- Focus:
  - Emergency food and nutrition.
  - Primary health care.
  - Protection and peacekeeping support.

### 4. Private Philanthropy and Informal Altruism

Informal:

- Strong clan and ethnic group support.
- Churches and faith-based organizations central to social support.

Formal:

- Domestic formal philanthropy is minimal; international NGOs dominate.

### 5. Cause-Area Context

South Sudan’s CESET profile:

- Under‑5 mortality: extremely high (~90–110 per 1,000\*).
  - Neonatal disorders.
  - Pneumonia, diarrhea.
  - Severe acute malnutrition.
- Maternal mortality: among the highest in the world.
- Conflict and violence.
- Flooding and climate shocks.

### 6. Cluster Scenarios & Triads

Clusters:

- Global humanitarian donors and peacebuilding organisations.
- South Sudanese diaspora.

Triads:

- **Upper Nile Child & Famine Prevention Triad**:
  - Combining health, nutrition, and conflict-sensitive logistics.
- **Peace and Governance Triad**:
  - Slowly shifting from pure emergency response to building resilient institutions.
