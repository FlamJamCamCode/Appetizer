## Altruism Country Deep Dive – Haiti (HT)

> **Status**: First-pass scaffold with approximate ranges (**\***). To be refined with Haitian budget, health, and aid data.

### 1. Macro Context

- Population: ~11.5M\*
- GDP (nominal): ~USD 15–20B/year\*
- GDP per capita: ~USD 1,300–1,700/year\*
- Low-income Caribbean country with chronic political instability, natural disasters, and high poverty.

### 2. Government Social Spending and Fiscal Flows

Approximate:

- Total government expenditure: ~20–25% of GDP\*.
- Social spending (health, education, social protection): ~8–10% of GDP\*.
  - Health: ~2–3% of GDP\*.
  - Education: ~3–4% of GDP\*.
  - Social protection: limited.

Fiscal constraints:

- Low tax revenues.
- High dependence on external support.

### 3. ODA – Heavy Recipient

- Haiti is heavily dependent on ODA and NGO support:
  - ODA and NGO inflows: several billion USD/year, particularly after major earthquakes and hurricanes\*.

Aid channels:

- Bilateral donors, multilaterals.
- Very large NGO presence (health, education, humanitarian).

### 4. Private Philanthropy and Informal Altruism

Formal:

- Domestic philanthropy modest.

Informal:

- Strong family and community support.
- Remittances from diaspora:
  - A major income source (several billion USD/year\*), supporting household consumption and education.

### 5. Cause-Area Context

Haiti faces:

- Elevated under‑5 mortality:
  - Neonatal disorders.
  - Diarrhea, pneumonia.
  - Malnutrition.
- Major burden from:
  - Communicable diseases (cholera outbreaks, HIV).
  - Maternal mortality.
  - Injuries from natural disasters and violence.

Cause-of-death data from [Our World in Data](https://ourworldindata.org/causes-of-death) and WHO show a profile similar to other low-income fragile states, with additional spikes due to disasters.

### 6. Cluster Scenarios

Clusters:

- Haitian diaspora in the US, Canada, France, and elsewhere.
- Global poverty and disaster-focused donors.

If:

- 1M diaspora Haitians\* contribute an extra USD 300/year to CESET-optimized projects:
  - ~USD 300M/year additional flows.

If targeted to:

- Child/maternal health.
- Clean water and sanitation.
- Disaster-resilient infrastructure.

then:

- Significant reductions in preventable deaths could be achieved even before governance improves.

### 7. NSS/Triad Considerations

Potential Triads:

- **Caribbean Disaster and Health Triad**:
  - Joint approach for Haiti and neighbouring small states.
- **Haiti Rebuild and Stabilization Triad**:
  - Coordinating long-term investments beyond short-term humanitarian cycles.
