## Altruism Country Deep Dive – Italy (IT)

> **Status**: First-pass scaffold with approximate ranges (**\***).

### 1. Macro Context

- Population: ~59M\*
- GDP (nominal): ~USD 2.1–2.2T/year\*
- GDP per capita: ~USD 37,000–40,000/year\*
- Core EU country with aging population and strong Catholic heritage.

### 2. Government Social Spending and Fiscal Flows

Approximate:

- Total social spending: ~27–29% of GDP\*.
  - Pensions: ~15% of GDP\*.
  - Health: ~7–8% of GDP\*.
  - Other social protection: ~4–6% of GDP\*.

Other:

- Education: ~4% of GDP.
- High public debt (>140% of GDP).

### 3. Official Development Assistance (ODA)

- ODA/GNI: ~0.2–0.3%\*.
- ODA volume: ~USD 5–6B/year\*.
- Focus:
  - Mediterranean and African neighbours.
  - Migration, humanitarian, and development.

### 4. Private Philanthropy

Formal:

- Private giving ~0.3–0.5% of GDP\*.
- Catholic charities (Caritas, etc.) play significant roles.

Informal:

- Family-based support is extremely strong; intergenerational transfers.

### 5. Cause-Area Context

Domestic:

- Leading causes of death:
  - CVD.
  - Cancers.
  - Dementia.
- Very low child mortality.

International:

- Italy-origin flows relevant for:
  - Migration routes.
  - Africa-focused health and development.

### 6. Cluster Scenarios & Triads

Clusters:

- Wealthy Northern industrial families.
- Catholic institutions.

Triads:

- **Mediterranean Migration and Health Triad**.
- **Catholic Global Health Triad**.
