## Altruism Country Deep Dive – Libya (LY)

> **Status**: First-pass scaffold with approximate ranges (**\***). To be refined with Libyan economic, aid, and health data given ongoing political fragmentation.

### 1. Macro Context

- Population: ~7M\*
- GDP (nominal): highly volatile, ~USD 40–60B/year\* depending on oil and conflict.
- GDP per capita: ~USD 6,000–9,000/year\* (nominal), varies sharply year to year.
- Oil-rich North African state with prolonged conflict, divided authorities, and disrupted services.

### 2. Government / De Facto Authority Social Spending

- Multiple competing authorities and off-budget flows.
- Historically, under Gaddafi:
  - High public spending and subsidies, including social sectors.
- Currently (approximate):
  - Social spending (health, education, subsidies) still substantial but:
    - Fragmented.
    - Data poor.

### 3. ODA and International Support

- Libya:
  - Receives targeted humanitarian and stabilization assistance.
  - Not a classic low-income ODA recipient, but:
    - Significant external involvement in:
      - Health system support.
      - Reconstruction.
      - Refugee and migrant issues.

### 4. Private Philanthropy and Informal Altruism

- Hard to quantify due to conflict and institutional breakdown.
- Likely:
  - Strong family and clan-based support.
  - Religious giving.
  - Diaspora remittances.

### 5. Cause-Area Context

Libya’s cause-of-death profile is shaped by:

- NCDs (CVD, cancers) as in other upper-middle-income countries.
- Conflict-related mortality and injuries.
- Road injuries.
- Regional disparities in child and maternal mortality.

Health systems:

- Severely affected by conflict, sanctions, and fragmentation.

### 6. Cluster Scenarios

Clusters:

- Libyan diaspora in Europe, North America, Gulf.
- Global humanitarian and reconstruction donors.

If:

- 200,000 Libyans abroad\* each gave an extra USD 500/year into well-governed Triads:
  - ~USD 100M/year.

Plus:

- Oil revenue shares, if channeled into transparent CESET-focused funds, could further:
  - Rebuild health and education.
  - Address conflict legacies (trauma, disabilities).

### 7. NSS/Triad Considerations

Potential Triads:

- **Libya Reconstruction and Health Triad**:
  - Linking ceasefire, institution building, and health system restoration.
- **Mediterranean Migration Triad**:
  - Addressing migrant and refugee suffering transiting through Libya.
