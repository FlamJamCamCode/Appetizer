### 4. Interpretive Notes for Nigeria (Young, Resource-Rich, Unequal \*)

- **Demographics and burden**  
  - Nigeria has a very large, young population (~220M), significant oil revenues, and high inequality.  
  - It faces major burdens from **malaria, maternal/child health issues, NCDs, and insecurity**, with large regional differences (north vs south, rural vs urban).

- **Engine’s package story**  
  - High-commitment packages (Angels-lite, Tax & Match, Corporate & Billionaire Pledges) generate very large resource flows and DALY gains in the engine due to Nigeria’s size and low baseline health outcomes.  
  - Volunteer & Talent Surge is also potent, reflecting Nigeria’s growing base of educated professionals.

- **Equity and geography**  
  - Under current equity splits, most gains from health and cash-transfer components go to the **domestic poor**, especially in:
    - Rural northern states.  
    - Poor urban neighborhoods in megacities like Lagos.  
  - Education and climate components benefit **future generations** via improved human capital and resilience (e.g., to desertification and flooding).

- **Channels and constraints**  
  - Realistic implementation requires:
    - Improved governance and targeting of **public revenues**, including oil rents.  
    - Strong roles for **faith-based and NGO providers** where state delivery is weaker.  
    - Expanding **Nigerian corporate and diaspora philanthropy**, focusing on proven interventions rather than fragmented projects.

- **Next refinement steps**  
  - Use Nigeria-specific cost-effectiveness estimates (malaria control, maternal health, education programs) to refine cause parameters.  
  - Incorporate data on Nigeria’s actual social spending, philanthropy, and remittances into package parameter choices.
