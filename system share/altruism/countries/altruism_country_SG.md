## Altruism Country Deep Dive – Singapore (SG)

> **Status**: First-pass scaffold with approximate ranges (**\***). To be refined with Singapore budget, philanthropy, and financial centre data.

### 1. Macro Context

- Population: ~5.9M\*
- GDP (nominal): ~USD 400–450B/year\*
- GDP per capita: ~USD 70,000–85,000/year\*
- High-income city-state, global financial centre and trade hub.

### 2. Government Social Spending and Fiscal Flows

Singapore combines:

- Relatively lean traditional welfare state with:
  - Heavy use of mandatory savings (CPF – Central Provident Fund).
  - Asset-based welfare (homeownership).

Approximate public social spending\*:

- Social spending (health, education, targeted transfers): ~14–16% of GDP\*.
  - Health: ~4% of GDP (with copays and Medisave/Medishield elements).
  - Education: ~3–4% of GDP.
  - Social transfers and targeted schemes: ~2–4% of GDP\*.

### 3. International Aid and Philanthropy Role

Official aid:

- Singapore provides some development assistance and technical cooperation in ASEAN and beyond.
- ODA-like flows as % of GNI: modest (<0.1%)\*.

However, as a **wealth and financial hub**, Singapore hosts:

- Wealth management for high-net-worth individuals from across Asia and globally.
- Family offices and foundations that may target global philanthropy.

### 4. Domestic and International Private Philanthropy

Formal giving:

- Estimates: private giving ~0.7–1.0% of GDP\*.
- Significant foundation and corporate giving, particularly in:
  - Education and scholarships.
  - Health and hospitals.
  - Arts and culture.

Wealth and family offices:

- Large concentrations of regional and global wealth.
- Potential to structure **Triad-linked funds** that allocate part of managed assets to CESET-optimized charities globally.

### 5. Volunteering and Informal Altruism

Singapore has:

- Strong civil society in:
  - Community development councils.
  - Religious organizations (churches, mosques, temples).
  - Voluntary Welfare Organizations (VWOs), now SSAs.

Volunteering:

- Encouraged by state and corporate policies, though time-pressured working culture may limit rates.

### 6. Cause-Area Context

Domestic:

- Leading causes of death:
  - NCDs: cancers, cardiovascular, diabetes-related issues.
  - Very low child mortality and infectious disease burden.

International:

- Singapore-based philanthropy and investment can impact:
  - Health and education in ASEAN neighbours.
  - Climate and environmental projects in the region.
  - Global CESET reduction via internationally oriented foundations.

### 7. Cluster Scenarios

Clusters:

- High-net-worth individuals and family offices based in Singapore (local + foreign).

If:

- 10,000 HNW individuals\* with average net wealth of USD 50M each committed 1% of wealth/year:
  - 10,000 × 0.01 × 50M = **USD 5B/year**.

If scaled to 2–3% per year:

- Flows could reach **USD 10–15B/year**, targeted at:
  - Global health and poverty.
  - Animal welfare.
  - Climate/energy.

### 8. NSS/Triad Considerations

Singapore is promising for:

- **Financial Hub Triads**:
  - Creating vehicles that automatically route a fixed % of assets or returns into CESET-focused work.
- **Regional Altruism Triads**:
  - Coordinating philanthropy and impact investing across ASEAN.
