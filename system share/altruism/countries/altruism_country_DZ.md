## Altruism Country Deep Dive – Algeria (DZ)

> **Status**: First-pass scaffold with approximate ranges (**\***). To be refined with Algerian budget, aid, and philanthropy data.

### 1. Macro Context

- Population: ~45M\*
- GDP (nominal): ~USD 190–210B/year\*
- GDP per capita: ~USD 4,000–4,600/year\*
- Upper-middle-income hydrocarbon-rich North African republic with large state sector.

### 2. Government Social Spending and Fiscal Flows

Approximate:

- Total government expenditure: ~40–45% of GDP\*.
- Social spending (health, education, social protection): ~18–22% of GDP\*.
  - Health: ~6–7% of GDP\*.
  - Education: ~5–6% of GDP\*.
  - Social protection and subsidies: ~7–9% of GDP\* (fuel and food subsidies, transfers).

### 3. ODA – Limited Donor and Recipient

- Algeria:
  - Receives modest ODA.
  - Provides some support to neighbours (e.g. Sahrawi refugees).
- ODA given as % of GNI: very small (<0.05%)\*.

### 4. Private Philanthropy and Religious Giving

Formal:

- Some foundations and charity organizations.

Informal and religious:

- Zakat and sadaqah are significant; much giving is informal or via mosques.

### 5. Cause-Area Context

Domestic:

- Leading causes of death:
  - NCDs: CVD, cancers, diabetes, chronic respiratory diseases.
- Child mortality:
  - Under‑5 mortality ~20–25/1,000\*; causes dominated by neonatal issues and remaining infections.

International:

- Algeria-origin altruism could impact:
  - Neighbouring Sahel and Maghreb states via targeted support.

### 6. Cluster Scenarios

Clusters:

- Middle and high-income Algerians.
- Algerian diaspora (France, Europe).

If:

- 500,000 diaspora and elite Algerians\* give an extra EUR/USD 400/year:
  - ~USD 200M/year additional flows.

Targeted to:

- Youth employment.
- Health promotion (diabetes, CVD prevention).
- Support to high-burden neighbours.

### 7. NSS/Triad Considerations

Potential Triads:

- **Maghreb NCD and Youth Triad**:
  - Addressing emerging NCD crisis and youth unemployment.
- **Algeria–Sahel Solidarity Triad**:
  - Channeling surplus hydrocarbon rents into Sahel CESET relief.
