## Altruism Country Deep Dive – Peru (PE)

> **Status**: First-pass scaffold with approximate ranges (**\***).

### 1. Macro Context

- Population: ~34M\*
- GDP (nominal): ~USD 250–280B/year\*
- GDP per capita: ~USD 7,000–8,000/year\*.
- Upper-middle-income Andean country with major mining sector and strong regional inequalities.

### 2. Government Social Spending and Fiscal Flows

Approximate:

- Total government expenditure: ~23–27% of GDP\*.
- Social spending (health, education, social programmes): ~12–15% of GDP\*.
  - Health: ~5–6% of GDP\*.
  - Education: ~4–5% of GDP\*.
  - Social programmes: ~3–4% of GDP\*.

### 3. Official Development Assistance (ODA)

- Peru is a **recipient** of ODA:
  - Health.
  - Rural development.
  - Climate and environment (Amazon basin).
- ODA given as % GNI: small (<0.1%)\*.

### 4. Private Philanthropy and Informal Altruism

Formal:

- Corporate CSR in mining, energy, and utilities.

Informal:

- Family and community support.
- Indigenous community structures in Andean and Amazonian regions.

### 5. Cause-Area Context

Domestic:

- Leading causes:
  - CVD.
  - Cancers.
  - Diabetes.
- Child mortality:
  - Under‑5 mortality ~14–17/1,000\*, higher in rural/indigenous populations.

Environmental and climate issues:

- Glacier retreat, water stress.
- Amazon deforestation.

### 6. Cluster Scenarios & Triads

Clusters:

- Mining and extractive industry elites.
- Peruvian diaspora.

Triads:

- **Andean Health & Nutrition Triad**:
  - Targeting highland malnutrition and child mortality.
- **Amazon Conservation and Community Triad**.
