## Altruism Country Deep Dive – Eswatini (SZ)

> **Status**: First-pass scaffold with approximate ranges (**\***). Formerly Swaziland.

### 1. Macro Context

- Population: ~1.2M\*
- GDP (nominal): ~USD 5–6B/year\*
- GDP per capita: ~USD 4,000–5,000/year\*
- Small landlocked monarchy bordered by South Africa and Mozambique, with high HIV prevalence and SACU revenue dependence.

### 2. Government Social Spending and Fiscal Flows

Approximate:

- Total public expenditure: ~35–40% of GDP\* (boosted by SACU customs receipts).
- Social spending (health, education, social protection): ~18–20% of GDP\*.
  - Health: ~7–8% of GDP\*.
  - Education: ~7–8% of GDP\*.
  - Social protection: ~3–4% of GDP\*.

### 3. ODA and External Finance

- Eswatini receives:
  - ODA for health (especially HIV, TB) and governance.

### 4. Private Philanthropy and Informal Altruism

Informal:

- Traditional structures and extended family support.
- Church-based giving.

Formal:

- Local and international NGOs focusing on HIV, child health, and poverty.

### 5. Cause-Area Context

Eswatini’s CESET profile:

- Under‑5 mortality: ~45–55 per 1,000\*.
  - Neonatal disorders.
  - Pneumonia and diarrhea.
- Highest or near-highest adult HIV prevalence globally:
  - Historically massive AIDS mortality; improved with ART but still major CESET driver.

### 6. Cluster Scenarios & Triads

Clusters:

- SACU revenue pool.
- Global HIV donors.

Triads:

- **Eswatini HIV & Child Survival Triad**:
  - Aim for a “post-HIV” demonstration where a very high prevalence country attains near-normal life expectancy with few child deaths.
