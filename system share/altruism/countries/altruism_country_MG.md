## Altruism Country Deep Dive – Madagascar (MG)

> **Status**: First-pass scaffold with approximate ranges (**\***).

### 1. Macro Context

- Population: ~29M\*
- GDP (nominal): ~USD 14–16B/year\*
- GDP per capita: ~USD 450–550/year\*
- Large island state off Southeast Africa, extremely biodiverse, low income, and vulnerable to cyclones and climate change.

### 2. Government Social Spending and Fiscal Flows

Approximate:

- Total public expenditure: ~20–25% of GDP\*.
- Social spending (health, education, social protection): ~9–11% of GDP\*.
  - Health: ~2.5–3.5% of GDP\*.
  - Education: ~3–4% of GDP\*.
  - Social protection: ~1–2% of GDP\*.

### 3. ODA – Major Recipient

- ODA inflows: ~USD 0.8–1.2B/year\*.
- Focus:
  - Child and maternal health, malaria.
  - Nutrition and food security.
  - Environmental and conservation programmes.
  - Cyclone recovery and resilience.

### 4. Private Philanthropy and Informal Altruism

Formal:

- Numerous international and local NGOs, including major conservation organisations.

Informal:

- Family and community networks.

### 5. Cause-Area Context

Madagascar’s CESET profile:

- Under‑5 mortality: ~50–70 per 1,000\*.
  - Neonatal disorders.
  - Pneumonia, diarrhea.
  - Severe malnutrition in some regions.
- High rates of chronic child stunting.
- Cyclones and droughts cause periodic crises.

### 6. Cluster Scenarios & Triads

Clusters:

- Global conservation donors.
- Climate and adaptation funds.

Triads:

- **Madagascar Child & Nutrition Triad**:
  - Combine high-impact nutrition and health interventions with conservation-compatible livelihoods.
- **Blue & Green Biodiversity Triad**:
  - Tie global biodiversity funding explicitly to CESET reduction for local populations.
