### 4. Interpretive Notes for Uganda (Nilotic / Lake Victoria Region \*)

- **High burden and young population**  
  - Uganda has a very young population and continues to face significant burdens from **malaria, HIV, TB, and undernutrition**, along with education and employment challenges for youth.  
  - This makes **global health and education** causes particularly important in any altruism scenario.

- **Implications of the package engine**  
  - Packages like **Angels-lite** and **Tax & Match**, when interpreted as a mix of:
    - Domestic high-commitment giving and  
    - Focused international support,  
  - would channel large resources into Uganda’s highly cost-effective interventions (e.g., malaria prevention, deworming, basic health, targeted support for schooling).

- **Equity and regional focus**  
  - Under the engine’s equity splits, most of the gains from health and cash-transfer components in Uganda flow to **domestic poor** and, in the case of education, to **future_global** via long-run benefits.  
  - This aligns with Uganda’s reality, where rural and peri-urban communities around Lake Victoria and in the north carry disproportionate burdens.

- **Channels of altruism**  
  - Uganda sees substantial **ODA and NGO activity**, especially in health and education, alongside church-based and community giving.  
  - The most realistic instantiation of high-impact packages is thus a blend of:
    - Strengthened **public systems**,  
    - **NGO-led health and education programs**, and  
    - Encouraged **local and diaspora giving** tied to effective projects.

- **Next refinement steps**  
  - Incorporate Uganda-specific cost-effectiveness estimates from studies on malaria control, HIV treatment, and education programs.  
  - Reflect Uganda’s actual ODA and remittance flows in the package parameterization.
