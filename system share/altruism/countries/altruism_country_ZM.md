## Altruism Country Deep Dive – Zambia (ZM)

> **Status**: First-pass scaffold with approximate ranges (**\***).

### 1. Macro Context

- Population: ~20M\*
- GDP (nominal): ~USD 30–35B/year\*
- GDP per capita: ~USD 1,500–1,800/year\*
- Landlocked Southern African country with major copper mining sector and high rural poverty.

### 2. Government Social Spending and Fiscal Flows

Approximate:

- Total public expenditure: ~26–30% of GDP\*.
- Social spending (health, education, social protection): ~12–14% of GDP\*.
  - Health: ~4–5% of GDP\*.
  - Education: ~5–6% of GDP\*.
  - Social protection: ~2–3% of GDP\*.

### 3. ODA – Major Recipient

- ODA inflows: ~USD 1–1.5B/year\*.
- Focus:
  - HIV/AIDS and TB.
  - Malaria and child/maternal health.
  - Education and agriculture.

### 4. Private Philanthropy and Informal Altruism

Formal:

- NGOs and church-based organizations.

Informal:

- Extended-family support obligations.

### 5. Cause-Area Context

Zambia’s CESET profile:

- Under‑5 mortality: ~55–65 per 1,000\*.
  - Neonatal disorders.
  - Malaria.
  - Lower respiratory infections and diarrhea.
- HIV/AIDS: significant adult burden, though ART coverage improved.

### 6. Cluster Scenarios & Triads

Clusters:

- Mining companies and global copper value chains.

Triads:

- **Copper-for-Child-Survival Triad**:
  - Small levies or CSR flows on copper revenue into CESET-optimized health and nutrition.
