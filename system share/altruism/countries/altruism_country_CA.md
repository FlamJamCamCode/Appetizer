## Altruism Country Deep Dive – Canada (CA)

> **Status**: First-pass scaffold with approximate ranges (**\***). To be refined with Canadian budget, philanthropy, and health data.

### 1. Macro Context

- Population: ~40M\*
- GDP (nominal): ~USD 2.1–2.3T/year\*
- GDP per capita: ~USD 52,000–58,000/year\*
- High-income, resource-rich federation with strong public health system and multicultural population.

### 2. Government Social Spending and Fiscal Flows

Approximate:

- Total social spending: ~19–21% of GDP\*.
  - Health: ~7–8% of GDP\* (universal healthcare).
  - Pensions and old-age benefits: ~6–7% of GDP\*.
  - Other social programmes (employment insurance, family benefits, social assistance): ~4–6% of GDP\*.

Other:

- Education: ~5–6% of GDP.
- Defense: ~1.3–1.5% of GDP.

### 3. Official Development Assistance (ODA)

- ODA/GNI: ~0.33%\* (short of the 0.7% target but above US as % of GNI).
- Annual ODA volume: ~USD 7–8B/year\*.

Focus areas:

- Global health and development.
- Gender equality and inclusive growth.
- Humanitarian assistance.
- Climate change and environment.

### 4. Private Philanthropy

Canada:

- Private giving ~1.0–1.2% of GDP\*.
- Total donations ~CAD 20–30B/year\*.

Features:

- Charitable tax credit system.
- Strong role for:
  - Hospitals and universities.
  - Religious organizations.
  - Community and social-service charities.

### 5. Volunteering and Informal Altruism

- High volunteering rates:
  - Many Canadians contribute time to charities, sports, culture, and community organizations.

Informal altruism:

- Family and community support.
- Support to indigenous communities (both formal and informal).

### 6. Cause-Area Context

Domestic:

- Leading causes of death:
  - CVD.
  - Cancers.
  - Respiratory diseases.
  - Injury and mental health issues (including overdoses) in some regions.

International:

- Through ODA and private philanthropy, Canada supports:
  - Global health.
  - Poverty reduction.
  - Peace and security.

### 7. Cluster Scenarios

Clusters:

- High-income urban professionals (Toronto, Vancouver, Montreal, Calgary).
- Retirees with significant pension and asset income.

If:

- 1M high-income Canadians\* committed to donate an extra 5–10% of income:
  - Could generate additional **CAD 20–40B/year** for CESET-focused work.

### 8. NSS/Triad Considerations

Canada is well placed for:

- **Global Health and Development Triads**.
- **Climate and Arctic Triads**:
  - Addressing climate change and indigenous community resilience.
- **Migration and Refugee Triads**:
  - Leveraging Canada’s tradition of refugee resettlement.
