## Altruism Country Deep Dive – Eritrea (ER)

> **Status**: First-pass scaffold with approximate ranges (**\***). Intended for refinement as better data is integrated.

### 1. Macro Context

- Population: ~3.7M\*
- GDP (nominal): ~USD 2–3B/year\*.
- GDP per capita: ~USD 500–800/year\*.
- Small, militarized state on the Red Sea with a history of war with Ethiopia, severe political repression, and large diaspora.

### 2. Government Social Spending and Fiscal Flows

Data quality is poor due to limited transparency:

- Total public spending: ~25–30% of GDP\*.
- Social spending (health, education, limited social protection): perhaps ~10–12% of GDP\*, with:
  - Health: ~3–4% of GDP\*.
  - Education: ~4–5% of GDP\*.
  - Other social programmes: small.

Many services are delivered through:

- Highly centralized state structures.
- Party- and military-affiliated entities.

### 3. ODA and External Flows

- Eritrea:
  - Historically received ODA, then at times restricted or expelled UN/NGOs.
  - Current ODA flows are limited but exist via selective channels (UN, a few bilaterals).
- Large diaspora remittances constitute a key income source.

### 4. Private Altruism and Remittances

Informal:

- Strong diaspora support:
  - Remittances to families.
  - “2% diaspora tax” reportedly levied by the government on diaspora income in some jurisdictions\*.

Religious/community giving:

- Christian (Orthodox, Catholic, Protestant) and Muslim communities provide support.

### 5. Cause-Area Context

Eritrea’s CESET profile (using regional proxies and sparse data):

- Under‑5 mortality: ~40–50 per 1,000\* (improved from very high levels).
  - Neonatal disorders.
  - Pneumonia and diarrhea.
  - Malnutrition.
- Maternal mortality: high.
- Adult mortality:
  - Communicable diseases (TB, HIV—lower than some neighbours).
  - NCDs rising.
  - Effects of chronic undernutrition and harsh labour conditions.

### 6. Cluster Scenarios & Triads

Clusters:

- Eritrean diaspora in Europe, North America, Middle East.

Triads:

- **Eritrean Diaspora Protection & Health Triad**:
  - Helping diaspora route support into health and nutrition in ways that are:
    - CESET-efficient.
    - As politically safe as possible.
- **Red Sea Stability Triad** (with Djibouti, Yemen, Ethiopia/Sudan):
  - Maritime security, refugee flows, and food import stability.
