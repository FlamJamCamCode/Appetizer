## Altruism Country Deep Dive – United Kingdom (UK)

> **Status**: First-pass scaffold with approximate ranges (**\***). To be refined with UK budget, ONS, and philanthropy data.

### 1. Macro Context

- Population: ~67M\*
- GDP: ~USD 3.1–3.3T/year\*
- GDP per capita: ~USD 45,000–50,000/year\*
- High-income European country with historically strong welfare state and philanthropic culture.

### 2. Government Social Spending and Fiscal Flows

Approximate social expenditure (all government levels)\*:

- Total social spending: ~22–24% of GDP.
  - Public pensions: ~7–8% of GDP\*.
  - Health (NHS): ~9–10% of GDP\*.
  - Other social protection (unemployment, disability, family benefits, housing): ~5–6% of GDP\*.

Other:

- Education: ~4–5% of GDP.
- Defense: ~2% of GDP (NATO commitments).

### 3. Official Development Assistance (ODA)

- Historically:
  - UK met the **0.7% of GNI** target for many years.
- Recent cuts:
  - Reduced to around **0.5% of GNI**\*.
- ODA volume:
  - ~USD 15–20B/year depending on exchange rates and cuts\*.

Key channels:

- FCDO (Foreign, Commonwealth & Development Office).
- Multilateral contributions (UN, World Bank, etc.).

### 4. Private Philanthropy

UK has:

- Private giving ~0.8–1.0% of GDP\*.
- Total donations ≈ GBP 10–15B/year\* (individuals, trusts/foundations, corporates).

Characteristics:

- Large charity sector with hundreds of thousands of registered charities.
- Strong legacy-giving tradition.
- Prominent foundations (Wellcome Trust, etc.).

### 5. Volunteering and Informal Altruism

- High rates of:
  - Volunteering (sports, community, faith groups).
  - Informal caring (elderly, disabled family members).

CAF and national surveys:

- In England:
  - ~61% of men and ~72% of women reported donating to charity in a given year\*.

### 6. Cause-Area Context

Within the UK:

- Leading causes of death:
  - Cardiovascular diseases.
  - Cancers.
  - Dementia/Alzheimer’s.
  - Chronic respiratory diseases.
- Very low child mortality and infectious disease burdens.

Internationally:

- UK-origin ODA and private philanthropy significantly support:
  - Global health (vaccines, HIV, malaria, TB).
  - Humanitarian responses.
  - Education, governance, climate.

### 7. Cluster Scenarios

Potential clusters:

- High-income professionals in London and other major cities.
- Wealthy retirees in UK and abroad.

Examples:

- If 1M high-income individuals (\*top few percent) gave an extra 5–10% of income:
  - Additional flows of **GBP 20–40B/year** could be generated for CESET-optimized interventions.

### 8. NSS/Triad Considerations

UK is:

- A historic and current **“top altruist state”** via its ODA and philanthropic infrastructure.
- Well-positioned to host:
  - **Global Health Triads** (aligned with UK research strengths).
  - **Climate and Development Triads**.
  - **Effective Altruism-inspired Triads** (EA community has strong presence).
