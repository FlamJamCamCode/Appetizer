## Altruism Country Deep Dive – Bangladesh (BD)

> **Status**: First-pass scaffold with approximate ranges (**\***). To be refined with Bangladeshi budget, aid, and health data.

### 1. Macro Context

- Population: ~170M\*
- GDP (nominal): ~USD 420–450B/year\*
- GDP per capita: ~USD 2,400–2,700/year\*
- Lower-middle-income, densely populated country with rapid development and large garment export sector.

### 2. Government Social Spending and Fiscal Flows

Approximate:

- Total government expenditure: ~15–18% of GDP\*.
- Social spending (health, education, social protection): ~8–10% of GDP\*.
  - Health: ~2–3% of GDP\*.
  - Education: ~2–3% of GDP\*.
  - Social protection: ~2–3% of GDP\*.

### 3. ODA – Recipient

- Bangladesh is a significant ODA recipient:
  - Multilaterals and bilaterals funding:
    - Health (especially child/maternal).
    - Education.
    - Infrastructure and climate adaptation.
    - Support for Rohingya refugees.
- ODA inflows: several billion USD/year\*.

### 4. Private Philanthropy and Informal Altruism

Formal:

- Increasing domestic philanthropy and CSR.
- Presence of large NGOs (e.g. BRAC, Grameen) that are both recipients and implementers of altruistic flows.

Religious giving:

- Zakat and other Islamic charity flows are substantial.

Informal:

- Family support and rural-urban remittances.

### 5. Cause-Area Context

Bangladesh has achieved:

- **Major reductions in child mortality** over the last decades.
- Yet:
  - Under‑5 mortality remains above rich-country levels.
  - Key causes: neonatal disorders, pneumonia, diarrhea, malnutrition.

Other burdens:

- Maternal mortality.
- Infectious diseases (TB, dengue).
- NCDs (CVD, diabetes, especially in urban populations).
- Climate vulnerability:
  - Cyclones, floods, sea-level rise affecting low-lying areas.

### 6. Cluster Scenarios

Clusters:

- Domestic middle and upper income groups.
- Bangladeshi diaspora (Gulf, UK, North America).

If:

- 3M diaspora Bangladeshis\* contribute an extra USD 300/year each to CESET-optimized channels:
  - ~USD 0.9B/year.

If:

- Domestic zakat flows (~billions of USD/year potential\*) are partially re-routed to:
  - Child survival.
  - Maternal health.
  - Climate-resilient livelihoods.

Then:

- Bangladesh-origin altruism could substantially accelerate:
  - Ending preventable child and maternal deaths.
  - Building climate resilience.

### 7. NSS/Triad Considerations

Potential Triads:

- **Child and Maternal Survival Triad**:
  - Leveraging Bangladesh’s proven NGO capacity (BRAC, etc.) at scale.
- **Climate and Livelihoods Triad**:
  - Targeting coastal and riverine communities.
- **Zakat and Microfinance Triad**:
  - Combining religious giving with evidence-based poverty relief instruments.
