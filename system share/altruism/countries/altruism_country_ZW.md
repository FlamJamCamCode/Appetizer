## Altruism Country Deep Dive – Zimbabwe (ZW)

> **Status**: First-pass scaffold with approximate ranges (**\***).

### 1. Macro Context

- Population: ~15M\*
- GDP (nominal): very uncertain; rough range ~USD 20–25B/year\*.
- GDP per capita: ~USD 1,300–1,700/year\*.
- Landlocked Southern African country with history of hyperinflation, political repression, and high education levels relative to income.

### 2. Government Social Spending and Fiscal Flows

Approximate:

- Total public expenditure: ~25–30% of GDP\* (but highly volatile).
- Social spending (health, education, social protection): ~12–14% of GDP\*.
  - Health: ~4–5% of GDP\*.
  - Education: ~5–6% of GDP\*.
  - Social protection: ~2–3% of GDP\*.

### 3. ODA – Significant Recipient

- ODA inflows: hundreds of millions USD/year\*.
- Focus:
  - Health (HIV, TB, malaria).
  - Food security.
  - Governance and human rights.

### 4. Private Philanthropy and Informal Altruism

Formal:

- NGOs, churches, and diaspora-led charities.

Informal:

- Extensive family remittances from Zimbabweans abroad (South Africa, UK, etc.).

### 5. Cause-Area Context

Zimbabwe’s CESET profile:

- Under‑5 mortality: ~55–65 per 1,000\*.
  - Neonatal disorders.
  - HIV/PMTCT issues historically.
  - Malaria and infections.
- HIV prevalence high but improved with ART.

### 6. Cluster Scenarios & Triads

Clusters:

- Zimbabwean diaspora.
- Agriculture and resource sector producers.

Triads:

- **Zimbabwe Health & Education Restitution Triad**:
  - Aiming to restore human capital eroded by economic collapse.
