## Altruism Country Deep Dive – Sierra Leone (SL)

> **Status**: First-pass scaffold with approximate ranges (**\***).

### 1. Macro Context

- Population: ~8.5M\*
- GDP (nominal): ~USD 4–5B/year\*
- GDP per capita: ~USD 500–600/year\*
- Low-income West African coastal state with post-conflict legacy and Ebola outbreak history.

### 2. Government Social Spending and Fiscal Flows

Approximate:

- Total government expenditure: ~25–30% of GDP\* (ODA-supported).
- Social spending (health, education, social protection): ~12–14% of GDP\*.
  - Health: ~4–5% of GDP\*.
  - Education: ~4–5% of GDP\*.
  - Social protection: ~2–3% of GDP\*.

### 3. ODA – Heavy Recipient

- ODA inflows: several hundred million USD/year\*.
- Focus:
  - Health (Ebola recovery, maternal and child health).
  - Education.
  - Governance and infrastructure.

### 4. Private Philanthropy and Informal Altruism

Formal:

- Local and international NGOs present.

Informal:

- Family, community, and religious mutual aid.
- Diaspora remittances important for household survival.

### 5. Cause-Area Context

Burden:

- Under‑5 mortality: ~100+ per 1,000\* (among the highest).
  - Neonatal disorders.
  - Malaria.
  - Pneumonia and diarrhea.
- Maternal mortality extremely high.
- Infectious diseases (Ebola history, Lassa, etc.).

### 6. Cluster Scenarios & Triads

Clusters:

- Sierra Leone diaspora.
- High-burden global health donors.

Triads:

- **West Africa Child & Maternal Survival Triad**:
  - Sierra Leone as a priority node.
