## Altruism Country Deep Dive – Pakistan (PK)

> **Status**: First-pass scaffold with approximate ranges (**\***). To be refined with Pakistani budget, aid, and philanthropy data.

### 1. Macro Context

- Population: ~240M\*
- GDP (nominal): ~USD 350–400B/year\*
- GDP per capita: ~USD 1,400–1,700/year\*
- Lower-middle-income, nuclear-armed state with large rural population, political volatility, and strong Islamic norms.

### 2. Government Social Spending and Fiscal Flows

Approximate:

- Total government expenditure: ~20–23% of GDP\*.
- Social spending (health, education, social protection): ~7–9% of GDP\*.
  - Health: ~1–2% of GDP\* (very low).
  - Education: ~2–3% of GDP\*.
  - Social protection (Benazir Income Support, Ehsaas etc.): ~2–3% of GDP\*.

High fiscal pressure:

- Debt service.
- Defense expenditures.

### 3. ODA – Recipient

- Pakistan receives substantial ODA and concessional finance:
  - From multilaterals (World Bank, IMF) and bilaterals.
  - From China (BRI, CPEC).
- ODA inflows: a few billion USD/year\*.

### 4. Private Philanthropy and Zakat

Pakistan is often cited as:

- One of the **most charitable nations** by share of population that gives.

Zakat and Sadaqah:

- Large-scale religious giving:
  - Some estimates: zakat flows of several billion USD/year\* domestically.
  - Significant philanthropic institutions (e.g. Edhi Foundation, Shaukat Khanum, etc.).

### 5. Volunteering and Informal Altruism

- Strong culture of:
  - Helping neighbours.
  - Community relief in disasters (floods, earthquakes).

CAF and other surveys:

- Very high rates of donating and helping strangers compared to many countries.

### 6. Cause-Area Context

Within Pakistan:

- Significant under‑5 mortality:
  - Neonatal disorders.
  - Diarrhea and pneumonia.
  - Malnutrition.
- Large burden of:
  - Maternal mortality.
  - Infectious diseases (TB, hepatitis).
  - NCDs (CVD, diabetes).
- Recurrent disasters (floods, earthquakes) cause spikes in mortality and economic damage.

### 7. Cluster Scenarios

Clusters:

- High-income Pakistanis domestically.
- Overseas Pakistani diaspora (UK, Gulf, North America).

If:

- 5M diaspora Pakistanis\* gave an incremental USD 500/year each into highly effective CESET-focused channels:
  - USD 2.5B/year additional flows.

If:

- Domestic zakat flows are partially reoriented toward:
  - Evidence-based health, nutrition, and education interventions.

Then:

- Pakistan-origin altruism could:
  - Dramatically cut child and maternal mortality.
  - Strengthen health and education systems.

### 8. NSS/Triad Considerations

Potential Triads:

- **Zakat-Optimized Health and Education Triads**:
  - Aligning religious giving with highest CESET reduction.
- **Disaster and Climate Resilience Triads**:
  - Flood-resistant infrastructure, early warning systems.
