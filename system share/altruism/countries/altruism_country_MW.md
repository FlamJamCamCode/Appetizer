## Altruism Country Deep Dive – Malawi (MW)

> **Status**: First-pass scaffold with approximate ranges (**\***).

### 1. Macro Context

- Population: ~20M\*
- GDP (nominal): ~USD 13–15B/year\*
- GDP per capita: ~USD 650–750/year\*
- Landlocked low-income country in Southern/East Africa with high rural population, agriculture dependence, and frequent climate shocks.

### 2. Government Social Spending and Fiscal Flows

Approximate:

- Total public expenditure: ~26–30% of GDP\* (heavy ODA component).
- Social spending (health, education, social protection): ~13–15% of GDP\*.
  - Health: ~4–5% of GDP\*.
  - Education: ~5–6% of GDP\*.
  - Social protection: ~2–3% of GDP\*.

### 3. ODA – Heavy Recipient

- ODA inflows: ~USD 1–1.5B/year\*.
- Focus:
  - HIV/AIDS, malaria, TB.
  - Maternal and child health, nutrition.
  - Education and agriculture.

### 4. Private Philanthropy and Informal Altruism

Formal:

- NGOs and faith-based organizations central to service delivery.

Informal:

- Strong family and village-level support systems.
- Church support networks.

### 5. Cause-Area Context

Malawi’s CESET profile:

- Under‑5 mortality: ~55–70 per 1,000\*.
  - Neonatal disorders.
  - Malaria.
  - Diarrheal diseases and pneumonia.
- HIV/AIDS burden historically high.
- Recurrent food insecurity due to droughts/floods.

### 6. Cluster Scenarios & Triads

Clusters:

- Malawi-focused global health donors.

Triads:

- **Malawi Child Health & Nutrition Triad**.
- **Climate & Agriculture Triad**:
  - Drought-resistant crops and input support.
