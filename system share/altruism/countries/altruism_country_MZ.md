## Altruism Country Deep Dive – Mozambique (MZ)

> **Status**: First-pass scaffold with approximate ranges (**\***).

### 1. Macro Context

- Population: ~33M\*
- GDP (nominal): ~USD 18–20B/year\*
- GDP per capita: ~USD 550–650/year\*
- Low-income, coastal Southern African country with large rural population, gas discoveries, and history of civil war and cyclones.

### 2. Government Social Spending and Fiscal Flows

Approximate:

- Total public expenditure: ~25–30% of GDP\* (ODA-supported).
- Social spending (health, education, social protection): ~12–14% of GDP\*.
  - Health: ~4–5% of GDP\*.
  - Education: ~4–5% of GDP\*.
  - Social protection: ~2–3% of GDP\*.

### 3. ODA – Major Recipient

- ODA inflows: several hundred million to ~USD 1–2B/year\*.
- Focus sectors:
  - HIV/AIDS, malaria, TB, maternal and child health.
  - Education, agriculture.
  - Cyclone/flood resilience and humanitarian response.

### 4. Private Philanthropy and Informal Altruism

Formal:

- Local NGOs and faith-based organizations.

Informal:

- Family and community mutual aid in rural areas.
- Religious giving (Christian, Muslim).

### 5. Cause-Area Context

Mozambique’s CESET profile:

- Under‑5 mortality: ~70–80 per 1,000\*.
  - Neonatal disorders.
  - Malaria.
  - Lower respiratory infections and diarrhea.
- High HIV prevalence in adults.
- Cyclones and floods causing acute crises (e.g. Idai, Kenneth).

### 6. Cluster Scenarios & Triads

Clusters:

- Mozambique-focused global health donors.
- Natural gas investors (CSR potential).

Triads:

- **Mozambique Child Survival & Malaria Triad**.
- **Cyclone Belt Resilience Triad**:
  - Linking Mozambique with Malawi, Madagascar, and other cyclone-prone states.
