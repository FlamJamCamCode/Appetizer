## Altruism Country Deep Dive – Mali (ML)

> **Status**: First-pass scaffold with approximate ranges (**\***). To be refined with Malian budget, aid, and health data.

### 1. Macro Context

- Population: ~22M\*
- GDP (nominal): ~USD 18–22B/year\*
- GDP per capita: ~USD 800–1,000/year\*
- Low-income, landlocked Sahel country with significant security challenges and rural poverty.

### 2. Government Social Spending and Fiscal Flows

Approximate:

- Total government expenditure: ~20–25% of GDP\*.
- Social spending (health, education, social protection): ~8–10% of GDP\*.
  - Health: ~3–4% of GDP\*.
  - Education: ~4–5% of GDP\*.
  - Social protection: limited coverage.

### 3. ODA – Heavy Recipient

- Mali is heavily dependent on ODA:
  - ODA inflows often **>8–10% of GNI**\*.
  - Major donors: EU, France, multilateral agencies.

Aid targets:

- Health and nutrition.
- Education.
- Governance and security sector reform.
- Rural development.

### 4. Private Philanthropy and Informal Altruism

Formal philanthropy:

- Minimal domestic philanthropic infrastructure.

Informal:

- Village-level solidarity and extended family networks.
- Islamic giving (zakat, sadaqah).

### 5. Cause-Area Context

High burdens in:

- Under‑5 mortality:
  - Neonatal disorders.
  - Malaria.
  - Diarrhea and pneumonia.
  - Malnutrition.
- Maternal mortality.
- Adult infectious diseases.

Violence:

- Conflict and insecurity in various regions contribute to:
  - Direct deaths.
  - Indirect deaths via displacement, food insecurity, and disrupted services.

### 6. Cluster Scenarios

External clusters:

- Sahel-focused donors and philanthropists.
- Malian diaspora.

Scenario:

- A dedicated **Sahel Triad** channeling ~USD 2–3B/year across Niger, Mali, Chad, Burkina Faso:
  - Could significantly:
    - Reduce under‑5 deaths.
    - Stabilize food systems.
    - Build basic resilience.

### 7. NSS/Triad Considerations

Mali fits into:

- **Sahel Child Survival Triad**:
  - Focused on integrated health and nutrition.
- **Sahel Stability Triad**:
  - Combining community-level conflict resolution, livelihoods, and basic services.
