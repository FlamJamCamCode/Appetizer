## Altruism Country Deep Dive – South Africa (ZA)

> **Status**: First-pass scaffold with approximate ranges (**\***). To be refined with South African budget, philanthropy, and health data.

### 1. Macro Context

- Population: ~60M\*
- GDP (nominal): ~USD 400–450B/year\*
- GDP per capita: ~USD 6,500–7,500/year\*
- Upper-middle-income country with extreme inequality and dual economy.

### 2. Government Social Spending and Fiscal Flows

Approximate social expenditure\*:

- Total social spending: ~15–18% of GDP.
  - Social grants (pensions, child support, disability): ~3–4% of GDP\*.
  - Health: ~4–5% of GDP\*.
  - Education: ~6–7% of GDP\*.

Other:

- Public debt service and SOE support are significant fiscal pressures.

### 3. ODA – Donor and Recipient

- Mainly a **recipient** of ODA:
  - HIV/AIDS, TB, and health programmes.
  - Governance, education, and development projects.
- Some limited regional aid given to neighbours.

### 4. Private Philanthropy and Informal Altruism

Formal:

- Corporate social investment (CSI) from big companies.
- Foundations tied to major business families.

Informal:

- Strong mutual aid within communities.
- Church and faith-based giving.
- Remittances within Southern Africa.

Estimates of total formal giving: ~0.2–0.4% of GDP\*.

### 5. Cause-Area Context

Within South Africa:

- High burden of:
  - HIV/AIDS.
  - Tuberculosis.
  - Violence and homicide.
  - Road injuries.
  - Non-communicable diseases (growing).

Child mortality:

- Has declined, but remains high relative to rich countries.

### 6. Cluster Scenarios

Clusters:

- High-income households and corporate elites.

If:

- 200,000 high-income South Africans\* gave an extra USD 10,000/year equivalent:
  - ~USD 2B/year for CESET-focused work.

If major corporates increased CSI by 0.5–1% of profits:

- Additional hundreds of millions of USD/year.

### 7. NSS/Triad Considerations

Potential Triads:

- **HIV/TB Overcome Triad**:
  - Focused on ending the HIV/TB co-epidemic using best global practices.
- **Violence and Youth Opportunity Triad**:
  - Addressing homicide and youth unemployment.

South Africa also serves as:

- A regional hub for health and development efforts for Southern Africa.
