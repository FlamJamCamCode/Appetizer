### 4. Interpretive Notes for Djibouti (Horn of Africa / Port State \*)

- **Small, strategic economy**  
  - Djibouti is a small, lower-middle-income country with a strategic port on the Red Sea, hosting multiple foreign military bases.  
  - GDP per capita is higher than many neighbors, but the population is small and inequality, refugee presence, and regional spillovers make vulnerability patterns complex.

- **Altruism channels and packages**  
  - Domestic tax-based packages (Tax & Match) could mobilize resources, but the **absolute scale** is modest given the small population.  
  - The biggest altruistic levers likely involve:
    - **Foreign aid and base-hosting rents** earmarked for social services.  
    - **Regional cooperation** on refugees, health, and climate adaptation.  
  - In the engine, packages that channel funds into **basic health**, **cash transfers**, and **education** would map to strengthening social protection and services for both citizens and refugees.

- **Climate and future generations**  
  - Djibouti is highly exposed to climate impacts (heat, drought, sea-level rise).  
  - The climate-related components of packages (e.g., in Corporate & Billionaire Pledges or Solidarity Surcharges) would disproportionately benefit the **future_global** and **domestic_poor** equity groups in the current model.

- **Next refinement steps**  
  - Obtain better data on refugee populations, social spending, and aid composition.  
  - Reflect Djibouti’s role as a host of foreign bases by modeling part of the altruistic flows as **international commitments** rather than purely domestic choices.
