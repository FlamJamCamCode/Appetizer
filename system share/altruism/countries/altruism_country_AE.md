## Altruism Country Deep Dive – United Arab Emirates (UAE, AE)

> **Status**: First-pass scaffold with approximate ranges (**\***). To be refined with UAE budget, aid, and philanthropy data.

### 1. Macro Context

- Population: ~10M\* (large expatriate share).
- GDP (nominal): ~USD 500–550B/year\*.
- GDP per capita: ~USD 45,000–55,000/year\* (higher for citizens).
- High-income federation with significant oil and non-oil sectors, and major expat workforce.

### 2. Government Social Spending and Fiscal Flows

Approximate:

- Total government expenditure: ~30% of GDP\*.
- Social spending:
  - Health: ~4% of GDP\*.
  - Education: ~4–5% of GDP\*.
  - Subsidies and social benefits for citizens are significant but often not coded as “social spending” in OECD sense.

### 3. International Aid and Development Flows

The UAE is:

- A significant donor of development and humanitarian aid.
- Some years has reported ODA-like flows above 1% of GNI when including:
  - Grants and concessional loans.
  - In-kind and project support.

ODA/GNI:

- Often reported around 0.5–1.0%\*.

Focus areas:

- Humanitarian aid in MENA region.
- Infrastructure and development projects.
- Support to Islamic and Arab partner countries.

### 4. Private Philanthropy and Religious Giving

Zakat and Sadaqah:

- As in Saudi Arabia, zakat obligations (around 2.5% of qualifying wealth) are significant.
- Some collected via official channels; much given directly.

Formal philanthropy:

- Foundations linked to royal families and major business groups.
- Corporate social responsibility and large-scale donations, including for global causes.

### 5. Informal Altruism and Remittances

Remittances:

- Expatriate workers send large remittances to home countries (India, Pakistan, Bangladesh, Philippines, etc.), representing:
  - Massive indirect altruistic flows supporting families and communities abroad.

Informal giving:

- Religious charities.
- Community initiatives.

### 6. Cause-Area Context

Domestic:

- Leading causes of death:
  - Non-communicable diseases (CVD, diabetes, obesity).
  - Injuries (especially road accidents).
  - Some occupational risks.

International:

- UAE-origin aid and philanthropy targets:
  - Humanitarian crises (e.g. Yemen, Syria).
  - Development in MENA, Africa, South Asia.

### 7. Cluster Scenarios

Clusters:

- High-wealth citizens and business elites.
- Large corporate groups.

If:

- 50,000 wealthy individuals\* with average net wealth USD 10M committed 1% of wealth/year:
  - Flows ~USD 5B/year for CESET-optimized work.

Combined with:

- Official aid and zakat-inspired commitments, UAE could:
  - Substantially impact health, food security, and reconstruction across several conflict-affected regions.

### 8. NSS/Triad Considerations

Potential Triads centered on UAE:

- **Ummah Humanitarian Triad**:
  - Coordinating relief and development across the Muslim world.
- **Zakat Optimization Triad**:
  - Similar to Saudi case but with UAE’s strong financial and logistics infrastructure.
- **Climate and Water Triad**:
  - Addressing water scarcity, desalination, and climate resilience in arid regions.
