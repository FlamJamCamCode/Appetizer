## Altruism Country Deep Dive – Venezuela (VE)

> **Status**: First-pass scaffold with approximate ranges (**\***).

### 1. Macro Context

- Population: ~29M\*
- GDP (nominal): highly uncertain; rough range ~USD 60–90B/year\* post-crisis.
- GDP per capita: ~USD 2,000–3,000/year\*.
- Former upper-middle-income oil state now in prolonged economic and political crisis with massive emigration.

### 2. Government Social Spending and Fiscal Flows

Pre-crisis:

- High social spending funded by oil revenues.

Post-crisis:

- Severe contraction of:
  - Formal social services.
  - Health system.
  - Social protection in real terms.

Data quality:

- Very limited and contested in recent years.

### 3. ODA and Humanitarian Flows

- Venezuela is now:
  - A significant target of humanitarian assistance (food, health).
  - Source of ODA to others: essentially none.

### 4. Private Philanthropy and Informal Altruism

Formal:

- Domestic philanthropy constrained by crisis and political environment.

Informal:

- Massive role for:
  - Diaspora remittances.
  - Community mutual aid.

### 5. Cause-Area Context

Domestic burden:

- Child and maternal mortality have worsened.
- Causes of death:
  - NCDs (CVD, diabetes, cancers).
  - Infectious diseases reappearing (measles, malaria in some regions).
  - Malnutrition-related conditions.

Clusters:

Triads:

  - Combining food, health, and institutional rebuilding.
