## Altruism Country Deep Dive – Sudan (SD)

> **Status**: First-pass scaffold with approximate ranges (**\***).

### 1. Macro Context

- Population: ~46M\*
- GDP (nominal): highly uncertain, ~USD 30–40B/year\* post-secession and amid conflict.
- GDP per capita: ~USD 700–900/year\*.
- Large Northeast African country with long history of civil wars, recent coups, and economic crisis.

### 2. Government / De Facto Authority Social Spending

- Fragmented authority and ongoing conflict.
- Historically:
  - Low formal social spending.
  - High military and security spending.

Approximate:

- Total public spending: ~20–25% of GDP\*.
- Social spending: ~8–10% of GDP\*, much of it ODA-backed.

### 3. ODA and Humanitarian Assistance

- Sudan is a **major humanitarian crisis context**:
  - ODA and humanitarian inflows: billions USD/year\*.
  - Focus:
    - Food assistance.
    - Health and nutrition.
    - Support for IDPs and refugees.

### 4. Private Altruism and Remittances

Informal:

- Family and tribal/clan support.
- Diaspora remittances important.
- Religious giving (zakat) significant but under-measured.

Formal:

- Local and international NGOs; Islamic charities.

### 5. Cause-Area Context

CESET profile:

- Under‑5 mortality: very high (~60–80 per 1,000\*).
  - Neonatal disorders.
  - Diarrhea, pneumonia.
  - Malnutrition.
- Maternal mortality: very high.
- Conflict-related deaths, especially with recent fighting in Khartoum and Darfur.

### 6. Cluster Scenarios & Triads

Clusters:

- Sudanese diaspora.
- Global humanitarian donors.
- Regional actors (Gulf states, neighbours).

Triads:

- **Sudan Emergency Health & Food Triad**:
  - Focused on keeping health/nutrition systems functioning amid conflict.
- **Nile Basin Stability Triad**:
  - Longer-term architecture linking Ethiopia–Sudan–Egypt.
