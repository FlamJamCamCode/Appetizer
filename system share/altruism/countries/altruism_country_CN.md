## Altruism Country Deep Dive – China (CN)

> **Status**: First-pass scaffold with approximate ranges (**\***). To be refined with Chinese budget, philanthropy, and OWID/WHO data.

### 1. Macro Context

- Population: ~1.41B\*
- GDP (nominal): ~USD 17–18T/year\*
- GDP per capita: ~USD 12,000–13,000/year\*
- Upper-middle-income, rapidly developed economy with large internal disparities.

### 2. Government Social Spending and Fiscal Flows

Approximate social expenditure\*:

- Total social spending: ~12–16% of GDP.
  - Health: ~5–6% of GDP\*.
  - Pensions and social security: ~5–6% of GDP\*.
  - Other social programmes: ~2–4% of GDP\*.

Other:

- Education: ~4% of GDP.
- Defense: ~1.7–2.0% of GDP.

### 3. International Aid and Development Finance

China is:

- A major provider of development finance through:
  - Policy banks (China Exim Bank, China Development Bank).
  - Belt and Road Initiative (BRI) projects.
- Many flows are:
  - Loans, export credits, and investments rather than grant-based ODA.
- DAC-equivalent ODA/GNI estimate: ~0.04–0.1%\*.

### 4. Private Philanthropy and Informal Altruism

Formal giving:

- Growing philanthropic sector:
  - Large tech and business fortunes creating foundations.
  - Corporate philanthropy.
- Estimates: formal giving <0.5% of GDP\*.

Informal:

- Family support is extremely important.
- Significant rural-urban remittances.
- Strong mutual-aid responses during disasters.

### 5. Volunteering and Civil Society

- Rapidly expanding civil society within the parameters allowed by the state.
- Volunteer work around:
  - Disaster relief.
  - Poverty alleviation campaigns.
  - Environmental projects.

### 6. Cause-Area Context

Within China:

- Leading causes of death:
  - Non-communicable diseases:
    - Cardiovascular diseases.
    - Cancers.
    - Chronic respiratory diseases.
  - Injuries (road injuries) still significant.
- Child mortality has dropped dramatically but remains above rich-country levels.
- Environmental health:
  - Air pollution-related morbidity and mortality are important.

Internationally:

- China’s development finance and aid impact:
  - Infrastructure, energy, and health systems in Africa, Asia, Latin America.

### 7. Cluster Scenarios

Clusters:

- High-net-worth individuals in major cities (Beijing, Shanghai, Shenzhen, etc.).
- Overseas Chinese diaspora controlling large wealth.

If, for example:

- 1M HNW individuals in China\* with average net wealth USD 5M committed 1% of wealth/year:
  - 1M × 0.01 × 5M = **USD 50B/year**.

At 2% of wealth:

- **USD 100B/year**, capable of:
  - Dramatically impacting global child mortality and infectious disease burdens.
  - Funding large parts of decarbonization and climate adaptation.

### 8. NSS/Triad Considerations

China is a potential anchor for:

- **Infrastructure and Health Systems Triads** in developing countries.
- **Air Pollution and Environmental Health Triads** domestically and abroad.
- **Aging Triads** as China also faces rapid population aging.
