### 4. Interpretive Notes for Kenya (Nilotic / Lake Victoria Region \*)

- **Health and education context**  
  - Kenya is a lower-middle-income country with substantial progress in education and health, but persistent burdens from **malaria, HIV, TB, and maternal/child conditions**, especially in rural and informal-urban areas.  
  - There is a large ecosystem of NGOs and community-based organizations, particularly in global health and education.

- **Engine’s package ranking and interpretation**  
  - The engine currently identifies **Angels-lite** as the top package by DALYs-equivalent impact for Kenya.  
  - This reflects that:
    - Concentrated giving from a committed minority (e.g., high-income Kenyans and global donors focused on Kenya),  
    - Directed to highly effective causes (malaria prevention, deworming, basic health, targeted cash),  
    - Produces much larger modeled health gains than moderate, broad-based norm shifts.

- **Cause composition and equity**  
  - For Kenya, the health-heavy cause mix in the engine (malaria, deworming, basic health) aligns reasonably well with the real disease burden, especially around Lake Victoria and coastal regions.  
  - Equity splits imply that a majority of DALYs from these components accrue to **domestic poor** (rural and peri-urban communities), with some share to **future_global** via education and climate-related benefits.

- **State vs NGO vs private channels**  
  - Kenya has:
    - Significant **government health and education spending**,  
    - A strong **NGO and faith-based provider** presence, and  
    - An emerging culture of domestic philanthropy and corporate social investment.  
  - This suggests that packages combining:
    - Modest **tax-based** increases,  
    - Strong **NGO/EA-style targeting** of funds, and  
    - Encouraged **middle-class and diaspora giving**,  
  - are plausible pathways for turning the model’s scenarios into reality.

- **Next refinement steps**  
  - Use Kenya-specific cost-effectiveness literature (e.g., for malaria and deworming in East Africa) to refine cause DALY-per-dollar values.  
  - Incorporate Kenya’s actual ODA, remittance, and philanthropy data to better distinguish domestic vs foreign altruistic flows.
