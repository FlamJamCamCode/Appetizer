## Altruism Country Deep Dive – Chad (TD)

> **Status**: First-pass scaffold with approximate ranges (**\***). To be refined with Chadian budget, aid, and health data.

### 1. Macro Context

- Population: ~18M\*
- GDP (nominal): ~USD 11–13B/year\*
- GDP per capita: ~USD 600–800/year\*
- Landlocked Sahel country with oil revenues, high poverty, and recurring conflict/insecurity.

### 2. Government Social Spending and Fiscal Flows

Approximate:

- Total government expenditure: ~20–25% of GDP\*.
- Social spending (health, education, social protection): ~6–8% of GDP\*.
  - Health: ~3% of GDP\*.
  - Education: ~3–4% of GDP\*.
  - Social protection: very limited.

Oil revenues:

- Provide fiscal resources but have historically been:
  - Volatile.
  - Partly directed to security and debt service.

### 3. ODA – Heavy Recipient

- ODA inflows significant, often **>8% of GNI**\*.
- Focused on:
  - Health and nutrition.
  - Food security and humanitarian relief.
  - Governance, security, and refugee support (Chad hosts refugees from neighbouring conflicts).

### 4. Private Philanthropy and Informal Altruism

Formal:

- Very limited domestic philanthropic institutions.

Informal:

- Family and clan-based support.
- Religious giving (Islam, Christianity).

### 5. Cause-Area Context

Chad has:

- Extremely high under‑5 mortality:
  - Neonatal conditions.
  - Malaria.
  - Diarrhea and pneumonia.
  - Severe malnutrition.
- Significant maternal mortality.
- Burdens from:
  - Conflict and displacement.
  - Climate stress (Sahel droughts, Lake Chad shrinkage).

### 6. Cluster Scenarios

External clusters:

- Sahel-focused NGOs, philanthropic foundations.
- Global health and nutrition donors.

Scenario:

- Targeted additional funding of **USD 500M–1B/year** for:
  - Health systems.
  - Nutrition.
  - Water and sanitation.
  - Livelihood resilience.

Could:

- Dramatically shrink the cause-of-death profile dominated by preventable child and maternal causes.

### 7. NSS/Triad Considerations

Chad fits naturally into:

- **Sahel Child Survival Triad**.
- **Water, Climate, and Livelihoods Triad**:
  - Building adaptation and sustainability under changing climate.
