## Altruism Country Deep Dive – Kazakhstan (KZ)

> **Status**: First-pass scaffold with approximate ranges (**\***). To be refined with Kazakh budget, philanthropy, and health data.

### 1. Macro Context

- Population: ~19M\*
- GDP (nominal): ~USD 230–250B/year\*
- GDP per capita: ~USD 12,000–13,000/year\*
- Upper-middle-income, resource-rich Central Asian state with Soviet legacy and growing regional role.

### 2. Government Social Spending and Fiscal Flows

Approximate:

- Total government expenditure: ~20–25% of GDP\*.
- Social spending (health, pensions, social programmes): ~10–12% of GDP\*.
  - Health: ~3–4% of GDP\*.
  - Pensions and social security: ~5–6% of GDP\*.
  - Other social assistance: ~2–3% of GDP\*.

### 3. ODA – Emerging Donor/Recipient

- Kazakhstan:
  - Receives some ODA.
  - Also provides some regional assistance (to other Central Asian states and Afghanistan).
- ODA given as % of GNI: small (<0.1%)\*.

### 4. Private Philanthropy and Informal Altruism

Formal:

- Emerging philanthropic foundations tied to:
  - Energy and mining wealth.
  - Financial sector.

Informal:

- Strong family and clan-based support structures.
- Islamic giving plays a role (though secular state).

### 5. Cause-Area Context

Within Kazakhstan:

- Leading causes of death:
  - NCDs (CVD, cancers).
  - Injuries (road accidents).
  - Some legacy of environmental health issues (e.g. Aral Sea, former nuclear test sites).

Child mortality:

- Higher than Western Europe but much lower than Sahel; improving over time.

### 6. Cluster Scenarios

Clusters:

- High-wealth individuals tied to oil, gas, and mining sectors.
- Emerging middle class in urban centers (Almaty, Astana).

If:

- 50,000 high-wealth individuals\* with average net wealth USD 5M committed 1% of wealth/year:
  - ~USD 2.5B/year.

Could fund:

- Regional Triads on:
  - Environmental remediation.
  - Health systems strengthening.
  - Education and youth opportunity in Central Asia.

### 7. NSS/Triad Considerations

Potential Triads:

- **Central Asia Environment and Health Triad**:
  - Addressing legacy pollution, air quality, and related NCDs.
- **Regional Stability and Development Triad**:
  - Economic diversification and human capital development.
