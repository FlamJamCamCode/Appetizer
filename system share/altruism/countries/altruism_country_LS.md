## Altruism Country Deep Dive – Lesotho (LS)

> **Status**: First-pass scaffold with approximate ranges (**\***).

### 1. Macro Context

- Population: ~2.3M\*
- GDP (nominal): ~USD 2–3B/year\*
- GDP per capita: ~USD 850–1,100/year\*
- Landlocked enclave within South Africa; mountainous, lower-middle-income, with high HIV prevalence and large migrant labour patterns.

### 2. Government Social Spending and Fiscal Flows

Approximate:

- Total public expenditure: ~40–45% of GDP\* (substantial SACU customs revenue).
- Social spending (health, education, social protection): ~20–22% of GDP\*.
  - Health: ~7–8% of GDP\*.
  - Education: ~8–9% of GDP\*.
  - Social protection: ~4–5% of GDP\*.

### 3. ODA and External Finance

- Significant ODA inflows:
  - Health (notably HIV/AIDS, TB).
  - Rural development.

### 4. Private Philanthropy and Informal Altruism

Informal:

- Remittances from migrant workers in South Africa.
- Church and community-based support.

Formal:

- Local NGOs and faith-based organisations.

### 5. Cause-Area Context

Lesotho’s CESET profile:

- Under‑5 mortality: ~60–70 per 1,000\*.
  - Neonatal disorders.
  - Lower respiratory infections and diarrhea.
- HIV prevalence:
  - Among the highest in the world; significant adult mortality without ART.

### 6. Cluster Scenarios & Triads

Clusters:

- SACU customs revenue sharing.
- Mining wages and remittances.

Triads:

- **Lesotho HIV & Child Survival Triad**:
  - Focused on making Lesotho a “zero preventable child death + controlled HIV” demonstration state.
