Branching Navigation - Git-Style

Main branch (chapters) with sub-branches that appear in a side column only while you are inside that chapter. Think of it as a book index crossed with git branches: contextual, scoped, and merging back when done.

What to Look For

  • Main branch: Scroll a bit and a horizontal chapter bar appears under the header.
  • Chapter progress: Each chapter button fills from left to right as you progress through its content.
  • Contextual branches: When you enter a chapter, its sub-branches appear in the right-hand column.
  • Scoped branches: Sub-branches vanish when you move to another chapter – they don't clutter the global view.
  • Click navigation: Click a chapter or sub-branch to jump there; scroll again to see progress update.
  • Edge case: Chapter 4 has no sub-branches – notice that the side column stays empty there.
